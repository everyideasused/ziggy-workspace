## Part 2: Service Weekend Plan

### Saturday: Wrangler JKU (Coolant Flush + Oil Change)

**Time Estimate:** 3–4 hours  
**Order of operations:** Oil change first (engine warm), then coolant flush.

#### Oil Change — 2013 Wrangler (Detailed Procedure)

**Preparation:**
1. Park on level ground, set parking brake, chock wheels
2. Run engine 5–10 minutes to warm oil (flows better, suspends contaminants)
3. Gather tools: 13mm socket, 24mm deep socket, torque wrench, drain pan (at least 7 qt), shop towels, new crush washer (M14), new filter cartridge + O-ring

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Position drain pan** under oil pan drain plug (driver side, toward rear of pan)

2. **Remove drain plug** — 13mm socket, counterclockwise. Oil will shoot out initially, then stream. Let drain 10–15 minutes until dripping stops.

3. **Inspect drain plug** — Clean magnet tip (catches metal debris). Install new M14 crush washer.

4. **Reinstall drain plug** — Hand thread first to avoid cross-threading aluminum pan. Torque to **25 ft-lb** (34 Nm). Don't over-torque — aluminum strips easily.

5. **Move to filter housing** — Passenger side, front of engine. Large black plastic cap with 24mm hex.

6. **Remove filter cap** — 24mm deep socket + extension. Loosen slowly — some oil will drip. Remove cap with filter attached.

7. **Remove old filter cartridge** — Pull straight out of cap. It will be saturated with oil.

8. **Replace O-ring** — Pry old large O-ring from cap groove with small screwdriver. Clean groove. Install new O-ring from filter kit. Lightly lubricate with fresh oil.

9. **Install new filter cartridge** — Push new cartridge firmly into cap until it seats fully (you'll feel/hear it click into place).

10. **Reinstall cap** — Hand thread first. Torque to **18 ft-lb** (24 Nm). This is critical — plastic housing cracks if over-torqued.

11. **Fill engine** — Remove oil fill cap on valve cover. Add 5.5 qt of 5W-20 synthetic. Wait 2 minutes, check dipstick. Add remaining 0.5 qt to reach full mark.

12. **Start engine** — Run 30 seconds, check for leaks under filter housing and drain plug.

13. **Check oil level** — Wait 2 minutes, recheck dipstick. Top off if needed.

14. **Reset oil change monitor** — Key ON (don't start), press gas pedal to floor 3 times within 10 seconds. Turn key off. Restart — light should be off.

**Air Filter Replacement:**

1. Open hood, locate air box behind battery on driver side
2. Remove 6 screws (T20 Torx or Phillips screwdriver)
3. Lift top half of housing, remove old filter
4. Inspect air box for debris, clean if needed
5. Install new filter with rubber gasket facing down/seated
6. Replace top half, install 6 screws snug (don't over-torque plastic)

#### Coolant Flush — 2013 Wrangler (HOAT System)

**Preparation:**
- Engine must be **cool** — hot coolant causes severe burns
- 15+ qt drain pan (catch pan, not just oil pan)
- 4+ gallons distilled water
- 2 gallons HOAT coolant (amber/Zerex G-05)
- Funnel with long neck
- Coolant tester strips (optional but recommended)

**Capacity:** ~13 qt | **Type:** HOAT (G-05/Zerex — amber color, NOT green)

**⚠️ CRITICAL:** Using OAT (purple) or universal green will damage the water pump and cause corrosion. Use HOAT only.

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Drain radiator** — Locate petcock on bottom driver side of radiator (white plastic). Place drain pan. Turn petcock counterclockwise 2–3 turns (don't remove completely). Collect 2–3 gallons. Close petcock.

2. **Check for block drain** — Some JK blocks have a drain plug near the starter. If accessible, remove (14mm) to drain block. If not accessible, skip — radiator drain is sufficient for most flushes.

3. **First flush cycle** — Remove radiator cap. Add 2 gallons distilled water through radiator fill neck. Reinstall cap loosely.

4. **Run engine** — Start, run 10 minutes with heater ON full blast (opens heater core circuit). Monitor temp gauge — should stay normal.

5. **Drain again** — Let cool 30 minutes. Open petcock, drain. Water will be dirty/cleaner depending on old coolant condition.

6. **Repeat flush** — If water is still colored/discolored, repeat steps 3–5 until water runs clear.

7. **Final fill** — Close all drains. Mix 50/50: 1 gallon HOAT concentrate + 1 gallon distilled water in clean container. Pour into radiator.

8. **Top off** — Add more 50/50 mix until level stabilizes at COLD FULL mark in reservoir.

9. **Burp system** — Run engine 15 minutes with radiator cap OFF. Heater ON full blast. Squeeze upper radiator hose periodically to help air escape. Add coolant as level drops.

10. **Final check** — Install cap when full and no more bubbles. Drive 10 minutes, recheck level when cool. Top off if needed.

**JK Specific:** The heater core sits high in the system. If heat blows cold after refill, air is trapped in core. Park nose-up on incline, run with cap off, squeeze hoses until heat returns.

#### Coolant Flush — 2013 Wrangler (HOAT System)

**Capacity:** ~13 qt | **Type:** HOAT (G-05/Zerex — amber color, NOT green)

| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| 1. Drain radiator | Petcock bottom driver side. Catch 2–3 gal |
| 2. Drain block | Some JK blocks have a drain plug near starter. If not, skip |
| 3. Close drains | Reinstall petcock (don't overtighten) |
| 4. Fill with distilled | 2 gal distilled water |
| 5. Run engine | 10 min, heater ON full blast |
| 6. Drain again | Repeat until water runs clear |
| 7. Final fill | 50/50 HOAT + distilled to COLD FULL line |
| 8. Burp system | Run with cap OFF, squeeze hoses, add as needed |

**JK Specific:** The heater core is high in the system. Keep the heater ON during fill to purge air from the core.

### Sunday: Grand Cherokee WK2 (Coolant Flush + Oil Change)

**Time Estimate:** 5–6 hours (air suspension, EPB considerations, thermostat add-on)  
**Order of operations:** Oil change first, then coolant flush.

#### Oil Change — 2014 Grand Cherokee (Detailed Procedure)

**Note:** Same 3.6L Pentastar as Wrangler — procedure is identical. Only differences are access (more room on GC) and oil life reset method.

**Preparation:**
1. Park level, parking brake, chock wheels
2. Warm engine 5–10 minutes
3. Tools: 13mm socket, 24mm deep socket, torque wrench, drain pan, shop towels, new M14 crush washer, new filter cartridge + O-ring

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Position drain pan** under oil pan drain plug (driver side, rear of pan)

2. **Remove drain plug** — 13mm socket. Let drain 10–15 minutes.

3. **Inspect drain plug** — Clean magnet tip. Install new M14 crush washer.

4. **Reinstall drain plug** — Hand thread, then torque **25 ft-lb** (34 Nm).

5. **Filter housing** — Passenger front of engine. Large black plastic cap, 24mm hex.

6. **Remove filter cap** — 24mm deep socket. Loosen slowly (oil drips).

7. **Remove old cartridge** — Pull straight out of cap.

8. **Replace O-ring** — Pry old O-ring from cap groove. Clean groove. Install new O-ring, lube with fresh oil.

9. **Install new cartridge** — Push firmly into cap until seated/clicks.

10. **Reinstall cap** — Hand thread, torque **18 ft-lb** (24 Nm). Critical — plastic cracks.

11. **Fill engine** — Add 5.5 qt 5W-20 synthetic. Wait 2 min, check dipstick. Add remaining 0.5 qt to full.

12. **Start engine** — Run 30 sec, check for leaks.

13. **Reset oil life monitor** — Use steering wheel controls:
    - Press MENU button repeatedly until "VEHICLE INFO" displays
    - Scroll to "OIL LIFE"
    - Press and hold OK button until display shows "100%"
    - (Alternative: Key ON, gas pedal 3x in 10 sec also works on most WK2)

**Air Filter Replacements:**

*Engine Air Filter:*
1. Open hood, locate large rectangular housing behind driver headlight
2. Four metal spring clips — unhook all four (no tools)
3. Lift top half of housing
4. Remove old filter, inspect housing for debris
5. Install new filter (orientation doesn't matter — rectangular)
6. Close top, re-hook four clips — 2 minutes total

*Cabin Air Filter:*
1. Open passenger glove box
2. Locate white plastic stops on each side of glove box (behind rubber bumpers)
3. Push both stops inward simultaneously while lowering glove box — it will drop down freely
4. Locate white plastic filter frame behind glove box
5. Slide filter frame out (may be tight — pull firmly)
6. Note airflow direction arrow on old filter (points DOWN toward floor)
7. Remove old filter from frame, insert new filter with arrow pointing DOWN
8. Slide frame back into housing until it clicks/stops
9. Lift glove box, push stops back into place — 5 minutes total

#### Critical GC Difference: Electric Parking Brake (EPB)

If you touch the rear brakes for any reason (you won't for oil/coolant, but good to know):

**⚠️ CANNOT use C-clamp on rear calipers.** The EPB motor is integrated. Requires scan tool (JScan/AlfaOBD) to retract pistons. For oil/coolant service, not needed — but if you ever do rear brakes, it's electronic retract or caliper damage.

#### Coolant Flush — 2014 Grand Cherokee (OAT System)

**Preparation:**
- Engine **cool** — OAT burns same as any coolant
- 15+ qt drain pan
- 4+ gallons distilled water
- 2 gallons Mopar OAT coolant (purple concentrate)
- Long-neck funnel
- Air suspension: If you need clearance, set to "Off-Road 2" (max height) before starting. Normal ride height is fine for most pans.

**Capacity:** ~13 qt | **Type:** Mopar OAT (purple — Extended Life)

**⚠️ CRITICAL DIFFERENCE:** The WK2 uses **OAT (Organic Acid Technology)** — purple coolant. HOAT (amber) or universal green will damage the water pump, corrode aluminum, and void any remaining warranty on cooling components. Purple only.

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Drain radiator** — Petcock located bottom of radiator, driver side. Place drain pan. Turn petcock counterclockwise 2–3 turns (don't remove). Collect 2–3 gallons. Close petcock finger-tight (don't over-torque plastic).

2. **Drain reservoir** — Coolant reservoir is on passenger side. Either:
   - Siphon out with turkey baster/vacuum pump, OR
   - Disconnect lower hose from reservoir, let drain into pan

3. **Check for block drains** — On 3.6L WK2, there may be drain plugs on engine block near bellhousing. If accessible, remove (14mm). If not, skip — radiator flush is sufficient for most maintenance.

4. **First flush cycle** — Remove radiator cap. Add 2 gallons distilled water through fill neck. Cap loosely.

5. **Run engine** — Start, run 10 minutes, heater ON full blast.

6. **Drain again** — Cool 30 min. Open petcock, drain. Observe color — should be cleaner than first drain.

7. **Repeat** — If water still shows color, repeat steps 4–6 until draining clear.

8. **Thermostat window** — While system is empty, now is the time to replace thermostat (see below). If not replacing, proceed to fill.

9. **Final fill** — Close all drains. Mix 50/50: 1 gallon Mopar OAT purple + 1 gallon distilled. Pour into radiator.

10. **Top off** — Continue adding 50/50 until level stabilizes at COLD FULL on reservoir.

11. **Burp system** — Run 15 minutes, cap OFF, heater ON full. Squeeze upper hose. Add coolant as level drops. WK2 has higher heater core than JK — may take longer to purge air.

12. **Final check** — Cap on when full. Drive 10 minutes. Recheck level when cool.

#### Thermostat Replacement — 2014 Grand Cherokee

**Why now:** System is already drained for coolant flush. Thermostat housing is where lower radiator hose connects to water pump. 20–30 minute job while empty.

**Tools needed:**
- 10mm socket + short extension
- Ratchet
- Small flat screwdriver (for hose clamp)
- Shop towels
- New thermostat kit (Mopar 5184977AE or Gates/Stat equivalent — includes housing + O-ring + thermostat as one unit)

**⚠️ CRITICAL:** The 3.6L Pentastar uses an **integrated thermostat/housing assembly**. You cannot replace just the thermostat element. You replace the entire plastic housing unit. The kit comes with O-ring pre-installed.

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Locate housing** — Follow lower radiator hose from radiator to engine. Where it meets the engine (driver side, lower front) is the thermostat housing.

2. **Remove hose** — Use pliers or screwdriver to squeeze spring clamp, slide it back on hose. Grasp hose near housing, twist while pulling to break seal. Pull hose off nipple. Some coolant will dribble — have towel ready.

3. **Clear access** — Move hose aside. You should see 2–3 bolts (10mm) holding the black plastic housing to the aluminum water pump.

4. **Remove bolts** — 10mm socket + short extension. Remove all bolts, set aside. Note: Bolts may have thread sealant on them — this is normal.

5. **Remove housing** — Plastic housing is sealed with O-ring. It may be stuck from heat cycles. Gently pry with small flat screwdriver at seam — **do not scratch the aluminum sealing surface on the water pump**. Work around edges until housing pops free.

6. **Inspect surfaces** — Clean any old O-ring material, debris, or corrosion from aluminum water pump surface. Use plastic scraper or rag — don't gouge aluminum.

7. **Prepare new unit** — Unbox new thermostat kit. Verify O-ring is seated in groove. Apply thin film of fresh coolant or silicone paste to O-ring (helps seating, prevents pinching).

8. **Install new housing** — Align bolt holes. Press housing firmly onto water pump — O-ring will compress. Hand-thread all bolts first to ensure alignment.

9. **Torque bolts** — **8.9 Nm (79 in-lb)**. This is critical:
   - Aluminum water pump threads strip easily
   - 79 in-lb is approximately finger-tight + 1/4 turn with ratchet
   - If you don't have inch-pound torque wrench: snug with 3/8" ratchet using only wrist strength, then stop
   - **DO NOT over-torque** — stripped threads = water pump replacement ($$$)

10. **Reconnect hose** — Slide hose onto housing nipple until it seats fully. Position spring clamp over barb area.

11. **Pressure test** — After coolant refill and burping, run engine to temp and check for weeping at housing seam. Tighten slightly if needed (but you shouldn't need to if torqued correctly).

**Installation tip:** Take photo of bolt positions before removal. Some Pentastar variants have different length bolts — though most WK2 3.6L have uniform length.

**Time:** 20–30 minutes while system is drained.

## Safety Notes

1. **Hot coolant:** Let engine cool 30+ min before opening. OAT/HOAT both hot enough to cause burns.
2. **Antifreeze toxicity:** Keep pets/kids away. Coolant is sweet-tasting and lethal. Collect all spills.
3. **Torque specs:** Oil filter cap at 18 ft-lb — plastic housing cracks easily. Drain plug at 25 ft-lb — aluminum pan strips easily.
4. **Ground contamination:** Use cardboard or drip pan — coolant kills grass, pets love the taste.
