## 📋 Step-by-Step Procedures

### 🛢️ Oil Change Procedure (Both Vehicles — Same Process)

**Estimated Time:** 30-45 minutes per vehicle  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ (Easy)

#### Preparation
1. **Warm up engine** — Run for 5 minutes to suspend contaminants in oil
2. **Park on level surface** — Engage parking brake, chock rear wheels
3. **Gather tools:** 13mm socket + 3/8" ratchet, 24mm socket + 1/2" ratchet, oil drain pan, funnel, rags, new oil, new filter
4. **Remove engine cover** — Pull straight up (friction fasteners)
5. **Twist off oil filler cap** — Counter-clockwise
6. **Pull up dipstick** — 1 inch (prevents vacuum during drain)

#### Drain Oil
7. **Loosen oil filter cap FIRST** — 24mm socket, turn counter-clockwise (allows oil in filter cavity to drain to sump)
8. **Slide under passenger side** — Locate oil drain bolt on black oil pan
9. **Position drain pan** — Under drain bolt
10. **Loosen drain bolt** — 13mm socket, turn counter-clockwise (⚠️ **oil may be hot**)
11. **Spin out bolt by hand** — Let oil drain 5+ minutes until slow drip
12. **Replace drain bolt** — Torque to **20 lb-ft** (no separate washer needed — integrated seal)
13. **Double-check tightness** — No leaks

#### Replace Oil Filter
14. **Remove old filter cap** — Pull out cartridge, discard
15. **Remove old o-ring** — Pull off cap threads, discard
16. **Lubricate new o-ring** — Light coat of new oil (prevents leaks, easier removal next time)
17. **Install new o-ring** — Push onto filter cap threads
18. **Insert new cartridge filter** — Push into cap
19. **Lower filter + cap into housing** — Hand-tighten clockwise (prevent cross-threading)
20. **Tighten with 24mm socket** — **Hand-tight + 1/4 to 1/3 turn** (⚠️ **Do NOT over-tighten — plastic threads!**)
21. **Double-check tightness** — No leaks

#### Fill with New Oil
22. **Insert funnel** — Into oil filler hole
23. **Pour 5-5.5 quarts** — Leave 0.5-1qt headroom (better to under-fill than over-fill)
24. **Replace oil cap** — Twist clockwise
25. **Push down dipstick** — Fully seated
26. **Run engine 2-3 minutes** — Circulate oil, check for leaks under vehicle
27. **Shut off engine** — Let rest 5-10 minutes (oil drains to pan)
28. **Check oil level** — Remove dipstick, wipe clean, re-insert, pull out
29. **Top off if needed** — Slowly add oil until level reaches upper cross-hatch "SAFE" area
30. **Replace engine cover** — Push down to seat rubber fasteners
31. **Reset oil change indicator** — Turn ignition to RUN (don't start), press gas pedal 3x within 10 seconds, turn off

**⚠️ Safety Notes:**
- Wear nitrile gloves — used oil may be carcinogenic
- Watch for hot oil — can cause burns
- Don't over-tighten plastic oil filter cap — will crack
- Check driveway for leaks next few days

### 🌡️ Coolant Flush Procedure

**Estimated Time:** 1-1.5 hours per vehicle  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ (Moderate)

#### Grand Cherokee (OAT Purple) — 13qt Capacity

1. **Cold engine only** — Never open cooling system when hot (pressure burns!)
2. **Locate radiator drain (petcock)** — Bottom driver side of radiator
3. **Place drain pan** — Under radiator drain
4. **Open petcock** — Turn counter-clockwise, let coolant drain completely
5. **Remove radiator cap** — Speeds draining
6. **Open engine drain plugs (if accessible)** — Engine block may have drain plugs (check service manual)
7. **Flush with distilled water** — Close petcock, fill radiator with distilled water, run engine 5 min, drain, repeat 2-3x until clear
8. **Close all drains** — Tighten petcock
9. **Mix coolant** — 2 gallons Mopar 68163848AC concentrate + 2 gallons distilled water = 4 gallons 50/50 mix (~13qt needed)
10. **Fill radiator** — Pour 50/50 mix until full
11. **Fill overflow reservoir** — To MAX line
12. **Burp cooling system** — Run engine with radiator cap off, heater on HIGH, squeeze upper radiator hose to release air pockets
13. **Top off as needed** — Add coolant as air escapes
14. **Replace radiator cap** — When no more bubbles, engine at operating temp
15. **Test coolant** — Hydrometer or test strips should show -34°F freeze protection (50/50 mix)
16. **Check for leaks** — Let engine cool, check hoses, clamps, radiator

#### Wrangler (HOAT Orange) — 13qt Capacity

**Same procedure as Grand Cherokee, BUT:**
- Use **Mopar 68048953AC (HOAT) or Zerex G-05** — **NOT the purple OAT coolant!**
- **NEVER mix OAT and HOAT** — completely flush system first
- Final mix: 2 gal HOAT concentrate + 2 gal distilled water

**⚠️ Safety Notes:**
- **Never open radiator cap when hot** — pressurized coolant can cause severe burns
- **Coolant is toxic and sweet-smelling** — keep away from pets, clean up spills immediately
- **Dispose of old coolant properly** — take to auto parts store or hazardous waste facility
- **Do NOT mix coolant types** — OAT (purple) ≠ HOAT (orange) — cross-contamination voids warranty

### 🌡️ Thermostat Replacement (Grand Cherokee Only)

**Estimated Time:** 45-60 minutes  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ (Moderate-Advanced)

**⚠️ Combined with Coolant Flush:** Do thermostat replacement BEFORE refilling with new coolant.

1. **Drain coolant** — Follow steps 1-6 from coolant flush (must drain below thermostat level)
2. **Locate thermostat housing** — Upper radiator hose connects to it (front of engine)
3. **Disconnect upper radiator hose** — Loosen hose clamp, twist hose to break seal, pull off
4. **Remove thermostat housing bolts** — Usually 2-3 bolts (10mm or 13mm — verify in service manual)
5. **Pull off old thermostat housing** — May need gentle prying (don't damage mating surface)
6. **Clean mating surface** — Scrape off old gasket material, wipe clean with rag
7. **Install new thermostat + housing** — Mopar 68253514AA (203°F) includes housing, thermostat, gasket
8. **Torque housing bolts** — Service manual spec (typically 10-15 lb-ft — **verify before tightening**)
9. **Reconnect upper radiator hose** — Push onto housing, tighten clamp
10. **Proceed with coolant refill** — Follow coolant flush steps 8-16

**⚠️ Safety Notes:**
- **Verify torque specs** — plastic housings crack easily if over-tightened
- **Check for leaks** — Run engine to operating temp, inspect housing and hose connections
- **Thermostat failure symptoms:** Overheating, temp fluctuations, "Check Engine" light

**📊 Difficulty Rating:** This job is moderate-to-advanced because:
- Requires draining coolant (messy, toxic)
- Involves removing/installing housing with gasket (must be clean, properly torqued)
- Air pockets in cooling system can cause overheating if not burped properly
- **Recommendation:** If Nathan is uncomfortable, defer to a shop ($150-250 labor + parts)

### 🌬️ Air Filter Replacements (Both Vehicles)

**Estimated Time:** 10-15 minutes total  
**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ (Very Easy)

#### Engine Air Filter

1. **Open hood** — Locate air filter box (black plastic, top of engine)
2. **Unlatch air filter box** — Usually 4-6 metal clips or screws
3. **Lift lid** — Remove old filter
4. **Wipe out box** — Remove any dirt, leaves, debris
5. **Insert new filter** — Mopar 68257791AB or K&N 33-2457 (GC) / 33-2364 (JKU)
6. **Close lid** — Snap or screw clips back on
7. **Double-check seal** — No gaps (unfiltered air damages engine)

#### Cabin Air Filter

**Grand Cherokee:**
1. **Open glove box** — Empty contents
2. **Squeeze sides** — Release glove box stops, let it drop down
3. **Locate cabin filter door** — Behind glove box (usually has a tab or clip)
4. **Slide out old filter** — Note airflow direction arrow on filter
5. **Insert new filter** — Mopar 68071668AA or WIX 24809 (arrow points **toward airflow**)
6. **Close filter door** — Snap back into place
7. **Re-install glove box** — Lift up, snap stops back in

**Wrangler:**
⚠️ **Note:** Early JKU models (2007-2010) did NOT come with cabin air filters. 2013 JKU may or may not have one — **verify before ordering!**
- If equipped: Same procedure as Grand Cherokee (behind glove box)
- If NOT equipped: Skip cabin filter on shopping list
