# Restaurant TI Estimating — QSR & FSR

> Covers: Chick-fil-A, Panera, Starbucks (drive-thru/café), and comparable formats.
> This is one of the most complex TI types — kitchen, MEP, drive-thru, and brand standards all interact.

---

## Restaurant Cost Drivers (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Kitchen size and complexity** — Commercial kitchen = 2–3× the MEP cost of equivalent office SF
2. **Drive-thru** — Adds $150,000–$400,000+ in lane, technology, canopy, and exterior infra
3. **Service size** — Electrical service for restaurants often requires upgrade (400A–800A)
4. **Grease systems** — In-ground interceptor, Type I hoods, exhaust, make-up air
5. **Brand standards** — Proprietary finishes, specified vendors, required mock-up approvals
6. **Seating density and finish level** — Fast casual vs. fine dining = 2–4× finish cost

## QSR Benchmarks (Quick Service Restaurant)

**Prototype formats:** Chick-fil-A, McDonald's, Taco Bell, Panera, Starbucks drive-thru

| Format | Typical SF | Hard Cost Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| QSR standalone (single lane DT) | 2,000–3,500 SF | $180–$320/SF | Excludes FF&E, signage, utility connections |
| QSR standalone (dual lane DT) | 2,500–4,000 SF | $220–$380/SF | Dual lane premium significant |
| QSR end-cap TI | 1,800–2,800 SF | $160–$280/SF | No site work; existing shell |
| QSR inline TI | 1,500–2,500 SF | $140–$240/SF | Shared walls limit MEP routing |
| Coffee (drive-thru only) | 500–1,000 SF | $400–$700/SF | Small footprint inflates $/SF |
| Coffee (full café) | 1,200–2,000 SF | $200–$350/SF | Bar, seating, full MEP |

**Chick-fil-A Specific Notes:**
- Brand-managed construction with proprietary vendors — hard costs often $350–$500/SF at current standards
- Dual drive-thru preferred; technology package adds $80,000–$150,000
- Extensive landscaping and site lighting above average
- Owner often manages FF&E separately; GC scope includes infra

**Panera Specific Notes:**
- Hearth oven requires dedicated gas service and structural support
- Digital ordering (kiosks, digital menu boards) — infra must be coordinated
- Typical: 3,500–4,500 SF; $180–$280/SF hard cost
- Renovation of existing Panera: $100–$180/SF (brand refresh program)

**Starbucks Specific Notes:**
- Very specific brand standards; Starbucks Design & Construction manages specs
- Drive-thru formats: $350,000–$700,000 total hard cost (including site work)
- Café formats: $250–$400/SF in tenant improvement
- Drive-thru lane, speaker, canopy, window assembly — $80,000–$200,000 of that total

## Restaurant MEP Critical Path

### Plumbing
- Determine drain layout before framing — slab core and cut must happen early
- Grease interceptor: **Confirm jurisdiction requirement** — some AHJs require in-ground, others accept under-sink traps
- In-ground interceptor: Must be sized by engineer (typically 1,000–2,000 gallon), requires excavation
- Hot water demand for commercial kitchen: typically dedicated tank water heater or instantaneous system
- Three-comp sink, prep sink, hand sinks, mop sink — count fixtures and verify rough-in location

### HVAC
- Kitchen exhaust: Type I hood for all cooking equipment with open flame or grease production
  - Hood CFM drives exhaust fan sizing
  - Exhaust drives make-up air unit sizing (typically 80–90% replacement)
  - Make-up air must be conditioned in most climates
- RTU sizing for dining area separate from kitchen conditioning
- Drive-thru window: heated/cooled vestibule or air curtain often required
- Confirm structural capacity for RTU weights before locating on roof

### Electrical
- Restaurant load calculation: kitchen equipment alone often 150–300A at 480V
- Demand factor analysis required — not all equipment runs simultaneously
- Verify utility service available (3-phase, 480V most common for commercial kitchen)
- Generator: most QSR brands require backup power for POS, drive-thru, walk-ins
  - Budget $35,000–$90,000 for generator + transfer switch + connections

## Restaurant Kitchen — What the GC Must Include

Even when equipment is owner-furnished:
- All rough-in (plumbing, electric, gas) to equipment locations
- Structural reinforcement per equipment schedule
- Floor drain layout per equipment schedule
- Hood installation (some brands provide hood; GC installs and connects)
- Ventilation shaft / chase construction
- Kitchen exhaust penetration through roof
- Grease duct enclosure (UL-listed, 2-hour rated) — **frequently missed**
- Walk-in cooler/freezer infra (floor preparation, electrical, refrigeration stub-out)
- Slab seal or epoxy finish in kitchen

**Grease Duct Enclosure — Frequently Missed Budget Item**
- UL-listed grease duct requires either:
  - Listed duct wrap (3M, Speedline): $15–$35/LF installed
  - Gypsum enclosure (2-hr rated): $20–$45/LF installed
- For a 30-LF vertical run: $10,000–$20,000 — often not in early estimates

## FSR Benchmarks (Full Service Restaurant)

Full-service restaurants have higher finish and seating costs, full bar is additional:

| Format | Typical SF | Hard Cost Range |
|---|---|---|
| Casual dining (Applebee's-tier) | 4,000–6,000 SF | $140–$220/SF |
| Fast casual elevated (Panera, Shake Shack) | 2,500–4,000 SF | $180–$300/SF |
| Polished casual (sit-down, full bar) | 3,500–6,000 SF | $220–$380/SF |
| Fine dining | 3,000–6,000 SF | $350–$600/SF |
| Bar-heavy concept | 2,000–4,000 SF | $300–$500/SF |

**Full Bar Add:** Budget $75,000–$200,000 additional for bar infrastructure (plumbing, ice, glass wash, CO2, walk-in back-bar, millwork)

## Common Restaurant Estimate Errors

1. **Missing grease duct enclosure** — $10,000–$25,000 gap
2. **Underestimating electrical service** — restaurants commonly need 400–800A service
3. **Ignoring slab work** — core and cut for kitchen drains = $15,000–$40,000
4. **Missing grease interceptor** — in-ground = $15,000–$40,000 installed
5. **Drive-thru scope gaps** — see drive-thru breakdown above
6. **Undersizing make-up air** — hood CFM governs; undersized MAU = failed inspection
7. **Roof penetrations for exhaust** — structural, flashing, UL-listed curb = $3,000–$8,000 each
8. **Fire suppression for hood** — Ansul or equivalent: $3,000–$7,000/hood
9. **Walk-in infra "by owner" assumption** — GC still has slab work, electric, stub-outs
10. **Utility connection fees** — separate from construction contract, often $30,000–$150,000
