# Retail TI Estimating — Soft Goods, Hard Goods & Specialty Retail

> Covers: Target, REI, and comparable retail formats across soft goods, sporting/outdoor, and big-box.

---

## Retail Cost Drivers (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Finish specification level** — Value retailer vs. luxury = 3–5× finish cost delta
2. **Ceiling strategy** — ACT, open-to-structure, or custom = major cost driver
3. **Lighting package** — Retail lighting is often 15–25% of electrical budget alone
4. **Storefront scope** — Full replacement vs. in-fill varies widely
5. **MEP density** — Retail MEP lighter than restaurant; big-box has extensive HVAC/electrical
6. **Brand standard compliance** — National retailers have strict prototype requirements

## REI — Key Estimating Considerations

REI is a premium outdoor/sporting goods brand with above-average finish standards:

- **Finish level:** Natural materials (wood, stone, exposed elements) — budget 1.5–2× standard retail
- **Ceiling:** Often open-to-structure with pendant lighting + wood elements — labor-intensive
- **Storefront:** Full glass facade preferred; significant glazing budget
- **Sustainability:** REI targets high LEED/green standards — may require commissioning, special MEP specs
- **Display fixtures:** Typically owner-furnished, but infra (blocking, power, data) by GC
- **Typical TI hard cost:** $120–$200/SF for prototype retail locations

**REI-Specific Scope Items:**
- Dedicated bike service area with compressed air, tool stations — requires rough-in
- Climbing wall structure (if included) — significant structural and specialty scope
- Café/espresso bar — standard coffee infra required
- Fitting rooms with enhanced lighting, privacy features

## Target — Key Estimating Considerations

Target operates prototype-driven construction with strict corporate standards:

**Full-Size Target (80,000–140,000 SF):**
- Ground-up: $60–$100/SF hard cost (structural, shell, and TI combined)
- Remodel: $30–$65/SF (varies enormously based on scope depth)
- Prototype refresh program: $15–$35/SF (finishes and MEP updates only)

**Target Small Format (20,000–40,000 SF):**
- Urban locations; multi-story sometimes; higher per-SF cost
- $80–$130/SF TI hard cost
- Full food/grocery section: add $15–$25/SF for refrigeration and food service

**Target MEP Notes:**
- Large retail requires significant HVAC: 1 ton per 250–400 SF rule-of-thumb
- Electrical service for full Target: 2,000–4,000A service common
- Extensive data/network for inventory and POS
- Target grocery section: refrigerated cases are OFE; GC provides infra (drains, power, data)

## Retail Finish Specifications by Tier

### Value / Budget Retail
- Floors: VCT or polished concrete
- Ceilings: Standard ACT (2×4, white)
- Walls: Painted GWB
- Lighting: Fluorescent/LED troffers
- Storefront: Standard aluminum

### Mid-Market Retail (Target, REI)
- Floors: LVT, polished concrete, or carpet tile
- Ceilings: Open-to-structure or premium ACT with accent lighting
- Walls: Painted GWB with feature walls (wood, panel, graphic)
- Lighting: Track and pendant LED with programmable controls
- Storefront: Enhanced aluminum with full-height glazing

### Premium / Luxury Retail
- Floors: Natural stone, hardwood, or large-format porcelain
- Ceilings: Gypsum with custom coffers, or open ceiling with acoustic treatment
- Walls: Custom millwork, stone, custom paint/wallcovering
- Lighting: Designer fixtures, custom lighting design
- Storefront: Curtain wall or custom storefront system

## Retail Electrical — Key Notes

Retail lighting is often the largest single electrical cost:
- Standard retail: 2–3 watts/SF lighting load
- High-end retail: 4–6 watts/SF lighting load
- Lighting controls are increasingly required by energy codes (Title 24, ASHRAE 90.1)
- Track lighting: $15–$35/LF for track; $150–$600/fixture
- Exterior signage requires dedicated circuit and disconnect per sign — budget $1,500–$3,000/sign location

**Large Format Retail Electrical Milestones:**
- 5,000 SF specialty: 200–400A service
- 20,000 SF mid-box: 800–1,200A service
- 100,000 SF big-box: 2,000–4,000A service

## Tenant Improvement Allowance (TIA) Context

Most retail is build-out via TIA from landlord. Key estimating nuances:

- **TIA is rarely enough** — National tenants negotiate hard but TIAs typically cover 60–80% of build-out cost
- **Above-TIA costs:** Brand upgrades, premium finishes, specialty items beyond landlord standard
- **Landlord work vs. tenant work:** Define the line clearly — who does HVAC, storefront, shell modifications?
- **TIA validation:** Estimator's job is to determine if the TIA is sufficient and where the gap is

**TIA Validation Worksheet:**
1. Establish total hard cost estimate (using this KB)
2. Identify what landlord is providing (shell delivery condition)
3. Calculate tenant's scope of work
4. Compare to TIA amount
5. Document gap and recommend negotiation strategy

## Common Retail Estimate Errors

1. **Ignoring existing conditions** — tenant gets space in "as-is" condition; survey before estimating
2. **Assuming standard MEP stub-out locations** — always confirm what landlord is delivering
3. **Missing storefront modification** — removing/reconfiguring storefront is $50,000–$150,000+
4. **Underestimating lighting** — retail lighting packages often $25–$50/SF for premium brands
5. **FF&E installation** — owner-furnished display fixtures still require GC coordination and infra
6. **Egress / code compliance** — occupancy change triggers full ADA compliance review
7. **Signage infrastructure** — conduit, disconnects, and structural backing for sign panels
8. **Fire alarm modification** — any storefront or partition change may trigger full FA upgrade
