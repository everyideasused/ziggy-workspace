# Appendix — Grocery & Supermarket Construction

> Industry appendix for [[Construction PM Knowledge Base]] and [[Sector Profiles — Retail, Commercial, Residential, Civil]]

---

## Overview

Grocery and supermarket construction is among the most complex retail build-outs due to heavy MEP requirements (refrigeration, kitchen/bakery, HVAC for varied temperature zones), food safety regulations, and the sheer volume of specialized equipment. A grocery store has more in common with a light industrial facility than a typical retail shop.

---

## What Makes Grocery Unique

### Refrigeration Systems
The single biggest differentiator. A typical grocery store has:
- **Walk-in coolers and freezers** (dairy, meat, produce, bakery, floral)
- **Refrigerated display cases** (reach-in and open multi-deck)
- **Rack refrigeration system** (centralized compressor system serving all cases)
- **Condenser units** (rooftop or ground-mounted, rejecting heat from refrigeration)
- **Glycol or CO2 secondary loop systems** (newer, more efficient designs)
- **Refrigerant piping** throughout the store (insulated copper or steel)
- **Energy management / controls** for the entire refrigeration plant

**Impact on construction:** Refrigeration piping must be installed early (often concurrent with rough MEP), requires specialized contractors, and the system commissioning is on the critical path for store opening.

### Temperature Zones
A grocery store operates multiple temperature environments simultaneously:

| Zone | Temperature | Areas |
|------|------------|-------|
| Sales floor (ambient) | 68-72°F | Main shopping area |
| Produce area | 65-68°F | Produce department |
| Walk-in coolers | 34-38°F | Dairy, deli, meat, produce prep |
| Walk-in freezers | -10 to 0°F | Frozen food, ice cream, meat storage |
| Bakery | 72-78°F | Oven area runs warm |
| Prep kitchens | 68-72°F | Deli, bakery, meat cutting |

**HVAC impact:** Separate zones with independent temperature control; dehumidification critical to prevent condensation on refrigerated cases.

### Food Safety & Health Department
- **Health department plan review** required in most jurisdictions
- **Commercial kitchen requirements:** Grease traps, three-compartment sinks, hand sinks at every prep station, commercial hood systems, fire suppression (Ansul) over cooking equipment
- **Floor drainage:** Floor drains required in wet areas (meat, seafood, bakery, produce prep)
- **Floor finishes:** Must be seamless, non-porous, easily cleanable in food prep areas (typically sealed concrete, quarry tile, or epoxy)
- **NSF-rated equipment:** All food-contact surfaces must be NSF International certified
- **Walk-in construction:** Insulated metal panels (IMP), vapor barriers, heated door frames (freezers), strip curtains

## Specialized MEP Systems

### Electrical — Heavy Load
| System | Typical Load |
|--------|-------------|
| Refrigeration compressors | 150-400+ kW |
| HVAC | 50-150 kW |
| Lighting (sales floor + back of house) | 30-80 kW |
| Bakery/deli cooking equipment | 50-100 kW |
| POS, IT, security | 10-20 kW |
| **Total typical grocery (45,000 SF)** | **300-800 kW** |

- Often requires **dedicated transformer** or upgraded utility service
- Emergency/standby **generator** common (refrigeration protection during power outages)
- Utility coordination and service upgrade can be a long-lead item (8-16+ weeks)

### Plumbing — Complex
- Grease interceptor (exterior, in-ground — sized per local code)
- Hot water demand is high (bakery, deli, cleaning)
- Multiple floor drains with proper slope-to-drain
- Backflow prevention on domestic water
- Reverse osmosis systems (some bakery/coffee programs)
- Ice machine drainage and water supply

### HVAC — Multi-Zone
- Rooftop units (RTUs) serving sales floor zones
- Make-up air units for kitchen hood exhaust
- Dedicated units for server/IT rooms
- Dehumidification integration with refrigeration heat reclaim
- Heat reclaim from refrigeration used for store heating (energy recovery)

## Equipment Procurement

Grocery equipment is highly specialized and often **owner-furnished / contractor-installed (OFCI)**:

### Owner-Furnished Equipment (Typical)
- Refrigerated display cases
- Rack refrigeration system
- POS / checkout systems
- Bakery ovens, proofers, mixers
- Deli slicers, fryers, rotisseries
- Pharmacy fixtures (if pharmacy included)
- Floral coolers
- Shopping carts and cart corrals

### Contractor-Furnished & Installed
- Walk-in cooler/freezer panels and doors
- Kitchen hood and fire suppression
- Shelving and gondola systems (sometimes OFCI)
- Checkout counters (sometimes OFCI)

### Lead Times
| Equipment | Lead Time |
|-----------|----------|
| Refrigeration rack system | 12-20 weeks |
| Refrigerated cases | 10-16 weeks |
| Walk-in cooler/freezer panels | 8-12 weeks |
| Commercial kitchen equipment | 8-14 weeks |
| POS / checkout systems | 6-10 weeks |
| Generator | 16-30 weeks |

**Critical:** Equipment procurement must begin at or before CD phase to avoid schedule delays.

## Construction Sequence — Grocery Specific

Standard sequence (see [[07 - Construction Execution]]) with these additions/modifications:

### During Rough-In Phase
- Refrigeration piping rough-in (concurrent with MEP rough-in)
- Grease trap installation (before slab pour in slab-on-grade)
- Floor drain installation with proper slope (critical — cannot be fixed after slab)
- Walk-in cooler/freezer slab preparation (depressed slabs for insulation + finished floor height match)
- Electrical service upgrade / transformer installation

### During Finish Phase
- Walk-in panel erection
- Refrigerated case set and connection
- Kitchen equipment set and connection
- Hood installation and fire suppression tie-in
- Shelving and gondola installation
- Checkout counter installation
- Refrigeration system commissioning (requires 2-4 weeks of run time before product load)

### Pre-Opening (After CO)
- Equipment testing and calibration
- Health department final inspection (separate from building CO)
- Product load (stocking shelves, filling cases)
- Staff training
- Soft opening → grand opening

## Common Risks — Grocery

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Utility service upgrade delays | Engage utility company in DD; confirm capacity and timeline |
| Refrigeration contractor coordination failures | Pre-qualify; involve in preconstruction; require coordination drawings |
| Floor drain slope errors | Verify during forming; laser-level confirmation before pour |
| Health department rejection | Pre-application meeting; involve food service consultant in design |
| Equipment delivery delays | Order at CD phase; track weekly; have backup suppliers identified |
| Refrigeration commissioning time | Build 2-4 weeks into schedule before store opening |
| Walk-in panel damage during install | Protect panels; schedule after other ceiling/overhead work is complete |
