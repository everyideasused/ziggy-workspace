# 04 - Entitlements & Permitting

> **Phase 4 of 10** | Predecessor: [[03 - Design — SD, DD, CD]] | Successor: [[07 - Construction Execution]] (permit is a predecessor to construction start)

---

## Purpose

Secure all governmental approvals required to legally build and occupy the project. This includes land-use entitlements (zoning), building permits, and all specialty permits. See also [[AHJ Research Methodology]] for the framework on researching any new jurisdiction.

---

## Entitlements vs. Permits — Key Distinction

| Type | What It Is | When It Happens | Risk Level |
|------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| **Entitlements** | Land-use approvals (zoning, variances, conditional use permits, subdivision plats, site plan approval) | Often during Feasibility or early Design | HIGH — discretionary, political, uncertain timeline |
| **Building Permits** | Approval to construct per submitted construction documents | After CDs are complete | MODERATE — technical review, more predictable |

---

## Entitlement Process

### Common Entitlement Types
- **By-Right Development:** Project conforms to existing zoning; no discretionary approval needed (fastest path)
- **Administrative Site Plan Review:** Staff-level review of site layout, landscaping, architecture (1-3 months)
- **Conditional Use Permit (CUP):** Permitted use subject to conditions imposed by planning commission/board
- **Variance:** Exception to specific zoning standards (setback, height, parking count)
- **Rezoning/Map Amendment:** Changing the zoning designation of the property (longest, highest risk)
- **Planned Development (PD):** Custom zoning with negotiated development standards
- **Subdivision/Plat Approval:** Dividing land into lots or creating new parcels

### Entitlement Process Flow
```
Pre-Application Meeting → Application Submittal → Completeness Review →
Staff Review → Public Notice Period → Public Hearing(s) →
Planning Commission → City Council (if required) → Approval with Conditions
```

### Key Entitlement Risks
- Public opposition (NIMBYism)
- Environmental review requirements (CEQA in California, NEPA for federal involvement)
- Political changes between application and decision
- Conditions of approval that add significant cost

## Building Permit Process

### Permit Application Package (Typical)
- [ ] Completed application form(s)
- [ ] Construction documents (architectural, structural, MEP, civil, landscape)
- [ ] Project Manual / specifications
- [ ] Site plan showing utilities, grading, drainage
- [ ] Title report or proof of ownership/authority
- [ ] Energy compliance documentation (IECC, Title 24, local energy code)
- [ ] Fire and life safety plans
- [ ] Accessibility compliance documentation (ADA, state accessibility codes)
- [ ] Stormwater management plan
- [ ] Demolition plan (if applicable)
- [ ] Asbestos/hazmat survey (if existing building)
- [ ] Application fees (plan review + permit fees, typically based on construction value)
- [ ] Contractor information and licensing (some jurisdictions require at permit)

### Plan Review Process
```
Application Submitted → Intake/Completeness Check (1-5 days) →
Distribution to Review Disciplines → Plan Review Period →
Comments Issued → Applicant Response/Resubmission →
Re-review → Approval → Permit Issuance → Pay Fees → Pickup Permit
```

### Review Disciplines (Typical)
| Discipline | What They Review |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Building/Architectural | Code compliance, egress, occupancy, accessibility |
| Structural | Foundation, framing, connections, loads |
| Mechanical | HVAC systems, ventilation, energy compliance |
| Electrical | Wiring, panels, lighting, load calculations |
| Plumbing | Piping, fixtures, water heater, backflow |
| Fire/Life Safety | Sprinklers, alarms, rated assemblies, egress |
| Civil/Site | Grading, drainage, erosion control, utilities |
| Zoning | Setbacks, parking, use compliance |
| Health Department | Food service, public pools (if applicable) |
| DOT/Traffic | Driveway access, traffic studies (if applicable) |

### Common Plan Review Comment Categories
- Code violations requiring design changes
- Incomplete information requiring additional details
- Calculation deficiencies (structural, MEP)
- Missing or outdated reference codes
- Deferred submittals (items reviewed during construction)

## Specialty Permits & Approvals

Beyond the building permit, projects often require:

| Permit/Approval | When Required |
|----------------|---------------|
| Demolition Permit | Before demolishing any existing structure |
| Grading/Earthwork Permit | Site work, excavation, fill |
| Right-of-Way Permit | Work in public ROW (sidewalk, utilities) |
| Environmental Permit | Wetlands, stormwater, air quality |
| Fire Alarm Permit | Separate from building permit in many jurisdictions |
| Fire Sprinkler Permit | Often filed by the fire protection subcontractor |
| Sign Permit | All exterior signage |
| Mechanical Permit | HVAC installation |
| Electrical Permit | Electrical installation |
| Plumbing Permit | Plumbing installation |
| Elevator Permit | Any vertical transportation |
| Health Permit | Food service, medical, childcare facilities |
| Liquor License | Restaurants/bars serving alcohol |
| TCO/CO | Temporary and final occupancy approval |

## Timeline Benchmarks

| Activity | Typical Duration |
|---------|-----------------|
| Permit application intake | 1-5 business days |
| Plan review (first round) | 2-8 weeks (varies widely by jurisdiction) |
| Applicant response time | 1-3 weeks |
| Re-review | 1-3 weeks |
| Permit issuance | 1-5 business days after approval |
| **Total (straightforward project)** | **4-12 weeks** |
| **Total (complex or difficult jurisdiction)** | **3-12+ months** |

### Factors that increase permit timeline:
- Large or complex projects
- Under-resourced building departments
- Historic district review
- Environmental review requirements
- Plan review comments requiring design changes
- Deferred submittal items
- Third-party review requirements

---

## Strategies for Accelerating Permitting

1. **Pre-application meetings** with the building department to identify issues early
2. **Expedited review services** (many jurisdictions offer for additional fees)
3. **Third-party plan review** (some jurisdictions accept private plan review firms)
4. **Phased permitting** (foundation permit while superstructure plans are reviewed)
5. **Early submission** of civil/site plans separately from building plans
6. **Complete submittals** — incomplete packages are the #1 cause of delays
7. **Permit expediting services** — specialists who manage the process and relationships
8. **AHJ relationship management** — respectful, proactive communication

## Transition to Next Phase

**The building permit is a critical predecessor to construction start.** Without it:
- No legal construction activity can occur
- GC cannot mobilize to site
- Insurance coverage may be void

The permit also establishes the **approved scope** — any changes during construction require either RFI/ASI clarification or formal permit revision/amendment.
