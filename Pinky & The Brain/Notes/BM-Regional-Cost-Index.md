## Union vs. Open Shop Premium

Labor rates and productivity differ significantly between union and open shop markets:

| Market Type | Labor Cost Premium | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Full union (NYC, Chicago, SF, Boston, Seattle) | +25–50% vs. open shop | Prevailing wage often required on public projects |
| Mixed market | +10–25% | Union trades on some scopes |
| Open shop | Baseline | Most of Southeast, South-Central, Mountain |

**Prevailing wage trigger:** Any project receiving public funding, on public property, or federally assisted — confirm Davis-Bacon or state prevailing wage requirements.

## Escalation History & Forecast

Historical construction cost escalation (national average):

| Year | Annual Escalation | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 2018 | 4–6% | Steady growth |
| 2019 | 3–5% | Moderate |
| 2020 | 3–6% | COVID disruption; variable |
| 2021 | 12–20% | Supply chain crisis; lumber spike |
| 2022 | 10–18% | Steel, copper, fuel surcharges |
| 2023 | 4–8% | Cooling but still elevated |
| 2024 | 3–5% | Near-normal range |
| 2025 (forecast) | 3–6% | Watch tariff impacts on steel/aluminum |

**Escalation Application Rule:**
Apply escalation from estimate date to **midpoint of construction**:
```
Escalation adjustment = Annual rate × (months to construction midpoint / 12)

Example: Estimate in January 2025; construction starts September 2025 (8 months out); 
duration 6 months; midpoint = December 2025 (11 months from estimate)
Adjustment = 4% × (11/12) = 3.7% escalation factor
```

| Dallas/Houston | 0.4–0.8% | |
| Phoenix | 0.5–1% | |
| Suburban/small city | 0.3–0.7% | |

**Utility Connection / Development Fees (Separate from Permit)**
These are not construction costs but are capital expenditures often missed:
- Water/sewer tap fees: $5,000–$80,000+ depending on meter size and municipality
- Electric utility (transformer, service): $10,000–$100,000+ for commercial service upgrades
- Always request utility fee estimates from each utility provider at project kickoff
