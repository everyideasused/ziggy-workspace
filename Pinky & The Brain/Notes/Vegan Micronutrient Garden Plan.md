# Vegan Micronutrient Garden Plan for Zone 7a

**Target Location:** 37216 (Nashville, TN)  
**USDA Hardiness Zone:** 7a  
**Bed Size:** 8×4 ft (32 sq ft per bed)  
**Goal:** Maximize micronutrient density while achieving caloric self-sufficiency

---

## Summary: The Bottom Line

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Minimum beds for 100% calories** | 8-12 beds (256-384 sq ft) |
| **Minimum beds for majority (60%+) food** | 4-6 beds (128-192 sq ft) |
| **Maximum realistic self-sufficiency** | 80-90% of food needs |
| **Key micronutrient gaps** | B12 (supplement required), Vitamin D, EPA/DHA |

---

## Zone 7a Climate Overview

- **First frost:** Late October - Early November
- **Last frost:** Late March - Early April  
- **Growing season:** ~210-230 days
- **Summer:** Hot, humid (85-95°F highs)
- **Winter:** Mild (occasional teens, mostly 30-50°F)
- **Rainfall:** ~50 inches annually, well-distributed

**Tennessee excels at:** Sweet potatoes, greens (kale, collards), tomatoes, peppers, squash, beans, okra

## Bed Layout Recommendations

### Minimum Viable Garden (4 beds = 128 sq ft)
**Target: 40-50% of food needs, high micronutrient density**

| Bed | Contents | Primary Calories |
|-----|----------|------------------|
| **Bed 1** | Sweet potatoes (full bed) | ~16,000-32,000 cal |
| **Bed 2** | Kale, collards, chard (half) + spinach/winter greens (half) | Low cal, high nutrition |
| **Bed 3** | Tomatoes + peppers + basil | Medium cal, high nutrition |
| **Bed 4** | Beans (pole/dried) + winter squash | ~5,000-8,000 cal |

### Majority Food Garden (6 beds = 192 sq ft)
**Target: 60-70% of food needs**

| Bed | Contents |
|-----|----------|
| **Bed 1** | Sweet potatoes |
| **Bed 2** | Irish potatoes (spring) → winter squash (summer) |
| **Bed 3** | Kale, collards, chard (year-round) |
| **Bed 4** | Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, basil |
| **Bed 5** | Pole beans + lima beans + cowpeas |
| **Bed 6** | Root crops (carrots, beets, turnips) + onions + garlic |

### Near Self-Sufficiency (8 beds = 256 sq ft)
**Target: 75-85% of food needs**

Add to 6-bed plan:
- **Bed 7**: Okra + summer squash + cucumbers
- **Bed 8**: Grain amaranth or quinoa + flax + sunflowers (seeds/oil)

### Full Self-Sufficiency (12 beds = 384 sq ft)
**Target: 90-95% of food needs**

Add to 8-bed plan:
- **Bed 9**: Additional sweet potatoes or corn
- **Bed 10**: Legume rotation (nitrogen fixation focus)
- **Bed 11**: Perennial fruits (strawberries, asparagus)
- **Bed 12**: Experimentals/medicinals/herbs

## Calorie Math: The Reality

**Daily vegan caloric need:** ~2,000-2,400 calories
**Annual need:** ~730,000-880,000 calories

### Calories Per 8×4 Bed (Annual Yield Estimates)

| Crop Type | Avg Annual Yield | Calories/Bed |
|-----------|------------------|--------------|
| Sweet potatoes | 60 lbs | 24,000 |
| Irish potatoes | 30 lbs | 10,500 |
| Winter squash | 50 lbs | 10,000 |
| Dried beans | 12 lbs | 6,000-7,000 |
| Tomatoes | 40 lbs | 3,600 |
| Greens | 25 lbs | 2,500-3,000 |

**Reality Check:**
- 8 beds focused on sweet potatoes + squash + beans = ~200,000-250,000 calories/year
- That's only 25-30% of annual needs from these 8 beds
- **You need 12-16 beds minimum for 100% caloric self-sufficiency**
- Most realistic: 6-8 beds providing 50-70% of food + supplemental calories from grains/staples

## Key Recommendations

### Start With (Priority Order):

1. **Sweet potatoes** — Tennessee's #1 crop, massive calories, stores forever
2. **Kale + Collards** — Year-round harvests, micronutrient champions
3. **Pole beans** — Protein + calories + nitrogen fixation
4. **Tomatoes** — Versatile, productive, preservation potential
5. **Winter squash** — Calories + long storage

### Don't Forget:

- **B12 supplement** — Non-negotiable for vegans
- **Composting system** — Essential for soil fertility
- **Seed saving** — Cost reduction, adaptation
- **Preservation plan** — Canning, dehydrating, root cellar for winter
- **Perennial fruits** — Long-term investment (blueberries, figs, blackberries)

### Realistic Expectations:

| Garden Size | % Food Self-Sufficiency | Time Investment |
|-------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| 4 beds | 30-40% | 5-8 hrs/week |
| 6 beds | 50-65% | 8-12 hrs/week |
| 8 beds | 65-80% | 12-16 hrs/week |
| 12 beds | 80-95% | 20+ hrs/week |
