# 🔄 Vegan Classic Dish Swaps

> **Purpose:** Comprehensive guide to converting any non-vegan dish to an excellent vegan version. Covers dairy, egg, and meat substitutions with technique, rationale, and restaurant-quality execution. This is the Michelin-level approach — we're not accepting inferior results.

---

## The Philosophy of Vegan Swaps

Poor swaps fail because they try to mimic the texture/flavor of animal products. Excellent swaps **recreate the function** — the richness, the umami, the binding, the creaminess — with plant ingredients that are sometimes superior. The goal is never "close enough." It's a dish that stands on its own.

## Dairy Substitutions

### Milk

| Non-Vegan | Vegan Swap | Best For | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------|
| Whole milk (1:1) | Full-fat oat milk | Baking, sauces, cereals | Best neutral flavor |
| Whole milk (1:1) | Full-fat soy milk | All-purpose, highest protein | Curdles with acid (like dairy milk) |
| Whole milk (1:1) | Cashew milk | Creamy applications | Richest non-dairy option |
| Skim milk | Rice milk | Light sauces, thin pancakes | Thin consistency |
| Buttermilk | Soy milk + 1 tbsp ACV or lemon | Baking (activates baking soda), marinades | Let sit 5 min to curdle |
| Evaporated milk | Full-fat coconut milk | Soups, sauces, pies | Richest option |
| Condensed sweetened milk | Full-fat coconut milk + sugar, reduced | Desserts | Reduce on stovetop |

### Cream and Heavy Cream

| Non-Vegan | Vegan Swap | Ratio | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Heavy cream (sauces) | Full-fat coconut cream | 1:1 | Mild coconut flavor; use in savory if acceptable |
| Heavy cream (sauces) | Cashew cream | 1:1 | **Best neutral flavor** — soak cashews, blend smooth |
| Heavy cream (sauces) | Oat cream (Oatly barista) | 1:1 | Good in coffee, mild flavor |
| Heavy cream (whipping) | Chilled coconut cream, whipped | 1:1 | Must be very cold; stabilize with arrowroot |
| Sour cream | Cashew sour cream | 1:1 | Cashews + lemon + ACV + salt → blend |
| Sour cream | Full-fat coconut yogurt + lemon | 1:1 | Tangier option |
| Crème fraîche | Soaked cashews + lemon + white wine vinegar | 1:1 | Blend very smooth |

**Master Cashew Cream Recipe:**
```
1 cup raw cashews (soaked 4 hrs or boiled 15 min)
½–¾ cup water (less = thicker)
1 tbsp lemon juice
Pinch of salt
Blend on high 2–3 min until completely smooth

Variations:
- Sweet: add vanilla, maple syrup → dessert cream
- Savory: add nutritional yeast, garlic → cheese sauce base
- Sour: add more lemon + ACV → sour cream
- Ranch: add dill, onion powder, garlic powder, chives
```

### Cheese

This is where vegan cooking requires the most skill. Processed vegan cheeses vary wildly. Making from scratch is often superior.

**Aged/Sharp Cheese Flavor:**
The "aged cheese" flavor complex = umami + salt + fat + acid + slight bitterness
- Nutritional yeast provides glutamates (umami)
- Cashews provide fat and creamy texture
- Lemon juice + ACV provide acid
- Miso provides fermented depth
- White pepper provides slight bite

**Cashew Parmesan (5 min, keeps 3 weeks):**
```
1 cup raw cashews
4 tbsp nutritional yeast
1 tsp garlic powder
1 tsp salt
Pulse in food processor until fine breadcrumb texture
Use exactly like parmesan
```

**Cashew Mozzarella (Stretchy):**
```
1 cup cashews (soaked)
1 cup water
3 tbsp tapioca starch (creates stretch)
2 tbsp nutritional yeast
1 tbsp lemon juice
1 tsp salt
Blend smooth → cook on medium heat, stirring constantly, until it pulls from sides
Pour into molds or use immediately
```

**Nacho Cheese Sauce:**
```
1 cup cashews (soaked)
1 cup water
1 roasted red pepper
3 tbsp nutritional yeast
1 tbsp lemon juice
1 tsp garlic powder
1 tsp onion powder
1 tsp smoked paprika
½ tsp cumin
Jalapeño to taste
Blend until smooth. Heat gently.
```

**Ricotta:**
```
1 block firm tofu, pressed
2 tbsp nutritional yeast
1 tbsp lemon juice
1 tbsp EVOO
1 tsp garlic powder
Salt + basil + pepper
Mash/blend to desired texture (chunky or smooth)
Use in lasagna, stuffed shells, cannelloni
```

**Feta:**
```
Extra-firm tofu, cubed
Marinade: ½ cup lemon juice, ¼ cup ACV, 2 tbsp EVOO,
          1 tsp dried oregano, 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp garlic powder
Marinate 24–48 hrs in refrigerator
Result: Tangy, crumbly, salty — excellent in salads
```

**Brie/Camembert Style:**
Cashew + probiotic capsule fermentation (advanced):
Soak cashews → blend → add probiotic powder → ferment 24–36 hrs at room temp → shape → refrigerate → surface ages naturally

### Butter

|------------|----------|-------|-------|
| Sautéing | EVOO or avocado oil | 1:1 | Better for high heat |
| Baking (fat) | Vegan butter (Miyoko's, Earth Balance) | 1:1 | Miyoko's = best quality |
| Baking (fat) | Refined coconut oil | 1:1 | Neutral flavor when refined |
| Toast/spreading | Vegan butter | 1:1 | Miyoko's is closest to dairy |
| Brown butter (noisette) | Vegan butter + lemon juice (after browning) | 1:1 | Water and milk solids brown; use Miyoko's |

### Yogurt

| Application | Swap | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| Breakfast/parfait | Coconut yogurt (Coyo, Culina) | Thickest, most like Greek |
| Breakfast/parfait | Soy yogurt | Highest protein; good tang |
| Smoothies | Any plant yogurt | Flavor note varies |

## Egg Substitutions

### The Science of Eggs in Cooking

Eggs perform multiple functions — different swaps for different functions:

| Egg Function | What the Egg Does | Vegan Swap |
|-------------|------------------|-----------|
| Binding | Proteins coagulate to hold ingredients | Flax/chia egg, aquafaba |
| Leavening | Trapped air + steam create lift | Aquafaba (whipped), baking powder |
| Emulsifying | Lecithin binds water and fat | Soy lecithin, aquafaba |
| Moisture | Adds liquid to batter | Plant milk, applesauce |
| Richness | Fat from yolk | Extra oil, avocado |
| Crust/wash | Proteins brown and crisp | Plant milk + maple syrup, cornstarch slurry |
| Scrambled/fried | Protein coagulates | Tofu + kala namak + turmeric |

### The Egg Swap Matrix

| Egg Use | Best Swap | Quantity | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------|-------|
| Binding (baked goods) | Flax egg | 1 tbsp ground flax + 3 tbsp water, sit 5 min | Mild flavor, hearty texture |
| Binding (baked goods) | Chia egg | 1 tbsp chia + 3 tbsp water, sit 5 min | Neutral flavor |
| Binding (baked goods) | Commercial Egg Replacer (Bob's Red Mill) | Per package | Reliable, neutral |
| Leavening | Aquafaba (whipped) | 3 tbsp per egg | Whip to stiff peaks for soufflés, meringue |
| Emulsifying (mayo) | Aquafaba or silken tofu | 3 tbsp per egg | + mustard for mayo emulsification |
| Custard/pastry cream | Silken tofu + cornstarch | 1 egg = ¼ cup silken tofu | + vanilla for sweet applications |
| Quiche/frittata | Silken tofu + cornstarch + kala namak | Per recipe | Add nutritional yeast for eggy flavor |
| French toast | Chickpea flour batter | ¼ cup chickpea flour + ¼ cup plant milk | Better crust than egg-based |
| Egg wash (golden crust) | Plant milk + maple syrup | Brush on | Or use aquafaba alone |
| Scrambled eggs | Crumbled firm tofu + kala namak | 1:1 | Kala namak is the magic — sulfurous egg aroma |
| Fried egg (whole) | Silken tofu round + turmeric + kala namak | 1 silken tofu slice per egg | Pan-fry until golden edges |
| Deviled eggs | Potato halves + cashew filling | Per recipe | Scoop potato, fill with cashew mayo mix |
| Mayonnaise | Aquafaba + oil emulsion | ¾ cup aquafaba → 1 cup oil + mustard, blend | Stable, convincing mayo |
| Hollandaise | Cashew cream + lemon + turmeric | Per recipe | Blend smooth, heat gently |

### Aquafaba — The Egg White Miracle

Aquafaba is the liquid from cooked chickpeas (or canned chickpeas). It contains proteins and starches that mimic egg white behavior remarkably closely.

**Ratios:**
- 3 tbsp aquafaba = 1 egg white (or 1 whole egg in most baking)
- Whipped to stiff peaks = meringue, mousse, soufflé

**Best uses:**
- Meringues (works almost identically to egg white)
- Chocolate mousse (whip to stiff peaks, fold into melted chocolate)
- Macarons (requires refinement but achievable)
- Waffles and pancakes (fluffier than flax egg)
- Royal icing and pavlova

## Meat and Fish Substitutions

### The Function Framework

Before choosing a swap, identify what the meat is doing in the dish:
1. **Protein mass** — bulk of the dish
2. **Texture** — chew, pull, flake
3. **Flavor** — umami, fat, smokiness
4. **Color** — browning cues
5. **Satiety** — fat + protein together

### Ground Meat

| Dish | Vegan Swap | Technique |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Taco meat | Tempeh crumble + black beans | Crumble tempeh → sauté with onion, garlic, cumin, chili, smoked paprika, lime |
| Bolognese | Tempeh + mushroom crumble | Pulse mushrooms in food processor + crumbled tempeh; deglaze with red wine |
| Burger patty | Black bean + oat + flax | Mash beans + cooked oats + flax egg + spices → press → pan-fry or grill |
| Meatballs | Lentil + sunflower seed | Process lentils + seeds + herbs → roll → bake |
| Meatloaf | Lentil + walnut + oat | Same as meatballs, press into loaf pan |
| Chili | Lentils + black beans + bulgur | Cook together in chili spices; bulgur provides chew |
| Lasagna (meat layer) | Tempeh crumble + lentils | Season with Italian herbs, red wine, miso |
| Stuffed peppers | Quinoa + black beans + corn | Complete protein + texture + flavor |
| Shepherd's pie | Lentil + mushroom | Cook in broth until thick; top with mashed potato |
| Sloppy joe | Tempeh crumble + BBQ sauce | Sauté + simmer in sauce |

| Short ribs (braised) | Jackfruit or seitan | Slow braise in red wine + herbs + vegetable broth |

| Stir-fry beef | Thinly sliced seitan | Marinate in soy + sesame → high heat flash cook |

| Bacon | Tempeh bacon or rice paper bacon | Tempeh: thin slice → marinate → pan-fry until crispy |

### "The Umami Stack" — Replicating Meat Depth

The reason meat "tastes like meat" is primarily umami compounds — glutamates, inosinate, guanylate. Plant-based sources:

| Compound | Plant Sources |
|---------|--------------|
| Glutamates | Tomatoes (especially sun-dried), nutritional yeast, miso, soy sauce/tamari, seaweed, mushrooms |
| Guanylate (5'-GMP) | Dried shiitake mushrooms (very high) |
| Inosinate (5'-IMP) | Not naturally in plants — but glutamate + guanylate create synergistic umami stronger than either alone |

**The Umami Bomb Formula for any dish:**
- Mushrooms (dried shiitake or fresh) = guanylate
- Tomato paste or sun-dried tomatoes = glutamates
- Miso or tamari = glutamates + salt
- Nutritional yeast = glutamates
- Onion/garlic = aromatics that amplify umami perception
