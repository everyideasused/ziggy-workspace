# 🧬 Vegan Macronutrient Science

> **Purpose:** PhD-level reference on protein, carbohydrates, and dietary fat within a vegan context — sources, quality metrics, targets, timing, and decision frameworks for optimizing a plant-based lean bulk.

---

## Protein — The Critical Macro

### Why Protein Is the Central Challenge

Vegan protein sources differ from animal sources in three key ways:
1. **Amino acid profiles** — most plant proteins are low in one or more essential amino acids
2. **Digestibility** — plant protein matrix and anti-nutritional factors reduce bioavailability
3. **Leucine density** — the anabolic trigger is lower per gram of plant protein

This does NOT mean vegan athletes can't build muscle — it means the strategy must be intentional.

### Protein Digestibility-Corrected Amino Acid Score (PDCAAS)

The gold standard for protein quality (1.0 = perfect):

| Source | PDCAAS | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Soy protein isolate | 1.00 | Complete — equivalent to whey |
| Tofu | 0.97 | Near-complete |
| Tempeh | 0.92 | Fermentation improves digestibility |
| Seitan | 0.25 | Very low lysine — must pair |
| Lentils | 0.52 | Low methionine |
| Rice protein | 0.47 | Low lysine |
| Pea protein | 0.89 | Excellent — low in methionine only |
| Hemp seed protein | 0.66 | Low lysine |

**DIAAS (newer metric) favors soy, pea, and potato protein** as closest to complete plant sources.

### Leucine Threshold for Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS)

Leucine is the anabolic trigger. MPS requires ~2.5–3g leucine per meal.

| Source | Leucine/30g protein |
|--------|-------------------|
| Whey (reference) | ~3.0g |
| Soy protein | ~2.3g |
| Pea protein | ~2.1g |
| Rice protein | ~2.1g |
| Seitan | ~1.8g |
| Lentils | ~1.6g |

**Practical implication:** Vegans need ~10–15% more total protein per meal OR leucine-enriched blends to reliably hit MPS threshold. Target 35–45g protein per meal (not 25–30g as often cited for omnivores).

### Amino Acid Complementarity

You do NOT need to combine proteins in a single meal (the myth). But you DO need all essential amino acids across your daily intake.

**Key deficiencies by source type:**
- **Grains/seitan** — Low in lysine → pair with legumes
- **Legumes** — Low in methionine → pair with grains or seeds
- **Corn** — Low in tryptophan and lysine → pair with beans
- **Rice** — Low in lysine → pair with peas or lentils

**Lysine-rich vegan foods (the most limiting amino acid):**
- Tempeh (highest bioavailable)
- Edamame / soy foods
- Lentils
- Pumpkin seeds
- Quinoa
- Seitan is lysine-poor — never rely on it as sole protein source

### Daily Protein Targets for Lean Bulk

Vegan athletes should target **1.0–1.2g per lb of bodyweight** (vs 0.7–0.8g for sedentary individuals):

| Bodyweight | Minimum | Optimal | Notes |
|------------|---------|---------|-------|
| 150 lbs | 150g | 165–180g | Lower end of bulk |
| 160 lbs | 160g | 175–185g | — |
| 170 lbs | 170g | 180–190g | — |
| 180 lbs | 180g | 190–200g | Upper range of bulk |

**Distribute evenly:** 4 meals of 40–45g each is superior to front-loading. MPS is stimulated per meal, not cumulatively.

### High-Protein Meal Architecture

To hit 40g protein per meal from plants:

| Protein Base | Amount | Protein |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Tempeh | 200g | 38g |
| Firm tofu | 350g | 28g + boost with 3 tbsp hemp (10g) |
| Seitan | 150g | 37g |
| Edamame | 300g | 33g |
| Lentils + hemp seeds | 250g + 4 tbsp | 22g + 13g = 35g |
| Pea protein shake | 1 scoop (30g powder) | 24g + 100g tofu = 32g |

## Carbohydrates — Fuel for Performance

### Carbohydrate Functions

1. **Primary fuel** for high-intensity training (glycolysis)
2. **Muscle glycogen** replenishment post-workout
3. **Protein-sparing** — adequate carbs prevent muscle catabolism
4. **Insulin signaling** for nutrient partitioning (muscle uptake)
5. **Fiber substrate** for gut microbiome health

### Glycemic Index vs. Glycemic Load

GI is misleading alone — GL (GI × portion size / 100) is more useful:

| Food | GI | GL (typical serving) | Verdict |
|------|----|---------------------|---------|
| White rice | 72 | 43 | High GL — use strategically post-workout |
| Oats | 55 | 13 | Moderate — excellent breakfast |
| Sweet potato | 70 | 22 | Moderate-high — good bulking carb |
| Lentils | 32 | 5 | Low — high fiber, slow release |
| Chickpeas | 28 | 8 | Excellent — stable energy |
| Banana | 51 | 13 | Good pre/post-workout |
| Quinoa | 53 | 13 | Complete protein + moderate GL |
| Dates | 42 | 18 | Dense calories — good for surplus |

### Carbohydrate Timing for Lean Bulk

| Window | Strategy | Sources |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Pre-workout (1-2 hrs) | 30–60g simple/moderate carbs | Oats, banana, rice cakes |
| Intra-workout | Optional — only if >90 min training | Dates, banana |
| Post-workout (within 2 hrs) | 60–100g carbs + protein | Rice, sweet potato, fruit |
| Evening | Lower GI, higher fiber | Legumes, vegetables |
| Rest days | Reduce by 50–75g total | Keep protein constant |

### Whole Food Carbohydrate Hierarchy

**Tier 1 (eat daily):** Oats, sweet potato, legumes (lentils, chickpeas, black beans), quinoa, fruit, leafy greens

**Tier 2 (eat regularly):** Brown rice, whole grain bread, butternut squash, beets, corn

**Tier 3 (use strategically):** White rice (post-workout), pasta, dates/dried fruit (caloric surplus)

### Carbohydrate Targets

| Bodyweight | Daily Carbs | Training Day | Rest Day |
|------------|------------|--------------|----------|
| 150 lbs | 300–350g | 330–380g | 250–280g |
| 160 lbs | 320–375g | 360–400g | 270–300g |
| 170 lbs | 340–400g | 380–420g | 290–320g |
| 180 lbs | 360–420g | 400–450g | 300–340g |

## Dietary Fat — Hormones, Brain, and Cell Function

### Essential Fatty Acid Challenge for Vegans

The vegan omega-3 problem is the most clinically significant fat issue:

| Fatty acid | Function | Vegan status |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| ALA | Precursor to EPA/DHA | Abundant in flax, chia, walnuts |
| EPA | Anti-inflammatory, cardiovascular | **Conversion from ALA is only 5–10%** |
| DHA | Brain, eye, cell membrane function | **Conversion from ALA is only 2–5%** |

**Solution:** Algae-based EPA/DHA supplementation (200–300mg DHA + EPA daily) — this is where fish get their omega-3s.

### Fat Quality Hierarchy

**Anti-inflammatory fats to prioritize:**
- Avocado + avocado oil (monounsaturated)
- Walnuts (ALA omega-3)
- Flaxseed / flaxseed oil (ALA)
- Chia seeds (ALA + fiber)
- Hemp seeds (ideal 3:1 omega-6:omega-3 ratio)
- Olives / olive oil (EVOO — polyphenols)

**Neutral fats (use in moderation):**
- Coconut oil (mostly saturated — fine in small amounts, but not a health food)
- Sesame oil (high heat, small amounts for flavor)
- Sunflower / safflower oil (high omega-6, use sparingly)

**Fats to minimize:**
- Refined seed oils in high omega-6 ratios (corn, soybean, sunflower in large amounts)
- Partially hydrogenated oils (trans fats — rare now but check labels)
- Ultra-refined coconut/palm oil in processed foods

### Saturated Fat and Vegan Diets

Vegans typically have lower saturated fat intake, but watch:
- Coconut oil (92% saturated)
- Coconut milk/cream (high sat fat)
- Processed vegan meats (often high sat fat from coconut)

**Target:** <10% of total calories from saturated fat. For a 2,700 cal diet: <30g saturated fat/day.

### Fat Targets

| Bodyweight | Daily Fat | Key Sources |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| 150 lbs | 70–85g | Avocado, nuts, seeds, EVOO |
| 160 lbs | 75–90g | + algae oil supplement |
| 170 lbs | 80–95g | — |
| 180 lbs | 85–100g | — |

## Putting It Together: Daily Macro Distribution

For lean bulk at 150 lbs (2,700 cal target):

| Macro | Target | Calories | % |
|-------|--------|----------|---|
| Protein | 165g | 660 | 24% |
| Carbs | 335g | 1,340 | 50% |
| Fat | 78g | 700 | 26% |

**Template meal split (4 meals):**
- Breakfast: 40g protein / 70g carbs / 20g fat
- Lunch: 40g protein / 90g carbs / 20g fat
- Post-workout: 40g protein / 100g carbs / 15g fat
- Dinner: 45g protein / 75g carbs / 23g fat

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Under-eating protein and blaming plant foods** — Track for 7 days before drawing conclusions
2. **Over-relying on seitan as sole protein** — Lysine-poor; always combine with legumes
3. **Ignoring leucine** — Hit 35–45g protein/meal, not 20–25g
4. **Fear of carbs during bulk** — Carbs are muscle-sparing and performance-critical
5. **ALA instead of algae DHA** — Conversion rates are too low; supplement directly
6. **Too little fat** — Below 50g/day impairs testosterone synthesis and fat-soluble vitamin absorption
