# V-Shape Calisthenics + Kettlebell Program

> **Goal:** Build a bigger butt, pecs, shoulders, arms, and achieve a V-shape physique using only bodyweight and a kettlebell in 30-minute daily sessions.
> **Weight Goal:** 150 lbs → 180 lbs (lean bulk over ~12–18 months)

---

## Program Overview

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| **Schedule** | 6 days on / 1 day off |
| **Session Length** | 30 minutes |
| **Split** | Upper / Lower alternating |
| **Equipment** | Single kettlebell (recommend 16kg–24kg to start) + pull-up bar |
| **Progression Cycle** | 4-week mesocycles |
| **Assessment** | Every 4 weeks |
| **Current Weight** | 150 lbs |
| **Target Weight** | 180 lbs |
| **Target Timeline** | 12–18 months (lean bulk) |

| Monday | **Upper Push** | Pecs, shoulders, triceps |
| Tuesday | **Lower** | Glutes, quads, hamstrings |

| Thursday | **Lower** | Glutes, posterior chain |

## Progression System

### Within a Mesocycle (Weeks 1–4)

Progress using this hierarchy — move down when the top option is maxed:

1. **Add reps** — Hit the top of the rep range for all sets before adding anything
2. **Add a set** — Cap at 4 working sets per exercise
3. **Slow the tempo** — Add 1s to the eccentric (lowering) phase
4. **Reduce rest** — Drop by 10–15s (minimum 30s)
5. **Upgrade the variation** — See progression ladder below
6. **Add KB load** — When you've outgrown your current bell, go up 4kg

### Exercise Progression Ladders

**Push-ups:**
Knee push-ups → Standard → Wide grip → Decline (feet elevated) → Archer → One-arm (assisted)

**Pike push-ups:**
Pike on floor → Feet elevated pike → Wall walk negatives → Wall handstand push-ups

**Diamond push-ups:**
Standard diamond → Deficit diamond → Weighted (KB on back with partner) → Pseudo planche

**Hip thrusts:**
BW both legs → BW single leg → KB on hips (both legs) → KB single leg → Feet elevated + KB

**Goblet squat:**
BW squat → Goblet squat → Goblet squat w/ pause → Goblet squat w/ 1.5 reps → Heavier KB

**Pull-ups:**
Dead hang holds → Negative pull-ups (5s down) → Band-assisted pull-ups → Pull-ups → Weighted pull-ups (KB between feet) → L-sit pull-ups → Archer pull-ups

**Chin-ups:**
Negative chin-ups → Band-assisted → Chin-ups → Weighted chin-ups → One-arm chin-up negatives

**KB Swing:**
Two-hand swing → One-hand swing → Heavier KB → Swing to snatch

## Mesocycle Planning

### Mesocycle 1 (Weeks 1–4): Foundation
- Focus on form and mind-muscle connection
- Use the lower end of rep ranges
- Establish baseline measurements and benchmarks

### Mesocycle 2 (Weeks 5–8): Volume
- Push toward top of rep ranges
- Add sets where possible (3 → 4)
- Introduce tempo manipulation (slow eccentrics)

### Mesocycle 3 (Weeks 9–12): Intensity
- Progress to harder variations
- Reduce rest periods by 10–15s
- Consider heavier KB if available

### Mesocycle 4 (Weeks 13–16): Peak + Deload
- Weeks 13–15: Push hardest variations and loads
- Week 16: Deload — reduce volume by 40%, keep intensity. Reassess all benchmarks

*After Mesocycle 4, reassess goals and build the next 16-week block.*

## Nutrition Plan: 150 → 180 lb Lean Bulk

You can't build muscle in a deficit. At 150 lbs targeting 180, you need a structured, sustained surplus. This is a ~30 lb gain, and done right (lean bulk), roughly 15–20 lbs of that will be muscle and the rest manageable fat that you can cut later if needed.

### Your Daily Targets

| Metric | Daily Target | How to Think About It |
|---|---|---|
| **Calories** | ~2,600–2,800 | Your estimated TDEE at 150 lbs with daily training is ~2,300–2,400. Add 300–400 surplus for growth. |
| **Protein** | 150–180g | 1g per pound of bodyweight. Non-negotiable. Scale up as weight increases. |
| **Carbs** | 300–350g | Fuel for training + recovery. Don't fear carbs during a bulk. |
| **Fat** | 70–85g | Hormone support. Don't go below 60g. |
| **Water** | 75–90 oz (minimum) | Half your bodyweight in oz as floor. More on training days / hot days. |
| **Sleep** | 7–9 hours | This is when you grow. Treat it like a workout. |

> **As you gain weight, recalculate:** For every 10 lbs gained, add ~150–200 calories and 10g protein to your daily targets. Ziggy should flag this at assessments.

### Scaling Targets as You Grow

| Bodyweight | Calories | Protein | Water (min oz) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **150 lbs** (start) | 2,600–2,800 | 150–180g | 75 |
| **160 lbs** | 2,800–3,000 | 160–180g | 80 |
| **170 lbs** | 3,000–3,200 | 170–185g | 85 |
| **180 lbs** (target) | 3,200–3,400 | 180–200g | 90 |

## Expected Progress Timeline: 150 → 180 lbs

### Quarter 1 (Weeks 1–12): Foundation + First Visible Changes
**Target weight: 155–158 lbs**

Performance: Pull-ups +3–5 reps per set. Push-up max +10–15. KB swing volume roughly doubles. You'll progress at least one step on most variation ladders.

Body: Glutes respond first — expect clothes fitting differently by week 8. Shoulders start capping out with visible roundness. Pecs firming and filling but not dramatic yet. Arms show more definition, especially triceps. Shoulder-to-waist ratio improves +0.02–0.05.

Feel: Noticeably stronger, better posture, tighter fit in the right places. You'll see it before others do.

### Quarter 2 (Weeks 13–24): Shape Starts Showing
**Target weight: 160–165 lbs**

Performance: Pull-ups in the 8–12 range or adding weight. Pike push-ups progressing toward wall HSPU negatives. KB movements may feel easy — potentially need a heavier bell.

Body: V-taper becomes visible to others. Lats from pull-ups give real width. Glutes have meaningful size — people comment. Upper chest filling in. Tricep horseshoe appearing. Shoulder-to-waist ratio approaching 1.40–1.45.

Feel: You look athletic. The mirror reflects someone who clearly trains.

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 25–36): Intermediate Territory
**Target weight: 165–170 lbs**

Performance: Working advanced variations — archer push-ups, weighted pull-ups, HSPU progressions. Strength gains slow but don't stop. Total volume significantly higher than Q1.

Body: V-shape locks in. Lats + delts + tighter waist create the frame. Glute growth continues at a slower rate. Pecs catch up — chest looks built. Bicep peak visible. Shoulder-to-waist ratio 1.45+.

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 37–48): Solidification
**Target weight: 170–175 lbs**

Performance: Solidly intermediate. Weighted pull-ups, HSPU work, heavy KB. PRs less frequent but more meaningful.

Body: Side-by-side photos from month 1 are dramatic. V-shape is your default silhouette. 20–25 lbs gained with most of it in the right places. This is where you and Ziggy decide: keep running this program with harder progressions or restructure for more specific goals.

### Quarters 5–6 (Months 13–18): The Final Push to 180
**Target weight: 175–180 lbs**

The last 5–10 lbs are the slowest. Gains are real but incremental. You're filling out your frame completely. At 180 lbs with this training background, you'll carry the weight well — broad shoulders, built chest, strong glutes, visible arms. The V-shape at 180 vs 150 is a completely different person.

## Quick Reference: Workout Rotation

To avoid confusion, here's the repeating cycle:

```
Week 1:  Mon=A  Tue=B1  Wed=C  Thu=B2  Fri=A  Sat=B1  Sun=Rest
Week 2:  Mon=C  Tue=B2  Wed=A  Thu=B1  Fri=C  Sat=B2  Sun=Rest
(Repeat)
```

Where:
- **A** = Upper Push (Pecs/Shoulders/Triceps)
- **B1** = Lower (Glute/Quad Focus)
- **B2** = Lower (Posterior Chain/Glute Focus)
- **C** = Upper Pull + Arms (Back/Biceps)

This ensures each workout hits **3x per 2 weeks** with even recovery.
