# Sector Profiles — Retail, Commercial, Residential, Civil

> Cross-cutting reference for [[Construction PM Knowledge Base]]

---

## Retail Construction

### Characteristics
- Typically tenant-driven on leased premises (landlord owns building)
- High velocity — many locations, tight timelines, brand consistency required
- Prototype-driven design with jurisdictional adaptations
- TIA-funded construction is the norm (see [[Tenant Improvement Allowances]])
- Landlord approval required for design and construction
- Brand standards dictate finishes, signage, fixtures, and layout

### Typical Project Types
| Type | Scope | Budget Range | Duration |
|------|-------|-------------|----------|
| New retail build-out (inline) | 1,000-5,000 SF interior TI | $75-250/SF | 6-12 weeks |
| Pad site (ground-up) | Free-standing building | $150-350/SF | 3-6 months |
| Restaurant build-out | Full kitchen, dining, bar | $150-400/SF | 8-16 weeks |
| Refresh/remodel | Finishes, fixtures, minor layout | $30-100/SF | 3-6 weeks |
| Roll-out program (100+ locations) | Varies | Varies | Ongoing |

### Retail-Specific PM Considerations
- **Landlord coordination:** Work letter compliance, building access, after-hours requirements, base building tie-ins
- **Lease deadlines:** Rent commencement dates create hard schedule deadlines
- **Multi-location programs:** Standardized processes, templated drawings, jurisdiction tracking
- **Store opening coordination:** Construction → FF&E → merchandising → training → grand opening
- **Prototype management:** National prototype adapted per location and jurisdiction

## Commercial Office Construction

### Characteristics
- Can be either owner-occupied or tenant-driven
- Often involves sophisticated MEP systems (data centers, trading floors)
- Higher finish quality expectations than retail
- Base building vs. tenant improvement distinction important

### Typical Project Types
| Type | Scope | Budget Range | Duration |
|------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Office TI (basic) | Open plan, conference rooms | $50-100/SF | 6-10 weeks |
| Office TI (high-end) | Executive, law firm, finance | $100-250/SF | 10-16 weeks |
| Ground-up office building | Core + shell + TI | $200-500/SF | 12-24 months |
| Data center | Heavy MEP, redundant systems | $500-1,500/SF | 12-24 months |

### Commercial-Specific PM Considerations
- **Base building coordination:** HVAC capacity, electrical capacity, structural loading
- **After-hours work:** Occupied buildings often require evening/weekend construction
- **Noise and dust control:** Adjacent tenant protection requirements
- **IT/AV integration:** Often complex and on the critical path for move-in

## Residential Construction

### Characteristics
- Highly regulated (building codes, warranty laws, HOA requirements)
- Consumer-facing — quality expectations and emotional investment are high
- Varies enormously from single-family custom to large multifamily

### Typical Project Types
| Type | Scope | Budget Range | Duration |
|------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Single-family custom | One-off design | $200-600+/SF | 8-18 months |
| Single-family production | Tract/subdivision | $100-200/SF | 4-6 months |
| Townhome/attached | Multi-unit attached | $120-250/SF | 6-12 months |
| Multifamily (wood frame) | 3-5 story apartments | $150-300/SF | 12-24 months |
| Multifamily (concrete/steel) | 6+ story | $250-500/SF | 18-36 months |
| Home renovation/addition | Varies | $150-400/SF | 6-16 weeks |

### Residential-Specific PM Considerations
- **Home warranty laws:** Many states have implied warranties (1-2-10 year structures)
- **Homeowner communication:** Frequent updates, selection management, change order sensitivity
- **Selections process:** Extensive material/finish selections by the homeowner
- **Municipal utility and impact fees:** Often significant for new subdivisions
- **HOA architectural review:** Additional approval layer beyond AHJ

## Civil / Infrastructure Construction

### Characteristics
- Publicly funded (typically)
- Competitive bidding requirements (lowest responsible bidder)
- Prevailing wage / Davis-Bacon requirements common
- Heavy civil equipment and earthwork
- Environmental compliance is paramount
- Long design and permitting timelines

### Typical Project Types
| Type | Scope | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| Roadway construction | Grading, paving, drainage, signage | 6-24 months |
| Water/sewer infrastructure | Pipe, pump stations, treatment | 6-36 months |
| Bridge construction | Structural, heavy civil | 12-48 months |
| Parks and recreation | Grading, structures, amenities | 6-18 months |
| Stormwater management | Detention, retention, outfall | 3-12 months |
| Site development (subdivision) | Mass grading, utilities, roads | 6-18 months |

### Civil-Specific PM Considerations
- **Unit price contracts** are common (payment based on actual quantities)
- **Material testing** is extensive (soils, concrete, asphalt, pipe testing)
- **Environmental permits** (NPDES, wetlands, USACE) can take months
- **Right-of-way** acquisition and utility relocation
- **Public stakeholder engagement** (community meetings, traffic management)
- **Prevailing wage compliance** and certified payroll requirements
- **As-built requirements** for public acceptance and dedication
