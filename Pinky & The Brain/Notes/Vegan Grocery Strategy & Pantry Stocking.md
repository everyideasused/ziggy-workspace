# 🛒 Vegan Grocery Strategy & Pantry Stocking

> **Purpose:** Complete reference for maintaining a well-stocked vegan kitchen optimized for Nathan's lean bulk. Covers pantry foundations, perishable planning, budget strategy, and the grocery list generation process.

---

## The Stocked Pantry Philosophy

A well-stocked pantry makes good eating automatic. With the right 50 ingredients always on hand, Nathan can make hundreds of combinations without a special shopping trip. The goal: reduce decision fatigue and remove friction between intention and execution.

| Hemp seeds | $10.00 | 144g | $0.69 |
| Edamame (frozen) | $3.00 | 72g | $0.42 |

**Budget protein priority: Dried legumes + vital wheat gluten → tempeh → edamame → tofu → hemp seeds**

### The 80/20 Grocery Budget Rule

- **80% whole foods** (dried legumes, grains, vegetables, fruits, nuts/seeds)
- **20% specialty items** (tempeh, vegan yogurt, protein powder, specialty sauces)

The whole food foundation is extremely affordable. The expensive part of vegan eating is often convenience items — minimize these.

### Store Strategy (Nashville Context)

| Store | Best For | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Whole Foods / Fresh Market | Tempeh, specialty items, quality produce | Higher cost — targeted purchases |
| Kroger / Publix | Everyday produce, canned goods, soy milk | Middle ground |
| Trader Joe's | Hemp seeds, nut butters, frozen edamame, affordable tempeh | Best value for specialty items |
| Asian grocery (H Mart, etc.) | Miso, tamari, dried mushrooms, tofu, seaweed, gochujang | Best prices on Asian pantry |
| Indian grocery | Dried spices (far cheaper than Whole Foods), dal, lentils, hing | 80% cheaper than mainstream |
| Costco | Oats, nuts, olive oil, frozen berries, nutritional yeast, plant milk | Bulk savings |
| Amazon / Thrive Market | Vital wheat gluten, specialty items, supplements | Convenience + occasional deals |

**Rule:** Buy spices from Indian and Asian groceries. Buy bulk staples from Costco. Buy specialty and fresh from Whole Foods or Trader Joe's.

## Label Reading for Hidden Animal Products

### Hidden Non-Vegan Ingredients to Watch

| Ingredient | Source | Found In |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Casein, whey | Dairy | Protein bars, "non-dairy" creamers |
| Gelatin | Animal bones/skin | Capsules, gummies, marshmallows, panna cotta |
| L-cysteine | Feathers/hair | Commercial bread dough conditioners |
| Natural flavors | Sometimes animal-derived | Processed foods — requires contacting manufacturer |
| Carmine (E120) | Cochineal insects | Red-colored foods, beverages |
| Shellac (E904) | Lac insects | Candy coatings, citrus fruit wax |
| Rennet | Calf stomach | Most conventional cheese |
| Isinglass | Fish bladder | Beer, wine clarification |
| Mono- and diglycerides | Sometimes animal fat | Bread, margarine |
| Vitamin D3 (from lanolin) | Sheep wool | Fortified foods — check for plant-derived D3 |
| Omega-3 (from fish) | Fish oil | Fortified orange juice, eggs, baby formula |
| Lard | Pork fat | Traditional tortillas, refried beans, pie crusts |
| Anchovies | Fish | Worcestershire sauce, caesar dressing, many pasta sauces |

### App Recommendations

- **Is It Vegan?** — Scan barcodes for quick check
- **Barnivore** — Beer and wine vegan status
- **HappyCow** — Restaurant finder
