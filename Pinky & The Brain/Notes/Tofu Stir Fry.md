## 📋 Ingredients

### Stir Fry
- 14 oz block extra-firm tofu
- 2 tbsp canola oil (or high smoke point oil)
- 1/4 cup chopped green onions
- 2 cups baby spinach (or kale, bok choy)

### Garlic Ginger Stir Fry Sauce
- 1/2 cup low sodium soy sauce (or tamari for GF)
- 1/4 cup seasoned rice vinegar
- 1/4 cup water
- 3 large cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp fresh grated ginger
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp pure maple syrup
- 1 tbsp cornstarch

### For Serving (Optional)
- Cooked rice or noodles
- Hot sauce
- Sesame seeds

---

## 👨‍🍳 Instructions

1. **Prepare tofu** – Drain and pat dry with paper towels, pressing to squeeze out excess moisture. Slice into small cubes (1/2 inch).

2. **Make sauce** – In a mason jar, add all sauce ingredients. Seal lid tightly and shake until well combined. Set aside.

3. **Pan fry tofu** – Heat oil in large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add tofu and pan fry until golden brown on all sides. *TIP: Let cook for 3-4 minutes before stirring to prevent sticking.*

4. **Add vegetables** – Once tofu is golden, add green onions and spinach. Stir fry until spinach wilts (few minutes).

5. **Add sauce** – Lower heat to low-medium and stir in sauce. Stir constantly until sauce thickens and coats everything. Remove from heat.

6. **Serve** – Scoop over rice or noodles, top with hot sauce and sesame seeds. Enjoy!

---

## 📝 Notes
> *Tweaks, substitutions, what worked, what didn't.*

- **No pressing needed** – Extra-firm tofu works without pressing. If using firm tofu, press first.
- **Add more veggies** – Bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, baby corn, cauliflower, mushrooms, zucchini all work great
- **Flexible protein** – Can use tempeh or rehydrated soy curls instead
- **Leftovers** – Store in fridge for 3 days, reheat until warm
- **Recipe makes lots of sauce** – Perfect for serving with rice/noodles. Could use 2 blocks tofu for this amount of sauce.
