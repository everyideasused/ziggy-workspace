## VISION

### Glasses & Contacts: The Retail Markup Problem
Traditional optical shops (LensCrafters, Pearle Vision) mark up frames 200-1,000%. Luxottica owns most "different" frame brands AND most optical retail chains.

### Where to Buy Glasses
- **Zenni Optical**: Complete prescription glasses from $6.95. Seriously. Quality is adequate for daily use. Excellent for backup pairs, kids' glasses, and prescription sunglasses.
- **EyeBuyDirect**: Similar pricing to Zenni, slightly different frame selection. $6-30 complete.
- **Warby Parker**: $95 complete (frames + lenses). Higher quality frames than Zenni/EBD. Home try-on program (5 frames mailed free). In-store locations for fitting.
- **Costco Optical**: Competitive pricing on both frames and lenses. Good selection of quality frames. 2-3x cheaper than LensCrafters for equivalent quality.
- **GlassesUSA**: Online retailer, frequent 60-70% off sales. Strong selection.
- **Insurance**: If you have vision insurance (VSP, EyeMed), use your frame allowance at Costco or an independent optician — NOT at LensCrafters where the allowance barely covers the markup.

### Contacts
- **1-800-Contacts**: Price match guarantee. Convenient.
- **Costco Optical**: Often cheapest for annual supply.
- **Discount Contact Lenses (discountcontactlenses.com)**: Competitive pricing.
- **Insurance auto-ship**: Some vision plans offer contacts at discounted rates through auto-ship.
- **Daily vs. monthly**: Daily disposables cost more per year but eliminate cleaning, reduce infection risk, and often win on convenience value.

### Eye Exams
- **Costco**: $75-100 without insurance. Optometrist is independent but located in Costco.
- **America's Best**: 2 pairs + eye exam for $69.95 frequently advertised.
- **Walmart Vision Center**: Competitive exam pricing.
- **Independent optometrist**: Often $100-150. Supports local business, sometimes more thorough exams.

## DENTAL

### Dental Insurance vs. Discount Plans
- **Dental insurance**: Typical max benefit $1,000-2,000/year. Premium: $20-50/month. For routine cleanings and one filling, insurance roughly breaks even. For major work, the annual cap limits value.
- **Dental discount plans**: Not insurance — membership plans that give 10-60% off at participating dentists. $80-200/year. Often better value than insurance for people who only need cleanings.
- **Best discount plans**: DentalPlans.com (comparison shopping), Cigna Dental Savings, Aetna Vital Savings.

### Where to Save on Dental Work
- **Dental schools**: Teaching clinics at accredited dental schools provide care at 50-70% below private practice. Supervised by faculty. Longer appointments, excellent care.
- **Community health centers**: HRSA-funded health centers offer sliding-scale dental services. FindaHealthCenter.hrsa.gov.
- **Dental tourism**: Mexico (border towns like Los Algodones), Costa Rica, Thailand. Major procedures (crowns, implants, veneers) at 50-80% below US pricing. Research practitioners thoroughly — check credentials, reviews, and before/after portfolios.
- **Negotiate**: Dentists will often offer 5-15% cash discount for uninsured patients paying at time of service. Ask.

## HEARING

### OTC Hearing Aids (Game Changer Since 2022)
The FDA opened the market for over-the-counter hearing aids in 2022. This dramatically reduced costs for mild-moderate hearing loss:
- **Prescription hearing aids**: $2,000-7,000 per pair through audiologist
- **OTC hearing aids**: $200-1,500 per pair
- **Top OTC options**: Sony CRE-C10, Jabra Enhance, Lexie (Bose-powered), Sony CRE-E10
- **Costco Hearing Centers**: Kirkland Signature hearing aids at $1,400-1,800/pair (prescription level) — dramatically below audiologist pricing for equivalent technology.
- **Best for**: Mild to moderate hearing loss. Severe loss still requires prescription fitting.

## MEDICAL BILLS: NEGOTIATION & REDUCTION

### The Medical Bill System
Hospital "chargemaster" prices are essentially fictional — inflated starting points for negotiation. Nobody is expected to pay the listed price.

### How to Reduce Medical Bills
1. **Ask for an itemized bill**: Errors are common (30-80% of bills contain errors). Review every line item.
2. **Ask for the cash/self-pay price**: Often 30-60% less than the billed amount. Hospitals have uninsured/self-pay discount policies.
3. **Negotiate**: "Is there a prompt-pay discount?" "Can you reduce this to a level comparable to Medicare rates?" Billing departments have authority to reduce.
4. **Payment plan**: Interest-free payment plans are almost always available. $50-100/month until paid. Keeps bills out of collections.
5. **Financial assistance**: Non-profit hospitals are legally required to offer charity care. Ask about financial assistance programs. Income thresholds are often higher than people expect.
6. **Medical bill advocates**: Services like Resolve Medical Bills or Patient Advocate Foundation negotiate on your behalf (typically charge a percentage of savings).
7. **MDsave**: Price transparency platform — buy medical procedures at pre-negotiated rates before receiving care.

### CareCredit
- Medical credit card with 0% promotional financing (6-24 months depending on amount)
- Available for medical, dental, vision, veterinary, and cosmetic procedures
- **Caution**: If not paid in full by end of promo period, interest retroactively applied to full original balance at 26.99% APR
- Use only with a plan to pay in full before promo ends

## PRESCRIPTION OPTIMIZATION (EXPANDED)

### The GoodRx + Cost Plus Strategy
- **GoodRx**: Free app/card. Compare prices across pharmacies for your specific medication. Often beats insurance copay.
- **Cost Plus Drugs (Mark Cuban)**: Transparent pricing = manufacturer cost + 15% markup + $5 pharmacy fee + $5 shipping. Often 70-90% below retail.
- **Amazon Pharmacy**: Prime members get discounts on generic and brand medications. Compare to GoodRx.
- **Costco Pharmacy**: Often cheapest cash-price option. You do NOT need a Costco membership to use the pharmacy (federal law).

### Mail-Order Pharmacy Savings
- **90-day supply**: Almost always cheaper per pill than 30-day supply.
- **Insurance mail-order**: Typically lowest copay option for maintenance medications.
- **Cost Plus Drugs ships**: 90-day supply with flat shipping fee.

## GOLDEN RULES: HEALTH & MEDICAL

1. **Buy glasses online** (Zenni, EyeBuyDirect, Warby Parker). Optical shops mark up 200-1,000%.
2. **Costco for everything vision**: Exams, glasses, contacts — consistently best value.
3. **Always request an itemized medical bill.** Errors are in 30-80% of bills.
4. **GoodRx before every prescription.** Often beats insurance copay.
5. **Cost Plus Drugs for maintenance medications.** Transparent pricing, massive savings.
6. **Dental schools for major dental work.** 50-70% savings, supervised care.
7. **OTC hearing aids** for mild-moderate loss: $200-1,500 vs. $2,000-7,000 prescription.
8. **Negotiate medical bills.** Ask for cash price, prompt-pay discount, financial assistance.
9. **CareCredit 0% financing** — useful but pay in full before promo ends or interest hits retroactively.
10. **Costco pharmacy doesn't require membership.** Check prices there for every medication.
