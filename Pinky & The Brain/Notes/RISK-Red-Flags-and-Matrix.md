# Risk Matrix & Estimate Red Flags

---

## RISK-Red-Flags — Estimate Warning Signs

These are patterns that indicate an estimate is likely to blow up. Flag these immediately.

### Red Flag 1: MEP < 30% of TI Hard Cost
**What it means:** Plumbing, HVAC, electrical, and fire systems are almost certainly underestimated.
**Action:** Audit each MEP trade independently. Restaurant should be 40–55%; office 30–45%.

### Red Flag 2: No Line Item for Existing Conditions Survey
**What it means:** Team is estimating blind — no one has verified what's actually in the space.
**Action:** Budget $2,000–$8,000 for conditions survey before finalizing estimate.

### Red Flag 3: "Utility Connections by Owner / Utility" Without Budget
**What it means:** $30,000–$200,000+ in fees is unaccounted for in the project budget.
**Action:** Always assign a placeholder allowance. Contact each utility for actual quote.

### Red Flag 4: No Grease Trap / Interceptor in Restaurant Estimate
**What it means:** Most jurisdictions require grease interceptor for food service — this is non-negotiable.
**Action:** Budget in-ground interceptor ($15,000–$40,000) unless AHJ specifically accepts under-sink.

### Red Flag 5: Single Bid on MEP Trades
**What it means:** No market validation. Could be 15–30% over or under actual market.
**Action:** Require minimum 3 bids per trade. One bid is not a market check.

### Red Flag 6: GC General Conditions > 18% or < 6%
**What it means:** Either gold-plated or understaffed project.
- >18%: Review headcount and duration — are they over-staffing?
- <6%: Are they planning to manage this remotely? Inadequate site management = problems.
**Action:** Request itemized general conditions breakdown.

### Red Flag 7: Schedule Shorter Than Realistic Build Time
**What it means:** GC will either miss schedule (causing LD exposure) or add crews (adding cost).
**Action:** Cross-check schedule against industry norms. Compress → premium.

### Red Flag 8: Estimate Has No Escalation Applied
**What it means:** Pricing today's dollars for tomorrow's project = automatic under-budget.
**Action:** Always apply escalation factor. Even 4%/year over 12 months = meaningful delta on large projects.

### Red Flag 9: Allowances > 10% of Total Budget
**What it means:** Too much is undefined. Allowances always get spent, often exceeded.
**Action:** Drive design decisions to replace allowances with real scope before GMP.

### Red Flag 10: No Contingency or Contingency < 5% at CD Phase
**What it means:** There is zero budget flexibility for change orders, site conditions, or design issues.
**Action:** Minimum 5% post-award contingency is industry standard. Below 3% is reckless.

### Red Flag 11: Structural Steel Not Addressed in Restaurant
**What it means:** Commercial kitchens have heavy equipment; RTUs add roof load; hood system requires support. If no structural line item — something is missing.
**Action:** Confirm structural engineer reviewed equipment list and rooftop equipment loads.

### Red Flag 12: No Fire Alarm or Fire Suppression Modification Budget
**What it means:** Nearly every TI modification triggers fire alarm and/or sprinkler changes. Omitting these is a scope gap.
**Action:** Budget minimum $5,000–$25,000 for FA/FP modifications on any TI.

## RISK-Unknowns-Matrix by Project Type

### Restaurant (QSR / FSR)

| Unknown | Probability | Cost Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Slab thickness / reinforcement insufficient for kitchen loads | Medium | $10K–$40K | Structural investigation |
| Existing MEP not in expected location | High | $5K–$25K | Conditions survey |
| Grease interceptor size/location | High | $15K–$40K | AHJ pre-application |
| Electrical service capacity insufficient | Medium-High | $20K–$80K | Utility letter of intent |
| Fire suppression system outdated | Medium | $15K–$50K | FA/FP survey |
| Unknown asbestos (pre-1980 building) | Low-Medium | $10K–$60K | Environmental survey |
| Drive-thru lane impacts (utilities, grades) | Medium | $10K–$50K | Topographic survey |
| RTU structural support inadequate | Medium | $8K–$25K | Structural review of roof |

### Retail TI

| Unknown | Probability | Cost Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Existing HVAC inadequate for new layout | Medium | $20K–$80K | Mechanical survey |
| Storefront modification more extensive than expected | Medium-High | $30K–$100K | Detailed storefront survey |
| ADA path of travel upgrades required | Medium | $10K–$50K | ADA survey |
| Electrical service upgrade needed | Medium | $15K–$60K | Utility letter |
| Unknown mold/moisture in walls | Low | $10K–$80K | Pre-construction investigation |

### Ground-Up

| Unknown | Probability | Cost Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Soil bearing capacity insufficient | Medium | $30K–$200K | Geotechnical investigation |
| Underground utilities not as mapped | Medium | $10K–$50K | Private utility locate |
| Wetlands / environmental encumbrance | Low-Medium | $50K–$500K+ | Phase I/II ESA |
| Utility service costs higher than expected | High | $20K–$200K | Early utility coordination |
| Permit timeline longer than expected | High | Indirect only | Early AHJ pre-app |

## RISK-Contingency-Guide

### Contingency by Phase

| Phase | Base Contingency | Adjust Up If | Adjust Down If |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-design / ROM | 20–30% | Complex program, new market, unusual site | Very simple scope, known site |
| Schematic Design | 15–20% | Incomplete program, value-engineering needed | Clean site, simple scope |
| Design Development | 10–15% | Unknown existing conditions, complex MEP | 90%+ design decisions made |
| 100% Construction Documents | 5–10% | Unusual conditions, volatile market | Simple TI, experienced team |
| Post-Award (Owner Contingency) | 3–7% | Complex project, OFE coordination | Simple, experienced team |

### Contingency for Specific Risk Factors

Add to base contingency as applicable:

| Risk Factor | Additional Contingency |
|---|---|
| Occupied building (phased construction) | +5–10% |
| Historic building | +5–15% |
| Unknown subsurface conditions | +5–10% |
| Aggressive schedule (fast-track) | +3–7% |
| High-volatility material (steel, copper) | +2–5% |
| New geography / unknown subcontractor market | +3–7% |
| Complex brand standards / owner approvals | +3–5% |
| Highly prescriptive specs (no substitutions) | +2–5% |
