# Barcelona: 9-Day Itinerary for Nathan & Bibba
**April 10–18, 2027** | Spring season | Vegan-friendly planning

## Pre-Trip Logistics

### Weather & Packing
- **Temperature:** 15–20°C (60–68°F), mild spring
- **Rainfall:** Moderate chance (40–50mm for the month)
- **Pack:** Cotton shirts, shorts, light jeans, light sweater/jacket for evenings and AC
- **Essentials:** Comfortable walking shoes, sunscreen, light rain jacket

### Getting Around
- **T-casual card** (rechargeable transit card) — Buy at airport, covers metro, buses, trams
- **Neighborhoods are walkable** — Most main areas within 20–30 minutes on foot
- **Metro is efficient** — Color-coded lines, cheap, frequent

### Vegan Dining Notes
Barcelona has a strong vegan scene. Key restaurants:
- **Rasoterra** (Gothic Quarter) — Elegant, seasonal organic, must-book
- **Teresa Carles / Flax & Kale** — Pioneer vegan chain, multiple locations
- **Billy Brunch and Garden** — Great breakfast/brunch vibe
- **La Riera** (Les Corts) — Vegetarian with excellent vegan options
- **Most restaurants** offer veggie versions; always ask ("versió vegana")

---

## Day 1: Saturday, April 10 — Arrival & Gothic Quarter

### Morning/Afternoon
- **Arrive at Barcelona-El Prat Airport**
  - Get T-casual card at airport (€11 + credit, unlimited zones 1–2)
  - Take Metro L9 South → L3 toward Lesseps, exit at your hotel area
- **Check in, rest, freshen up**

### Late Afternoon
- **Explore Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic)** — Start walking around hotel area
  - Narrow medieval streets, plazas, hidden corners
  - Window shop, get a feel for the neighborhood
  - Plaça Reial — Historic plaza with palm trees (great photo spot)

### Evening
- **Dinner:** **Rasoterra** (Barri Gòtic)
  - Reservation essential (book online or call +34 933 01 48 13)
  - Chef Daniele Rossi's seasonal organic vegan cuisine
  - Sculptural, elegant, locally sourced
  - Budget: €35–45pp with wine

### Night
- Walk along **Les Rambles** (main tree-lined boulevard) toward waterfront
- Grab gelato, soak in evening vibe, return to hotel

---

## Day 2: Sunday, April 11 — El Born & Picasso Museum

### Morning
- **Breakfast:** **Billy Brunch and Garden** (El Born)
  - Casual, cozy, excellent pastries and coffee
  - Vegan options clearly marked
  - Budget: €12–15pp

### Mid-Morning
- **Picasso Museum** (Museu Picasso)
  - Skip the queue with advance tickets (buy online)
  - Focus on early career and period rooms (don't try to see everything)
  - ~2 hours is solid
  - Budget: €14 (online), €12 (in-person)

### Afternoon
- **Wander El Born**
  - Browse vintage shops, galleries, bookstores
  - Santa María del Mar — Gothic basilica, awe-inspiring interior
  - Mercat de Santa Caterina — Historic covered market (stop for a drink)

### Evening
- **Aperitif:** Pintxos and wine at a local bar in El Born
- **Dinner:** **Teresa Carles** (Gothic Quarter or El Born location)
  - Pioneer vegan restaurant, solid casual food
  - Budget: €18–25pp

### Night
- **Magic Fountain show** (Montjuïc) — If energy permits
  - Performance times vary (8pm–10pm depending on season)
  - Beautiful light + water + music spectacle
  - Free admission (stand in plaza)
  - Takes ~20 min to walk down from city or grab metro

---

## Day 3: Monday, April 12 — Gaudí (Sagrada Familia + Casa Batlló)

### Morning
- **Breakfast:** Local café with cortado and tostada

### Late Morning
- **Sagrada Familia** (Basílica i Templo Expiatorio de la Sagrada Família)
  - Pre-book tickets online (€26 + tower access; 2-hour time slots)
  - Spine-tingling interior — organic curves, light through stained glass
  - Climb the tower for panoramic views (Nativity or Passion side, not both)
  - Allow 2–2.5 hours
  - Pro tip: Go early (10am slot) to beat crowds

### Lunch
- **Nearby café** on Plaça de la Sagrada Familia or walk to L'Eixample proper

### Afternoon
- **Casa Batlló** (Passeig de Gràcia)
  - Gaudí's modernist masterpiece (exterior is stunning, interior is magical)
  - Pre-book online to skip lines (€25)
  - Focus on main floors — roof and upper levels can feel crowded
  - Allow 1.5 hours
  - Alternative: If Batlló feels overwhelming, **Casa Milà (La Pedrera)** is equally good but less touristy

### Evening
- **Stroll Passeig de Gràcia** — Barcelona's main boulevard
  - Upscale shops, people-watching, beautiful building facades
  - **Sunset walk** toward Plaça de Catalunya

### Night
- **Dinner:** **Flax & Kale** (L'Eixample location on Passeig de Gràcia)
  - Trendy vegan spot, modern vibe
  - Budget: €20–28pp
- Retire early — big day!

---

## Day 4: Tuesday, April 13 — Park Güell + La Pedrera

### Morning
- **Breakfast:** Pastisseria with cappuccino

### Late Morning
- **Park Güell** (Parc Güell)
  - Pre-book morning ticket (€14; 9am–10am slot is ideal)
  - Whimsical Gaudí park — mosaic pathways, terrace views, dragon gate
  - Allow 1.5–2 hours to explore main areas
  - Climb to top terrace for city panorama
  - Note: Crowds build after 11am, so early is critical

### Lunch
- **In the park** or nearby café in Gràcia neighborhood
  - Local joints around the park have decent food

### Afternoon
- **Casa Milà (La Pedrera)** — If you skipped yesterday
  - Pre-book online (€25)
  - Rooftop is the highlight (chimney sculptures, 360° views)
  - Interior is less ornate than Batlló but equally interesting
  - 1–1.5 hours

### Late Afternoon
- **Gràcia neighborhood** (if energy permits)
  - Bohemian, hip area above L'Eixample
  - Plazas with outdoor cafés, local bookstores, galleries
  - Less touristy than Gothic Quarter
  - Wander and get lost intentionally

### Evening
- **Dinner:** **La Riera** (Les Corts, requires transport)
  - Or **light tapas** at a casual spot in Gràcia
  - Budget: €20–30pp

### Night
- Early night — rest up for Montjuïc day

---

## Day 5: Wednesday, April 14 — Montjuïc (Art + Gardens + Magic Fountain)

### Morning
- **Breakfast:** Café near hotel or in neighborhood

### Mid-Morning
- **Funicular up to Montjuïc** (or cable car for dramatic entry)
  - T-casual card covers funicular
  - Or walk from Poble Sec (20–25 min, uphill but scenic)

### Late Morning
- **National Art Museum of Catalonia (MNAC)**
  - Pre-book or visit early to avoid crowds
  - Romanesque murals + modern art (pick one era to focus on)
  - Budget: €12–15
  - Allow 2–2.5 hours (or 1 hour if selective)

### Lunch
- **MNAC café** (decent food, view over city)
- Or **Xemei** (nearby, local spot)

### Afternoon
- **Explore Montjuïc Terraces & Gardens**
  - Jardins del Mirador (sweeping city views)
  - Jardí Botànic (if interested in local flora)
  - Explore plazas and sculptures
  - Relax, sit, photograph
  - Allow 2 hours

### Late Afternoon
- Optional: **Joan Miró Foundation** (if you have energy)
  - Modern art collection, smaller than MNAC
  - €15

### Evening
- **Magic Fountain Show** (at dusk, 8pm–10pm depending on season)
  - Free
  - Arrive 20–30 min early for good spot
  - 15–20 min performance
  - Absolutely stunning — don't miss this

### Night
- **Casual dinner** near Magic Fountain plaza or walk back to central neighborhood
- Rest — big days done!

---

## Day 6: Thursday, April 15 — Barceloneta & Beach Day

### Morning/Afternoon
- **Slow morning** — Sleep in, coffee at hotel

- **Metro to Barceloneta neighborhood**
  - Working-class beach village, less touristy than Gothic Quarter
  - Narrow streets, local tapas bars, fishing heritage

- **Beach time** (Platja de la Barceloneta)
  - Walk, wade, sit with a book
  - April water is cool (15–17°C) — swimming optional, wading is refreshing
  - Beach cafés (xiringuitos) for drinks and snacks

### Lunch
- **Beach chiringuito** — Paella or seafood (they can do vegan paella)
  - Budget: €12–18pp

### Afternoon
- **More beach time or explore the neighborhood**
  - Mercat de la Boqueria (food market on Les Rambles) — If you haven't been
  - Sample vegan tapas, fresh juices, local produce
  - Browse flower stalls, art, energy

### Evening
- **Dinner:** **Casual seafood spot** in Barceloneta
  - Or **Teresa Carles** if you want guaranteed vegan
  - Budget: €18–28pp

### Night
- Early night, recover from culture overload

---

## Day 7: Friday, April 16 — Choice Day (Pick One)

### Option A: Modernisme Architecture Deep Dive
- **Sant Pau Art Nouveau Site** (north of city center)
  - Less touristy Gaudí-era buildings, hospital complex, stunning frescoes
  - Day trip feel, authentic Barcelona
  - Metro L5 to Sant Pau/Santa Tecla
  - Budget: €15

### Option B: Day Trip to Montserrat Monastery
- **Montserrat** (1 hour train from Barcelona)
  - Dramatic rock formations, benedictine monastery, spiritual vibe
  - Scenic train ride through Catalonia
  - Hiking trails, viewpoints
  - Lunch at monastery
  - Return by evening
  - Budget: €30–40pp (train + entry + food)

### Option C: Rest Day + Neighborhood Exploration
- **Pick one neighborhood you missed:**
  - **Poblenou** (hip, artsy, beachy, galleries and studios)
  - **Sant Antoni** (design district, vintage shops, bohemian vibe)
  - **Sarrià** (quiet residential, local feel, village atmosphere)
- Wander, eat at random spots, take photos, experience local Barcelona

**Recommendation for you two:** Montserrat is magical in spring. Go.

### Evening
- Return to central Barcelona
- Light dinner, rest

---

## Day 8: Saturday, April 17 — Revisit + Local Experiences

### Morning/Afternoon
- **Pick ONE favorite spot from earlier days and revisit**
  - Longer coffee at a café in El Born
  - Lunch at a restaurant you want to repeat
  - Retrace a path you loved

### Late Afternoon
- **Parc de la Ciutadella** (if you haven't been)
  - Green lungs of Barcelona, lake, sculptures
  - Less touristy than major landmarks
  - Great for couples — scenic walks, benches, people-watching
  - Free, beautiful

### Evening
- **Dress up, have a nice dinner**
  - Book a special vegan restaurant (Rasoterra again? Somewhere new?)
  - Splurge a little — it's the penultimate night
  - Budget: €40–55pp

### Night
- **Nightlife (optional)**
  - Barcelona is a late-night city (dinner at 9–10pm is normal)
  - Go to a vermouth bar (aperitif culture is huge)
  - Live music venue if something appeals
  - Or just walk the city lit up at night

### Morning

### Afternoon
