# PT-Ground-Up-Commercial — Estimating Guide

> Covers: freestanding commercial buildings, pad sites, retail centers, restaurant prototypes, medical buildings, and mixed-use ground-up development. From raw site to certificate of occupancy.

---

## Ground-Up vs. TI — The Mindset Shift

Ground-up estimating requires thinking in phases and systems that TI work never triggers:
- **Site work and civil** can equal 20–40% of total project cost on challenging sites
- **Utility connections** are budgeted in the design, not discovered later
- **Building envelope** (structure + skin) typically 30–45% of total hard cost
- **Long lead items** must be ordered months before construction starts
- **Soft costs** (A/E, permits, testing) are 8–15% of hard cost vs. 3–8% for TI

## Phase-by-Phase Scope Breakdown

### Phase 1: Site Preparation & Civil

**Must estimate before breaking ground:**
- Demolition of existing improvements (if any)
- Environmental remediation (if required — budget $50K–$500K+ for contaminated sites)
- Earthwork: cut/fill balance, import/export
- Stormwater management (detention pond, underground vault, bioswale)
- Erosion and sediment control
- Rough grading and sub-base prep

**Critical unknowns:**
- Soil bearing capacity (geotechnical report required — $5K–$15K)
- Rock (if present: add $40–$120/CY for blasting/breaking)
- High water table (dewatering: $20K–$100K+)
- Existing underground utilities (locate before estimate)

### Phase 2: Utilities & Infrastructure

**On-site utilities (developer's responsibility):**
- Water main from street to building
- Sanitary sewer from building to main
- Storm drainage system
- Gas service from meter to building
- Electric service: underground conduit from transformer to building
- Telecom/data conduit

**Off-site utility connections (often missed):**
- Water tap and meter fees: $5K–$50K+
- Sewer connection fee: $5K–$80K+ (based on fixture units)
- Electric utility transformer and service: $15K–$150K+
- Gas meter and regulator: $5K–$30K
- Traffic signal or turn lane (sometimes required by city): $50K–$300K+

**Rule:** Budget $75K–$300K for utility connections on a typical ground-up pad site. Verify with each utility and municipality before finalizing.

### Phase 3: Foundation

**Foundation types and cost:**

| Type | When Used | Cost Range |
|---|---|---|
| Conventional spread footings + slab | Good bearing soils, no frost | $8–$14/SF |
| Thickened edge slab (monolithic) | Small buildings, good soil | $9–$15/SF |
| Drilled piers (caissons) | Poor bearing, expansive soils | $14–$25/SF |
| Grade beams + piers | Soft/expansive soils | $14–$22/SF |
| Mat/raft foundation | Very poor soils, heavy loads | $18–$30/SF |

**Soil condition triggers:**
- Clay-heavy soils (Southeast, Texas, Midwest): expansive; may require moisture barrier, post-tension slab
- Rock near surface (Appalachian corridor, parts of Nashville): blasting adds significant cost
- Fill areas: bearing capacity unknown; test pits or borings required

### Phase 4: Structure

**Construction types (IBC):**

| Type | Description | Typical Use | Cost Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Type VB (wood frame) | Wood stud walls, wood/TJI roof | Small restaurants, retail <10,000 SF | Baseline |
| Type IIIB (masonry + wood) | CMU walls, wood roof | Retail, restaurants, small commercial | +5–10% |
| Type IIB (non-combustible) | Metal stud, metal deck, steel | Mid-size commercial, retail | +10–20% |
| Type IIA (1-hr rated) | Protected steel, concrete deck | Medical, larger commercial | +15–25% |
| Type IA (2-hr rated) | Fully protected, concrete | High-rises, hospitals | +30–50% |

**Structural framing systems:**

| System | $/SF Installed | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Conventional steel (WF beams/cols) | $18–$35/SF | Mid-to-large commercial |
| Open web steel joists + deck | $12–$22/SF | Retail, warehouse, large spans |
| Tilt-up concrete panels | $10–$20/SF of wall | Large footprint, single-story |
| Wood frame (platform) | $8–$16/SF | Small buildings, residential-scale |
| Pre-engineered metal building | $15–$28/SF | Warehouse, industrial, back-of-house |

### Phase 6: Building Systems (MEP)

On ground-up, MEP must be sized for the full building from day one:
- Electrical service: size for 100% occupancy + 20% growth factor
- HVAC: size for all tenant types; pad for future rooftop equipment
- Plumbing: size drain, water, gas for full program
- Fire suppression: design for highest-hazard occupancy

## Soft Costs for Ground-Up

Ground-up projects carry significantly higher soft costs than TI:

| Soft Cost Category | % of Hard Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Architectural & Engineering | 5–10% | Full service A/E from SD through CA |
| Civil Engineering | 1–3% | Site design, drainage, utilities |
| Geotechnical (soils report) | 0.2–0.5% | Required for foundation design |
| Environmental (Phase I/II) | 0.1–0.3% | Required for financing; Phase II if needed |
| Survey (topographic, boundary) | 0.1–0.3% | |
| Owner's Project Manager / CM | 2–5% | If using outside PM |
| Permit fees | 0.5–2% | Based on construction value |
| Utility connection fees | 1–3% | Widely variable; verify early |
| Testing & Special Inspections | 0.5–1.5% | Structural, MEP, soils |
| Commissioning | 0.3–1% | MEP Cx; required on some projects |
| Builder's Risk Insurance | 0.5–1.5% | Annual premium on project value |
| **Total Soft Cost** | **10–25%** | Higher on complex/first-time projects |

## Common Ground-Up Estimate Errors

1. **Missing utility connection fees** — always $75K–$300K+ on commercial pad
2. **Underestimating site work** — challenging sites can push site work to 40% of project
3. **Rock or poor soils** — geotechnical report before finalizing foundation budget
4. **Wet utilities undersized** — size for full occupancy from day one
5. **Traffic impact fees** — some jurisdictions charge per-trip fees: $5K–$50K+
6. **Off-site improvements** — city may require sidewalk, curb cuts, turn lane as permit condition
7. **No escalation applied** — ground-up takes 12–24 months; escalation is significant
8. **Soft costs underestimated** — first-time developers routinely undercount A/E and PM fees
