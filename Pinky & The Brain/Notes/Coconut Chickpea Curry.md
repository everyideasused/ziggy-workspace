## ЁЯУЛ Ingredients

### Curry
- 2 tbsp coconut oil
- 1 medium red or yellow onion, diced
- 14 oz fresh or canned tomatoes, diced
- Sea salt & ground black pepper, to taste
- 16 oz (1 can) chickpeas, drained & rinsed
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1┬╜ tbsp garam masala
- 1 tsp curry powder
- 1/4 tsp cumin
- 13.5 oz can full-fat coconut milk
- 2 tsp coconut flour (optional, for thickening тАУ can use regular flour)
- 1 small lime, juice of **тЖР DON'T SKIP!**

### For Serving
- Basmati rice, brown rice, or naan bread
- Fresh cilantro (optional)

## ЁЯСитАНЁЯН│ Instructions

1. **Heat oil** тАУ In deep pot over medium-high heat, add coconut oil.

2. **Cook aromatics** тАУ Add onions and tomatoes. Grind sea salt and pepper over mixture and stir. Lower heat to medium and cook until tomato juices release and onions soften, about 10 minutes. *This step develops flavorтАФdon't rush it!*

3. **Add chickpeas and spices** тАУ Mix in chickpeas, garlic, garam masala, curry powder, and cumin. Stir to combine.

4. **Add coconut milk** тАУ Stir in coconut milk and coconut flour (if using, helps thicken). Bring to a boil, then reduce to medium-low and simmer 10-12 minutes.

5. **Season and finish** тАУ Taste and add more salt/pepper if desired. Remove from heat and squeeze lime juice over the top, stirring to combine. **Don't skip the limeтАФit brings out all the flavors!**

6. **Serve** тАУ Let cool slightly, then serve over rice or with naan. Enjoy!

## ЁЯУЭ Notes
> *Tweaks, substitutions, what worked, what didn't.*

- **Always use the lime** тАУ This is THE most important tip. Lime juice brings out all the layers of flavor. Essential!
- **Don't rush the aromatics** тАУ Saut├йing onions/tomatoes for full 10 min makes a huge difference
- **Use full-fat coconut milk** тАУ Light coconut milk reduces richness and creaminess. Worth the extra fat!
- **Make it your own** тАУ Add sweet potatoes, bell peppers, spinach, broccoli, cauliflower, carrots, etc. Adjust seasonings for additions.
- **Add heat** тАУ Crushed red pepper flakes (1/4 tsp), harissa paste, sriracha, or scotch bonnet pepper sauce
- **Tomato substitute** тАУ If allergic, use pumpkin or butternut squash puree for thickness/creaminess
- **Storage** тАУ Refrigerate in airtight container 3-4 days. Reheat in microwave or stovetop. May need to add coconut milk/water if too thick.
- **Freezer-friendly** тАУ Freeze in single servings for 2-3 months. Perfect for meal prep!
- **Protein boost** тАУ Add crispy tofu cubes to bump protein to 15-20g per serving
