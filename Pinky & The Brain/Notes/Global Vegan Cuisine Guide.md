# 🌍 Global Vegan Cuisine Guide

> **Purpose:** Cuisine-by-cuisine reference for authentic vegan cooking techniques, flavor profiles, pantry essentials, and signature dishes. Covers Nathan's preferred cuisines — Mexican, Italian, Asian, Mediterranean, Indian — with enough depth to cook at a genuine level, not a "vegan adaptation" level.

---

## Mexican Cuisine

### The Authentic Vegan Foundation

Mexican cuisine is naturally plant-forward. The challenge is the traditional use of lard (manteca) in beans and masa, and dairy (cheese, crema, butter) as finishers. Strip these out and the underlying cuisine — beans, masa, chili, tomato, avocado — is stunning vegan cooking.

### Pantry Essentials

**Chilies (dried — most important):**
| Chili | Flavor | Heat | Use |
|-------|--------|------|-----|
| Ancho | Fruity, raisin, mild | Low | Mole, enchilada sauce, marinades |
| Guajillo | Bright, fruity, tangy | Low-medium | Salsa roja, birria broth, soups |
| Pasilla | Earthy, chocolate notes, raisin | Low-medium | Mole negro, enchilada sauce |
| Mulato | Rich, chocolate, smoke | Low | Complex moles |
| Chipotle (dried/canned in adobo) | Smoky, rich | Medium-hot | Everything — marinades, soups, tacos |
| Árbol | Bright red heat, sharp | Hot | Salsa de árbol, finishing heat |
| Cascabel | Nutty, woodsy | Mild | Salsas, soups |

**Core pantry:**
- Mexican oregano (different from Mediterranean — more citrusy)
- Cumin (toasted whole → ground fresh)
- Epazote (herb — critical for black beans; reduces gas and adds authentic flavor)
- Masa harina (Maseca for tortillas, tamales)
- Piloncillo (raw cane sugar) for traditional recipes
- Canned chipotles in adobo
- Tomatillos (fresh or canned)

### Foundational Techniques

**Toasting dried chilies (essential):**
```
Remove stems and seeds
Heat dry skillet or comal to medium
Press chili flat → toast 15-30 seconds per side until fragrant and slightly puffed
Do NOT burn — bitter immediately if burned
Soak in hot water 15-20 min → blend with soaking liquid
```

**Charring tomatoes and tomatillos:**
```
Place tomatoes/tomatillos + onion + garlic directly on gas flame OR
under broiler on sheet pan
Char until blackened in spots — adds depth and smokiness
Blend charred vegetables → base for salsas and moles
```

**Nixtamalization (masa):**
Corn treated with calcium hydroxide (cal) → nixtamal → ground into masa. Dramatically improves protein quality (releases niacin), calcium content, and flavor. This is why Mexican corn and masa taste so different from plain corn flour.

### Signature Vegan Dishes

**Tacos al Pastor (Vegan):**
- Protein: Marinated tempeh or jackfruit
- Adobo: Ancho + guajillo + chipotle + pineapple + achiote paste + garlic + cumin + orange juice
- Method: Marinate overnight → roast or grill → serve in corn tortillas with diced pineapple, white onion, cilantro, salsa verde

**Black Bean Mole Enchiladas:**
- Mole negro: Ancho + mulato + pasilla + charred tomato + tomatillo + chocolate (70%+) + sesame + plantain + cinnamon + cumin + charred bread
- Filling: Black beans + caramelized onion + roasted pepper + toasted pumpkin seeds
- Finish: Drizzle with mole, top with vegan crema (cashew sour cream) + toasted sesame

**Vegan Birria:**
- Traditional: Goat in chili broth → Vegan: Jackfruit + seitan + dried chili broth (guajillo + ancho + chipotle)
- Method: Deep chili broth → braise protein → serve with consommé for dipping + white onion + cilantro + lime

**Pozole Verde (Vegan):**
- Broth base: Tomatillo + serrano + pepitas + epazote + hominy
- Protein: White beans + mushrooms + jackfruit
- Traditional garnishes: Shredded cabbage + dried oregano + lime + radish + toasted pepitas

**Tamales (Vegan):**
- Masa: Masa harina + warm broth (kombu-based) + coconut oil (replaces lard) + baking powder + salt
- Filling options: Black bean + chipotle, rajas (poblano) + corn, roasted tomatillo salsa + jackfruit
- Steam 60–90 min; done when masa pulls cleanly from husk

## Italian Cuisine

### The Vegan Italian Paradigm

Italian cooking at its best is extraordinarily plant-forward — cucina povera (peasant cooking) is mostly legumes, grains, vegetables, and olive oil. The challenge is cheese (parmesan, ricotta, mozzarella) and seafood in coastal dishes.

### Pantry Essentials

- EVOO (high quality — it matters more in Italian cooking than any other cuisine)
- Pecorino Romano → cashew parmesan (see [[Vegan Classic Dish Swaps]])
- San Marzano canned tomatoes (DOP certified)
- Parmigiano-Reggiano analogue: nutritional yeast + cashews
- Dried pasta (bronze-die cut holds sauce better)
- '00' flour for fresh pasta
- Arborio / Carnaroli rice (risotto)
- Farro, barley (whole grains for minestre)
- Dried porcini mushrooms (umami bomb for ragù)
- Capers, olives, sun-dried tomatoes
- Anchovies → white miso + seaweed (1 tsp + ½ tsp)

### Regional Guide

| Region | Character | Signature Vegan Dishes |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| Tuscany (cucina povera) | Olive oil, beans, hearty bread | Ribollita, Panzanella, Fagioli all'Uccelletto |
| Sicily | North African influence, eggplant, capers, almonds | Caponata, Pasta con le Melanzane, Arancini (vegan) |
| Naples | Tomato, pizza, pasta | Pizza Margherita (vegan cheese), Pasta e Fagioli, Puttanesca |
| Emilia-Romagna | Rich, egg pasta, ragù | Lentil Bolognese, homemade pasta |
| Venetia | Risotto, polenta, cicchetti | Risotto con Funghi Porcini, Polenta e Fagioli |
| Liguria | Pesto, focaccia, beans | Classic Pesto (vegan), Focaccia, Minestrone alla Genovese |

### Foundational Techniques

**Soffritto:**
```
Onion + carrot + celery (2:1:1 ratio) — finely diced
Low heat + EVOO + salt — cook 15-20 min
Not browned — soft, sweet, translucent
This is the base of 80% of Italian dishes
```

**Pasta e Fagioli (Perfect Vegan Comfort Food):**
```
Soffritto → add garlic → tomato paste (cook 2 min) → San Marzano tomatoes
Add cooked borlotti/cannellini beans (half whole, half puréed for body)
Rosemary + bay + kombu (hidden umami)
Add short pasta (ditalini) in the last 10 min
Finish with EVOO, pepper, cashew parmesan
```

**Risotto Technique:**
```
Toast arborio in oil until translucent → add wine → absorb fully
Add warm broth ONE LADLE AT A TIME — stirring releases starch → cream texture
Do NOT add cold stock
Total: ~18-20 min of active stirring
Finish (mantecatura): Remove from heat → add 2 tbsp vegan butter + cashew parmesan → rest 2 min covered → serve immediately
The resting is critical — it allows the starch to set for proper consistency
```

**Pesto Genovese (Authentic Method):**
Mortar and pestle (not blender) for traditional texture:
```
Basil + garlic → bruise in mortar to paste
Add toasted pine nuts → work in
Add cashew parmesan + EVOO gradually
Salt → taste
If using blender: pulse (don't blend continuous) → add EVOO through feed tube
```

### High-Protein Italian Applications

| Dish | Protein Strategy | Protein |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| Pasta Bolognese | Tempeh + lentil crumble + mushroom | ~35g/serving |
| Lasagna | Lentil ragù + tofu ricotta + cashew béchamel | ~40g/serving |
| Protein pasta | Chickpea or lentil pasta + tempeh ragù | ~45g/serving |
| Ribollita | White beans (cannellini) + farro + vegetables | ~25g/serving |
| Risotto | Add white beans blended into broth + pea protein swirl | ~30g/serving |

## Asian Cuisines

### Japanese

**Flavor profile:** Umami + subtle sweetness (mirin) + salt + dashi depth

**Essential pantry:**
- Kombu (kelp) — foundation of dashi
- Dried shiitake — second layer of dashi
- Miso (shiro/white, aka/red, awase)
- Tamari or soy sauce (tamari = gluten-free)
- Mirin (sweet rice wine)
- Sake (or dry white wine)
- Rice vinegar
- Toasted sesame oil
- Sesame seeds
- Nori sheets

**Vegan dashi (the foundation of Japanese cooking):**
```
Kombu: Cold water soak 30 min → heat gently to 60°C (do not boil) → remove
Shiitake: Rehydrate dried shiitake in separate water → add soaking liquid to dashi
Result: Deeply savory, complex broth — the base of ramen, miso soup, sauces
```

**Signature vegan Japanese dishes:**
- Miso ramen (kombu-shiitake dashi + red miso + sesame paste + soy)
- Agedashi tofu (deep-fried silken tofu in dashi + ponzu)
- Vegetable tempura (ice-cold batter → hot oil → instant crust)
- Nasu dengaku (grilled eggplant + miso glaze)
- Inari sushi (tofu pockets + sushi rice)
- Gomae (sesame-dressed spinach or green beans)

### Chinese

**Flavor profile:** Umami + heat + aromatics (garlic, ginger, scallion) + five-spice complexity

**Essential pantry:**
- Doubanjiang (spicy broad bean paste) — Sichuan cooking
- Fermented black beans (douchi)
- Chinese five-spice
- Star anise
- Sichuan peppercorn (numbing heat — hua jiao)
- Shaoxing rice wine
- Chinkiang black vinegar (complex, essential for dumplings)
- Dried wood ear mushrooms
- Toasted sesame paste
- Lee Kum Kee hoisin (vegan varieties)
- Oyster sauce → use vegan mushroom oyster sauce

**Key techniques:**
- **Wok hei (breath of the wok):** Extremely high heat + tossing → slight char and smokiness impossible to replicate on low heat. Use the highest heat your stove allows; don't overcrowd.
- **Velveting:** Marinate protein in cornstarch + soy + shaoxing wine → coat → brief blanch in oil or water → silky texture in stir-fry
- **Ma po tofu (vegan):** Silken or medium tofu + doubanjiang + fermented black beans + Sichuan peppercorn + broth + chili oil. No meat needed — the fermented pastes carry everything.

### Thai

**Flavor profile:** Equal parts sweet, sour, salty, spicy — aromatics (lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime, Thai basil)

**Essential pantry:**
- Lemongrass (fresh or frozen — dried is poor substitute)
- Galangal (different from ginger — cannot substitute)
- Kaffir lime leaves (essential; dried acceptable)
- Thai basil (different from Italian basil)
- Coconut milk + coconut cream
- Tamarind paste (fruity acid)
- Fish sauce → coconut aminos + seaweed flakes (1:1 swap)
- Shrimp paste → 1 tbsp miso + 1 tsp seaweed powder
- Palm sugar (or coconut sugar)
- Makrut lime zest

**Curry pastes (make fresh):**
The difference between authentic Thai and mediocre Thai is the paste. Fresh-made paste → mortar and pestle or high-powered blender:
```
Green curry paste: Lemongrass + galangal + kaffir lime zest + green chili + shallot + garlic + cilantro root + Thai basil + coriander seed + cumin + shrimp paste (omit or sub miso)
Red curry paste: As above but red chilies, no green herbs
Massaman: Red base + dried chilies + cardamom + cinnamon + clove + star anise
```

**The pad thai technique (vegan):**
- Fish sauce → coconut aminos
- Shrimp/chicken → extra-firm tofu (pressed) + tofu puffs
- Tamarind sauce (tamarind + palm sugar + coconut aminos — caramelized)
- Very high heat → do not overcrowd → wok hei essential
- Rice noodles soaked (not cooked) then finished in wok

### Korean

**Flavor profile:** Fermented funk (kimchi, gochujang, doenjang) + sesame + garlic + sweet-savory

**Essential pantry:**
- Gochujang (Korean chili paste — deep umami + heat + sweet)
- Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes — bright red, fruity, medium heat)
- Doenjang (Korean fermented soybean paste — stronger than miso)
- Sesame oil (toasted)
- Perilla leaves (kkaennip) — similar to shiso
- Napa cabbage
- Daikon radish

**Bibimbap (vegan):** Rice bowl with individually seasoned vegetables (spinach, bean sprouts, mushrooms, zucchini, carrot) + gochujang sauce + fried tofu or tempeh + toasted sesame. The artistry is in the individual vegetable preparation.

**Korean fried "chicken" (vegan):**
Cauliflower florets → batter + fry → toss in gochujang + soy + garlic + ginger + sesame + maple (balanced sweet-savory-spicy)

## Mediterranean Cuisine

### The Foundational Pantry

- EVOO (generous amounts — this cuisine runs on olive oil)
- Za'atar (thyme + sesame + sumac blend)
- Sumac (fruity, tart powder — often used like lemon)
- Dukkah (nut + spice blend — Egyptian)
- Preserved lemons (fermented — transformative)
- Harissa (North African chili paste)
- Pomegranate molasses (sweet-sour-fruity)
- Tahini
- Chickpeas, lentils, fava beans
- Freekeh, bulgur, couscous (Moroccan)
- Barberries (Persian)

### Regional Profiles

| Region | Key Flavors | Signature Vegan Dishes |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| Lebanese | Lemon + za'atar + tahini + parsley | Hummus, Fattoush, Tabbouleh, Kibbeh (lentil), Mujaddara |
| Greek | EVOO + lemon + oregano + fresh herbs | Spanakopita (vegan feta), Fasolada, Imam Bayildi |
| Moroccan | Ras el hanout + preserved lemon + olives | Tagine, Couscous with vegetables, Harira soup |
| Turkish | Sumac + pomegranate + bulgur | Mercimek Köftesi (lentil), Fasulye, Börek (vegan) |
| Israeli | Tahini + za'atar + everything | Shakshuka (vegan), Sabich, Sabzi Khordan |
| Egyptian | Cumin + coriander + dill + fava | Ful Medames, Koshari, Molokhia (vegan) |
| Persian | Saffron + barberries + walnuts + herbs | Ghormeh Sabzi (vegan), Fesenjan (walnut pomegranate stew) |

### Technique: The Perfect Hummus

```
500g dried chickpeas → soak overnight → cook until very soft (add baking soda last 10 min)
Hot chickpeas + ice water → blender (not food processor)
200ml tahini first → blend
120ml fresh lemon juice → blend
4 garlic cloves → blend
1 tsp salt, ½ tsp cumin
Thin with ice water until silky smooth (the cold water is the secret — it emulsifies and lightens)
Pass through fine sieve for Michelin-level smoothness
Plate with EVOO pool, paprika, parsley, whole chickpeas, toasted pine nuts
```

## Indian Cuisine

### The Spice Framework

Indian cooking is built on systematic spice layering — not random spice adding. The framework:

1. **Whole spices in hot oil first** (mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried chili — they pop and bloom)
2. **Aromatics second** (onion, then ginger + garlic paste)
3. **Ground spices third** (after onion softens — cook 1–2 min)
4. **Wet ingredients fourth** (tomato, yogurt — stir to combine with spice base)
5. **Protein/vegetables last** (simmer until tender)
6. **Tempering last (tarka/tadka)** — another round of spices in hot oil poured over finished dish

### Essential Indian Pantry

**Whole spices (toast whole before grinding when possible):**
- Cumin seeds
- Mustard seeds (black)
- Fenugreek seeds
- Coriander seeds
- Cardamom (green and black)
- Cloves
- Cinnamon sticks
- Star anise
- Dried red chilies (Kashmiri — mild red color, less heat)

**Ground spices:**
- Turmeric
- Cumin powder
- Coriander powder
- Garam masala (finish, not for cooking — loses flavor with heat)
- Kashmiri chili powder (color without fire)
- Amchur (dried mango powder — fruity acid)
- Asafoetida (hing) — use sparingly; powerful; reduces legume gas

**Wet ingredients:**
- Ginger-garlic paste (equal parts fresh — blend and refrigerate)
- Curry leaves (fresh or frozen — essential for South Indian cooking)
- Tamarind paste
- Coconut milk (South Indian)
- Tomato purée or fresh tomatoes

### Signature High-Protein Vegan Indian Dishes

**Dal (lentil soup) — Most important Indian preparation:**
```
Red lentils: Fast-cooking, sweet, used for everyday dal
Toor dal: Yellow pigeon peas — mild, South Indian sambhar base
Chana dal: Split chickpeas — rich, Punjabi dal
Urad dal: Black gram — makhani base, creamy when cooked whole
Black whole urad: Dal makhani base (vegan with coconut cream)
```

**Chana Masala (highest protein Indian dish):**
Chickpeas + tomato-onion masala + chole masala spice blend + amchur + black salt. One of the most complete vegan meals possible — protein + iron + fiber.

**Palak Tofu (Saag):**
Tofu replaces paneer 1:1 in texture when extra-firm and well-pressed. Spinach + onion + tomato + cream (cashew or coconut) + garam masala → cubed tofu added at end.

**Rajma:** Kidney beans in rich tomato-onion gravy — Punjabi comfort food. One of the best vegan kidney bean preparations in global cuisine.

## American Cuisine (Vegan Reinvention)

### Comfort Food Classics — Vegan Execution

| Dish | Protein Strategy | Key Technique |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| BBQ "pulled pork" | Young green jackfruit + seitan | Slow braise in BBQ sauce |
| Mac and cheese | Cashew + potato + carrot sauce | Blend until silky smooth |
| Buffalo "wings" | Cauliflower or seitan | Double-coat + bake or fry |
| Chili | Kidney + black beans + lentils + tempeh | Layer textures |
| Loaded potato skins | Cashew sour cream + vegan cheddar | Bake potato, scoop, refill |
| Breakfast burrito | Tofu scramble + black beans + guac | Kala namak for egg flavor |
| Philly "cheesesteak" | Seitan thin-sliced + mushroom | Cast iron sear + cashew cheese |
| Crab cakes | Hearts of palm + chickpea | Old Bay + pan-fry |

### Southern Cuisine (Vegan)

- **Hoppin' John:** Black-eyed peas + rice + collard greens + smoked paprika (liquid smoke optional)
- **Fried "chicken":** Seitan cutlet + buttermilk (plant milk + ACV) soak → seasoned flour → fry
- **Gumbo:** Okra (the natural thickener) + filé powder + dark roux (flour + oil, cooked to dark chocolate) + vegetables + beans
- **Biscuits and gravy:** Vegan biscuits + tempeh sausage crumble + plant milk white gravy
- **Collard greens:** Long-braised with smoked paprika + apple cider vinegar + liquid smoke + broth
