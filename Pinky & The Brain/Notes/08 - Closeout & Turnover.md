# 08 - Closeout & Turnover

> **Phase 8 of 10** | Predecessor: [[07 - Construction Execution]] | Successor: [[09 - Post-Construction & Warranty]]

---

## Purpose

Formally conclude the construction contract, transfer the completed project to the owner, and ensure all administrative, financial, and documentation obligations are satisfied.

**Critical principle:** Closeout begins during construction, not after. Smart PMs begin collecting closeout documents from the first month of construction.

---

## Closeout Process — Sequential Steps

### Step 1: Substantial Completion
- **Definition:** The point at which the project is sufficiently complete for the owner to use for its intended purpose (per AIA A201 General Conditions)
- Architect issues **Certificate of Substantial Completion** (AIA G704)
- Triggers: start of warranty period, potential cessation of liquidated damages, owner's insurance obligation begins
- Owner may begin occupancy (with TCO if CO not yet issued)

### Step 2: Punch List Generation
- Joint walkthrough: Owner, Architect, GC (and PM/CM if applicable)
- Document all incomplete, deficient, or non-conforming work
- Organize punch items by: location, responsible sub, priority, due date
- Photograph each item for documentation
- GC distributes items to responsible subcontractors
- Track completion percentage weekly

### Step 3: Final Inspections
- Schedule all required final inspections with AHJ (see [[04 - Entitlements & Permitting]])
- Building final, fire final, MEP finals, health department (if applicable)
- Address any inspection corrections immediately
- Obtain **Certificate of Occupancy (CO)** — the legal authorization to occupy

### Step 4: Commissioning
- Verify all building systems operate as designed
- HVAC: Test and balance (TAB), controls commissioning, setpoints verified
- Electrical: Panel schedules verified, emergency systems tested, lighting controls programmed
- Plumbing: Flow tests, backflow prevention certification, water heater operation
- Fire protection: Fire alarm acceptance test, sprinkler flow test, fire pump test
- Elevator: Inspection and certification
- Low-voltage: Data/telecom testing, security system commissioning
- For detailed commissioning scope, sector-specific sequences, and PM management checklist, see [[Commissioning & Systems Turnover]]

### Step 5: Closeout Documentation Package
The GC must deliver a complete closeout package:

| Document | Description |
|----------|------------|
| As-built drawings | Marked-up construction drawings showing actual conditions (field changes) |
| O&M manuals | Operation and maintenance manuals for all equipment/systems |
| Warranty letters | Manufacturer and subcontractor warranty documentation |
| Equipment list | All installed equipment with model/serial numbers, contact info |
| Test reports | All commissioning, testing, and balance reports |
| Attic stock | Spare materials (paint, tile, carpet, ceiling tile) per spec requirements |
| Keys and access | All keys, access cards, codes, master key schedules |
| Training | Owner training on building systems operation (HVAC, fire alarm, security) |
| Final lien waivers | Unconditional lien waivers from GC and all subcontractors |
| Permit close-out | All permits finaled with AHJ |
| As-built survey | Final survey showing completed improvements |
| Hazmat documentation | Any abatement documentation and clearances |
| Specialty certifications | Fire sprinkler certification, elevator certificate, etc. |

### Step 6: Financial Closeout
- Final change order log reconciled — all PCOs resolved (approved, denied, or withdrawn)
- Final pay application submitted with:
  - 100% completion on all SOV line items
  - All approved change orders incorporated
  - Final retainage release request
  - Unconditional final lien waivers from GC and all subs
- Owner's final review and payment approval
- Retainage release (may be partial until all punch items complete)
- Construction loan final draw (if applicable)

### Step 7: Demobilization
- Contractor removes all equipment, trailers, temporary facilities
- Site is cleaned and restored to final condition
- All construction debris removed
- Temporary utilities disconnected, permanent utilities activated
- Security fencing removed

### Step 8: Project Handover
- Formal handover meeting with owner
- Transfer of all closeout documents
- Owner training on systems
- Emergency contact list for warranty issues
- Transfer of insurance from builder's risk to permanent property insurance
- For builder's risk to permanent insurance transition and post-completion coverage requirements, see [[Insurance & Bonding During Construction]]

## TIA Closeout (Retail/Commercial Tenants)

For projects funded by a Tenant Improvement Allowance (see [[Tenant Improvement Allowances]]):
- Compile all required TIA reimbursement documentation
- Obtain landlord sign-off on completed improvements
- Submit final TIA draw with: invoices, lien waivers, CO, photos
- Reconcile TIA spent vs. allowance — document any overage or unused balance
- Confirm rent commencement trigger has been met

---

## Duration: 2-8 weeks (from substantial completion to final completion)

**Warning:** Closeout often drags because of:
- Slow punch list completion by subcontractors
- Missing closeout documents (O&M manuals are notoriously late)
- Outstanding change order negotiations
- Delayed final inspections
- Retainage disputes

---

## Transition to Next Phase

**Gate criteria to advance to [[09 - Post-Construction & Warranty]]:**
1. CO issued
2. Punch list 100% complete (or acceptable holdback established)
3. All closeout documents received and accepted
4. Final payment processed
5. Retainage released (or retained per outstanding items)
6. Project formally turned over to owner
