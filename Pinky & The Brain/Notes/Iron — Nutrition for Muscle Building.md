# MODULE 05 — NUTRITION
### PhD-Level Reference for AI Fitness Agent | Vegan-Optimized

## OVERVIEW

Nutrition is the other half of the adaptation equation. No training plan produces its full potential without adequate protein, appropriate calories, micronutrient sufficiency, and strategic timing. For Nathan, as a vegan pursuing body recomposition, nutrition requires intentional planning — not because a vegan diet is inadequate, but because optimal muscle-building nutrition on any diet requires attention to detail.

## MACRONUTRIENT FRAMEWORK

### Caloric Targets by Phase

**First: Calculate Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)**

Nathan's TDEE estimate (Harris-Benedict + activity multiplier):

| Variable | Value |
|---|---|
| Weight | 147.7 lbs (67.0 kg) |
| Height | 5'9" (175.3 cm) |
| Age | 44 |
| Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) | ~1,713 kcal/day |

BMR Formula (Mifflin-St Jeor, more accurate than Harris-Benedict):
BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age + 5
= 10(67) + 6.25(175.3) − 5(44) + 5
= 670 + 1095.6 − 220 + 5 = **~1,551 kcal**

Activity multipliers:
- Sedentary (desk job, minimal movement): × 1.2 → ~1,861 kcal
- Lightly active (1–3 workouts/week): × 1.375 → ~2,133 kcal
- Moderately active (3–5 workouts/week): × 1.55 → ~2,404 kcal
- Very active (6–7 workouts/week): × 1.725 → ~2,675 kcal

**Nathan's estimated TDEE range**: ~2,200–2,500 kcal/day depending on training volume.

**Agent note**: TDEE formulas are estimates ±10–20%. Real TDEE is determined empirically — track intake and bodyweight for 2–3 weeks to find the true maintenance level.

---

### Caloric Targets by Goal Phase

**Recomp Phase (Current)**: Maintenance calories ± 0–200 kcal
- Target: 2,200–2,400 kcal/day
- On training days: Can eat slightly above (up to +200 kcal)
- On rest days: Can eat slightly below (down to −200 kcal)
- This "calorie cycling" approach supports muscle building on training days and fat oxidation on rest days

**Lean Bulk Phase** (when body fat reaches ~14–15%): Modest surplus
- Target: TDEE + 200–400 kcal/day
- Expected gain: 0.5–1 lb/week; aim for slow gain to minimize fat accumulation
- Maximum rate: Never exceed +500 kcal without monitoring body fat

**Cut Phase** (if Nathan wants an accelerated fat loss push): Deficit
- Target: TDEE − 300–500 kcal/day
- Expected loss: 0.6–1 lb/week (aggressive enough to lose fat; conservative enough to preserve muscle)
- **Protein requirement increases during a cut** — muscle is at risk when in deficit
- Never exceed −750 kcal deficit; this accelerates muscle loss

## PROTEIN — THE MOST CRITICAL MACRONUTRIENT

### Why Protein Matters
Muscle protein synthesis (MPS) requires adequate amino acids. The training stimulus creates the demand; protein provides the raw material. Without sufficient protein, the body cannot build or maintain lean tissue regardless of training quality.

### Protein Targets for Nathan

| Scenario | Daily Protein Target |
|---|---|
| Minimum effective (maintenance) | 0.7g per lb bodyweight = ~103g |
| Recomp (current goal) | 0.9–1.1g per lb = **133–163g** |
| Active muscle building | 1.0–1.2g per lb = **148–177g** |
| Aggressive cut (preserving muscle) | 1.2–1.4g per lb = **177–207g** |

**Agent's working target for Nathan**: **150–175g protein per day**

## VEGAN PROTEIN — COMPLETE AMINO ACID PROFILE

### The Essential Amino Acid Problem
Nine essential amino acids (EAAs) must come from diet — the body cannot synthesize them:
1. Histidine
2. Isoleucine
3. Leucine ← Most important for muscle protein synthesis
4. Lysine ← Most commonly deficient in plant diets
5. Methionine
6. Phenylalanine
7. Threonine
8. Tryptophan
9. Valine

**The leucine threshold**: Muscle protein synthesis requires ~2–3g of leucine per meal to maximally stimulate growth. This is why plant proteins often require larger serving sizes than whey protein to achieve the same anabolic response.

### Vegan Protein Sources — Ranked by Quality and Practicality

**Tier 1 — Complete or Near-Complete Proteins (Excellent)**

| Source | Protein per 100g/serving | Leucine/100g | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Soy protein isolate | 90g/100g powder | ~7.9g | Complete amino acid profile; most researched plant protein |
| Tofu (firm) | 17g/100g | ~1.3g | Complete; versatile; accessible |
| Tempeh | 19g/100g | ~1.5g | Complete; fermented = better digestibility |
| Edamame | 11g/100g | ~0.9g | Complete; easy snack |
| Seitan (vital wheat gluten) | 25g/100g | ~1.7g | Very high protein; NOT suitable for gluten intolerance |
| Nutritional yeast | 50g/100g | ~3.1g | Complete; B12 often fortified |

**Tier 2 — High Protein, Incomplete (Excellent when combined)**

| Source | Protein per 100g | Limiting Amino Acid | Combine With |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lentils | 9g (cooked) | Methionine | Rice, quinoa, seeds |
| Black beans | 8.9g (cooked) | Methionine | Whole grains |
| Chickpeas | 9g (cooked) | Methionine + Tryptophan | Seeds, grains |
| Pumpkin seeds | 19g/100g | Lysine | Legumes |
| Hemp seeds | 31g/100g | Lysine | Legumes |
| Chia seeds | 17g/100g | Lysine | Legumes |
| Quinoa | 14g/100g (dry) | Relatively complete | Stand-alone or with legumes |
| Pea protein powder | 80g/100g powder | Methionine | Combine with rice protein |

**The Classic Combination Rule**:
Legumes (high lysine, low methionine) + Grains/Seeds (high methionine, low lysine) = Complete amino acid profile. They do NOT need to be eaten at the same meal — the body pools amino acids throughout the day.

**Agent application**: Ensure Nathan's daily diet includes a variety of both legumes and grains/seeds. High-quality protein powders (pea + rice blend, soy isolate) fill gaps efficiently.

### Vegan Protein Powder Guide

**Best Options for Nathan**:

1. **Pea + Brown Rice Blend** (Gold standard for vegan athletes)
   - Complements each other's amino acid profiles perfectly
   - ~25g protein per serving
   - BCAA profile approaches whey
   - Look for: Momentous Essential Plant, Orgain Sport, Vega Sport

2. **Soy Protein Isolate**
   - Complete amino acid profile
   - Highest leucine content of plant proteins
   - Well-researched for muscle building
   - Note: Soy is safe in normal dietary amounts; phytoestrogen concerns are not supported by evidence in healthy males at normal consumption levels

3. **Hemp Protein**
   - Contains omega-3s and fiber
   - Lower protein density (~50% protein vs. 80–90% for isolates)
   - Best for whole-food supplementation, not primary protein source

**Protein powder timing**: Use to fill gaps, especially post-workout and in the morning. Not a replacement for whole food protein sources.

## LEUCINE DOSING STRATEGY

Because plant proteins have lower leucine density than animal proteins, Nathan should target:
- **2.5–3g leucine per meal** to maximize MPS
- Larger serving sizes of plant proteins to achieve this threshold
- Supplementing 1–2g extra leucine (available as a supplement) if servings are small

### Leucine per serving — practical guide

## CARBOHYDRATES

### Function
- Primary fuel for high-intensity training
- Muscle glycogen replenishment (depleted during heavy lifting and interval training)
- Insulin response supports amino acid uptake into muscle
- Supports thyroid function (low carb long-term can depress T3 and T4)

### Carbohydrate Targets

| Phase | Carbs | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Recomp | 40–50% of calories | ~220–280g/day at 2,300 kcal |
| Lean bulk | 45–55% of calories | Higher carbs for training performance |
| Cut | 30–40% of calories | Reduce carbs, not protein |

### Best Vegan Carbohydrate Sources

**Performance carbohydrates (before/during/after training)**:
- Oats, rice, quinoa, sweet potato, banana, dates, whole grain bread

**Sustained energy carbohydrates**:
- Legumes (beans, lentils), root vegetables, whole grains

**Agent note**: Fiber is important for gut health and micronutrient absorption, but very high fiber intake can cause GI discomfort during training. Time high-fiber foods away from training sessions.

## FATS

### Function
- Essential fatty acids (omega-3 and omega-6) — cannot be synthesized
- Testosterone production (dietary fat is required for steroid hormone synthesis)
- Fat-soluble vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K)
- Cell membrane integrity
- Anti-inflammatory function (omega-3s)

### Fat Targets
- **Minimum**: 20% of total calories (below this, testosterone production suffers)
- **Optimal**: 25–35% of total calories
- At 2,300 kcal → 64–89g fat/day

### Vegan Fat Sources

**Omega-3 (ALA → EPA/DHA conversion is inefficient in humans — supplement directly)**:
- Flaxseed, chia seeds, hemp seeds, walnuts (ALA sources)
- **Algae-based omega-3 oil**: The gold standard for vegan EPA/DHA. Algae is where fish get their omega-3s. 500–1,000mg EPA+DHA daily from algae oil is Nathan's target.

**Monounsaturated fats (anti-inflammatory, heart health)**:
- Avocado, avocado oil, olive oil, almonds, cashews

**Saturated fats (in moderation — needed for testosterone)**:
- Coconut oil (MCT content; some evidence for performance)
- Dark chocolate (>85% cacao)

**Agent note on omega-3**: Vegan omega-3 is the most common deficiency in vegan athletes. Low EPA/DHA impairs recovery, inflammation resolution, and cognitive function. Algae oil supplementation is non-negotiable for Nathan.

## VEGAN MICRONUTRIENT OPTIMIZATION

### The Critical Vegan-Specific Nutrients

#### Vitamin B12
- **Status**: Not found in plant foods (some algae contain pseudo-B12 that is not bioavailable)
- **Consequence of deficiency**: Macrocytic anemia, neurological damage, elevated homocysteine (cardiovascular risk)
- **Target**: 250–500mcg cyanocobalamin daily OR 1,000mcg every other day
- **Sources**: Fortified foods, supplementation (mandatory for vegans)

#### Vitamin D3
- **Status**: D3 is animal-derived (from lanolin/wool); vegan D3 is from lichen
- **Function**: Testosterone production, bone health, immune function, muscle function
- **Target**: 2,000–5,000 IU/day lichen-derived D3, ideally with K2 (100–200mcg MK-7)
- **Testing**: Serum 25(OH)D should be 50–80 ng/mL; get tested annually

#### Iron
- **Status**: Plant iron is non-heme iron — 2–3x less bioavailable than heme iron
- **Consequence of deficiency**: Fatigue, reduced oxygen transport, impaired training performance
- **Best vegan sources**: Lentils, fortified cereals, tofu, tempeh, pumpkin seeds, spinach
- **Absorption tip**: Consume vitamin C with iron-rich foods (increases absorption 3–6x). Avoid coffee/tea within 1 hour of iron-rich meals (tannins block absorption).
- **Target**: Men need ~8mg/day. Athletes may need more. Get serum ferritin tested.

#### Zinc
- **Status**: Phytates in grains and legumes bind zinc and reduce absorption
- **Function**: Testosterone production, immune function, protein synthesis
- **Best vegan sources**: Pumpkin seeds, hemp seeds, lentils, chickpeas, oats
- **Absorption tip**: Soak and sprout legumes and grains to reduce phytate content
- **Target**: 11mg/day (men); supplement with 15–25mg zinc if diet is insufficient
- **Note**: Zinc and copper compete — if supplementing zinc >25mg, add 1–2mg copper

#### Calcium
- **Status**: Without dairy, must be sourced intentionally
- **Function**: Bone density, muscle contraction, nerve signaling
- **Best vegan sources**: Fortified plant milk (300mg/cup), tofu made with calcium sulfate (350mg/100g), kale, bok choy, broccoli, fortified OJ
- **Target**: 1,000mg/day

#### Iodine
- **Status**: Most plant foods are iodine-poor; seaweed is variable and can provide too much
- **Function**: Thyroid hormone synthesis; metabolic rate regulation
- **Target**: 150mcg/day
- **Best source**: ¼ teaspoon iodized salt OR 150mcg iodine supplement

#### Selenium
- **Status**: Depends on soil content where crops are grown; variable in plant foods
- **Function**: Thyroid function, antioxidant defense, testosterone production
- **Best source**: 2–3 Brazil nuts per day (~70–100mcg selenium) — the most reliable vegan source
- **Target**: 55mcg/day; do not exceed 400mcg (toxicity risk)

#### Vitamin K2
- **Status**: K1 (phylloquinone) is abundant in leafy greens; K2 (menaquinone) is scarce in plant foods
- **Function**: Directs calcium to bones (not arteries); works synergistically with D3
- **Best vegan source**: Natto (fermented soybeans — highest food source of K2); sauerkraut has small amounts
- **Supplement**: 100–200mcg MK-7 (long-acting K2 form)

#### Creatine
- **Status**: Found almost exclusively in animal products; vegans have lower baseline muscle creatine stores
- **Function**: Phosphocreatine system — powers high-intensity efforts (swings, pull-ups, sprints); increases strength, power, and lean mass
- **Research**: Creatine is the most studied and evidence-supported sports supplement in existence. Vegans show LARGER strength and body composition improvements from creatine supplementation than omnivores because they start from a lower baseline.
- **Target**: 3–5g creatine monohydrate daily (no loading phase needed; consistency is what matters)
- **Timing**: Timing doesn't matter significantly — take it with any meal

## MEAL TIMING STRATEGY

### General Principles
- Protein distribution across 4–6 meals produces better MPS than 1–2 large meals
- 30–50g protein per meal is the practical sweet spot for MPS stimulus
- Pre- and post-workout nutrition matters but is less critical than daily totals

### Pre-Workout Meal (1–2 hours before training)
**Goal**: Available amino acids + glycogen for performance
**Composition**: High carbohydrate + moderate protein + low fat + low fiber (fat and fiber slow digestion)

**Example**:
- Oatmeal (60g dry) with 1 scoop pea protein (25g) + banana → ~45g protein, 75g carbs
- OR rice + tofu stir-fry (light)

### Post-Workout Meal (Within 2 hours)
**Goal**: Initiate muscle protein synthesis, replenish glycogen
**Composition**: High protein + moderate carbohydrate + low fat

**Example**:
- Protein shake (35–40g) + fruit
- OR tempeh + rice + vegetables
- **Leucine priority**: Ensure this meal hits 2.5–3g leucine

### Pre-Sleep Meal (30–60 min before bed)
**Goal**: Sustain overnight MPS during the longest fasting period
**Composition**: Slow-digesting protein (casein equivalent for vegans)

**Best vegan option**: Soy protein (slowest-digesting plant protein) + small amount of fat (slows absorption further)
- Example: Soy protein shake + 1 tbsp peanut butter
- OR: Large serving of tofu/tempeh + small handful of nuts

### Rest Day Eating
- Slightly lower carbohydrates (less need for glycogen replenishment)
- Maintain protein at full target
- Fat can be slightly higher (satiety on lower-calorie day)

## HYDRATION

### Targets
- **Baseline**: 0.5–0.7 oz per lb bodyweight = **74–104 oz (2.2–3.1 liters)** per day
- **Training days**: Add 16–24 oz (500–700ml) per hour of training
- **Hot weather**: Add additional 16–32 oz

### Electrolytes
Sweat depletes sodium, potassium, magnesium, and chloride. Signs of electrolyte imbalance: cramping, headache, fatigue, brain fog.

**Vegan electrolyte sources**:
- **Sodium**: Salt food appropriately (athletes should not fear salt)
- **Potassium**: Banana, avocado, sweet potato, lentils
- **Magnesium**: Dark leafy greens, pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate, legumes
- **Supplement option**: Electrolyte powder without added sugars for training days

### Magnesium Special Note
Magnesium deficiency is extremely common (estimated 50–70% of US population is deficient). For athletes:
- Supports muscle relaxation (reduces cramps)
- Supports sleep quality (activates GABA receptors — promotes deep sleep)
- Supports testosterone production
- **Supplement**: 300–400mg magnesium glycinate or malate before bed (most bioavailable forms; avoid oxide)

## ALCOHOL AND PERFORMANCE

### Impact on Nathan's Goals
- **Muscle protein synthesis**: Even moderate alcohol consumption (2–3 drinks) acutely suppresses MPS by up to 37% post-exercise
- **Sleep**: Disrupts REM sleep and growth hormone secretion
- **Testosterone**: Acutely suppresses testosterone
- **Calories**: Alcohol provides 7 kcal/gram with no nutritional value; displaces protein and carbs
- **Fat storage**: Alcohol is prioritized for metabolism over fat; effectively pauses fat oxidation

**Agent guidance**: Not prescriptive. Nathan can decide his relationship with alcohol. The agent should simply provide the data when relevant and not moralize.

## TRACKING TOOLS AND METHODS

### Calorie/Macro Tracking
- **Cronometer**: Best app for vegan micronutrient tracking (shows amino acid profiles, B12, iron, etc.) — highly recommended over MyFitnessPal for Nathan
- **Daily tracking**: Most effective during initial phases to understand baseline intake
- **Long-term**: Once patterns are established, intuitive eating with periodic spot-checking is sustainable

### Body Composition Tracking
- **DEXA scan**: Gold standard; available at some gyms and medical facilities; do every 3–6 months
- **Bioelectrical impedance**: Consumer devices (Withings, Tanita); useful for tracking trends despite accuracy limitations; always measure under the same conditions (morning, fasted, same hydration state)
- **Tape measurements**: Most practical for Nathan's use. Track monthly:
  - Waist (at navel)
  - Hips (at widest point)
  - Chest (at nipple line)
  - Shoulders (at widest point)
  - Upper arm (flexed, at peak)
  - Thigh (mid-thigh)

### Progress Photo Protocol
- Same time of day (morning, fasted)
- Same lighting
- Same poses: Front relaxed, front flexed, side, back
- Every 4 weeks minimum
