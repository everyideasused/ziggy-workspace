# Classic 4-Ingredient Neapolitan Pizza Dough (Cold Ferment)

Authentic Neapolitan pizza dough using just four ingredients and a long cold fermentation. This traditional method develops deep flavor and creates the perfect texture for true Neapolitan-style pizza.

## Overview

- **Yield:** 3 dough balls (~285g each, makes 12" pizzas)
- **Total Time:** 24-72 hours (cold ferment)
- **Style:** Traditional Neapolitan
- **Hydration:** 60-65%

## Ingredients

**Only 4 ingredients — nothing else:**
- 500g Tipo 00 flour
- 300-325g water (60-65% hydration — adjust based on flour absorption)
- 15g fine sea salt
- 0.5-1g active dry yeast (tiny amount for slow, long rise)

## Method

### 1. Mix Ingredients
- Add water to large bowl
- Dissolve yeast in water
- Add flour and salt
- Mix by hand until no dry flour remains

### 2. Knead
- Knead dough on lightly floured surface for 8-10 minutes
- Dough should become smooth and elastic
- Proper kneading is crucial for gluten development

### 3. Bulk Fermentation (Room Temperature)
- Form into a ball and place in lightly oiled bowl
- Cover with damp towel
- Let rise at room temperature for 1-2 hours
- Dough should increase in volume by about 30%

### 4. Divide & Shape
- Turn dough onto lightly floured surface
- Divide into 3 equal portions (~285g each)
- Shape each portion into a tight, smooth ball
- Use cupped hand technique to create surface tension

### 5. Cold Fermentation (The Key Step)
- Place dough balls in lightly oiled container with tight lid
- Or place on tray, cover tightly with plastic wrap
- **Refrigerate for 24-72 hours**
- Longer ferment = deeper flavor (48-72 hours is ideal)

### 6. Bring to Room Temperature
- Remove dough balls from refrigerator 1-2 hours before baking
- Let come to room temperature — this is essential for easy stretching
- Dough should be relaxed and puffy

## Baking

### For Pizza Oven (800-900°F)
- Preheat oven and stone for 30+ minutes
- Bake 60-90 seconds, rotating halfway

### For Home Oven (500-550°F)
- Preheat oven and steel/stone for 45-60 minutes
- Bake 5-8 minutes on bottom rack

## The Philosophy

### Why No Sugar?
Traditional Neapolitan dough doesn't need sugar. The tiny amount of yeast and long fermentation naturally develop flavor. Sugar would speed fermentation but sacrifice complexity.

### Why No Oil?
Oil inhibits gluten development and creates a different texture. True Neapolitan dough relies purely on flour, water, salt, and time.

### Why So Little Yeast?
Less yeast = slower fermentation = more flavor. The long cold ferment gives enzymes time to break down starches into sugars, creating deep, complex flavor.

### Why Cold Ferment?
Cold temperatures slow yeast activity while allowing enzymatic activity to continue. This develops flavor while preventing over-fermentation. It also makes the dough easier to work with.

## Hydration Guide

**60% (300g water):**
- Easier to handle
- Better for beginners
- Slightly less extensible

**65% (325g water):**
- More authentic Neapolitan texture
- More extensible (easier to stretch thin)
- Requires more skill to handle

**Start at 60% and work your way up as you gain experience.**

## Storage

- **Refrigerator:** Optimal at 48-72 hours; can go up to 96 hours but may develop sour notes
- **Freezer:** Freeze shaped balls wrapped tightly for up to 3 months; thaw overnight in fridge, then bring to room temp 2 hours before using

## Troubleshooting

### Dough Too Sticky
- Flour may have high absorption — reduce water by 10-15g next time
- Don't add flour during cold ferment; handle with wet hands instead

### Dough Won't Stretch
- Not at room temperature yet — wait longer
- Over-kneaded — knead less next time
- Not fermented long enough — wait 24+ hours

### Dough Tears When Stretching
- Gluten too tight — let rest 5 minutes and try again
- Stretched too aggressively — work more gently from center

### Sour/Off Flavor
- Over-fermented — use dough by 72 hours
- Too much yeast — reduce to 0.5g next time

## Notes

- **Vegan Status:** 100% vegan — just flour, water, salt, yeast
- **No Shortcuts:** The long ferment is non-negotiable for true Neapolitan flavor and texture
- **Tipo 00 Flour:** Essential for authentic texture; bread flour will work but won't be the same
- **Traditional Method:** This is how pizza dough has been made in Naples for generations
