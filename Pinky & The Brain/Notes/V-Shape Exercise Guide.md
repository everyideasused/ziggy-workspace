# V-Shape Program — Exercise Guide

> **How to use:** Reference this guide when learning a new exercise or when Ziggy flags a form issue. Each exercise includes a brief form description, key cues, common mistakes, and a video link. Bookmark this in your Fitness folder.

---

## Day A Exercises: Upper Push

### Pike Push-ups (Elevated Feet)
**Targets:** Anterior deltoids, upper chest, triceps

**Form:** Start in a push-up position, then walk your feet toward your hands until your hips are high and your body forms an inverted V. Hands shoulder-width apart. Bend elbows and lower the TOP of your head toward the floor — your head goes forward past your hands, not straight down between them. Press back up to the V position. Elevate feet on a chair or bench to increase difficulty.

**Key cues:**
- Keep forearms vertical throughout — don't flare elbows out
- Head goes FORWARD and down, not straight down between hands
- Full lockout at top — shrug shoulders toward ears
- Inhale down, exhale up

**Common mistakes:** Dipping head between hands (turns it into a push-up), flaring elbows, sagging hips, incomplete range of motion.

📹 [Antranik — Pike Push-up Form Guide](https://antranik.org/pike-pushups/) *(best written breakdown with photos)*
📹 [FitnessFAQs — Pike Push-up to HSPU Progression](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sLNSEQnk4Gw)

### Wide Grip Push-ups
**Targets:** Pectorals (emphasis on outer/lower chest), anterior deltoids

**Form:** Standard push-up position but hands placed wider than shoulder width — roughly 1.5x shoulder width. Lower chest to the floor with a slow 3-second count on the way down. Touch chest to floor, then press up. Keep core tight and body in a straight line.

**Key cues:**
- Hands wider than shoulders, fingers pointed slightly outward
- Slow 3s eccentric (lowering) for maximum pec stretch
- Squeeze chest hard at the top
- Don't let hips sag or pike up

**Common mistakes:** Going too wide (shoulder strain), half reps, letting hips sag, flaring elbows to 90° (keep ~45°).

📹 [Jeff Nippard — Push-up Variations for Chest Growth](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IODxDxX7oi4)
📹 [Calisthenicmovement — Perfect Push-up Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IODxDxX7oi4)

### KB Floor Press
**Targets:** Pectorals, triceps, anterior deltoids

**Form:** Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor. Hold the kettlebell in one hand at chest level with the bell resting on the back of your forearm. Press straight up until arm is fully extended. Pause 2 seconds at the top squeezing your chest, then lower slowly until your upper arm touches the floor. The floor limits your range of motion, protecting your shoulders while still hitting the chest.

**Key cues:**
- Upper arm rests on floor at bottom — this is your "reset" each rep
- Press straight up, not out toward your feet
- Squeeze the chest hard at the top for a 2s pause
- Keep your wrist straight — don't let the KB bend it back

**Common mistakes:** Pressing at an angle, not pausing at the top, rushing the lowering phase, arching the back excessively.

📹 [Eric Leija (Primal Swoledier) — KB Floor Press](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NM1dOMKMGCM)

### Diamond Push-ups
**Targets:** Triceps (primary), inner chest

**Form:** Standard push-up position, but bring hands close together so thumbs and index fingers touch, forming a diamond or triangle shape. Lower your chest to your hands — keep elbows tracking backward along your sides, not flaring out. Press back up to full extension.

**Key cues:**
- Diamond shape directly under your chest (not your face)
- Elbows stay tight to your body — they track backward
- Full range of motion — chest touches hands
- Keep core locked — don't let hips sag

**Common mistakes:** Hands too far forward (under face instead of chest), elbows flaring wide, half reps, hips sagging.

📹 [Calisthenicmovement — Diamond Push-up Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J0DnG1_S3lg)
📹 [THENX — Tricep Push-up Variations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sIvJTfGxdFo)

### KB Halo
**Targets:** Shoulders (all three heads), rotator cuff, core stability

**Form:** Stand tall, feet hip-width apart, core braced. Hold the kettlebell by the horns (handles) upside down at chest level. Circle the KB around your head — passing it behind your head and back to the front. Keep the KB close to your head throughout. Alternate directions each set.

**Key cues:**
- Keep the KB close — it should almost graze your head
- Move the KB around your head, not your head around the KB
- Stay tall — don't lean or twist your torso
- Controlled speed — this is mobility work, not a power move

**Common mistakes:** Swinging the KB too far from the head, leaning or twisting, going too fast, using a KB that's too heavy (go light on these).

📹 [Mark Wildman — KB Halo Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EHvXfEgVUQY)

### KB Overhead Press
**Targets:** Anterior and lateral deltoids, triceps, core

**Form:** Clean the KB to the rack position — bell resting on the back of your forearm, elbow tight to your body, wrist straight. Brace your core and squeeze your glute on the working side. Press the KB straight overhead until your arm is fully locked out — bicep should finish near your ear. Lower under control back to the rack.

**Key cues:**
- Start in a solid rack position — elbow tight to ribs
- Squeeze the glute on the pressing side for stability
- Press in a straight line — the KB should finish directly over your shoulder
- Full lockout at top — shrug slightly into the bell
- Don't lean back — if you need to lean, the weight is too heavy

**Common mistakes:** Pressing forward instead of straight up, leaning back, loose core, not locking out fully, starting from a bad rack position.

📹 [Pavel Tsatsouline / StrongFirst — KB Press](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cKx8xE8jJZs)
📹 [Mark Wildman — KB Strict Press Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_2dulYCTS2w)

## Day B1 Exercises: Lower Body (Glute + Quad)

### KB Goblet Squat
**Targets:** Quads, glutes, core, upper back

**Form:** Hold the KB by the horns at chest level, elbows pointing down. Feet slightly wider than shoulder width, toes turned out 15–30°. Sit straight down between your legs — push your knees out over your toes. Go as deep as you can while keeping your chest up and back flat. Drive through your whole foot to stand, squeezing glutes hard at the top.

**Key cues:**
- Elbows stay inside your knees — use them to push knees out at the bottom
- Chest stays tall — the KB counterbalances you
- Depth: hip crease below knee crease is the goal
- Drive up through heels AND toes — whole foot
- Squeeze glutes hard at lockout

**Common mistakes:** Leaning forward, heels coming up, knees caving in, not going deep enough, losing chest position.

📹 [Dan John — The Goblet Squat (the inventor)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MeIiIdhvXT4)
📹 [Squat University — Goblet Squat Deep Dive](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fv8_IFDjR6A)

### KB Bulgarian Split Squat
**Targets:** Quads, glutes (especially the front leg), hip flexor stretch on rear leg

**Form:** Stand about 2 feet in front of a chair or bench. Place one foot behind you on the surface — laces down. Hold the KB in goblet position at your chest. Lower straight down until your rear knee nearly touches the floor. Your front shin should stay relatively vertical. Drive up through the front heel.

**Key cues:**
- Most of your weight is on the FRONT leg — the back leg is just for balance
- Front shin stays vertical or only slightly forward
- Torso can lean slightly forward for more glute emphasis
- Go deep — rear knee nearly touches the floor
- Drive up through the front heel

**Common mistakes:** Stance too narrow (losing balance), front knee shooting way past toes, leaning too far forward, back foot too high (use knee height or lower).

📹 [Jeff Nippard — Bulgarian Split Squat Masterclass](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2C-uNgKwPLE)
📹 [Ben Patrick (Knees Over Toes Guy) — BSS Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q1gjGChIBZE)

### KB Sumo Deadlift
**Targets:** Glutes, inner thighs (adductors), hamstrings

**Form:** Wide stance — feet roughly 1.5–2x shoulder width, toes pointed out 45°. KB on the floor between your feet. Push hips back and down to grab the KB handle with both hands. Chest up, back flat, shoulders over the KB. Drive through the floor, extending hips and knees together. Squeeze glutes aggressively at the top. Lower by pushing hips back first.

**Key cues:**
- Wide stance with toes turned out significantly
- Hips and shoulders rise at the same rate
- SQUEEZE glutes hard at the top — this is the money moment
- Control the descent — don't just drop the weight
- Keep the KB close to your body

**Common mistakes:** Rounding the back, pulling with the arms, not squeezing at the top, stance too narrow (makes it a conventional deadlift).

📹 [Mark Wildman — KB Sumo Deadlift](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SQ4W-9hpAiI)

### Bodyweight Hip Thrust (Feet Elevated)
**Targets:** Glutes (primary), hamstrings

**Form:** Sit on the floor with your upper back against a bench or couch. Place feet on an elevated surface (chair, step) hip-width apart. Roll hips up by driving through your heels until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze glutes hard at the top for a full 2 seconds. Lower under control.

**Key cues:**
- Upper back rests on the bench — the bench edge should be at the bottom of your shoulder blades
- Drive through your heels
- At the top: straight line from shoulders to knees — don't hyperextend your lower back
- 2-second squeeze at the top — really contract the glutes
- To progress: place KB on your hips, or go single leg

**Common mistakes:** Hyperextending the back at the top (arching), pushing through toes instead of heels, not squeezing long enough at the top, rushing reps.

📹 [Bret Contreras (The Glute Guy) — Hip Thrust Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SEdqd1n0cvg)
📹 [Jeff Nippard — Hip Thrust Science](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xDmFkJxPzeM)

### KB Swing
**Targets:** Glutes, hamstrings, core, grip, explosive hip power

**Form:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, KB about a foot in front of you. Hike the KB back between your legs like a football snap — arms straight, hips pushed way back. Explosively snap your hips forward and squeeze your glutes to launch the KB up. The KB floats to chest height from hip power alone — your arms don't pull it up. Let it fall back and hike it between your legs again.

**Key cues:**
- This is a HIP HINGE, not a squat — hips go back, not down
- The power comes from your hips snapping forward — arms are just ropes
- At the top: standing tall, glutes squeezed, abs braced — like a standing plank
- KB should float to about chest height
- Don't lean back at the top — stand tall
- Breathe out sharply at the top (the "hike" breath)

**Common mistakes:** Squatting the swing (hips drop instead of hinge back), using arms to lift the KB, leaning back at the top, not enough hip snap, KB going overhead (that's an American swing — we don't want that).

📹 [Mark Wildman — KB Swing Tutorial (the best beginner guide)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yHxcTn1UeAc)
📹 [StrongFirst — Hardstyle Swing](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYHbu2LRqD0)
📹 [Dan John — Fixing Common Swing Mistakes](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sSESeQAir7M)

### Wall Sit Hold
**Targets:** Quads (isometric endurance), mental toughness

**Form:** Stand with your back flat against a wall. Slide down until your thighs are parallel to the floor — knees at 90°. Feet should be far enough forward that your shins are vertical. Press your entire back flat against the wall. Hold.

**Key cues:**
- Thighs PARALLEL to the floor — no cheating high
- Shins vertical — feet out far enough
- Back flat against the wall — no arching
- Breathe normally — don't hold your breath

**Common mistakes:** Not going low enough (thighs above parallel), feet too close to the wall, holding breath.

📹 [Calisthenicmovement — Wall Sit Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y-wV4Lz-g4k)

## Day C Exercises: Upper Pull + Arms

### Pull-ups (Overhand)
**Targets:** Latissimus dorsi (back width), biceps, forearms, rear deltoids

**Form:** Grab the bar with an overhand grip slightly wider than shoulder width. Start from a dead hang — arms fully extended, shoulders "packed" (pulled slightly down and back). Pull yourself up by driving your elbows down toward your hips. Chin clears the bar at the top. Lower under control all the way back to a dead hang.

**Key cues:**
- Dead hang start every rep — full arm extension
- Initiate by pulling shoulder blades down and together
- Drive elbows DOWN toward your hips — not back
- Chin over the bar at the top
- Control the lowering — don't just drop
- If you can't do pull-ups yet: start with negatives (jump to the top, lower for 5 seconds)

**Common mistakes:** Half reps (not going to dead hang), kipping/swinging, chin not clearing the bar, pulling with arms first instead of engaging lats.

📹 [FitnessFAQs — Pull-up Progression (0 to 10+)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tB3X4TjTIes)
📹 [Jeremy Ethier — The PERFECT Pull-up](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eGo4IYlbE5g)
📹 [Chris Heria (THENX) — Pull-up Tutorial for Beginners](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tB3X4TjTIes)

### Chin-ups (Underhand)
**Targets:** Biceps (more than pull-ups), latissimus dorsi, forearms

**Form:** Grab the bar with an underhand (supinated) grip, hands shoulder-width apart. Dead hang start. Pull up driving your chest toward the bar. Chin clears the bar. Lower slowly — aim for a 3-second negative for bicep growth.

**Key cues:**
- Underhand grip, shoulder width — this hits biceps harder than pull-ups
- Pull your chest to the bar, not just your chin over
- 3s slow negative on every rep — this is where biceps grow
- Dead hang at the bottom each rep

**Common mistakes:** Same as pull-ups — half reps, kipping, rushing the negative. Also: gripping too narrow (can strain wrists).

📹 [Jeff Nippard — Pull-ups vs Chin-ups (science comparison)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UfhT0OSUU0w)
📹 [FitnessFAQs — Chin-up Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=brhRXlOhWAM)

### KB Gorilla Row
**Targets:** Latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, biceps, core

**Form:** Place the KB on the floor. Take a wide stance straddling it. Hinge at the hips until your torso is close to parallel with the floor. Grab the KB with one hand, row it to your hip — elbow drives straight back past your ribcage. Squeeze the lat at the top for a beat. Lower and repeat. The other arm can rest on your thigh or hang.

**Key cues:**
- Wide base for stability — feet wider than shoulders
- Torso nearly parallel to the floor
- Row to your HIP, not your chest — elbow drives back
- Squeeze the lat at the top — feel your back contract
- Keep hips square — don't rotate your torso to cheat the row up

**Common mistakes:** Standing too upright (reduces lat engagement), rowing to chest instead of hip, rotating torso, using momentum.

📹 [Eric Leija — KB Gorilla Row](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bAfsBi-VY_k)
📹 [Mark Wildman — KB Row Variations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HhHhCMWmGYM)

---

### KB Renegade Row
**Targets:** Lats, core (anti-rotation), shoulders (stability)

**Form:** Get into a push-up position with one hand on the KB, the other on the floor (or on a second KB if you have one). Feet wide for stability. Brace your core hard. Row the KB to your hip while keeping your hips completely square to the floor — they should NOT rotate. Lower the KB and repeat.

**Key cues:**
- Wide foot stance for stability
- HIPS STAY SQUARE — this is the whole point. Don't rotate.
- Row to the hip, not the chest
- Brace core like you're about to get punched
- Go lighter than you think — this is harder than it looks

**Common mistakes:** Hips rotating with each row, feet too narrow, going too heavy, rushing reps.

📹 [ATHLEAN-X — Renegade Row Done Right](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Bv2KwICzfQ)

---

### KB Bicep Curl
**Targets:** Biceps brachii, brachialis, forearm flexors

**Form:** Stand tall, hold the KB by the handle in one hand, arm fully extended at your side. Curl the KB up by bending at the elbow — keep your upper arm pinned to your side. Squeeze the bicep at the top. Lower slowly for a 3-second count. The slow eccentric is where the growth happens.

**Key cues:**
- Upper arm stays glued to your side — only the forearm moves
- 3-second lowering on EVERY rep — this is non-negotiable for growth
- Full extension at the bottom — don't cheat the range of motion
- Squeeze hard at the top

**Common mistakes:** Swinging the body, upper arm drifting forward, fast/jerky lowering, not going to full extension.

📹 [Mark Wildman — KB Curl Variations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MbWi0j5vuIQ)

---

### Dead Hang
**Targets:** Grip strength, forearms, shoulder decompression, lat stretch

**Form:** Grab the bar with an overhand grip, shoulder width. Let your body hang with arms fully extended. Relax your shoulders toward your ears (passive hang) or actively pull shoulder blades slightly down (active hang). Breathe normally. Hold as long as possible.

**Key cues:**
- Arms fully extended — don't bend elbows
- Relax everything except your grip
- Breathe normally — don't hold your breath
- Great for spinal decompression after heavy training

**Common mistakes:** Bending elbows, holding breath, gripping too narrow.

📹 [Calisthenicmovement — Dead Hang Benefits and Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dRmVGOXRzn0)

## Day B2 Exercises: Lower Body (Posterior Chain + Glute)

### KB Single-Leg Deadlift
**Targets:** Glutes (unilateral), hamstrings, balance, core

**Form:** Stand on one leg, KB in the opposite hand. Hinge at the hip — push your standing hip backward while your free leg extends straight behind you. Your torso and back leg should form one straight line as you lower the KB toward the floor. Go as low as your hamstring flexibility allows. Drive through the standing heel to return to upright. Squeeze the glute at the top.

**Key cues:**
- Think "hinge," not "bend" — push the hip back
- Back leg and torso move as ONE unit — like a seesaw
- Keep hips square — don't open up the hip of the floating leg
- KB in the OPPOSITE hand from the standing leg
- Slow and controlled — this is a balance challenge too
- Touch the floor if you can, but don't round your back to get there

**Common mistakes:** Rounding the back, hip of the floating leg opening up, bending the standing knee too much, rushing (this needs to be slow).

📹 [Squat University — Single Leg Deadlift Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iDKmW_2a8EM)
📹 [Mark Wildman — KB Single Leg Deadlift](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VhwkCdV1qkA)

### Reverse Lunge (KB Goblet)
**Targets:** Glutes, quads, hip flexor stretch

**Form:** Hold KB in goblet position at your chest. Step BACKWARD (not forward) into a lunge. Lower until your back knee nearly touches the floor. Lean your torso slightly forward to emphasize the glute of the front leg. Drive through the front heel to return to standing.

**Key cues:**
- Step BACK — reverse lunges are easier on the knees than forward lunges
- Slight forward lean for more glute activation
- Back knee nearly touches the floor each rep
- Drive up through the front heel
- Keep the front shin relatively vertical

**Common mistakes:** Stepping forward instead of back, not going deep enough, torso too upright (reduces glute emphasis), pushing off the back foot instead of driving through the front.

📹 [Jeff Nippard — Lunge Biomechanics](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QE_hU8XX48I)
📹 [Ben Patrick — Reverse Lunge Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UST0mtOxNYk)

### Nordic Curl Negative
**Targets:** Hamstrings (eccentric strength), knee injury prevention

**Form:** Kneel on a pad with your feet anchored under something heavy (couch, loaded barbell, partner holds ankles). Start upright — body in a straight line from knees to head. Slowly lower your body toward the floor by extending at the knees while keeping hips extended (don't bend at the waist). Fight gravity the entire way down for a 5-second count. Catch yourself with your hands at the bottom and push yourself back up to the start.

**Key cues:**
- STRAIGHT LINE from knees to shoulders the entire time — no bending at the hips
- 5 seconds down minimum — fight gravity every inch
- Catch yourself with hands at the bottom — you WILL need to at first
- Push back to start with your hands — the negative (lowering) is the exercise
- This is brutally hard — even 3–5 reps is great

**Common mistakes:** Bending at the hips (turns it into a back extension), going down too fast, not anchoring feet securely.

📹 [Knees Over Toes Guy — Nordic Curl Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qLMgJJBnIm8)
📹 [FitnessFAQs — Nordic Curl Progression](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ORPT4_FHJmI)

### Single Leg Glute Bridge
**Targets:** Glutes (unilateral), hamstrings, core stability

**Form:** Lie on your back, one foot flat on the floor close to your butt, the other leg extended straight up (or bent and held to chest). Drive through the working heel and lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knee. Squeeze the glute hard for 2 seconds at the top. Lower under control.

**Key cues:**
- Drive through the HEEL of the working leg
- 2-second squeeze at the top — really feel the glute contract
- Keep hips level — don't let the non-working side drop
- To progress: place KB on the hip of the working side
- Foot close to your butt emphasizes glute; further away emphasizes hamstring

**Common mistakes:** Pushing through toes, hips not level, not squeezing at top, hyperextending the lower back.

📹 [Bret Contreras — Single Leg Glute Bridge](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AVAXhy6pl7o)

---

### KB Goblet Squat Pulse
**Targets:** Quads, glutes (endurance finisher)

**Form:** Same setup as the goblet squat. Lower to the bottom position. Instead of coming all the way up, only come up about a quarter of the way, then drop back to the bottom. Repeat in quick succession for 20 reps. Stay in the bottom range the entire time — this keeps constant tension on the quads and glutes.

**Key cues:**
- Stay in the bottom quarter of the movement — never stand fully up
- Quick but controlled pulses — this is a burn-out finisher
- Keep chest up — don't collapse forward as you fatigue
- Breathe continuously — don't hold your breath

**Common mistakes:** Coming up too high (loses the tension), rounding forward as fatigue hits, holding breath.

📹 [Pulse squats are commonly demonstrated in bootcamp-style videos — use the same goblet squat form from Dan John's tutorial above, but stay in the bottom quarter and pulse]

## Warm-Up Exercises

### World's Greatest Stretch
**Targets:** Hip flexors, thoracic spine, hamstrings, groin — the single best all-in-one warm-up move

**Form:** Step into a deep lunge. Place the hand on the same side as your front leg on the floor inside your foot. Rotate your torso and reach the other arm straight up toward the ceiling. Hold for a breath. Return hand to floor, straighten the front leg to stretch the hamstring. Repeat on the other side.

📹 [Eric Cressey — World's Greatest Stretch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bCnS5UT4ias)

### Inchworms
**Targets:** Hamstrings, shoulders, core — full body warm-up

**Form:** Stand tall. Bend at the waist and walk your hands out to a plank position. Pause. Walk your feet toward your hands in small steps. Stand up. Repeat.

📹 [ATHLEAN-X — Inchworm Warm-up](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VSp3cSF4mwc)
