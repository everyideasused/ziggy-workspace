# 10 - Asset Stabilization & Operations

> **Phase 10 of 10** | Predecessor: [[09 - Post-Construction & Warranty]]

---

## Purpose

The transition from completed construction to a fully operational, revenue-generating (or purpose-serving) asset. This phase is most relevant for development projects and may not apply to simple tenant build-outs.

---

## Key Activities by Sector

### Commercial / Retail
- **Tenant move-in coordination** (FF&E delivery, data/telecom activation)
- **Lease-up / marketing** for multi-tenant projects
- **Rent commencement** — verify trigger conditions are met per lease
- **TIA final reconciliation** — all reimbursements collected (see [[Tenant Improvement Allowances]])
- **Property management transition** — from development team to operations team
- **Tenant operations manual** delivered

### Residential (Multifamily)
- **Certificate of Occupancy** → begin leasing
- **Model units** prepared for showing
- **Lease-up velocity** tracked (units/month)
- **Stabilization** typically defined as 90-95% occupancy sustained for 90 days
- **Construction-to-permanent loan conversion** triggered at stabilization

### Residential (For-Sale)
- **Closings** begin after CO
- **Home buyer orientations and walkthroughs**
- **HOA formation and transition** from developer to homeowners
- **Common area completion** and turnover

### Civil / Infrastructure
- **Acceptance by public entity** (city, county, state DOT)
- **As-built records** filed with public agency
- **Maintenance bond period** (typically 1-2 years)
- **Dedication of public improvements** (roads, utilities, easements)

---

## Financial Stabilization

- **Development budget final reconciliation:** All costs captured, contingencies closed, final profit/loss calculated
- **Permanent financing:** Convert construction loan to permanent mortgage at stabilization
- **Operating budget established:** Transition from capital budget to operating budget
- **Asset valuation:** Appraisal for refinancing or sale

---

## Duration: 6-18 months post-completion (varies widely by market and project type)
