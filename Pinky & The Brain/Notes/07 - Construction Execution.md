# 07 - Construction Execution

> **Phase 7 of 10** | Predecessor: [[06 - Preconstruction]] | Successor: [[08 - Closeout & Turnover]]

---

## Purpose

Physical construction of the project. This is where the design becomes reality and where the majority of time, money, and risk are concentrated.

---

## Construction Sequence (Typical New Build)

### Sitework & Foundations
1. Erosion control / SWPPP installation
2. Demolition of existing structures (if applicable)
3. Clearing and grubbing
4. Mass grading / earthwork
5. Underground utilities (storm, sanitary, water, gas, electric, telecom)
6. Foundation excavation
7. Foundation forming and reinforcement
8. **INSPECTION: Footing/Foundation**
9. Foundation concrete pour
10. Underslab MEP rough-in
11. **INSPECTION: Underslab plumbing/electrical**
12. Slab-on-grade preparation and pour
13. Waterproofing / dampproofing

### Structural Frame
14. Structural steel erection OR wood/metal framing
15. Floor/roof decking
16. Concrete topping slabs (if applicable)
17. **INSPECTION: Rough framing**

### Building Envelope
18. Exterior sheathing and weather barrier
19. Window and door installation
20. Exterior cladding / finish material installation
21. Roofing system installation
22. Building is now "dried in" — weather-tight

### Rough-In (MEP + Fire Protection)
23. Mechanical rough-in (ductwork, piping, equipment setting)
24. Electrical rough-in (conduit, wire pulls, panels)
25. Plumbing rough-in (supply, waste, vent piping)
26. Fire protection rough-in (sprinkler piping, heads)
27. Low-voltage rough-in (data, security, AV)
28. **INSPECTION: Rough MEP** (mechanical, electrical, plumbing, fire)
29. Insulation installation
30. **INSPECTION: Insulation**

### Interior Finishes
31. Drywall hang, tape, and finish
32. **INSPECTION: Drywall (some jurisdictions)**
33. Interior painting (primer + first coat)
34. Ceiling grid and tile installation
35. Flooring installation (tile, carpet, LVT, hardwood)
36. Millwork and casework installation
37. Door hardware installation
38. Toilet accessories and specialties
39. Final paint touch-up

### MEP Trim-Out
40. Electrical trim (devices, fixtures, panels energized)
41. Plumbing trim (fixtures, faucets, water heater)
42. Mechanical trim (grilles, diffusers, thermostats, controls)
43. Fire alarm installation and programming
44. Low-voltage trim (data jacks, cameras, AV equipment)

### Sitework Completion
45. Parking lot paving (asphalt or concrete)
46. Curb and gutter
47. Sidewalks and hardscape
48. Landscaping and irrigation
49. Exterior lighting
50. Signage installation
51. Striping and ADA signage

### Final Activities
52. Systems commissioning and testing
53. Punch list walkthrough and corrections
54. **INSPECTION: Final building, MEP, fire**
55. Certificate of Occupancy

## Ongoing Management Activities During Construction

### OAC Meetings (Owner-Architect-Contractor)
- Typically weekly
- Agenda: schedule update, RFI log, submittal status, change orders, safety, quality, financial update
- Meeting minutes distributed within 48 hours
- Action items tracked with responsible party and due dates

### RFI Process (Requests for Information)
```
Contractor identifies question → Submits RFI to Architect →
Architect reviews (often consults engineer) → Issues response →
GC implements response → Cost/schedule impact assessed
```
- Track: RFI #, date submitted, date needed, date received, days open
- Excessive RFIs may indicate incomplete CDs

### Submittal Process
```
Subcontractor prepares submittal → GC reviews → Forwards to Architect →
Architect reviews → Returns with action (Approved / Approved as Noted / 
Revise and Resubmit / Rejected) → GC distributes to sub
```
- **Deferred submittals:** Items flagged during permitting for AHJ review during construction

### Change Management
```
Issue identified → PCO (Potential Change Order) logged →
Scope defined → Pricing requested from GC/sub →
Owner reviews cost and schedule impact → Approve/Reject →
If approved → COR becomes formal Change Order → SOV updated
```
- See [[Financial Management & Billing]] for financial tracking
- ASI (Architect's Supplemental Instruction): Design changes initiated by architect
- CCD (Construction Change Directive): Owner-directed changes before pricing agreed

### Quality Control
- GC's quality control plan (material testing, inspections, mock-ups)
- Architect's construction administration (CA) site visits
- Special inspections (structural steel, concrete, fireproofing — per IBC)
- Third-party testing (soils, concrete, steel, welding)

### Safety Management
- Daily safety briefings (toolbox talks)
- Weekly safety inspections
- Incident reporting and investigation
- OSHA compliance monitoring
- Hot work permits, confined space entry, fall protection

### Schedule Management
- Weekly schedule updates (actual vs. planned)
- 3-week look-ahead updates
- Critical path monitoring
- Float tracking
- Delay documentation (weather days, owner delays, unforeseen conditions)

### Financial Management
- Monthly pay applications (see [[Financial Management & Billing]])
- Change order tracking
- Cost-to-complete forecasting
- Retainage tracking
- Lien waiver collection

## Key Milestones During Construction

| Milestone | Significance |
|-----------|-------------|
| Building Dried-In | Weather-tight; interior work can begin regardless of weather |
| Above-Ceiling Complete | All MEP rough-in above ceiling finished; ceiling can close |
| Power On | Permanent electrical service energized |
| Substantial Completion | Project is sufficiently complete for intended use; triggers warranty period and potential liquidated damages cutoff |
| Punch List Complete | All deficient items corrected |
| Final Completion | All contractual obligations met; triggers final payment and retainage release |

## Duration Benchmarks

| Project Type | Typical Construction Duration |
|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Retail tenant improvement (1,500-5,000 SF) | 6-12 weeks |
| Retail pad site (new build) | 3-6 months |
| Restaurant build-out | 8-16 weeks |
| Office tenant improvement | 6-14 weeks |
| Ground-up commercial (10,000-50,000 SF) | 6-12 months |
| Multi-family residential (wood frame) | 12-18 months |
| Institutional (hospital, school) | 18-36 months |
| Civil/infrastructure | Highly variable (6-36+ months) |

## Common Risks During Construction

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Unforeseen site conditions | Owner contingency; geotech report; site investigation |
| Material delivery delays | Early procurement of long-lead items; tracking log |
| Subcontractor performance issues | Pre-qualification; performance bonds; backup subs identified |
| Weather delays | Schedule float; seasonal planning |
| Design errors discovered during construction | RFI process; E&O insurance on design team |
| Change order disputes | Clear change management process; documentation discipline |
| Safety incidents | Safety plan; training; PPE enforcement |
| Inspection failures | Pre-inspection walkthroughs; quality control program |
