# Stakeholder Directory

> Cross-cutting reference for all phases of the [[Construction Program Management - Master Reference]]

---

## Core Project Stakeholders

### Owner / Developer / Client
- **Role:** Commissions the project, provides funding, defines objectives, holds ultimate decision authority
- **Active phases:** ALL (1-10)
- **Key responsibilities:** Approve design, authorize budget, make scope decisions, execute contracts, approve pay applications
- **Variants:** Owner-occupant, investor/developer, tenant (if tenant-driven), public entity

### Owner's Representative / Construction Manager (Agency CM)
- **Role:** Acts on behalf of the owner to oversee the project; does NOT hold trade contracts
- **Active phases:** 2-9
- **Key responsibilities:** Schedule management, budget oversight, quality control, stakeholder coordination, reporting
- **Distinction from CMAR:** Agency CM does not take construction risk; CMAR holds trade contracts and assumes GMP risk

### Architect of Record (AOR)
- **Role:** Licensed architect responsible for the design; stamps and seals construction documents
- **Active phases:** 3-8 (design through construction administration)
- **Key responsibilities:** Program development, design, code compliance, construction documents, submittal review, RFI response, site observations, certificate of substantial completion
- **Sub-consultants typically under the architect:** Interior designer, landscape architect, specialty consultants

### Engineers (Structural, MEP, Civil)
- **Role:** Licensed professionals designing specific building systems
- **Active phases:** 3-8
- **Structural:** Foundation design, framing, lateral systems, connections
- **Mechanical:** HVAC systems, ventilation, energy compliance
- **Electrical:** Power distribution, lighting design, fire alarm, low-voltage
- **Plumbing:** Water supply, sanitary waste, storm drainage, gas piping
- **Civil:** Site grading, utilities, stormwater, roadways, site infrastructure

### General Contractor (GC)
- **Role:** Primary builder; holds contracts with subcontractors; responsible for means, methods, and site safety
- **Active phases:** 5-9 (procurement through warranty)
- **Key responsibilities:** Construction execution, subcontractor management, schedule, safety, quality, pay applications, closeout documentation
- **Key personnel:** Project Manager, Superintendent, Project Engineer, Safety Officer

### Construction Manager at Risk (CMAR)
- **Role:** Hybrid role — acts as CM during design, then takes on GC role during construction under a GMP
- **Active phases:** 3-9 (preconstruction services through warranty)
- **Key distinction:** Provides GMP; absorbs cost overruns; may share savings with owner

### Subcontractors
- **Role:** Specialized trade contractors performing specific scopes of work
- **Active phases:** 5-9
- **Major trades:** Concrete, steel, framing, MEP, roofing, drywall, flooring, painting, fire protection, elevator
- **Key responsibilities:** Execute their scope per contract, attend coordination meetings, provide submittals, warranty their work

### Program Manager / PMO
- **Role:** Oversees multiple projects within a program; manages portfolio-level performance
- **Active phases:** ALL
- **Key responsibilities:** Standardize processes, aggregate reporting, resource allocation, client relationship management, billing coordination across projects

## External Stakeholders

### Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ)
- See [[AHJ Research Methodology]] for detailed coverage
- Building department, fire marshal, zoning board, health department, DOT, utilities

### Landlord (Leased Properties)
- **Role:** Property owner who has granted a lease; must approve tenant improvements
- **Active phases:** 1-2 (lease negotiation), 3-4 (design/permit approval), 7-8 (construction/closeout)
- **Key responsibilities:** Approve plans, administer TIA disbursements, coordinate base-building access, issue landlord consent

### Lender / Financial Institution
- **Role:** Provides construction financing; has oversight rights
- **Active phases:** 2 (loan origination), 7-8 (draw inspections), 10 (conversion to permanent)
- **Key responsibilities:** Approve draws, conduct site inspections, monitor budget/schedule, approve change orders above threshold

### Real Estate Broker
- **Role:** Identifies sites, negotiates lease/purchase terms
- **Active phases:** 1-2
- **Key responsibilities:** Market analysis, site tours, LOI/lease negotiation, tenant representation

### Environmental / Geotechnical Consultants
- **Active phases:** 2 (due diligence), occasionally 7 (unforeseen conditions)

### Surveyor
- **Active phases:** 2 (ALTA survey), 6-7 (layout/staking), 8 (as-built survey)

### Materials Testing / Special Inspections
- **Active phases:** 7 (construction — concrete, steel, soils, fireproofing testing)

### Insurance Broker / Surety
- **Active phases:** 5-8 (bonding, builder's risk, GL, workers comp)

## Communication Cadence

| Meeting Type | Frequency | Attendees | Purpose |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| OAC Meeting | Weekly | Owner, Architect, Contractor, CM | Status, schedule, budget, issues |
| Subcontractor Coordination | Weekly | GC, active subs | Scheduling, conflicts, safety |
| Owner Progress Report | Monthly | Owner, CM/PM | Executive summary, financials |
| Design Team Coordination | Weekly (during design) | Architect, engineers, consultants | Discipline coordination |
| Safety Meeting | Weekly | GC, all on-site workers | Safety topics, incidents |
| Program/Portfolio Review | Monthly/Quarterly | PM, client leadership | Multi-project status |
