# Appendix — Petroleum & Fuel Station Construction

> Industry appendix for [[Construction Program Management - Master Reference]] and [[Sector Profiles — Retail, Commercial, Residential, Civil]]

---

## Overview

Petroleum and fuel station construction (gas stations, travel centers, EV charging stations) is one of the most heavily regulated sectors due to underground storage tanks (USTs), hazardous materials, and environmental liability. Even a simple canopy and pump replacement triggers layers of oversight that don't exist in typical retail.

---

## Unique Regulatory Framework

### Federal Agencies
| Agency | Jurisdiction |
|--------|-------------|
| EPA (Environmental Protection Agency) | UST regulations (40 CFR 280/281), Clean Water Act, SPCC plans |
| OSHA | Worker safety around flammable materials, confined space entry |
| DOT | Transportation of fuel, signage on public roadways |

### State Agencies
- **State Environmental Agency** (e.g., TCEQ in Texas, TDEC in Tennessee): UST registration, installation/removal permits, leak reporting, remediation oversight
- **State Fire Marshal:** Flammable/combustible liquid storage and dispensing permits
- **State Weights & Measures:** Fuel dispenser calibration and certification

### Local AHJs
- Standard building permit (see [[04 - Entitlements & Permitting]])
- Fire department: Flammable liquids storage permit, emergency shutoff requirements
- Health department: If food service is included (convenience store with food prep)
- Stormwater/environmental: Fuel canopy drainage, oil-water separators

---

## Underground Storage Tank (UST) Program

### UST Installation Requirements
- Tanks must be double-walled (primary + secondary containment)
- Piping must be double-walled with interstitial monitoring
- Spill containment (spill buckets) at fill ports
- Overfill prevention devices on all tanks
- Cathodic protection (corrosion prevention) for metallic components
- Release detection systems (automatic tank gauging, interstitial monitoring, SIR)
- Financial responsibility documentation (insurance or state fund)

### UST Permitting Sequence
```
State UST Installation Permit Application →
Site Assessment (if not new construction) →
Tank and Piping System Design →
Permit Issued →
Installation by Licensed UST Contractor →
Third-Party Inspection During Installation →
Tightness Testing (tanks and piping) →
State Inspector Final Approval →
UST Registration with State Agency →
Operational Compliance Monitoring Begins
```

### UST Removal (Decommissioning)
- Required when replacing tanks or closing a station
- State UST removal permit required
- Licensed UST contractor performs removal
- Soil sampling beneath and around tank cavity (required by most states)
- If contamination found: triggers remediation process (can take months to years)
- Tank disposal must follow hazardous waste regulations
- Closure report filed with state environmental agency

## Environmental Considerations

### Phase I/II ESA — Enhanced Requirements
- Standard Phase I (see [[02 - Feasibility & Due Diligence]]) PLUS specific petroleum history review
- Any current or former fuel station requires Phase II (soil/groundwater sampling) regardless of Phase I findings
- Historical fuel stations on adjacent properties also trigger concern
- LUST (Leaking Underground Storage Tank) database search is mandatory

### Remediation
- If contamination is found, remediation may be required before, during, or after construction
- **Remediation methods:** Soil excavation and disposal, soil vapor extraction, groundwater pump-and-treat, monitored natural attenuation
- **Timeline impact:** Can add 6 months to several years to a project
- **Cost impact:** $50,000 to $1M+ depending on extent
- **State UST funds:** Many states have cleanup funds that reimburse eligible costs (with deductibles)

### Stormwater Management
- Fuel canopy areas require containment and treatment before discharge
- Oil-water separators required at fuel islands
- Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) plan required if oil storage exceeds thresholds

## Construction Sequence — Fuel Station Specific

### Site Work (Before Standard Building Construction)
1. Demolition and UST removal (if existing station)
2. Environmental assessment and remediation (if contamination found)
3. Mass grading and utility installation
4. **UST installation** (before paving — tanks go in the ground early)
5. Piping runs from tanks to dispenser locations
6. Electrical conduit for dispensers, tank monitoring, POS systems
7. Concrete fuel island construction (raised islands with bollard protection)

### Building Construction
- Convenience store / travel center follows standard retail construction sequence
- Canopy construction (steel columns, canopy structure, fascia, lighting)
- Dispenser installation and connection to underground piping
- POS (point-of-sale) system installation and integration
- Tank monitoring system (ATG — automatic tank gauge) installation and programming

### Testing and Commissioning
- Hydrostatic testing of all piping
- Tank tightness testing
- Dispenser calibration (Weights & Measures certification)
- Emergency shutoff testing
- Fuel delivery coordination (first fill)
- POS system testing with fuel management software
- Environmental monitoring system activation

## Permits Checklist — Petroleum Station

- [ ] Standard building permit (see [[04 - Entitlements & Permitting]])
- [ ] State UST installation permit
- [ ] State UST removal permit (if replacing/removing existing tanks)
- [ ] State fire marshal flammable liquids permit
- [ ] Local fire department flammable storage permit
- [ ] EPA SPCC plan (if applicable)
- [ ] Stormwater/NPDES permit
- [ ] Weights & Measures dispenser certification
- [ ] Health department permit (if food service)
- [ ] Sign permit (canopy signage, price signs, directional)
- [ ] DOT/traffic driveway access permit
- [ ] Air quality permit (vapor recovery systems — required in some states)

## Key Stakeholders — Petroleum Specific

| Stakeholder | Role |
|------------|------|
| Petroleum Equipment Contractor | Licensed UST installer; separate from GC in most cases |
| Environmental Consultant | Phase I/II, remediation oversight, regulatory compliance |
| State Environmental Agency | UST permitting, inspection, and compliance |
| State Fire Marshal | Flammable liquids storage approval |
| Fuel Supplier | Delivery logistics, branding requirements, equipment specs |
| ATG / Monitoring Vendor | Tank gauge and leak detection systems |
| Weights & Measures Inspector | Dispenser calibration certification |

## Cost Considerations

| Item | Impact |
|------|--------|
| UST installation (3-4 tanks + piping) | $250,000–$600,000 |
| UST removal and disposal | $30,000–$100,000 per tank |
| Environmental remediation | $50,000–$1,000,000+ |
| Dispensers (6-8 multi-product) | $150,000–$300,000 |
| Canopy (steel, lit) | $100,000–$300,000 |
| Fuel management / POS system | $50,000–$150,000 |

## Common Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Contamination discovered during UST removal | Budget environmental contingency; engage consultant early |
| UST permit delays at state level | Apply early; some states have 60-90 day review periods |
| Fuel supplier branding requirements conflict with design | Coordinate brand standards with architecture in SD phase |
| Dispenser or ATG lead times | Order equipment at DD phase; 12-20 week lead times common |
| Weights & Measures inspector availability | Schedule early; required before fuel sales can begin |
