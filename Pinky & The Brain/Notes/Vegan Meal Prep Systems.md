# 🥡 Vegan Meal Prep Systems

> **Purpose:** Systematic approach to high-volume vegan cooking that supports consistent protein targets, caloric goals, and cuisine variety with minimum daily effort. This is the operational layer of nutrition — turning knowledge into consistent practice.

---

## The Lean Bulk Meal Prep Challenge

For a lean bulk at 150+ lbs, Nathan needs 2,600–2,800+ calories and 150–180g protein daily. The challenge:
- **High protein requires intentional preparation** — plant protein sources require more preparation than animal products
- **Variety prevents compliance failure** — eating the same meals daily is the fastest way to abandon a nutrition plan
- **Cooking daily is not realistic** — meal prep is the bridge between nutrition goals and real life

---

## The Sunday Prep System (90-min Weekly Cook)

### The 90-Minute Template

```
Minutes 0-10: Set up stations, preheat oven, start legumes on stove

Minutes 10-25: 
- Start batch protein (tempeh or seitan in oven or pan)
- Start grain cooking (rice cooker or pot)

Minutes 25-50:
- While proteins roast and grains cook:
  → Prep and chop all vegetables for the week
  → Make 2-3 sauces
  → Prep breakfast items (overnight oats, smoothie bags)
  → Marinate secondary proteins

Minutes 50-70:
- Proteins come out; second batch goes in if needed
- Assemble pre-built meal components into containers

Minutes 70-90:
- Portion proteins
- Label containers
- Clean as you go

Result: 4-5 days of meals ready to assemble in under 5 min/day
```

## The Component Approach (Superior to Pre-Built Meals)

### Why Components Beat Pre-Made Meals

Pre-made meals become boring by day 3. Components allow infinite variety from the same prep:

**The Weekly Component Batch:**

| Component | Amount Prepped | Meals It Serves |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Cooked grain (rotate weekly) | 4 cups dry → ~8 cups cooked | Bowls, stir-fry, salads |
| Roasted tempeh | 400g | 4-5 protein servings |
| Baked extra-firm tofu | 600g | 5-6 protein servings |
| Cooked legumes | 3 cups dried → ~7 cups cooked | Every meal |
| Roasted vegetables | 1 full sheet pan | 4-5 servings |
| Base sauce (tahini, peanut, etc.) | 1 cup | All week |
| Green sauce / herb sauce | 1 cup | Variety |

**Result:** 5 components × mix-and-match = 15+ distinct meals without cooking daily

### Weekly Component Rotation System

**Grains (rotate each week):**
- Week 1: Brown rice
- Week 2: Farro (higher protein, nutty)
- Week 3: Quinoa (complete protein)
- Week 4: Barley (beta-glucan)
- Occasional: Bulgur, millet, wild rice, sorghum

**Proteins (rotate each week):**
- Week 1: Tempeh (marinated + baked)
- Week 2: Seitan (homemade preferred)
- Week 3: Crispy tofu + lentil soup
- Week 4: Tempeh crumble (taco meat style) + baked chickpeas

**Sauces (rotate each week, make 2):**
- Tahini lemon + miso ginger
- Peanut + teriyaki
- Romesco + cashew alfredo
- Harissa yogurt + gochujang glaze

## Protein Prep Master Guide

### Tempeh — Weekly Batch (400g)

```
PREP:
1. Steam 400g tempeh (cut into cubes or slabs) — 12 min

MARINADE (make enough for all):
4 tbsp tamari, 2 tbsp maple syrup, 2 tbsp rice vinegar,
1 tbsp sesame oil, 1 tbsp sriracha, 4 garlic cloves (grated),
1 inch ginger (grated)

3. Marinate steamed tempeh 30 min - 2 hrs

COOK:
Option A (best texture): Air fry 375°F, 15 min, shake halfway
Option B (meal prep): Bake 400°F, 25 min, flip once
Option C (fastest): Pan-fry in batches, 4 min/side

STORE: Refrigerator, airtight container, 5-7 days
FREEZE: Yes — freeze in portioned bags; thaw in refrigerator
```

**Per 100g serving: 19g protein, 9g fat, 195 cal**

### Crispy Tofu — Weekly Batch (600g)

```
1. Press 2 blocks extra-firm tofu, 1+ hour (or use super-firm)
2. Cut into ¾" cubes
3. Toss in bowl:
   - 1 tbsp avocado oil
   - 1 tbsp cornstarch
   - 1 tbsp tamari
   - 1 tsp garlic powder
   - ½ tsp smoked paprika
   - Salt
4. Spread on parchment-lined sheet pan (space between pieces — critical)
5. Bake 425°F, 25-30 min, flip at 15 min
6. Or: Air fry 375°F, 18-20 min, shake at 10 min

STORE: Refrigerator 5 days
NOTE: Reheat in oven/air fryer (microwave makes soggy)
```

### Lentil Cook (All Varieties)

```
Red lentils (fastest):
- No soak needed
- 1 cup lentils + 2 cups water + pinch salt
- Bring to boil → simmer 12-15 min → soft and creamy (great for dal, soups)

Green/brown lentils:
- Optional: Soak 30 min (speeds cook time)
- 1 cup + 2 cups water + kombu piece + bay leaf
- Simmer 25-30 min until tender but not mushy
- Salt AFTER cooking (salt toughens skin during cooking)
- Remove kombu and bay leaf

FLAVOR: Add aromatics to pot during cooking:
  - Bay leaves (always)
  - Garlic cloves (smashed)
  - Cumin seeds or whole coriander
  - Piece of kombu (umami + easier digestion)

STORE: Refrigerator 5-7 days; freeze up to 3 months
PORTION: 1 cup cooked = 18g protein
```

### Chickpeas — Dried to Cooked (Weekly Batch)

```
1. Sort and rinse 2 cups dried chickpeas
2. Soak overnight in 3x water
3. Drain and rinse
4. Cover with fresh water by 2 inches
5. Bring to boil → skim foam → reduce heat
6. Simmer 60-90 min until creamy inside (pinch test — no resistance)
7. Add salt in last 10 min only

IMPORTANT: Cook until VERY soft — barely cooked chickpeas are less digestible
           and make inferior hummus

Yield: ~2 cups dry → ~5 cups cooked
STORE: 5-7 days refrigerator; freeze in 1-cup portions

CRISPY ROASTED CHICKPEAS (from cooked):
- Rinse + pat VERY dry (critical)
- Toss with oil + salt + spice (smoked paprika + cumin + garlic)
- Bake 425°F, 25-30 min, shaking every 10 min
- Remove from oven when golden — crisp further as they cool
- Use as: Protein-rich salad topper, snack, crouton substitute
```

## High-Protein Vegan Breakfast Prep

### Overnight Oats (Batch, 5 jars)

```
Per jar:
- ½ cup rolled oats (dry)
- 1 cup soy milk
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 1 tbsp hemp seeds
- 1 tsp maple syrup

Stir → refrigerate overnight (minimum 6 hrs, up to 4 days)

Morning: Top with 1 cup berries + 2 tbsp pumpkin seeds + 1 tbsp almond butter

Protein per jar: ~18g (oats 6g + hemp 6g + chia 3g + soy milk 7g)
With toppings: ~25g protein
```

### Tofu Scramble Prep (Batch for 3 days)

```
2 blocks firm tofu (crumbled)
1 onion (diced)
1 bell pepper (diced)
2 cups spinach or kale
4 garlic cloves (minced)
3 tbsp nutritional yeast
1 tsp turmeric
1 tsp garlic powder
1 tsp onion powder
½ tsp smoked paprika
½ tsp kala namak (add OFF heat at end)
Salt + pepper

Sauté aromatics → add tofu → spices → cook until moisture evaporates and tofu begins to brown → cool → refrigerate

STORE: 3-4 days
Reheat: Pan with drop of oil → crisp up again
Per serving: ~22-25g protein
```

### Protein Smoothie Freezer Bags

Prep 7 smoothie bags:
```
Per bag:
- 1 cup spinach (frozen portion)
- ½ cup frozen mango or berries
- 1 frozen banana (sliced)
- 2 tbsp hemp seeds
- 1 tbsp flaxseed (ground)

Freeze in Ziploc bags

Morning: Dump bag into blender + 1 cup soy milk + 2 scoops protein powder
         = 60-second breakfast with 45-55g protein
```

| Container Type | Use | Notes |
|---------------|-----|-------|

## Macro Tracking Integration

### Simple Tracking Method for Lean Bulk

Don't weigh everything every day. Use this system:

**1. Pre-calculate component macros once:**
```
100g tempeh (cooked weight) = 19g P / 9g F / 8g C / 195 cal
1 cup cooked lentils = 18g P / 1g F / 40g C / 230 cal
1 cup cooked brown rice = 5g P / 2g F / 45g C / 215 cal
1 cup chickpeas = 15g P / 4g F / 45g C / 269 cal
2 scoops pea protein = 48g P / 2g F / 4g C / 220 cal
3 tbsp hemp seeds = 10g P / 15g F / 3g C / 180 cal
1 tbsp nutritional yeast = 4g P / 0g F / 3g C / 30 cal
½ avocado = 2g P / 11g F / 9g C / 130 cal
2 tbsp tahini = 3g P / 8g F / 3g C / 90 cal
```

**2. Build "template meals" with known macros:**
Each week's grain bowl, breakfast bowl, and post-workout shake have consistent macros. Track the template, not every ingredient.

**3. Weekly review:**
Weekly weigh-in determines if caloric adjustment is needed. Not daily food obsession.

## The Anti-Boredom Protocol

### Why People Fail Their Nutrition Plan

The #1 reason nutrition compliance fails: **boredom**. The solution is structured variety:

**Rotation Rules:**
1. Never repeat the same grain two weeks in a row
2. Never repeat the same sauce two weeks in a row
3. Rotate cuisine weekly: Mexican → Asian → Italian → Mediterranean → Indian → repeat
4. One new recipe every 2 weeks (prevents stagnation without overwhelm)
5. Keep a "Favorites" shortlist — meals that are fast AND loved — for low-energy days

**The "Dish of the Week" Tradition:**
Sunday prep always includes one new dish to try. Even if it's imperfect, it expands the repertoire. The greatest chefs in the world got their recipes from cooking them repeatedly, not from reading about them.
