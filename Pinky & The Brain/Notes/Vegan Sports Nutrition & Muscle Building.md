# 🏋️ Vegan Sports Nutrition & Muscle Building

> **Purpose:** Evidence-based guide to building muscle on a vegan diet. Covers muscle protein synthesis mechanisms, leucine threshold, pre/intra/post-workout nutrition, recovery nutrition, and Nathan's specific lean bulk protocol.

---

## The Science of Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS)

### What Triggers Muscle Growth

Muscle hypertrophy requires three simultaneous conditions:
1. **Mechanical tension** — resistance training stimulus
2. **Metabolic stress** — metabolic byproducts accumulating in muscle
3. **Positive protein balance** — MPS > muscle protein breakdown (MPB)

Nutrition primarily addresses #3. The goal of sports nutrition is to **maximize the net protein balance window** across the day.

### The Leucine Trigger

Leucine is the branched-chain amino acid (BCAA) that acts as the primary anabolic signal for MPS via the mTORC1 pathway.

**Leucine threshold:** ~2.5–3.0g per meal to maximally stimulate MPS
**Duration of MPS response:** ~2–3 hours per feeding (MPS is "refractory" after this — you can't extend it by eating more)
**Implication:** 4–5 meals per day, each meeting leucine threshold, is superior to 2 large meals

### Why Vegans Must Eat More Protein Per Meal

| Protein source | Leucine per 30g protein |
|---------------|------------------------|
| Whey (reference) | ~3.0g |
| Soy protein isolate | ~2.3g |
| Pea protein | ~2.1g |
| Rice + pea blend | ~2.4g |
| Lentils | ~1.6g |
| Seitan | ~1.8g |

**Practical implication:** To reliably hit the leucine threshold from plant protein, aim for **35–45g protein per meal** (not 25–30g as per omnivore recommendations).

### BCAA and EAA Profiles That Matter

All 9 essential amino acids must be present for MPS to proceed:
- Histidine, Isoleucine, Leucine, Lysine, Methionine, Phenylalanine, Threonine, Tryptophan, Valine

**Lysine is the most limiting amino acid in plant-dominant diets.** Seitan (wheat gluten) is very low in lysine. Always pair seitan with lysine-rich foods (legumes, soy, pumpkin seeds).

## Pre-Workout Nutrition

### Goals
1. Top off muscle glycogen (carbohydrate fuel)
2. Provide amino acids to blunt exercise-induced MPB
3. Avoid GI distress during training

### Timing Windows

| Timing | Meal Size | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| 2–3 hrs pre | Full meal | Protein + complex carbs + moderate fat |
| 60–90 min pre | Small meal/snack | Simple carbs + some protein, low fat |
| 30 min pre | Minimal — small snack only | Fast carbs only if needed |

### Pre-Workout Meal Templates

**2–3 Hours Pre (Full Meal):**
- Oatmeal + protein powder + banana + almond butter
- Tofu scramble + whole grain toast + fruit
- Tempeh bowl + brown rice + vegetables

**60–90 Min Pre (Light Snack):**
- Rice cakes + almond butter + banana
- Smoothie with protein powder + oats + berries
- Whole grain toast + peanut butter + honey (if surplus is needed)

**30 Min Pre (Emergency Only):**
- Banana
- 4–6 dates
- Small handful of raisins

### Pre-Workout Nutrition Science

**Carbohydrate:** 1–4g/kg body weight depending on training duration and intensity
- For 30-min sessions: 0.5–1g/kg is adequate
- At 150 lbs (68kg): 35–70g carbs pre-workout is ideal

**Protein:** 0.3–0.4g/kg body weight
- At 150 lbs: 20–27g protein pre-workout
- Focuses on blunting exercise-induced muscle protein breakdown

**Fat:** Minimize pre-workout — slows gastric emptying and may cause GI distress

**Caffeine:** 3–6mg/kg body weight, 45–60 min pre-workout
- At 150 lbs: 200–400mg (1–2 cups strong coffee)
- Enhances strength performance 5–15%, reduces RPE
- Green tea provides gentler caffeine + L-theanine (smoother focus)

## Post-Workout Nutrition — The Anabolic Window

### The Real Anabolic Window

The "30-minute window" is real but overstated. For trained athletes eating sufficient protein overall:
- **True critical window:** Within 2 hours post-workout
- **Priority:** Protein first, carbs second, fat less critical immediately

### Post-Workout Goals
1. **Stimulate MPS** — hit leucine threshold (35–45g vegan protein)
2. **Replenish glycogen** — 0.8–1.2g carbs/kg body weight
3. **Rehydrate** — replace sweat losses
4. **Reduce cortisol** — carbohydrate intake blunts post-exercise cortisol spike

### Post-Workout Meal Templates

**Immediate (0–2 hrs):**
- Tempeh + sweet potato + steamed greens (high protein, high carb)
- Pea protein shake + banana + oats blended (fast, portable)
- Lentil + rice bowl + roasted vegetables (complete amino profile)
- Edamame fried rice with tofu (protein + carbs together)
- Tofu and vegetable stir-fry over white rice (fast carb + complete protein)

**Protein Shake Strategy (when whole food isn't accessible):**
- 2 scoops pea + rice protein blend: 40–50g protein
- Add banana: 27g carbs
- Add oats (1/3 cup): 20g carbs
- Total: ~45g protein, 47g carbs — excellent post-workout

### Glycogen Replenishment

Post-workout glycogen synthesis is most efficient in the first 4 hours (peak insulin sensitivity):

| Bodyweight | Carb target (0–4 hrs post) | Notes |
|------------|--------------------------|-------|
| 150 lbs | 80–120g | Sweet potato + rice or banana + oats |
| 160 lbs | 85–130g | — |
| 170 lbs | 90–140g | — |
| 180 lbs | 95–150g | — |

**Fast-absorbing carbs post-workout are appropriate here** — white rice, ripe banana, dates, dried fruit. This is the time to use higher GI carbs intentionally.

## Meal Timing for Lean Bulk

### Optimal Feeding Pattern

For maximum MPS stimulation with a lean bulk:

```
Meal 1 (Breakfast) — ~35-40g protein
Meal 2 (Lunch) — ~40-45g protein
[Training]
Meal 3 (Post-workout) — ~40-45g protein + highest carbs of day
Meal 4 (Dinner) — ~40-45g protein + moderate carbs
Optional: Slow-digesting protein before bed (15-20g)
```

**Total: 155–185g protein across 4–5 eating occasions**

### Pre-Sleep Protein

Overnight is a 7–8 hour fasting period — significant MPB can occur without protein.

**Best vegan pre-sleep options (slow-digesting):**
- 2 tbsp hemp seeds + banana smoothie (gentle, not too filling)
- Soy protein shake (casein-like slow digestion)
- Edamame + walnuts (protein + fat = slow release)
- Hummus + whole grain crackers (light but protein-present)

Target: 15–25g protein, low glycemic, consumed 30–60 min before sleep

## Creatine — The One Supplement That Consistently Works

### Why Creatine Is Critical for Vegan Athletes

Creatine is found naturally in meat and fish. Vegans have **significantly lower muscle creatine stores** (up to 50% lower) due to no dietary intake.

**Creatine functions:**
- Replenishes ATP rapidly during high-intensity efforts (first 10 seconds of effort)
- Increases cell volumization (muscle appears larger, performance improves)
- Enhances strength output 5–15% in most individuals
- Improves high-rep performance and power output

**Research in vegans:** Creatine supplementation shows LARGER performance gains in vegans than omnivores, because baseline stores are lower.

### Protocol
- **Form:** Creatine monohydrate (micronized) — most studied, cheapest, most effective
- **Dose:** 3–5g daily (no loading phase necessary for long-term use)
- **Timing:** Timing is not critical — take with carb-containing meal for marginally better uptake
- **Onset:** 3–4 weeks of consistent use to saturate stores fully
- **Vegan sources:** Creatine monohydrate is synthesized from non-animal sources

## Beta-Alanine for Training Endurance

### Mechanism
Beta-alanine raises muscle carnosine, buffering lactic acid during high-rep sets (8–15 rep range — exactly Nathan's training range).

**Effect:** Delays fatigue in the 60–240 second effort window. Particularly useful for calisthenics supersets and circuit-style training.

### Protocol
- **Dose:** 3.2–6.4g daily (split doses to reduce tingling/paresthesia)
- **Onset:** 4–6 weeks of consistent use
- **Vegans:** Beta-alanine is synthesized — fully vegan
- **Expect:** Initial skin tingling (harmless) — reduces with continued use

## Hydration for Performance

### Hydration and Muscle Function

Dehydration of just **2% body weight** causes measurable performance decrements:
- Strength: -2–8%
- Power: -5–10%
- Cognitive performance: -10–15%
- Protein synthesis is impaired in dehydrated state

### Hydration Strategy

| Timing | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Pre-workout | Urine pale yellow | Begin hydrating 4–6 hrs before training |
| During workout | 500–750ml/hr | Sweat rate varies; monitor thirst |
| Post-workout | 1.5x fluid lost | Weigh before/after if precision needed |
| Daily baseline | 75–90 oz (scales with bodyweight) | More on hot days / heavy training |

**Electrolytes matter** for intense training:
- Sodium: Replace via food (don't restrict salt as an athlete)
- Potassium: Banana, potato, avocado, beans
- Magnesium: Pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate, leafy greens

## Nutrition Periodization

### Training Days vs. Rest Days

**Training days:** Higher carbohydrates, maintained protein, similar fat
**Rest days:** Reduce carbs by 50–75g, maintain protein (muscle protein turnover continues at rest), slight caloric deficit acceptable

| Macro | Training Day (150 lbs) | Rest Day (150 lbs) |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Protein | 165–180g | 165–180g |
| Carbs | 330–380g | 270–310g |
| Fat | 70–85g | 70–85g |
| Calories | 2,700–2,900 | 2,400–2,600 |

### Lean Bulk Caloric Cycling (Optional Advanced Strategy)

Instead of constant surplus, some respond better to:
- Training days: +300–400 cal above TDEE
- Rest days: Maintenance (+0–100 cal)
- Weekly average: Still hits lean bulk surplus

Benefit: Less fat accumulation over time, better insulin sensitivity cycling

## Protein Shakes for Vegans — Building the Optimal Blend

### The Best Vegan Protein Sources for Supplementation

| Protein Powder | Pros | Cons |
|---------------|------|------|
| Pea protein | High leucine, excellent taste, complete near-amino-profile | Slightly low methionine |
| Rice protein | Good taste, complete when combined | Low lysine — don't use alone |
| Pea + Rice blend (70:30) | Near-complete profile, high leucine, great texture | Slightly processed |
| Soy protein isolate | Complete — equivalent to whey, high protein density | Some prefer to vary from whole soy |
| Hemp protein | Whole food feel, omega-3s, fiber | Lower protein density (~50%), grassy taste |
| Pumpkin seed protein | Zinc, iron, good flavor | Lower leucine |

**Recommended:** Pea + Rice blend as base protein powder. Supplement with hemp seeds in whole foods throughout the day.

### DIY High-Protein Vegan Shake

```
2 scoops pea/rice protein blend:    48g protein
1 cup soy milk:                     7g protein
2 tbsp hemp seeds:                  6g protein
1 tbsp nutritional yeast:           3g protein
1 banana:                           1g protein + 27g carbs
¼ cup oats:                         4g protein + 20g carbs
1 tbsp almond butter:               3g protein + 7g fat
──────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL: ~72g protein, ~50g carbs, ~15g fat | ~620 cal
```

This single shake covers nearly half of Nathan's daily protein target.
