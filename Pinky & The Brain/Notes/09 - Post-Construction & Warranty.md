# 09 - Post-Construction & Warranty

> **Phase 9 of 10** | Predecessor: [[08 - Closeout & Turnover]] | Successor: [[10 - Asset Stabilization & Operations]]

---

## Purpose

Manage warranty obligations, address deficiencies that emerge after occupancy, and capture lessons learned.

---

## Warranty Types & Periods

| Warranty Type | Typical Duration | Trigger Date |
|--------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **General workmanship** (GC) | 1 year | Substantial completion |
| **Roofing systems** | 10-20 years (manufacturer); 2 years (workmanship) | Substantial completion |
| **HVAC equipment** | 1-5 years (manufacturer) | Equipment start-up |
| **Plumbing fixtures** | Limited lifetime (manufacturer) | Installation |
| **Electrical equipment** | 1-3 years (manufacturer) | Installation |
| **Waterproofing / sealants** | 5-10 years | Substantial completion |
| **Structural** | Per code/contract (often 10 years) | Substantial completion |
| **Paint** | 1-2 years | Substantial completion |
| **Flooring** | 1-5 years depending on material | Installation |

---

## Warranty Administration

### 11-Month Warranty Walkthrough
- Conducted approximately 11 months after substantial completion (before the 1-year warranty expires)
- Owner, CM/PM, and GC walk the entire project
- Document all deficiencies attributable to workmanship or materials
- GC is obligated to correct items under warranty at no cost
- This is the owner's last opportunity to capture issues under the general warranty

### Warranty Claim Process
```
Owner identifies deficiency → Documents with photos/description →
Notifies GC in writing (per contract requirements) →
GC evaluates and assigns to responsible subcontractor →
Subcontractor corrects → Owner verifies → Issue closed
```

### Common Warranty Issues
- HVAC performance complaints (inadequate heating/cooling)
- Roof leaks
- Door/window seal failures
- Paint peeling or discoloration
- Flooring delamination or staining
- Cracking (concrete, drywall — distinguish structural from cosmetic)
- Plumbing leaks
- Exterior caulk/sealant failure

## Lessons Learned

After every project (or at minimum, quarterly for programs):

### Capture Categories
- **Schedule:** What caused delays? What accelerated the timeline?
- **Budget:** Where were overruns? Where did we save? Were estimates accurate?
- **Quality:** What deficiencies occurred? Were specs adequate?
- **Communication:** Did the OAC process work? Were RFIs timely?
- **Stakeholder:** Were expectations managed? Were decisions timely?
- **Process:** What would we do differently? What worked well?

### Documentation
- Lessons learned report distributed to all project stakeholders
- Added to the knowledge base for future projects
- Feed back into standardized workflows and templates
