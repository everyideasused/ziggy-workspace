# 🥤 Ultimate Vegan Micronutrient Smoothie

**24 out of 24 essential micronutrients at 100%+ Daily Value.**  
16 whole-food ingredients engineered for maximum nutrient density.  
Vitamin B complex and Vitamin D supplemented separately.

**Meal Type:** Breakfast/Daily | **Servings:** 1 (32 oz)  
**Prep:** 4 min | **Total:** 4 min  
**Calories:** ~1310 kcal | **Protein:** ~48-52g | **Favorite:** ⭐

---

## 📋 Ingredients

### Greens & Vegetables
- **Kale, raw** — 100g (~2 cups chopped) → Vit K 325%, Vit C 103%, Calcium, Iron
- **Canned pumpkin puree** — 130g (~1/2 cup) → Vit A 124%, Vit E, Iron, Potassium

### Fruit
- **Banana** — 1 medium (118g) → B6 33%, Potassium, sweetness
- **Kiwi** — 1 medium (75g) → Vit C 71%, Vit K 25%, Vit E
- **Blueberries** — 75g (~1/2 cup) → Vit K, anthocyanins, flavor

### Seeds & Nuts
- **Hemp hearts** — 30g (~3 tbsp) → Magnesium 50%, Zinc 27%, Manganese 104%
- **Sunflower seeds** — 30g (~2 tbsp) → Vit E 70%, B5 42%, B1 42%, Folate
- **Ground flaxseed** — 20g (~2 tbsp) → Omega-3 ALA 281%, Manganese, Magnesium
- **Brazil nut** — 1 nut (~5g) → Selenium 175% *(one nut is all you need)*
- **Tahini** — 15g (~1 tbsp) → Calcium, Iron, B1, Selenium, creaminess

### Soy Products
- **Fortified soy milk** — 480ml (2 cups) → Calcium 60%, Riboflavin 77%, liquid base
- **Firm tofu** — 150g (~5.3 oz) → Calcium 53%, Iron 51%, Molybdenum, protein

### Superfoods & Fortified
- **Nutritional yeast (fortified)** — 16g (~2 tbsp) → B-vitamin blanket: B1, B2, B3, B6, Folate
- **Spirulina** — 10g (~2 tsp) → Chromium 80%, Iron, Copper
- **Dulse flakes** — 3g (~1 tsp) → Iodine 100% *(only reliable vegan source)*
- **Soy lecithin granules** — 15g (~1 tbsp) → Choline 68% *(the vegan choline solution)*

### Supplements (separate)
- **Vitamin B complex** (including B12) — Per label (min 2.4 mcg B12)
- **Vitamin D** — 600 IU / 15 mcg

## 👨‍🍳 Daily Routine (~4 minutes)

**On Sunday:** Prep all ingredients (see Batch Prep System below)

**Every Morning:**
1. Pour 2 cups soy milk into blender
2. Add 150g tofu + 130g pumpkin puree (half-cup scoop from can)
3. Add 1 scoop dry mix (~124g) + 1 brazil nut + 1 tbsp tahini
4. Add 1 frozen fruit bag + handful of kale (~100g)
5. Blend 90 seconds
6. Pour into 32oz container
7. Take B complex + Vitamin D with first sip

### Blend Order (for standard blenders)
If using a standard blender (not Vitamix), blend in stages:
1. Soy milk + tofu + pumpkin + dry mix powder → 30 sec
2. Add kale → 30-45 sec until greens fully broken down
3. Add frozen fruit bag → 15 sec
4. Add seeds/nuts + tahini → 30-45 sec until creamy

*A Vitamix or Blendtec can handle it all in one 90-second blend.*

## 🗓️ Batch Prep System (Sunday Meal Prep)

**Time:** ~30 minutes | **Servings:** 7 (one week)

### Dry Mix Jar (7 servings)
Combine all of the following into one large mason jar. Shake to distribute. Store in fridge.

| Ingredient | Per Serving | x7 (Weekly) |
|---|---|---|
| Hemp hearts | 30g | 210g |
| Sunflower seeds | 30g | 210g |
| Ground flaxseed | 20g | 140g |
| Nutritional yeast | 16g | 112g |
| Spirulina | 10g | 70g |
| Soy lecithin granules | 15g | 105g |
| Dulse flakes | 3g | 21g |

**Total dry mix per serving: ~124g (~1 heaping cup scoop)**

### Fruit Freezer Bags (7 bags)
- **Per bag:** 1 banana (peeled, broken in half) + 1 kiwi (peeled, quartered) + 75g blueberries
- Lay flat in freezer
- Frozen fruit = thicker, colder smoothie with no ice needed

### Tofu Blocks
- Cut a 600g block of firm tofu into 4 portions (~150g each)
- Store submerged in water in fridge
- Lasts 5-7 days if you change the water every 2 days
- Open second block mid-week for remaining 3 days

### Pantry Stock
- Canned pumpkin puree (one 15oz can = ~3.3 servings)
- Bag of pre-washed kale
- Brazil nuts
- Tahini jar
- Fortified soy milk cartons

## 🔬 Design Notes

### Why These 16?
Every ingredient pulls double or triple duty. Nothing is here just for flavor.

**Hardest nutrients to solve on a vegan diet and what solves them:**
- **Choline** → Soy lecithin granules (375mg in 1 tbsp)
- **Iodine** → Dulse flakes (only reliable vegan whole-food source)
- **Selenium** → 1 brazil nut (175% DV from 5 grams)
- **Vitamin A** → Canned pumpkin puree (massive beta-carotene density, zero prep)
- **Vitamin E** → Sunflower seeds (best whole-food source at 70% DV per 30g)
- **Chromium** → Spirulina (80% DV from 10g, nothing else comes close)
- **B-vitamins** → Nutritional yeast (one ingredient blankets B1, B2, B3, B6, folate)
- **Calcium** → Triple source: tofu + soy milk + kale (all high-bioavailability forms)

### Nutrient Synergies
- **Vitamin C + Iron**: Kale vitamin C dramatically boosts non-heme iron absorption from tofu, seeds, and spirulina
- **Fat + Fat-soluble vitamins**: Hemp, flax, and tahini fats ensure absorption of vitamins A, E, K from kale and pumpkin
- **Folate + Choline**: Both at 100%+ means neither methylation pathway is stressed
- **Calcium sources**: Kale calcium is highly bioavailable (unlike spinach). Tofu and soy milk use calcium sulfate/carbonate with strong absorption rates

### Safety Notes
- **Selenium**: At 280% DV, do not add extra brazil nuts. 1 nut/day is the sweet spot. 4+ daily risks selenosis over time
- **Iron**: At 275% DV, fine for most people. Worth noting if hemochromatosis runs in your family
- **Iodine variance**: Dulse iodine content varies by batch. For precision, a standardized kelp supplement is more consistent
- **Spirulina sourcing**: Buy from reputable brands that test for heavy metals

## 🍃 Taste & Adjustments

The smoothie is slightly nutty and savory-sweet. Banana, kiwi, blueberries, and pumpkin provide enough natural sweetness to balance spirulina, nooch, and kale. Tahini adds creaminess.

- **Sweeter**: Add 2-3 pitted medjool dates (bonus potassium and iron)
- **Less spirulina taste**: Split the 10g dose. 5g in smoothie, 5g stirred into a shot of juice
- **Thicker**: Use frozen banana and freeze pumpkin puree in ice cube trays
- **Thinner**: Add water or more soy milk to desired consistency
