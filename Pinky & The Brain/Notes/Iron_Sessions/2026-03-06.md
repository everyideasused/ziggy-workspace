## Training Philosophy Notes

**Why 3 phases?**
- Phase 1: Can't build a house on a weak foundation — learn movements first
- Phase 2: Once foundation is solid, volume drives hypertrophy
- Phase 3: Maximize strength, strip fat, reveal the build

**Why lean bulk first?**
- Nathan is underweight for 5'9" at 147.7 lbs
- Beginner status = can build muscle + lose fat simultaneously
- Eating in surplus accelerates muscle gain without excessive fat gain
- Phase 3 handles final fat loss to reveal muscle built in P1/P2

**Why 30 min sessions?**
- Fits Nathan's schedule (M-F work week)
- High-density work (minimal rest, compound movements)
- Sustainability > perfection
- Can always add volume later if needed

**Why kettlebell + calisthenics?**
- Minimal equipment, maximum ROI
- Functional strength (carries over to real life)
- Joint-friendly (less impact than barbell)
- Scalable (bodyweight → KB → weighted vest → heavier KB)
