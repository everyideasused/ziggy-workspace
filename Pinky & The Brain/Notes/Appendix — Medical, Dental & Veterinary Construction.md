# Appendix — Medical, Dental & Veterinary Construction

> Industry appendix for [[Construction Program Management - Master Reference]] and [[Sector Profiles — Retail, Commercial, Residential, Civil]]

---

## Overview

Healthcare-related construction (medical offices, urgent care, dental, veterinary, outpatient surgery centers) is heavily regulated, equipment-intensive, and carries specialized MEP requirements. Even a simple dental office has infrastructure demands that far exceed a typical office build-out.

## Specialized Systems — Medical

### Medical Gas Systems
| Gas | Use | Where Required |
|-----|-----|---------------|
| Oxygen (O2) | Patient care, anesthesia | Surgery centers, urgent care, some medical offices |
| Nitrous oxide (N2O) | Sedation | Dental offices, surgery centers |
| Medical air | Respiratory therapy | Surgery centers, some urgent care |
| Vacuum (suction) | Surgical suction, dental suction | Nearly all healthcare facilities |
| Compressed air (dental) | Handpieces, air/water syringes | All dental offices |
| Nitrogen | Equipment operation | Some surgery centers |
| Waste anesthetic gas disposal (WAGD) | Scavenging exhaust gases | Where N2O or anesthesia is used |

**Regulatory:** Medical gas systems must be installed by certified medical gas installers and verified by third-party testing per NFPA 99 and ASSE 6010/6020/6030.

### Dental-Specific Infrastructure
- **Dental compressor:** Oil-free, medical-grade (housed in mechanical room or exterior)
- **Dental vacuum system:** Central vacuum with separator (housed in mechanical room)
- **Nitrous oxide manifold:** With alarm monitoring and room ventilation
- **Dental chair rough-in:** Water supply, drain, compressed air, vacuum, electrical (each operatory)
- **Darkroom or digital X-ray:** Lead-lined walls (or digital sensors eliminating darkroom need)
- **Panoramic X-ray:** Dedicated room with lead shielding
- **Sterilization center:** Autoclaves, ultrasonic cleaners, instrument storage

### Imaging / Radiology
- **X-ray:** Lead-lined walls (varies by kVp of equipment), floor, and sometimes ceiling
- **CT Scanner:** Reinforced floor (equipment weight 2,000-5,000+ lbs), dedicated cooling, lead shielding
- **MRI:** RF-shielded room (Faraday cage), vibration isolation, cryogen vent, NO ferromagnetic materials, dedicated chiller, structural reinforcement (magnets weigh 10,000-25,000+ lbs)
- **Radiation shielding design:** Must be designed by a qualified medical physicist; calculations submitted to state radiation control program

### Infection Control During Construction (ICRA)
For construction in or adjacent to operating healthcare facilities:
- **ICRA (Infection Control Risk Assessment):** Required before any construction in healthcare
- **Classification:** Class I (low risk) through Class IV (high risk) determines containment measures
- **Containment measures:** Dust barriers, negative air pressure, HEPA filtration, sealed penetrations, anteroom for worker entry/exit
- **Monitoring:** Air quality testing, surface sampling
- **Impact:** Adds cost, time, and complexity to any renovation in an active healthcare facility

## Specialized MEP — Healthcare

### HVAC
- **Air change rates:** Code-mandated minimum air changes per hour (ACH)
  - Operating rooms: 15-20 ACH, positive pressure
  - Exam rooms: 6 ACH
  - Waiting rooms: 6 ACH (may be higher post-COVID)
  - Isolation rooms: 12 ACH, negative pressure (if applicable)
- **Filtration:** MERV 14 minimum for surgery areas; HEPA for isolation
- **Temperature/humidity control:** Precise requirements in ORs (68-75°F, 20-60% RH)
- **Dedicated exhaust:** For sterilization areas, labs, isolation rooms, waste anesthetic gases

### Electrical
- **Emergency power:** Generator or battery backup for life safety systems
- **Isolated power systems:** Required in wet procedure locations (ORs)
- **UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply):** For critical equipment (imaging, anesthesia monitors)
- **Medical-grade outlets:** Hospital-grade receptacles in patient care areas

### Plumbing
- **Backflow prevention:** Critical on all domestic water connections
- **Hot water:** Higher temperature requirements for sterilization
- **Medical gas piping:** Copper with specific brazing and cleaning requirements per NFPA 99
- **Lab waste:** Acid-resistant waste piping if lab services present
- **Veterinary:** Floor drains in all animal treatment and kennel areas; drainage for tubs/grooming

## Regulatory Framework — Healthcare

### State Health Department / Facility Licensing
- Many states require **state health department plan review** for medical facilities (separate from local building permit)
- Ambulatory surgery centers have the most stringent requirements (comparable to hospitals)
- Some states require **certificate of need (CON)** before building new healthcare facilities
- Review timelines: 4-12+ weeks depending on state and facility type

### DEA (Drug Enforcement Administration)
- If the facility will store or dispense controlled substances: DEA registration and vault requirements
- Vault construction standards: Reinforced walls, floor, ceiling; specific lock and alarm requirements

### State Radiation Control
- Any facility with X-ray, CT, or radiation therapy: state registration and shielding verification
- Shielding plan designed by medical physicist
- Post-construction radiation survey required before use

### AAAHC / Joint Commission Accreditation
- Surgery centers seeking accreditation must meet additional design and construction standards
- Fire safety, emergency preparedness, infection control documentation

## Permits Checklist — Healthcare

Standard permits (see [[04 - Entitlements & Permitting]]) PLUS:
- [ ] State health department facility plan review (if required by state)
- [ ] State radiation control registration (if X-ray, CT, or radiation equipment)
- [ ] Medical gas installer certification verification
- [ ] Medical gas system third-party verification test
- [ ] DEA vault approval (if controlled substances)
- [ ] Certificate of Need (some states, some facility types)
- [ ] Pharmacy board approval (if pharmacy operations)
- [ ] Health department approval (veterinary — some jurisdictions)

## Common Risks — Healthcare

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| State health department review delays | Submit early; engage expediters; pre-application conferences |
| Medical gas testing failures | Use certified installers only; pre-test before calling for third-party verification |
| Imaging equipment structural requirements missed | Engage equipment vendor at SD phase for weight, vibration, shielding specs |
| ICRA containment failures (renovation) | Dedicated ICRA plan; daily monitoring; train all workers |
| Equipment lead times (dental chairs, imaging) | Order at DD phase; 10-20+ week lead times for imaging equipment |
| Lead shielding design changes after drywall | Finalize imaging equipment selection before DD; get physicist shielding calcs early |
| Infection control during construction in active facility | Phasing plan; negative air; sealed barriers; coordinate with facility infection control officer |
