# Financial Planning Milestones & Benchmarks

> Cross-cutting reference for [[Personal Finance Knowledge Base]]
> **Purpose:** Concrete benchmarks to measure progress. Where should you be, and what should you focus on next?

---

## The Financial Milestone Map

This is a sequential checklist of financial milestones, roughly in priority order. Not every item applies to every situation, and timing will vary — but the sequence is generally correct.

### Phase 1: Foundation (Current Focus)
- [ ] Created a budget or spending plan ([[Budgeting & Cash Flow Management]])
- [ ] Know exact monthly take-home pay, fixed expenses, and variable expenses
- [ ] Capturing full employer 401k match
- [ ] Have $1,000-$2,000 starter emergency fund
- [ ] All bills on auto-pay (no late payments)
- [ ] Tracking net worth monthly ([[Monthly Finance Review]])
- [ ] No new consumer debt being added
- [ ] Credit score above 670 and improving

### Phase 2: Debt Elimination
- [ ] All toxic debt eliminated (credit cards, store cards, payday loans)
- [ ] All bad debt eliminated (personal loans, high-rate auto loans)
- [ ] Only "tolerable" debt remains (mortgage, low-rate student loans, low-rate auto)
- [ ] Credit utilization below 30% (ideally below 10%)
- [ ] Credit score above 740

### Phase 3: Safety Net
- [ ] Emergency fund at 1 month of essential expenses
- [ ] Emergency fund at 3 months of essential expenses
- [ ] Emergency fund at 6 months of essential expenses (or appropriate tier)
- [ ] Emergency fund in a high-yield savings account, separate from checking
- [ ] Sinking funds established for predictable irregular expenses

### Phase 4: Growth Engine
- [ ] Contributing at least 15% of gross income to retirement (including employer match)
- [ ] Roth IRA opened and funded (or Backdoor Roth if income limits apply)
- [ ] 401k maxed ($23,500/year in 2025)
- [ ] HSA maxed (if eligible)
- [ ] Taxable brokerage account opened for additional investing
- [ ] Investment allocation set and documented
- [ ] Rebalancing on a regular schedule (annual)

### Phase 5: Optimization
- [ ] Tax strategy in place (Roth vs. Traditional allocation, tax-loss harvesting)
- [ ] Insurance coverage reviewed and adequate ([[Insurance & Risk Management - Personal]])
- [ ] Umbrella policy in place
- [ ] Estate documents drafted (will, power of attorney, healthcare directive)
- [ ] Beneficiaries up to date on all accounts
- [ ] Savings rate at 20%+ of gross income (stretch: 30-50%)

### Phase 6: Wealth Building
- [ ] Net worth positive and growing consistently
- [ ] Retirement on track per age-based benchmarks (see below)
- [ ] Multiple income streams or side income explored
- [ ] Real estate investment considered (if appropriate)
- [ ] Charitable giving strategy in place (if desired)
- [ ] Financial plan documented and reviewed annually

## Age-Based Retirement Benchmarks

### Savings Milestones (Fidelity Model)
Target retirement savings as a multiple of annual salary:

| Age | Target |
|-----|--------|
| 30 | 1× salary saved |
| 35 | 2× salary saved |
| 40 | 3× salary saved |
| 45 | 4× salary saved |
| 50 | 6× salary saved |
| 55 | 7× salary saved |
| 60 | 8× salary saved |
| 67 | 10× salary saved |

### If You're Behind
Don't panic. The most powerful lever is savings rate, not returns:
- Increasing savings from 10% to 15% of income has a larger impact than increasing returns from 7% to 8%
- Catch-up contributions are available after age 50
- Every percentage point of savings rate increase matters
- Reducing expenses in retirement (paid-off mortgage, lower tax bracket) changes the target number

## Key Financial Ratios to Monitor

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Range |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Savings Rate** | Total Saved ÷ Gross Income | 15-20% minimum, 30%+ ideal |
| **Housing Ratio** | Housing Cost ÷ Gross Income | ≤28% |
| **Debt-to-Income (DTI)** | All Debt Payments ÷ Gross Income | <36% (ideally <20% excluding mortgage) |
| **Emergency Fund Ratio** | EF Balance ÷ Monthly Essential Expenses | 3-6 months |
| **Liquid Net Worth** | Assets - Liabilities (excluding home equity) | Positive and growing |
| **Investment Fee Load** | Total Investment Fees ÷ Total Invested | <0.25% weighted average |
| **Insurance Coverage Ratio** | Life Insurance ÷ Annual Income | 10-15× (if dependents exist) |

## Annual Financial Health Check

Every year (ideally Q4 or early Q1), review these items:

**Income & Spending**
- Total income vs. last year
- Total expenses vs. last year and vs. budget
- Savings rate achieved vs. target
- Any new recurring expenses added

**Net Worth**
- Total assets (accounts, investments, property)
- Total liabilities (all debts)
- Net worth change vs. last year
- On track for retirement benchmarks?

**Investments**
- Contributions maximized? (401k, IRA, HSA)
- Asset allocation still appropriate?
- Rebalancing needed?
- Expense ratios acceptable?
- Tax-loss harvesting opportunities?

**Tax**
- Any life changes affecting tax strategy?
- Roth vs. Traditional allocation still optimal?
- Withholding correct? (not getting a big refund or owing a big amount)
- Charitable giving opportunities?

**Insurance**
- All coverage types adequate?
- Deductibles aligned with emergency fund?
- Life/disability still appropriate for life stage?
- Rates competitive? (shop if not)

**Estate**
- Will/trust current?
- Beneficiaries correct on all accounts?
- Power of attorney and healthcare directive in place?
