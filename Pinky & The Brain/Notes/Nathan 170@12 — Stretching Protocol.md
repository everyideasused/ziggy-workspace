# Nathan 170@12 — Stretching Protocol

**Progressive 3-Phase Flexibility System**  
**Goal:** Improve mobility, reduce injury risk, enhance recovery  
**Timeline:** 36 weeks (aligned with strength program phases)

---

## Protocol Overview

| Phase | Weeks | What Changes |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| **Phase 1** | 1-4 | Post-workout stretching only (M/W/F, 10 min each) |
| **Phase 2** | 5-8 | Add Saturday flexibility session (20-30 min) |
| **Phase 3** | 9+ | Optional T/Th post-conditioning stretch (5-10 min) |

**Core principle:** Add stretching gradually to avoid overwhelming the system. Build the habit first, then expand.

---

## Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Post-Workout Foundation

### Monday — Post-Lower Body (10 min)

**Focus:** Hips, hamstrings, glutes, quads, calves

**1. Hip Flexor Stretch (Couch Stretch)** — 60 sec per leg
- Rear knee on ground, shin against wall/couch
- Front foot flat, knee at 90°
- Squeeze glute, drive hips forward
- Feel stretch in front of rear hip
- **Breathing:** Exhale into the stretch, inhale to hold
- **Goal:** Full hip extension without lower back arch

**2. Pigeon Pose** — 60 sec per leg
- Front leg bent, shin parallel to mat
- Rear leg extended straight back
- Square hips forward
- Fold forward over front leg (chest to shin)
- Feel stretch in glute/hip of front leg
- **Breathing:** Deep belly breaths, relax into pose
- **Goal:** Chest to shin without hip rotation

**3. Seated Forward Fold (Hamstrings)** — 90 sec
- Sit with legs extended
- Hinge at hips (don't round back)
- Reach toward toes/shins
- Feel stretch behind knees and hamstrings
- **Breathing:** Exhale to fold deeper, relax shoulders
- **Goal:** Hands to toes, chest to knees

**4. Quad Stretch (Standing)** — 60 sec per leg
- Stand on one leg
- Pull opposite foot to glute
- Keep knees together
- Squeeze glute of stretched leg
- Feel stretch down front of thigh
- **Breathing:** Steady, maintain balance
- **Goal:** Heel to glute without arching back

**5. Calf Stretch (Wall Lean)** — 60 sec per leg
- Front foot flat, rear leg straight
- Lean into wall
- Keep rear heel down
- Feel stretch in rear calf
- **Breathing:** Exhale to sink deeper
- **Goal:** Full ankle dorsiflexion

**6. 90/90 Hip Stretch** — 60 sec per side
- Sit with front leg at 90°, rear leg at 90°
- Square hips forward
- Lean over front leg
- Feel stretch in front hip and rear glute
- **Breathing:** Relax into the position
- **Goal:** Even hip rotation bilaterally

**Total: ~9 minutes** (30-90 sec per stretch, 6 stretches)

---

### Wednesday — Post-Upper Body (10 min)

**Focus:** Shoulders, chest, lats, triceps, neck

**1. Doorway Chest Stretch** — 60 sec per side
- Arm on doorframe at 90°
- Step through doorway
- Feel stretch across chest and front shoulder
- **Breathing:** Exhale to open chest
- **Goal:** Full chest opening without shoulder discomfort
- **Variation:** Change arm angle (high/low) for different fibers

**2. Cross-Body Shoulder Stretch** — 60 sec per arm
- Pull arm across chest
- Opposite hand on elbow
- Keep shoulders down
- Feel stretch in rear shoulder
- **Breathing:** Steady, relax trap
- **Goal:** Full shoulder horizontal adduction

**3. Lat Stretch (Overhead Side Bend)** — 60 sec per side
- Stand, reach one arm overhead
- Bend to opposite side
- Feel stretch down side of torso
- **Breathing:** Exhale into bend
- **Goal:** Full rib expansion

**4. Tricep Stretch (Overhead)** — 60 sec per arm
- Reach arm overhead, bend elbow
- Opposite hand pulls elbow deeper
- Feel stretch down back of arm
- **Breathing:** Relax shoulder
- **Goal:** Hand to mid-back

**5. Thread the Needle** — 60 sec per side
- Start on hands and knees
- Thread one arm under opposite arm
- Shoulder and temple to ground
- Feel stretch across upper back and shoulder
- **Breathing:** Deep breaths into back
- **Goal:** Full thoracic rotation

**6. Child's Pose with Lat Focus** — 90 sec
- Knees wide, sit back on heels
- Reach arms forward
- Walk hands to one side for lat stretch
- Hold 45 sec per side
- **Breathing:** Belly breaths
- **Goal:** Full lat lengthening

**7. Neck Stretch (4-way)** — 30 sec total (hold each 7-8 sec)
- Ear to shoulder (left/right)
- Chin to chest
- Look up and slightly back
- Gentle holds, no forcing
- **Breathing:** Exhale into each stretch
- **Goal:** Full pain-free neck ROM

**Total: ~10 minutes**

### Friday — Post-Full Body (10 min)

**Focus:** Full body sequence, recovery emphasis

**1. Child's Pose** — 60 sec
- Rest position, deep breathing
- Arms extended or by sides
- Forehead to ground
- **Breathing:** 4-count in, 6-count out
- **Goal:** Full spinal decompression

**2. Cat-Cow Flow** — 10 reps (60 sec)
- Hands and knees
- Cow: Arch back, look up
- Cat: Round spine, tuck chin
- Flow between positions
- **Breathing:** Inhale cow, exhale cat
- **Goal:** Fluid spinal movement

**3. Downward Dog Hold** — 60 sec
- Inverted V shape
- Hands shoulder-width, feet hip-width
- Push hips up and back
- Pedal feet to stretch calves
- **Breathing:** Deep breaths
- **Goal:** Heels toward ground, straight legs

**4. Low Lunge (Hip Flexor + Quad)** — 60 sec per side
- Front foot flat, rear knee down
- Sink hips forward
- Reach arms overhead (optional)
- **Breathing:** Exhale to sink deeper
- **Goal:** Full hip extension

**5. Lizard Pose** — 60 sec per side
- Low lunge position
- Front foot wide, drop to elbows
- Feel deep hip stretch
- **Breathing:** Relax into pose
- **Goal:** Elbows to ground

**6. Seated Spinal Twist** — 60 sec per side
- Sit cross-legged
- Twist to one side
- Opposite hand on knee
- Look over shoulder
- **Breathing:** Exhale to twist deeper
- **Goal:** Full thoracic rotation

**7. Supine Figure-4 (Glute Stretch)** — 60 sec per side
- Lie on back
- Cross ankle over opposite knee
- Pull knee toward chest
- Feel stretch in glute
- **Breathing:** Relax into stretch
- **Goal:** Deep glute release

**8. Corpse Pose (Savasana)** — 90 sec
- Lie flat, arms by sides
- Close eyes, body scan
- Let everything relax
- **Breathing:** Natural, effortless
- **Goal:** Full body relaxation

**Total: ~10 minutes**

## Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8): Add Saturday Session

**Keep Monday/Wednesday/Friday post-workout stretches.**  
**Add:** Saturday morning flexibility/mobility session (20-30 min)

### Saturday — Dedicated Flexibility Session (30 min)

**Format:** Yoga-style flow, hold-based stretching, active mobility  
**Goal:** Deep flexibility work, recovery enhancement, mental reset  
**Equipment:** Yoga mat, optional blocks

**Part 1 — Dynamic Warm-up (5 min)**
1. Cat-Cow Flow — 10 reps
2. Thread the Needle — 5 per side
3. Hip Circles — 10 per direction
4. Arm Circles — 10 forward, 10 back
5. Leg Swings — 10 per leg (forward/back, side-to-side)

**Part 2 — Lower Body Focus (10 min)**
1. Lizard Pose — 90 sec per side
2. Pigeon Pose — 90 sec per side
3. Seated Forward Fold — 2 min
4. Butterfly Stretch — 90 sec (soles together, fold forward)
5. Happy Baby Pose — 90 sec (on back, hold feet)
6. Supine Figure-4 — 90 sec per side

**Part 3 — Upper Body Focus (8 min)**
1. Puppy Pose — 90 sec (knees down, chest to ground, arms extended)
2. Doorway Chest Stretch — 90 sec per side
3. Eagle Arms — 60 sec per side (wrap arms, lift elbows)
4. Cow Face Arms — 60 sec per side (one arm up, one down, clasp hands)
5. Thread the Needle — 90 sec per side

**Part 4 — Spinal Mobility (5 min)**
1. Supine Spinal Twist — 90 sec per side
2. Sphinx Pose — 90 sec (gentle backbend)
3. Child's Pose — 90 sec (rest)

**Part 5 — Integration (2 min)**
1. Corpse Pose — 2 min (eyes closed, body scan, gratitude)

**Total: ~30 minutes**

**Shorter version (20 min):** Cut Part 1 to 2 min, reduce hold times to 60 sec each

## Form Principles — Apply to ALL Stretches

1. **Breathing is non-negotiable**
   - Never hold your breath
   - Exhale to deepen stretch
   - Inhale to stabilize
   - 4-count in, 6-count out (promotes parasympathetic)

2. **Pain vs. Discomfort**
   - ✅ Good: Gentle pull, slight burn, "feels good" stretch
   - ❌ Bad: Sharp pain, shooting pain, joint pain, nerve tingling
   - If it's bad pain, back off immediately

3. **Progressive depth**
   - Start at 70% depth
   - Exhale to sink to 80-85%
   - Never force to 100%
   - Let time do the work

4. **Hold times matter**
   - 30 sec minimum for neurological release
   - 60 sec optimal for flexibility gains
   - 90 sec for deep connective tissue work

5. **Consistency > intensity**
   - Better to stretch 10 min daily than 60 min once
   - Habit first, flexibility second

6. **Post-workout timing**
   - Stretch within 5-10 min of finishing workout
   - Muscles are warm, pliable, receptive
   - Don't let them cool down and tighten

## Red Flags — When to Back Off

| Warning Sign | What It Means | Action |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| **Sharp pain** | Joint/nerve issue | Stop immediately, assess |
| **Numbness/tingling** | Nerve compression | Back off depth, reposition |
| **Pain after stretch** | Overstretched | Reduce depth, increase warm-up |
| **Decreased ROM** | Overtraining or injury | Rest day, evaluate |
| **Joint popping (with pain)** | Instability | Strengthen before stretching |

**If stretching causes problems:**
1. Skip that stretch for 1-2 sessions
2. Substitute with gentler variation
3. Check form with video
4. If persistent, consult physio

## Modification Library

**For tight hips:**
- Use blocks under hands in Lizard/Pigeon
- Elevate front leg in Pigeon
- Bend knees in Forward Fold

**For tight shoulders:**
- Lower arm angle in doorway stretch
- Use strap for Cow Face Arms
- Skip overhead reaches, work horizontal first

**For tight hamstrings:**
- Sit on folded towel in Forward Fold
- Bend knees slightly in Downward Dog
- Use strap around feet in seated stretches

**For low back sensitivity:**
- Avoid deep backbends
- Keep knees bent in Forward Fold
- Use Child's Pose as primary spinal stretch

## FAQ

**Q: Can I stretch on rest days?**  
A: Yes! Light stretching (10-15 min) on Saturday/Sunday is great for recovery. Just keep intensity moderate.

**Q: Should I stretch before workouts?**  
A: No. Save static stretching for after. Before workouts, do dynamic warm-ups (included in workout templates).

**Q: What if I skip a stretching session?**  
A: No stress. Get back to it next session. Consistency matters more than perfection.

**Q: How long until I see flexibility gains?**  
A: Noticeable progress in 2-3 weeks, significant changes in 6-8 weeks with consistent practice.

**Q: Can I add more stretching?**  
A: Yes, but don't sacrifice recovery. If you're doing extra, keep it light (e.g., 5 min before bed).
