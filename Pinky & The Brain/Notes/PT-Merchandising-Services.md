# PT-Merchandising-Services — Estimating Guide

> Covers: Retail resets, planogram resets, fixture installations, rollout programs, brand refresh programs, and national retail service estimating. This is a specialized discipline distinct from construction — driven by speed, scale, crew productivity, and program management rather than traditional building trades.

---

## What Merchandising Services Actually Is

Merchandising services occupies the space between construction and retail operations:

- **Not a building trade** — no permits, no licensed trades, no GC in most cases
- **Executed by retail service organizations (RSOs)** or specialty merchandising contractors
- **Night/overnight work** — most resets happen during off-hours to avoid store disruption
- **Speed-to-scale** — the value proposition is deploying dozens or hundreds of stores simultaneously
- **Program management intensive** — scheduling, materials management, quality control, reporting

**Common program types:**
- Fixture resets (gondola moves, section rearrangement, new fixture type introduction)
- Planogram resets (product adjacency changes, shelf height adjustments)
- New store sets (setting fixtures in a newly constructed or remodeled store)
- Brand/department refresh (coordinated fixture + signage + display swap across a fleet)
- Seasonal resets (retail calendar-driven floor changes)
- Rollout programs (new product category, new brand addition across network)

## Who Does This Work

### Tier 1: National Retail Service Organizations (RSOs)
Large-scale, full-service:
- WIS International
- Retail Data Systems (RDS)
- ActionLink
- Product Connections
- Advantage Solutions (retail services division)

**Best for:** 100+ store programs, complex resets, brands needing full program management

### Tier 2: Regional Retail Service Companies
Mid-size, often 1–3 state footprint:
- Numerous regional players — research by market
- Often subcontracted by national RSOs for coverage gaps

**Best for:** Regional rollouts, 10–100 store programs

### Tier 3: Specialty Construction Firms (Fixture Installation)
When fixtures require construction-adjacent work:
- Millwork installation
- Fixture anchoring (through floor or wall)
- Minor electrical (lighting in fixtures, plug-in vs. hardwired)
- Data/communication in fixture systems

**Best for:** High-complexity fixture installations with hardwired components

### Tier 4: DIY / Store Labor
The retailer's own staff perform resets:
- Typically used for minor planogram updates
- Not viable for major resets or new store sets
- True cost is often understated (opportunity cost, productivity loss)

## Merchandising Pricing Models

### Model 1: Per-Store Flat Rate
- **Used for:** Standardized resets across uniform store format
- **How to estimate:** Scope hours per store × hourly rate + materials + travel + management
- **Typical range:** $2,000–$15,000/store depending on scope
- **Client benefit:** Budget certainty
- **Contractor risk:** Non-standard stores, schedule changes, material delays

### Model 2: Per-Hour (T&M)
- **Used for:** Complex, variable scopes; pilot programs; undefined scope
- **How to estimate:** Estimated hours × bill rate
- **Crew rates (national RSO, T&M):**

| Role | Typical Bill Rate |
|---|---|
| Merchandiser (base level) | $22–$32/hour |
| Lead merchandiser | $28–$40/hour |
| Regional supervisor | $45–$65/hour |
| Project manager (program) | $65–$100/hour |
| Fixture installation technician | $35–$55/hour |

**Note:** These are bill rates, not labor costs. RSO margin is typically 30–50% on labor.

### Model 3: Per-Fixture or Per-Linear-Foot
- **Used for:** Gondola resets, shelf replacement, fixture swap programs
- **Typical ranges:**

| Unit | Range |
|---|---|
| Per gondola section reset (4-foot) | $35–$90/section |
| Per gondola section (full install, new) | $80–$200/section |
| Per linear foot of shelving (reset) | $8–$22/LF |
| Per linear foot (new install) | $20–$50/LF |
| Per display fixture (floor display, set) | $50–$200/fixture |
| Per peg hook replacement | $0.50–$2.00/hook |

### Model 4: Program Management Fee
- **Used for:** Full-program oversight including scheduling, QC, reporting
- **Typical:** 8–15% of total labor cost or $5,000–$25,000/month program management fee
- **Includes:** Program manager, dashboard reporting, exception management, client communication

## Cost Build-Up: Reset Program Estimate

### Step 1: Store Survey / Scope Definition

Before pricing any program, you need:
- Store count and geographic distribution
- Average store SF and format
- Fixture count per store (gondola sections, end caps, displays)
- Work scope per store (hours estimated)
- Store access constraints (off-hours only? Fire code limits? Inventory restrictions?)
- Materials to be furnished (by whom? pre-kitted or field-assembled?)
- Reporting requirements

**Without a scope definition — any price is a guess.**

### Step 2: Labor Hours per Store

Estimate hours using one of:
- **Time study from pilot stores** (most accurate)
- **Historical data from similar programs**
- **Parametric estimate:** fixture count × minutes per fixture

**Parametric rules of thumb:**

| Task | Time |
|---|---|
| Standard gondola section reset (4-ft, 5 shelves) | 20–40 min |
| Full gondola section remove + replace | 45–90 min |
| End cap takedown and reset | 30–60 min |
| Floor display set (standard) | 15–30 min |
| New gondola section install (anchor, level, shelve) | 60–120 min |
| Signage swap (per sign location) | 3–8 min |
| Overhead signage (per location, scissor lift) | 15–30 min |

### Step 3: Travel & Logistics

Overnight travel significantly increases cost for dispersed programs:

| Scenario | Add to Labor Cost |
|---|---|
| Day travel only (local crew) | +15–25% |
| Overnight stay (hotel + per diem) | +$120–$200/person/night |
| Fly-in crew (regional hub to market) | +$300–$600/person/trip |
| Long-haul multi-market travel | $400–$800/person/week |

**Travel planning rule:** Cluster stores geographically to minimize travel cost. Dispatching crews inefficiently can double the effective labor cost.

### Step 4: Materials

| Material | Notes |
|---|---|
| Fixtures (gondola, displays) | Typically owner-furnished; GC coordinates receipt |
| Shelving hardware (clips, brackets) | $0.50–$3.00/unit |
| Signage / headers | Often owner-furnished; installation by crew |
| Label holders / price strips | $0.25–$1.50/LF |
| Consumables (zip ties, tape, fasteners) | $50–$200/store |
| Freight to stores | $200–$1,500/store depending on volume and distance |

### Step 5: Program Management & Overhead

| Item | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Program manager (dedicated) | $8,000–$18,000/month |
| Program management software / reporting | $500–$3,000/month |
| Quality control visits (% of stores) | $75–$200/store visited |
| Call center / store support | $1,000–$5,000/month |
| Contingency (scope variance) | 10–15% on first program; 5–8% on repeat |

## Estimating by Program Type

### New Store Set (Construction Hand-Off to Fixture Set)

When construction turns over a shell or vanilla box, the merchandising team sets fixtures before grand opening:

| Store Type | Hours per Store | Cost Range |
|---|---|---|
| Small format specialty (< 3,000 SF) | 40–80 hours | $3,000–$8,000 |
| Mid-size retail (3,000–10,000 SF) | 80–200 hours | $8,000–$20,000 |
| Large format (10,000–40,000 SF) | 200–600 hours | $20,000–$60,000 |
| Big box (> 60,000 SF) | 600–2,000+ hours | $60,000–$200,000 |

### Brand Refresh Program (Fleet-Wide)

Coordinated update across 50–500+ stores:

| Scope Level | Per Store Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Light (signage + minor display swap) | $500–$2,500 | No fixture moves |
| Moderate (section resets + new displays) | $2,500–$7,500 | Some fixture moves |
| Heavy (floor reflow + all new displays) | $7,500–$20,000 | Major labor investment |
| Full remodel support (construction + reset) | $15,000–$50,000+ | Hybrid construction/merch |

### Seasonal / Planogram Reset

Recurring resets tied to retail calendar:

| Frequency | Per-Store Annual Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|

| Bi-annual (2x/year) | $2,000–$8,000/reset | Fashion retail |
| Annual major reset | $3,000–$15,000/reset | Home goods, sporting goods |

## Program Management Best Practices (from a PM perspective)

1. **Pilot before rollout** — always run 3–5 pilot stores before committing to full program pricing
2. **Time studies are gold** — actual measured hours vs. estimated hours is the #1 predictor of program profitability
3. **Geographic clustering** — dispatch crews from hub cities to clusters; never criss-cross markets
4. **Materials must arrive before crew** — track fixture delivery confirmations store-by-store
5. **Exception management process** — when a store isn't ready, what happens? Cost impact and decision tree needed
6. **QC sampling** — spot-check 10–20% of stores; photograph before and after
7. **Reporting cadence** — daily completion reporting during execution; client scorecard weekly
8. **Contingency** — 10% minimum on first-time programs with an unfamiliar retailer

## Key Differences from Construction Estimating

| Factor | Construction | Merchandising |
|---|---|---|
| Permits required | Yes (most scopes) | No (pure merchandise work) |
| Licensed trades | Yes (MEP) | No |
| Prevailing wage | Sometimes | Rarely |
| Schedule driver | Permit, inspections, inspections | Store access, inventory |
| Primary cost driver | Materials + trade labor | Labor + travel |
| Contingency basis | Design completeness, site conditions | Scope variance, store conditions |
| Quality measure | Code compliance, inspections | Photographic documentation, planogram compliance |
| Contract type | GMP, lump sum, T&M | T&M, per-store, program fee |
