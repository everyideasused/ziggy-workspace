## The Pairing Strategy (Iron + Vitamin C)

**Critical for vegans:** Plant iron (non-heme) has 5-12% absorption vs 14-18% from meat. **Vitamin C increases absorption 3-4x.**

### Iron-Rich Plants (Grow These)
- Spinach
- Kale
- Collards
- Swiss chard
- Parsley
- Edamame
- Black-eyed peas
- Lima beans

### Vitamin C-Rich Plants (Plant NEXT to iron sources)
- Bell peppers (169% DV per cup)
- Broccoli (135% DV)
- Brussels sprouts (81% DV)
- Strawberries (89% DV)
- Tomatoes (27% DV)

### 🎯 Pairing Layout (per 4×4 section)
```
[Spinach] [Bell Pepper] [Spinach] [Bell Pepper]
[Kale]    [Tomato]      [Kale]    [Tomato]
```

## Seasonal Strategy for Continuous Micronutrients

### Spring (March-May)
- **Spinach, kale, collards** (overwintered + new plantings)
- **Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage**
- **Peas** (folate, fiber)
- **Radishes** (quick C vitamin)

### Summer (June-August)
- **Sweet peppers** (peak vitamin C)
- **Tomatoes** (lycopene, C)
- **Sweet potatoes** (growing, not harvesting)
- **Swiss chard** (heat-tolerant greens)
- **Okra** (folate, K)

### Fall (September-November)
- **Brussels sprouts** (sweetest after frost)
- **Kale/collards** (flavor improves with cold)
- **Winter squash** (A, fiber)
- **Second spinach crop**

### Winter (December-February)
- **Overwintered kale & collards** (zone 7a)
- **Garlic** (growing, not harvesting)
- **Cold-frame spinach** (with protection)
- **Sprouts/indoor greens** (backup)

## The Must-Supplement List (Non-Negotiable)

No garden can provide these:

1. **Vitamin B12** — Bacteria-derived, not in plants
   - *Dose:* 250 mcg daily OR 2500 mcg weekly
   - *Form:* Methylcobalamin or cyanocobalamin

2. **Vitamin D** — Sun-dependent, insufficient in winter
   - *Dose:* 1000-2000 IU D3 (vegan D3 from lichen)
   - *Oct-April:* Definitely supplement
   - *May-Sept:* 15 min midday sun may suffice

3. **EPA/DHA Omega-3** — ALA converts poorly (5-10%)
   - *Dose:* 250-500 mg combined EPA/DHA
   - *Source:* Algae-based supplements

4. **Iodine** — Soil-dependent, unreliable
   - *Dose:* 150 mcg
   - *Source:* Iodized salt or kelp supplement

## Testing Recommendations

Get baseline bloodwork before garden season, then yearly:

| Test | Why | Target |
|------|-----|--------|
| **Complete blood count (CBC)** | Anemia check | Normal hemoglobin |
| **Ferritin** | Iron stores | 50-150 ng/mL |
| **B12** | Deficiency common | >400 pg/mL |
| **Vitamin D (25-OH)** | Seasonal variation | 40-60 ng/mL |
| **Zinc** | Immune function | 90-120 mcg/dL |
