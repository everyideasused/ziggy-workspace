# 🥣 Vegan Sauces & Condiments Bible

> **Purpose:** Master reference for every foundational sauce, dressing, marinade, and condiment in vegan cooking. Each recipe is designed to be restaurant-quality, nutrient-dense, and serve as a building block for multiple dishes.

---

## The Philosophy: Sauces Are the Difference

The gap between a mediocre vegan bowl and a restaurant-quality vegan bowl is almost always the sauce. A great sauce transforms:
- Plain grains → satisfying meal
- Roasted vegetables → craving-worthy
- Simple tofu → memorable protein
- Basic salad → something you look forward to

**Build a sauce rotation of 10–15 that you make well. Rotate weekly. Everything else builds from there.**

## Foundation Dressings

### The Tahini Master Sauce

This is the single most versatile vegan sauce. Adjust thickness for any application.

```
BASE RATIO:
¼ cup tahini (well-stirred — oil may separate)
3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
1-2 tbsp water (add until desired consistency)
1 small garlic clove (raw) or ½ tsp garlic powder
½ tsp salt
Whisk or blend until smooth — it will seize then loosen

VARIATIONS:
Tahini Caesar: + 1 tsp dijon + nutritional yeast + capers + more lemon
Tahini Miso: + 1 tbsp white miso + 1 tsp rice vinegar + 1 tsp maple
Green Tahini: + large handful parsley/cilantro + blended until green
Spicy Tahini: + 1 tsp harissa or sriracha + smoked paprika
Tahini Ranch: + dill + chive + onion powder + plant milk to thin
```

**Uses:** Grain bowls, falafel wrap, salad dressing, roasted vegetable dip, noodle sauce (thin with water + tamari)

**Nutrition (per 2 tbsp):** ~100 cal | 3g protein | 8g fat | zinc, calcium

### Everyday Vinaigrette (Emulsified)

```
1 tbsp dijon mustard (emulsifier)
2 tbsp champagne vinegar or white wine vinegar
1 small shallot, minced
½ tsp maple syrup
¼ tsp salt + cracked pepper
6 tbsp EVOO

Method: Whisk everything except oil → slowly drizzle oil while whisking → fully emulsified
OR: Blend all in blender → silky, stable

Variations:
Lemon herb: Lemon juice + fresh thyme/tarragon + EVOO + dijon
Balsamic: Balsamic + dijon + garlic + EVOO
Sherry: Sherry vinegar + 1 tsp miso + EVOO + smoked paprika
```

---

### Miso-Ginger Dressing

```
3 tbsp white miso
2 tbsp rice vinegar
1 tbsp fresh grated ginger
1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
1 tbsp maple syrup
2 tbsp water
1 tbsp tamari
Blend or whisk until smooth
```

**Uses:** Asian grain bowls, shredded cabbage slaw, grilled eggplant, noodle salad

---

### Creamy Herb Dressing (Ranch-Style)

```
½ cup raw cashews (soaked 4 hrs)
½ cup plant milk (unsweetened)
2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
1 tsp dijon
1 garlic clove
2 tbsp fresh dill
2 tbsp fresh chives
1 tbsp fresh parsley
½ tsp onion powder
½ tsp salt
Blend until completely smooth
```

## Pasta Sauces

### Tomato Sauce (Perfect Pomodoro)

The secret: long, slow cooking with very few ingredients. Simple is hard.

```
INGREDIENTS:
1 can (28 oz) San Marzano whole tomatoes
3 tbsp EVOO
4 garlic cloves (not minced — smashed whole)
Pinch of red pepper flakes
Salt

METHOD:
Heat oil + garlic on LOW heat — infuse 5-8 min (garlic should turn golden, not brown)
Remove garlic (optional: leave in for stronger flavor)
Add whole tomatoes — crush by hand into pan
Simmer 25-30 min until slightly reduced — do NOT stir frequently; let it bubble gently
Season with salt
Finish with EVOO drizzle

SECRET: Never use high heat. Low and slow intensifies without burning.
This makes ~2 cups of exceptional sauce.
```

### Lentil Bolognese

```
AROMATICS BASE (soffritto):
Onion + carrot + celery (2:1:1) — 20 min low heat in EVOO + salt

ADD:
3 garlic cloves, minced → 1 min
2 tbsp tomato paste → cook 3 min (caramelizes)
1 cup dry red wine → simmer until absorbed
1 cup dried green or brown lentils
2 cups canned San Marzano tomatoes
1 cup kombu or shiitake broth
½ oz dried porcini mushrooms (rehydrated; add soaking liquid)
2 sprigs fresh rosemary + 2 bay leaves

SIMMER: 30-35 min until lentils are tender and sauce is thick
FINISH: Nutritional yeast + EVOO + cashew parmesan + black pepper
Protein/serving: ~22g (lentils) + pasta protein
```

---

### Cashew Béchamel (White Sauce)

```
1 cup raw cashews (soaked + rinsed)
2 cups vegetable broth (warm)
2 tbsp nutritional yeast
1 tbsp lemon juice
1 tsp garlic powder
1 tsp onion powder
½ tsp white pepper
Salt to taste
Optional: pinch nutmeg

Blend on HIGH 3-4 minutes until completely smooth → heat gently in saucepan

Uses: Lasagna, gratin, moussaka, pasta bake, mac and cheese base
```

---

### Cashew Alfredo

```
As above béchamel base +
More nutritional yeast (3-4 tbsp)
1 tbsp white miso
2 tsp dijon
Fresh lemon juice
Reduce broth → thicker consistency

Toss with fettuccine → thin with pasta water as needed
Finish with cashew parmesan + cracked pepper + EVOO
```

## Asian Sauces

### Peanut Sauce

```
½ cup natural peanut butter (no added oil/sugar)
3 tbsp tamari
3 tbsp lime juice
2 tbsp maple syrup or coconut sugar
1 tbsp rice vinegar
1 tbsp fresh grated ginger
1 garlic clove (raw or roasted)
Chili flakes or sriracha to taste
¼-½ cup warm water (adjust consistency)
Blend until silky smooth

Uses: Satay, noodle sauce, spring rolls, dipping sauce, grain bowl drizzle
```

---

### Teriyaki Sauce

```
½ cup tamari
¼ cup mirin
3 tbsp sake or dry white wine
2 tbsp maple syrup or sugar
1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
1 garlic clove, minced
1 tsp cornstarch + 1 tbsp cold water (slurry for thickening)

Simmer tamari + mirin + sake + sweetener 3-5 min
Add slurry → cook 1-2 min until glossy
Add ginger + garlic off heat

VARIATION — Korean BBQ sauce:
As above + 2 tbsp gochujang + 1 tbsp sesame oil + sesame seeds
```

---

### Gochujang Glaze

```
3 tbsp gochujang
2 tbsp tamari
1 tbsp maple syrup
1 tbsp rice vinegar
1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
1 garlic clove (minced or grated)
1 tsp fresh ginger
1 tbsp water

Whisk → use as marinade, glaze for roasted vegetables, tempeh, cauliflower
```

---

### General Tso's Sauce (Vegan)

```
¼ cup tamari
¼ cup rice vinegar
3 tbsp maple syrup
2 tbsp hoisin
1 tbsp cornstarch
1 tsp toasted sesame oil
4 garlic cloves, minced
1 tbsp fresh ginger
½ tsp red pepper flakes

Combine → cook in wok after frying protein → toss to coat
```

## Middle Eastern / Mediterranean Sauces

### Harissa (North African Chili Paste)

```
6-8 dried red chilies (ancho + guajillo base) — soaked
2 roasted red peppers
4 garlic cloves
1 tsp cumin seeds (toasted)
1 tsp coriander seeds (toasted)
½ tsp caraway seeds (toasted)
3 tbsp EVOO
1 tbsp tomato paste
1 tsp smoked paprika
Salt + lemon juice

Blend to paste — add EVOO slowly for body
```

**Uses:** Grain bowls, roasted vegetables, shakshuka, pasta sauce, yogurt dip

---

### Chermoula

```
Large bunch cilantro + parsley
4 garlic cloves
1 tsp cumin
1 tsp paprika
½ tsp turmeric
Pinch cayenne
Juice of 1 lemon
3 tbsp EVOO
Salt

Blend to coarse herb paste

Uses: Moroccan marinade for grilled vegetables, dipping sauce, rice topping
```

---

### Baba Ganoush

```
2 large eggplants
3 garlic cloves (roasted in skin with eggplant)
3 tbsp tahini
2 tbsp lemon juice
2 tbsp EVOO
½ tsp cumin
Salt + smoked paprika to finish

METHOD:
Char eggplants directly over gas flame (or broil) until completely collapsed and charred
The char is the flavor — do not undercook
Scoop out flesh → drain in colander 15 min
Mix with all other ingredients — do not over-process (should be slightly chunky)
```

## American / BBQ Sauces

### Smoky BBQ Sauce

```
1 cup tomato sauce or ketchup
¼ cup apple cider vinegar
3 tbsp maple syrup
2 tbsp molasses (blackstrap — adds iron + depth)
1 tbsp tamari
1 tbsp dijon mustard
2 tsp smoked paprika
1 tsp garlic powder
1 tsp onion powder
½ tsp chipotle powder (or adobo sauce)
½ tsp black pepper

Simmer all together 15-20 min until slightly reduced and glossy
```

**Nutrition bonus:** Blackstrap molasses adds meaningful iron, calcium, and B6.

---

### Buffalo Sauce (Vegan)

```
½ cup Frank's RedHot (or any vinegar-based hot sauce)
¼ cup vegan butter (Miyoko's)
1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
1 tsp garlic powder
Pinch of salt

Melt butter → whisk in hot sauce + vinegar + garlic off heat → emulsified sauce
```

## Compound Sauces (Advanced)

### Romesco (Catalan Nut + Pepper Sauce)

```
2 roasted red peppers
1 cup tomatoes (roasted or canned)
½ cup almonds or hazelnuts (toasted)
3-4 garlic cloves (roasted)
2 tbsp sherry vinegar
1 tsp smoked paprika
¼ tsp cayenne
4 tbsp EVOO
Salt

Blend to slightly chunky texture (not smooth)
Use: Pasta, grilled vegetables, grain bowl, spread on bread
Nutritional bonus: Almonds add protein + vitamin E
```

---

### Mole Negro (Simplified 30-min Version)

```
4 dried ancho chilies (soaked + deseeded)
2 dried guajillo (soaked + deseeded)
1 charred tomato
1 charred tomatillo
3 garlic cloves (charred)
½ onion (charred)
2 tbsp pepitas (toasted)
1 tbsp sesame seeds (toasted)
1 corn tortilla (toasted) — thickener
30g dark chocolate (70%+)
1 tsp cumin
1 tsp coriander
½ tsp cinnamon
1 dried chipotle
2 cups vegetable broth

Blend all (except chocolate) → strain → cook in oil 5 min → add broth → simmer 20 min
Add chocolate → melt → adjust salt + consistency
```
