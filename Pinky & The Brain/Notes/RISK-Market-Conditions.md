| PVC conduit | Market-normalized | Resin prices | Stable |
| Natural gas | Regional; volatile | Weather; LNG exports | Variable |

### Tariff Watch (2025)

Current and proposed tariffs affecting construction:
- **Steel (Section 232):** 25% tariff on imported steel; domestic supply tightened; elevated base prices
- **Aluminum (Section 232):** 10% tariff; affects storefront, curtain wall, structural aluminum
- **Canadian lumber (softwood):** ~14% tariff; softwood framing lumber affected
- **Chinese goods (Section 301):** Affects electrical components, fixtures, HVAC equipment
- **Watch:** Any expansion of tariffs to additional goods will spike material costs rapidly

**Estimator action:** For projects with hard bid dates > 6 months out, include a material escalation contingency of 3–5% on steel, copper, and aluminum-heavy scopes.

## Subcontractor Market Intelligence

### Signs of a Bidder's Market (you have leverage)
- 4+ responsive bids per trade
- Bids coming in 5–10% below budget
- Subs calling asking if you have work
- Short lead times quoted
- GCs reducing margins

### Signs of a Seller's Market (GC/sub has leverage)
- 1–2 bids per trade (or no bids)
- Bids coming in 10–20% over budget
- Subs not responding to bid invites
- Long lead times; not holding prices
- GCs holding margins firm

**Current condition (2024–2025):** Mixed. Most markets are moderate-to-seller's market for MEP trades; more competitive for general construction.

## Market Intelligence Maintenance Protocol

Update this file quarterly by:
1. Checking 2–3 subcontractors per major trade for current pricing and availability
2. Reviewing ENR (Engineering News-Record) Construction Cost Index
3. Reviewing RSMeans quarterly update (if subscription active)
4. Checking material supplier quotes for major commodities
5. Reviewing utility transformer lead time with regional utility contacts
6. Updating lead time table with actual recent order data

**Update trigger events (update immediately):**
- Major tariff announcement
- Significant weather event (hurricane, flooding) affecting lumber/materials
- Major supply chain disruption (port strike, factory fire)
- Fed rate change (affects GC financing and project pipeline)
- Large regional project announcement (new stadium, major hospital, etc.) that will absorb local sub capacity
