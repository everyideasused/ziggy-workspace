# KNOWLEDGE BASE MODULE 07: INSURANCE & FINANCIAL PRODUCTS

## THE INSURANCE PRINCIPLE
Insurance is for catastrophic, low-probability events you can't self-fund. Every other "insurance" product is profit margin for the seller. This module helps you buy what you need and skip what you don't.

---

## AUTO INSURANCE

### How to Save (The System)
1. **Shop every 12 months**: Get 3-5 quotes at each renewal. Loyalty does NOT pay — insurers raise rates on retained customers.
2. **Comparison tools**: The Zebra, Policygenius, Gabi, Jerry — compare multiple carriers in minutes.
3. **Bundle home + auto**: 5-25% discount at most carriers.
4. **Raise deductible**: $500 → $1,000 saves 15-30% on premium. Keep the deductible amount in savings.
5. **Drop collision/comprehensive**: If your car is worth less than $5,000, the premium may exceed potential payout. Self-insure.
6. **Usage-based / telematics**: Safe, low-mileage drivers save 10-30% with State Farm Drive Safe, Progressive Snapshot, etc.
7. **Ask for every discount**: Multi-car, good driver, low mileage, defensive driving course, good student, military, professional organization, paperless, autopay, anti-theft device.
8. **Pay in full**: Insurers charge installment fees. Pay 6-month or annual premium in full for 5-10% savings.
9. **Credit score matters**: In most states, credit-based insurance score affects premium. Improving credit = lower insurance.

### Coverage You Need
- **Liability**: Minimum state requirements are often too low. Aim for 100/300/100 ($100K per person / $300K per accident bodily injury / $100K property damage).
- **Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist**: Essential. Many drivers are underinsured. Covers you if they hit you.
- **Umbrella policy**: $1M umbrella for $150-300/year. Covers above auto AND home liability. Anyone with meaningful assets should carry this.

## HOME / RENTERS INSURANCE

### Homeowners Insurance
- **Shop annually**: Same principle as auto. Quotes from 3-5 carriers.
- **Bundle with auto**: Discount applies both ways.
- **Replacement cost vs. actual cash value**: Always choose replacement cost. ACV = depreciated value = you get far less at claim time.
- **Increase deductible**: $1,000-2,500 deductible saves significantly on premium.
- **Home improvements that reduce premium**: New roof, updated electrical/plumbing, security system, storm shutters.
- **Don't over-insure**: Insure the dwelling replacement cost, not the property value (land doesn't burn down).
- **Flood insurance**: Standard homeowners does NOT cover flooding. If you're in or near a flood zone, purchase separately through FEMA NFIP or private flood insurance.
- **Inventory your possessions**: Video walkthrough of every room, stored in cloud. Critical for claims. Update annually.

### Renters Insurance
- **Costs**: $15-30/month for $20K-50K in personal property coverage.
- **Criminally underutilized**: Only ~55% of renters carry it. It covers theft, fire, water damage, liability.
- **Bundling**: Bundle with auto for additional discount.
- **Liability coverage**: Renters insurance includes $100K-300K liability. Protects if someone is injured in your unit.
- **Replacement cost rider**: Add this. Standard policy uses ACV (depreciated). Replacement cost pays for new equivalent.

## LIFE INSURANCE

### Who Needs It
- Anyone with financial dependents (spouse, children, elderly parents you support)
- Anyone with shared debt (mortgage, co-signed loans)
- Business owners with partners (buy-sell agreements)

### Who Doesn't Need It
- Single people with no dependents
- People with sufficient assets to self-fund survivor needs
- Children (life insurance on children is almost never necessary)

### Term Life vs. Whole Life
- **Term life**: Pure insurance. Pay premium, get coverage for a set term (10, 20, 30 years). If you die during the term, beneficiaries receive the death benefit. DRAMATICALLY cheaper than whole life. This is what 90%+ of people should buy.
- **Whole life / universal life / variable life**: Insurance + investment component. Expensive, complex, low returns on the investment portion. High commissions for agents (which is why they push it). Almost never the right choice for average consumers.
- **Rule of thumb**: Buy term life for 10-12x annual income. Invest the difference between term and whole life premiums in index funds. You'll come out ahead.

### Where to Buy
- **Online term life**: Bestow, Ladder, Haven Life — simplified underwriting, competitive rates.
- **Comparison**: Policygenius, SelectQuote, Zander Insurance — shop across carriers.
- **Through employer**: Group life (1-2x salary) is often free or cheap. But don't rely on it solely — it ends when you leave the job.

## HEALTH INSURANCE OPTIMIZATION

### HSA (Health Savings Account) — The Triple Tax Advantage
If you have a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP):
- **Contributions are tax-deductible** (reduces taxable income)
- **Growth is tax-free** (invest HSA funds like a retirement account)
- **Withdrawals for medical expenses are tax-free**
- **2024 limits**: $4,150 individual / $8,300 family (+ $1,000 catch-up if 55+)
- **Strategy**: If you can afford to, pay medical expenses out-of-pocket and let HSA funds grow invested. This creates a tax-free medical fund for retirement.
- **Best HSA providers**: Fidelity (no fees, excellent investment options), Lively, HSA Bank.

### FSA (Flexible Spending Account)
- **Use-it-or-lose-it**: Most FSAs expire at year-end (some have grace period or $610 rollover). Plan spending carefully.
- **Best uses**: Predictable annual medical expenses — prescriptions, glasses/contacts, dental work, copays.
- **Dependent Care FSA**: Up to $5,000/year for childcare expenses, pre-tax. If you pay for daycare, this is an immediate tax savings of $1,000-2,000+.

### Prescription Savings (Within Insurance)
- **90-day supply**: Always get 90-day for maintenance medications. Lower copay per day.
- **Mail-order pharmacy**: Most insurance plans have mail-order option at lower copay.
- **Step therapy / prior authorization**: If insurer denies a medication, ask your doctor about alternatives on your formulary, or file an appeal.
- **Manufacturer copay cards**: For branded medications, manufacturers often offer copay assistance cards that reduce your out-of-pocket to $0-30/month regardless of insurance.

## EXTENDED WARRANTIES: THE DECISION FRAMEWORK

### When Extended Warranties Are Worth It
- **Appliances with known reliability issues**: Certain refrigerator ice makers, dishwasher components. Check Consumer Reports reliability data.
- **Laptops / phones**: If you're accident-prone. AppleCare+ covers accidental damage. Worth it for phones you'll use 3+ years.
- **Vehicles outside factory warranty**: Third-party warranty (Endurance, CARCHEX) on reliable brands with 60-100K miles. Better than dealer-offered warranties at 50-60% the price.

### When Extended Warranties Are NOT Worth It
- **TVs**: Failure rate in first 5 years is very low for quality brands. Self-insure.
- **Small appliances**: Replace rather than repair. The warranty often costs 30-50% of replacement price.
- **Anything with redundant credit card coverage**: Many credit cards extend manufacturer warranty by 1-2 years for free.
- **New vehicles from reliable brands**: Toyota/Honda with factory 3-year/36K + 5-year/60K powertrain. Extended warranty costs more than expected repairs.

### Credit Card Extended Warranty (Free Coverage You're Probably Ignoring)
Many credit cards automatically extend the manufacturer's warranty by 1-2 years:
- **Chase**: 1 additional year (Sapphire, Freedom, various)
- **Citi**: 2 additional years (Double Cash, Premier)
- **Amex**: 1-2 additional years (varies by card)
- **Capital One**: 1 additional year (Venture, Quicksilver)
- **How it works**: Purchase item on card → manufacturer warranty expires → card benefit extends coverage → file claim with card issuer if item fails.
- **Keep receipts**: Required for claims. Store digitally.

| Everything else | Citi Double Cash (2%), Fidelity Visa (2%), Wells Fargo Active Cash (2%) | 2% |

### The Annual Fee Calculation
A card with $95 annual fee that earns 3% on $20,000/year of spending = $600 in rewards - $95 fee = $505 net.
A no-fee card earning 2% on that same spending = $400.
**The fee card wins by $105.** Always calculate net reward after fees.

### Sign-Up Bonus Strategy
- Credit card sign-up bonuses are the single most lucrative rewards opportunity.
- Chase Sapphire Preferred: 60-80K points after $4K spend in 3 months = $750-1,000+ in travel value.
- Amex Gold: 60K MR after $6K spend = $600-1,200 in value depending on redemption.
- **Strategy**: Apply for cards strategically when you have planned large spending (home improvement, travel, etc.) that naturally meets minimum spend requirements.
- **Chase 5/24 rule**: Chase denies applications if you've opened 5+ cards in 24 months. Plan Chase applications first.

## WARRANTY & PURCHASE PROTECTION STRATEGIES

### Credit Card Purchase Protection
Many premium cards offer 90-120 day purchase protection against damage or theft:
- **Chase Sapphire**: $500/claim, $50K/year
- **Amex (various)**: $1,000-10,000/claim depending on card
- **Citi**: Varies by card
- This is free insurance on everything you buy with the card. Use it.

### Return Protection
Some cards allow returns when retailers won't accept them:
- **Amex Return Protection**: Refund up to $300/item within 90 days if retailer refuses return
- This benefit is being reduced/eliminated on some cards — verify your current card terms.

## FINANCIAL PRODUCTS TO AVOID

### High-Cost Products That Rarely Benefit Consumers
- **Whole life insurance** (for most people): High commission, low return, complex. Buy term, invest the difference.
- **Mortgage life insurance**: Expensive declining-balance coverage. A standard term life policy is cheaper and more flexible.
- **Credit insurance** (credit card payment protection): Expensive, limited coverage. Build an emergency fund instead.
- **Debit card overdraft protection**: Essentially a high-interest short-term loan. Opt out and monitor balances.
- **Store credit cards** (without clear strategy): High interest rates (25-30% APR). Only use if: paying in full every month AND the rewards/discount structure is genuinely superior for that store.
- **Payday loans / title loans**: Predatory. APRs of 300-600%+. Avoid at all costs.
- **Dealer-installed add-ons**: VIN etching, fabric protection, paint protection, nitrogen in tires — all massive markup on near-zero value. Decline everything at the F&I desk.

## GOLDEN RULES: INSURANCE & FINANCIAL PRODUCTS

1. **Shop insurance annually.** Loyalty costs money. Get 3-5 quotes.
2. **Buy term life, invest the difference.** Whole life benefits the agent, not you.
3. **HSA is the best tax-advantaged account available.** Max it out if you have an HDHP.
4. **Credit card extended warranty is free.** Use it before buying separate extended warranties.
5. **Raise deductibles, lower premiums.** Keep deductible amount in savings.
6. **Umbrella policy ($1M for $150-300/year)** is the best insurance value available.
7. **Bundle home + auto** for 5-25% savings.
8. **Never buy extended warranties at point of sale.** If you need one, buy third-party for less.
9. **Use the right credit card for every category.** The difference between 1% and 5% on $50,000/year of spending = $2,000.
10. **Read your credit card benefits.** Purchase protection, trip insurance, rental car insurance, extended warranty — you're paying for these through the annual fee. Use them.
