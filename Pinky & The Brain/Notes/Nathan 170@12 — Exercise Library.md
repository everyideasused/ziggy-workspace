## Kettlebell Movements

### Goblet Squat

**Purpose:** Quad/glute strength, squat pattern mastery, core stability

**Setup:**
- Hold kettlebell by horns (handle down) at chest height
- Feet shoulder-width or slightly wider
- Toes pointed slightly out (10-15°)

**Execution:**
1. Take deep breath, brace core
2. Break at knees and hips simultaneously
3. Descend until hip crease is below knee (full depth)
4. Elbows stay inside knees (use them to push knees out)
5. Chest stays upright, eyes forward
6. Drive through full foot (heels + balls)
7. Stand fully, squeeze glutes at top

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Heels lifting (weight on toes)
- ❌ Knees caving inward
- ❌ Rounding lower back
- ❌ Not reaching full depth

**Cues:**
- "Sit between your legs"
- "Spread the floor apart with your feet"
- "Proud chest"
- "Elbows push knees out"

**Video Reference:** [Pavel Goblet Squat Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qa0hCz3gHnI)

### Kettlebell Swing

**Purpose:** Hip power, posterior chain (glutes/hams), cardiovascular conditioning

**Setup:**
- Kettlebell 12-18 inches in front of you
- Feet shoulder-width, toes forward
- Hinge to grip handle with both hands

**Execution:**
1. Hike kettlebell back between legs (like hiking a football)
2. Explosive hip snap forward (NOT a squat)
3. Kettlebell floats to shoulder height (arms are ropes)
4. Stand tall at top, glutes squeezed
5. Let kettlebell fall, catch with hinge (not squat)
6. Repeat immediately

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Squatting instead of hinging
- ❌ Using arms to lift (should be hip power)
- ❌ Rounding lower back
- ❌ Swinging above head (this is Russian swing, not American)

**Cues:**
- "Hinge, don't squat"
- "Pop the hips"
- "Arms are ropes"
- "Float, don't lift"
- "Tame the arc"

**Video Reference:** [StrongFirst Swing Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YSxHifyI6s8)

### Kettlebell Row (Single-Arm)

**Purpose:** Lat strength, scapular control, pulling pattern, postural muscles

**Setup:**
- Opposite hand and knee on bench (tripod position)
- Free hand holds kettlebell, hanging straight down
- Back flat (neutral spine)

**Execution:**
1. Brace core, maintain flat back
2. Pull kettlebell to hip (elbow close to body)
3. Squeeze shoulder blade back and down at top
4. Hold 1 second
5. Lower with control to full stretch

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Rotating torso (stay square)
- ❌ Shrugging shoulder up
- ❌ Using momentum/swinging
- ❌ Rowing to chest instead of hip

**Cues:**
- "Pull elbow to hip"
- "Squeeze shoulder blade back"
- "Stay square"
- "Control the descent"

**Video Reference:** [T-Nation KB Row](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7xGRN4u-E4Y)

### Kettlebell Overhead Press

**Purpose:** Shoulder strength, overhead stability, unilateral pressing power

**Setup:**
- Kettlebell in rack position (resting on forearm, handle across palm)
- Elbow close to ribs
- Wrist straight (not bent back)
- Feet shoulder-width, core braced

**Execution:**
1. Take breath, brace core
2. Press straight up (kettlebell path close to face)
3. Lock out elbow completely (bicep by ear)
4. Shrug shoulder slightly at top (active shoulder)
5. Lower with control back to rack position

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Leaning back excessively
- ❌ Pressing out (not up)
- ❌ Bent wrist at lockout
- ❌ Not fully locking out elbow

**Cues:**
- "Press up, not forward"
- "Bicep by ear at top"
- "Tight belly"
- "Lockout hard"

**Video Reference:** [StrongFirst Press](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6LVCw95A2wY)

### Kettlebell Clean

**Purpose:** Power development, transition movement for complexes, full-body coordination

**Setup:**
- Kettlebell on ground between feet
- Feet shoulder-width
- Hinge to grip handle with one hand

**Execution:**
1. Hike kettlebell back between legs (like swing)
2. Explosive hip snap
3. As kettlebell rises, pull elbow back and high
4. Rotate hand around kettlebell (insert hand into handle)
5. Catch in rack position (soft landing on forearm)
6. Stand tall

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Banging forearm (kettlebell flips too hard)
- ❌ Catching too high (should land in rack)
- ❌ Death grip (relax hand during rotation)
- ❌ Arm pull instead of hip power

**Cues:**
- "Hip snap first"
- "High pull"
- "Insert hand"
- "Soft landing"

**Video Reference:** [Kettlebell Clean Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qFONIv7k-Zw)

### Kettlebell Deadlift

**Purpose:** Hip hinge mastery, posterior chain strength, foundational movement

**Setup:**
- Kettlebell on ground between feet
- Feet hip-width
- Shins vertical (don't bring knees forward)

**Execution:**
1. Hinge at hips, reach down to grip handle
2. Pull slack out (engage lats, straighten arms)
3. Chest up, shoulders back
4. Drive through heels, extend hips
5. Stand tall, squeeze glutes
6. Reverse movement with control

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Rounding lower back
- ❌ Squatting instead of hinging
- ❌ Jerking weight off ground
- ❌ Not fully extending hips at top

**Cues:**
- "Push the floor away"
- "Hinge, don't squat"
- "Chest up"
- "Squeeze glutes hard at top"

**Video Reference:** [Deadlift Form](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JgPNhVg1FqI)

### Farmer Carry (Single-Arm)

**Purpose:** Grip strength, core stability, anti-lateral flexion, postural endurance

**Setup:**
- Pick up kettlebell with one hand (deadlift)
- Stand tall, shoulders back
- Walk with perfect posture

**Execution:**
1. Grip kettlebell HARD
2. Keep shoulders level (don't lean away from weight)
3. Core braced (resist side-bending)
4. Walk with normal gait
5. Breathe normally

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Leaning away from weight
- ❌ Shrugging shoulder up
- ❌ Holding breath
- ❌ Rushing (focus on quality)

**Cues:**
- "Stand tall"
- "Squeeze the handle"
- "Stay level"
- "Proud posture"

**Video Reference:** [Farmer Carry Tips](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xF7kVr4FUzQ)

## Calisthenics Movements

### Push-up (Full / Knee / Incline)

**Purpose:** Chest, triceps, anterior shoulder strength, core stability

**Setup (Full Push-up):**
- Hands shoulder-width, directly under shoulders
- Feet together, body in straight line (plank)
- Core braced, glutes engaged

**Execution:**
1. Lower chest to floor (or close to it)
2. Elbows at 45° from body (not flared out)
3. Maintain plank (don't sag hips or pike)
4. Press up explosively
5. Lock out at top

**Progressions:**
1. **Incline push-up:** Hands on bench/box (easier)
2. **Knee push-up:** Knees on ground (easier)
3. **Full push-up:** Standard
4. **Feet elevated:** Feet on bench (harder)
5. **Weighted vest:** Add external load (harder)

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Sagging hips (banana back)
- ❌ Piking hips (inverted V)
- ❌ Elbows flaring out 90°
- ❌ Not reaching full depth

**Cues:**
- "Plank position"
- "Elbows 45°"
- "Chest to floor"
- "Push the floor away"

**Video Reference:** [Perfect Push-up](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IODxDxX7oi4)

### Pull-up (Strict / Negative / Band-Assisted)

**Purpose:** Lat strength, biceps, grip, back thickness

**Setup:**
- Grip bar shoulder-width (or slightly wider)
- Dead hang with arms fully extended
- Shoulders engaged (don't relax completely)

**Execution (Strict Pull-up):**
1. Pull elbows down toward hips
2. Lead with chest (not chin)
3. Pull until chin clears bar
4. Lower with control to dead hang
5. Repeat

**Progressions:**
1. **Dead hang:** Build grip + shoulder stability
2. **Scapular pull-up:** Shoulder blade movement only
3. **Negative pull-up:** Jump to top, lower 5 seconds
4. **Band-assisted pull-up:** Resistance band reduces bodyweight
5. **Strict pull-up:** Full bodyweight

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Kipping/swinging
- ❌ Not reaching full lockout at bottom
- ❌ Chin not clearing bar
- ❌ Hunched shoulders

**Cues:**
- "Pull elbows down"
- "Chest to bar"
- "Full lockout"
- "Control descent"

**Video Reference:** [Pull-up Progression](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eGo4IYlbE5g)

### Bulgarian Split Squat

**Purpose:** Single-leg strength, quad/glute development, balance, hip mobility

**Setup:**
- Rear foot on bench/box (12-18 inches high)
- Front foot 2-3 feet in front of bench
- Torso upright

**Execution:**
1. Lower rear knee toward ground
2. Front shin stays vertical (knee doesn't pass toes excessively)
3. Descend until rear knee nearly touches ground
4. Drive through front heel to stand
5. Keep torso upright (don't lean forward)

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Front knee caving inward
- ❌ Leaning forward too much
- ❌ Not descending deep enough
- ❌ Pushing through front toes (should be heel)

**Cues:**
- "Vertical shin"
- "Drive through heel"
- "Chest up"
- "Knee tracks toes"

**Video Reference:** [Bulgarian Split Squat](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2C-uNgKwPLE)

### Single-Leg Deadlift

**Purpose:** Balance, hamstring/glute strength, hip stability, proprioception

**Setup:**
- Stand on one leg, slight knee bend
- Opposite leg will extend behind
- Weight in same-side hand (or opposite for balance challenge)

**Execution:**
1. Hinge at hip, reach weight toward floor
2. Back leg extends behind (creates straight line from head to heel)
3. Keep hips level (don't rotate)
4. Reach as low as mobility allows
5. Squeeze glute to return to standing

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Rotating hips open
- ❌ Rounding lower back
- ❌ Not extending back leg
- ❌ Losing balance (use wall for support if needed)

**Cues:**
- "Hinge at hip"
- "Back leg straight"
- "Hips stay square"
- "Squeeze glute to stand"

**Video Reference:** [Single-Leg RDL](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YADPGvU1JTs)

### Pike Push-up

**Purpose:** Shoulder strength, handstand preparation, pressing pattern variation

**Setup:**
- Hands shoulder-width on ground
- Hips high, creating inverted V shape
- Feet together or hip-width

**Execution:**
1. Lower head toward floor between hands
2. Elbows point back (not out to sides)
3. Lower until head nearly touches floor
4. Press back up to start
5. Maintain pike position throughout

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Elbows flaring out
- ❌ Not lowering head deep enough
- ❌ Walking feet forward (maintains difficulty)
- ❌ Sagging hips

**Cues:**
- "Point elbows back"
- "Head between hands"
- "High hips"
- "Vertical press"

**Video Reference:** [Pike Push-up Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x3ITjHYCBDc)

## Core & Mobility

### Plank

**Purpose:** Core stability, anti-extension strength, full-body tension

**Setup:**
- Forearms on ground, elbows under shoulders
- Feet together (or hip-width for easier)
- Body in straight line

**Execution:**
1. Brace core HARD
2. Squeeze glutes
3. Neutral neck (look at floor 1 foot ahead)
4. Breathe normally (don't hold breath)
5. Hold for prescribed time

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Sagging hips
- ❌ Piking hips
- ❌ Holding breath
- ❌ Shrugging shoulders

**Cues:**
- "Straight line"
- "Squeeze glutes"
- "Brace like someone's about to punch you"
- "Breathe"

---

### Dead Bug

**Purpose:** Core stability, coordination, anti-extension, breathing under tension

**Setup:**
- Lie on back
- Arms straight up toward ceiling
- Knees bent 90°, shins parallel to floor

**Execution:**
1. Press lower back into floor (engage core)
2. Extend opposite arm and leg simultaneously
3. Hover limbs 1-2 inches off floor
4. Return to start, switch sides
5. Breathe throughout (don't hold breath)

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Lower back arching off floor
- ❌ Moving too fast (control is key)
- ❌ Not fully extending limbs
- ❌ Holding breath

**Cues:**
- "Lower back glued to floor"
- "Move slowly"
- "Opposite arm/leg"
- "Exhale as you extend"

---

### Russian Twist

**Purpose:** Rotational core strength, oblique development

**Setup:**
- Sit with knees bent, feet on floor (or elevated for harder)
- Lean back slightly (45°)
- Hold kettlebell at chest

**Execution:**
1. Rotate torso, touch kettlebell to floor on one side
2. Rotate to opposite side, touch floor
3. Keep core braced throughout
4. Don't just move arms (rotate torso)

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ Only moving arms (no torso rotation)
- ❌ Rounding lower back excessively
- ❌ Feet lifting off floor (if on ground)
- ❌ Rushing (control the movement)

**Cues:**
- "Rotate from core"
- "Touch floor each side"
- "Controlled movement"
- "Breathe"

## Conditioning & Skill Work

### Running Form

**Posture:**
- Slight forward lean from ankles (not waist)
- Shoulders relaxed, not hunched
- Look ahead 10-20 feet (not down)

**Arm Swing:**
- Elbows bent 90°
- Swing from shoulder (not elbow)
- Hands relaxed (don't clench fists)
- Forward-back motion (not across body)

**Foot Strike:**
- Midfoot strike preferred (not heel)
- Land under your center of mass
- Quick cadence (170-180 steps/min)
- Quiet footsteps (soft landing)

**Breathing:**
- Nose breathing during easy efforts
- Mouth breathing during hard efforts
- Rhythmic pattern (e.g., 3 steps in, 2 steps out)

**Video Reference:** [Running Form Basics](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=brFHyOtTwH)

---

### Scapular Pull-up

**Purpose:** Shoulder strength, pull-up foundation, scapular control

**Setup:**
- Dead hang from pull-up bar
- Arms fully extended

**Execution:**
1. Without bending elbows, pull shoulder blades down and together
2. Your body will rise 1-2 inches
3. Hold top position 2 seconds
4. Lower with control
5. Repeat

**Cues:**
- "No elbow bend"
- "Shoulders to back pockets"
- "Small movement"

**Video Reference:** [Scapular Pull-up](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FgYoc4O-cio)

---

### Negative Pull-up

**Purpose:** Build strength for full pull-ups, eccentric loading

**Setup:**
- Jump or step to top position (chin over bar)
- Full grip on bar

**Execution:**
1. Start with chin over bar
2. Lower yourself slowly (5 seconds minimum)
3. Full arm extension at bottom
4. Step down or jump back up
5. Repeat

**Cues:**
- "Count to 5"
- "Control the descent"
- "Full lockout"

**Video Reference:** [Negative Pull-up Progression](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iMPq7VHoQZ8)

## Form Check Resources

**Film yourself:**
- Set phone at side angle for squats, deadlifts, swings
- Set phone at front angle for presses, push-ups
- Compare your form to video references
- Look for common mistakes

**When to deload / regress:**
- Form breaks down mid-set
- Pain (not soreness) during movement
- Can't complete prescribed reps
- Movement feels unstable

**Progression principles:**
- Master bodyweight before adding load
- Master light load before progressing weight
- Add reps before adding weight
- Perfect 12 reps before increasing load
