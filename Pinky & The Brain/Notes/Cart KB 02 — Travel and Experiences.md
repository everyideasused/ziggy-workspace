# KNOWLEDGE BASE MODULE 02: TRAVEL & EXPERIENCES

## WHY THIS MODULE MATTERS
Travel is the highest potential savings per transaction of any category. A $3,000 vacation optimized to $1,800 = $1,200 saved on a single purchase decision. Multiply across annual travel and the impact is enormous.

---

## FLIGHTS: THE COMPLETE SYSTEM

### When to Book (The Science)
- **Domestic flights**: Book 1-3 months before departure for lowest fares. Sweet spot: 6-8 weeks out.
- **International flights**: Book 2-8 months out. Sweet spot: 3-5 months for economy, 6-8 months for business/first.
- **Last-minute flights**: Rarely cheap (exception: error fares, flash sales). The "last minute deal" is largely a myth for air.
- **Day of week to book**: Tuesday-Wednesday historically slightly cheaper, but the gap has narrowed. More important: be flexible on departure day.
- **Cheapest days to FLY**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Saturday. Most expensive: Friday, Sunday.
- **Time of day**: Early morning (6am) and late night (red-eye) flights = cheapest. Midday/evening = premium.

### Price Tracking Tools
- **Google Flights**: Best overall flight search. Price tracking, calendar view (shows cheapest days), "Explore" for open-destination search. Set fare alerts.
- **Skiplagged**: Hidden city ticketing — book through a connecting city that's cheaper. Example: NYC→Dallas with stop in Nashville. Exit in Nashville, skip Dallas leg. Risk: airlines can penalize. Never check bags on skiplagged routes.
- **Hopper**: Mobile app. Predicts whether prices will rise or fall. "Buy now" vs. "Wait" recommendations. Color-coded calendar.
- **Scott's Cheap Flights / Going**: Email deals service. Free tier = limited deals. Premium ($49/year) = full access to error fares, mistake prices, and flash sales. Has delivered $200-400 round-trip international fares regularly.
- **Secret Flying**: Free deal feed for international fares.
- **Kayak Explore**: Visual map of cheapest destinations from your airport.
- **Momondo**: Strong for international fare comparison.

### Fare Class Intelligence
- **Basic Economy**: Cheapest. No seat selection, no changes, no carry-on (some airlines), boards last. Fine for solo short-haul. Avoid for anything else.
- **Main Cabin / Economy**: Standard. Seat selection, carry-on, some flexibility. This is the value tier.
- **Premium Economy**: 20-40% more than economy for extra legroom, better food, priority boarding. Worth it on flights >6 hours.
- **Business Class**: Use miles/points or error fares. Cash price rarely justifies cost except for ultra-long-haul where sleep quality matters for work performance.

### Budget Airlines (When They Win)
- **Southwest**: No change fees ever, 2 free checked bags, no assigned seats. Often competitive on domestic routes. Rapid Rewards points are flexible.
- **Spirit / Frontier**: Ultra-low base fares + fees for everything (bags, seats, water). Total cost often matches or exceeds legacy airlines once you add bags. Use ONLY for: no-bag trips, short flights, when you've priced total cost and it genuinely wins.
- **JetBlue**: Good value. Free carry-on + personal item on all fares. Strong transatlantic value (London routes).
- **Allegiant**: Niche small-city routes. Good if your route exists, expensive add-ons.
- **International budget**: Ryanair (Europe), EasyJet (Europe), AirAsia (Southeast Asia), Norwegian (transatlantic). Different fee structures — always calculate total cost.

### Credit Card Points for Flights
**The points game is the single most valuable travel hack:**
- **Chase Ultimate Rewards**: Transfer 1:1 to United, Hyatt, Southwest, Air France/KLM, British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, and more. Sapphire Reserve gives 1.5x value through portal.
- **Amex Membership Rewards**: Transfer to Delta, ANA, Air Canada, British Airways, Singapore Airlines. Platinum card = lounge access.
- **Capital One Miles**: Transfer to Air Canada, Avianca, Turkish, Emirates, and more.
- **Citi ThankYou Points**: Transfer to Turkish, Singapore, JetBlue, Air France/KLM.

**Strategy**: Earn points on everyday spending → transfer to airline partners → book business/first class for fraction of cash price. A business class ticket that costs $5,000 cash might cost 70,000 points that you earned from $7,000 of normal spending through a 10x dining bonus.

### Airline-Specific Tactics
- **Southwest Companion Pass**: Earn 125,000 points in a year and a companion flies free for the remainder of that year + the next full year. One of the most valuable travel perks in existence.
- **United MileagePlus Excursionist Perk**: Free stopover on round-trip award tickets. Add a free extra city.
- **Delta SkyMiles Flash Sales**: Occasional flash sales on award tickets. Follow @ThePointsGuy and similar on Twitter/X for alerts.

## HOTELS: SYSTEMATIC SAVINGS

### Where to Book
- **Book direct with hotel**: Usually best rate guarantee, loyalty points, and better cancellation flexibility.
- **Hotwire / Priceline "Express Deals"**: Opaque booking (you don't know exact hotel until after payment). 30-60% off. Use BetterBidding.com to identify which hotel you're getting before booking.
- **Hotels.com**: 10th night free through rewards program. Good for frequent travelers without brand loyalty.
- **Costco Travel**: Competitive rates with added perks (breakfast, resort credits). Often best value for resort packages and all-inclusives.
- **AAA / AARP**: 10-20% off at many chains. Always ask.

### Hotel Loyalty Programs (Worth Joining — All Free)
| Program | Best Feature | Sweet Spot |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Marriott Bonvoy | Massive portfolio (30+ brands), elite status perks | Marriott credit cards for instant Gold/Platinum |
| Hilton Honors | Points don't expire, 5th night free on award stays | Hilton Amex for instant Gold status |
| IHG One Rewards | Strong value in mid-range (Holiday Inn, Crowne Plaza) | 4th night free on award stays |
| Hyatt (World of Hyatt) | Best luxury redemption value (Park Hyatt, Andaz for 25-30K points) | Chase Sapphire → transfer to Hyatt |
| Best Western | Underrated — good budget hotels, points usable | No-status-required benefits |

### Hotel Pricing Tactics
- **Weekend vs. weekday**: Business hotels (downtown, airport) = cheaper weekends. Resort hotels = cheaper weekdays.
- **Book refundable, then rebook**: Book a refundable rate, continue monitoring price. If price drops, rebook at lower rate and cancel original.
- **Call the hotel directly**: Front desk can sometimes offer rates not available online, especially for longer stays.
- **Extended stay**: Negotiate weekly rates. Extended Stay America, Residence Inn, Home2 Suites = kitchenette savings on food.
- **Ask for upgrades at check-in**: Politely ask "Are there any complimentary upgrades available?" — especially effective late in the day when rooms are going to go unsold anyway.

## RENTAL CARS

### Where to Book
- **Costco Travel**: Consistently competitive, includes additional driver and other perks at no extra charge.
- **Autoslash**: Tracks your rental car booking and alerts you if a cheaper rate appears. Re-books automatically.
- **Turo**: Peer-to-peer car rental. Often cheaper for premium/luxury vehicles. Insurance coverage varies — read carefully.
- **Direct with rental company**: Sometimes has exclusive deals, especially with discount codes.
- **Credit card rental insurance**: Many premium cards (Chase Sapphire, Amex Platinum) include primary or secondary rental car insurance. Decline the rental company's CDW/LDW and save $15-30/day.

### Rental Car Pricing Intelligence
- **Book early, rebook often**: Rental car prices fluctuate. Book now, check weekly, rebook if cheaper. Most bookings are free cancellation.
- **Airport vs. off-airport**: Airport locations charge airport concession fees (10-15% surcharge). Off-airport (hotel shuttles, nearby locations) can save significantly.
- **Size matters**: Compact and economy = cheapest. But sometimes midsize is same price or $2-3/day more and dramatically more comfortable.
- **Skip the gas pre-purchase**: Almost always a bad deal unless you're returning with a truly empty tank. Refuel at gas stations near the airport instead.
- **Weekend vs. weekly rate**: A weekly rate (7 days) is often cheaper than a 5-day rental. Check if keeping the car the extra days costs less.
- **One-way fees**: Can be $200+. Sometimes avoidable if the rental company needs cars moved to that destination.

## VACATION PACKAGES & ALL-INCLUSIVES

### Where to Find the Best Deals
- **Costco Travel**: Best value for resort vacation packages (Hawaii, Caribbean, Mexico). Includes hotel + flight + extras (often resort credit, breakfast, or room upgrade). Price competitive and includes Costco-sourced perks.
- **Apple Vacations / Funjet**: Charter flight packages to Mexico/Caribbean. Sometimes unbeatable pricing.
- **BookVIP**: Deeply discounted resort stays in exchange for attending timeshare presentation. Know the deal: you'll sit through 90-120 minutes of high-pressure sales. Worth it if you can say no.
- **Groupon / Travelzoo**: Flash sale vacation deals. Read terms carefully — blackout dates, resort fees, etc.

### All-Inclusive Intelligence
- **When to book**: 2-4 months out for Caribbean/Mexico. Further out for peak season (December-April).
- **Off-season = massive savings**: Caribbean/Mexico May-November (excluding holiday weeks) = 30-50% below peak season. Weather risk is real (hurricane season June-November) but pricing is dramatically better.
- **Adults-only vs. family**: Adults-only resorts (Secrets, Excellence, Sandals) tend to be higher quality food/drink and quieter. Family resorts (Beaches, Moon Palace, Barcelo) have kids programs.

## CRUISES

### When to Book
- **Wave season (January-March)**: Cruise lines push hardest with incentives. Best time to book.
- **Last minute (30-60 days out)**: If flexible, significant discounts on unsold cabins. Use Vacations To Go, CruiseSheet.
- **Book early for**: Specific cabin locations, suites, peak season (summer Alaska, holiday Caribbean).

### Cruise Pricing Tactics
- **Inside cabin hack**: Interior cabins are 40-60% less than balcony. If you're active on excursions and won't spend time in the room, this is the smart play.
- **Repositioning cruises**: When ships move between seasons (e.g., Caribbean→Mediterranean in spring). One-way, long voyages at deep discounts. Often 50-70% off per-night equivalent.
- **Drink packages**: Calculate your consumption honestly. If you drink 5+ drinks/day, the package saves money. Otherwise, it's profit for the cruise line.
- **Shore excursions**: Book independently (Viator, GetYourGuide, local operators) for 30-60% less than ship-organized excursions. Risk: if you're late back, the ship leaves without you.
- **Casino/loyalty offers**: After your first cruise, expect promotional offers for return cruises at steep discounts.

## THEME PARKS, ZOOS & ATTRACTIONS

### Theme Parks
- **Disney World / Disneyland**: Never buy gate-price tickets. Use authorized resellers: Undercover Tourist, Park Savers, Get Away Today. Save $10-30/ticket. Multi-day tickets = lower per-day cost. Consider park hopper only if you'll actually hop — otherwise it's wasted money.
- **Universal Studios**: Annual pass often pays for itself in 2 visits. Express Pass dramatically improves experience but is expensive — evaluate wait time tolerance.
- **Six Flags**: Season pass + dining deal = incredible value if you visit 3+ times. Single-day gate price is a terrible deal.
- **Legoland, SeaWorld, Busch Gardens**: Buy online in advance. Gate price is always the worst price. Groupon frequently has tickets at 30-50% off.
- **AAA / Costco**: Both frequently offer discounted theme park tickets.

### Zoos, Aquariums & Museums
- **Annual pass break-even**: Calculate: annual pass cost ÷ single admission. If you'll visit that many times, buy the pass. Most zoo/aquarium annual passes pay for themselves in 2-3 visits.
- **Reciprocal membership programs**:
  - **AZA Reciprocal Admissions**: Many zoos honor other zoo memberships for free or discounted admission. Check before you travel.
  - **ASTC (Association of Science and Technology Centers)**: Science museum membership at one institution = free admission at 300+ museums.
  - **NARM (North American Reciprocal Museum Association)**: Art museum reciprocity.
- **Library passes**: Many public libraries lend museum/zoo/garden passes for FREE. Check your library's website.
- **Free museum days**: Most major museums have free admission days (monthly, quarterly, or specific events). Plan around these.
- **City tourism passes**: CityPASS, Go City — bundled admission to multiple attractions. Only worth it if you'll visit 3-4+ included attractions in a short trip. Calculate individual admission vs. pass price.
- **Groupon / LivingSocial**: Frequently have attraction tickets at 30-50% off.

## CONCERT & EVENT TICKETS

### Primary Market (Face Value)
- **Ticketmaster / AXS / Venue box office**: Primary sellers. "Dynamic pricing" means prices fluctuate with demand. Fees are 20-30% on top of face value.
- **Presale codes**: Artist, venue, credit card (Amex, Citi, Chase), and fan club presales happen before general sale. Sign up for artist newsletters and credit card entertainment programs.
- **Venue box office**: Sometimes avoids online service fees. Worth checking for local venues.

### Secondary Market (Resale)
- **StubHub, SeatGeek, Vivid Seats**: Compare all three. Prices vary significantly for identical seats.
- **Gametime**: Best for last-minute (same-day) tickets. Prices drop as event approaches — sellers desperate to sell rather than eat the ticket.
- **Day-of strategy**: For non-sellout events, prices crater within 2 hours of showtime. Maximum risk, maximum savings.
- **TickPick**: No buyer fees (price shown = price paid). Often cheapest after factoring fees.
- **Cash or Trade**: Face-value fan-to-fan exchange for music (especially jam band/Phish/Dead community).

### Sports Tickets
- **Season ticket holder resale**: Many teams have official resale marketplaces on their team apps. Often competitive with StubHub.
- **Weeknight games**: Tuesday-Thursday games = significantly cheaper than weekend, especially for baseball and hockey.
- **September/October MLB**: If a team is eliminated from playoff contention, ticket prices plummet.
- **Opening week premium**: First games of any season carry a 50-100% markup. Wait 2-3 weeks for prices to normalize.

## EXPERIENCES & ACTIVITIES

### Booking Platforms
- **Viator**: Largest selection of tours and activities. Read reviews carefully. Often 10-20% cheaper than booking through hotel concierge.
- **GetYourGuide**: Competes with Viator, sometimes better prices or exclusive offerings.
- **Airbnb Experiences**: Unique local-hosted activities. Quality varies — check reviews.
- **Groupon**: Local experiences (spa, dining, activities) at 40-60% off. Read fine print on restrictions and blackout dates.

### Activity-Specific Intelligence
- **Golf**: GolfNow, TeeOff for discounted tee times. Hot deals = leftover tee times at 40-60% off. Twilight rates (afternoon) = 30-50% less than prime morning times.
- **Ski passes**: Epic Pass, Ikon Pass — buy in spring (March-May) for next season at lowest price. Multi-resort passes pay for themselves in 3-5 days of skiing.
- **Spa services**: Groupon and Spafinder for discounted treatments. Tuesdays-Thursdays = best availability and occasional mid-week specials.
- **Fitness / wellness classes**: ClassPass for variety at discounted rates. Many studios offer first-class-free or intro packages.

## TRAVEL INSURANCE

### When It's Worth It
- **Expensive non-refundable trips**: Cruise + international flights = insure it.
- **Health coverage abroad**: US health insurance often doesn't cover international medical. Travel insurance fills the gap.
- **Adventure travel**: Helicopter evacuation, extreme sports coverage.
- **Hurricane season Caribbean travel**: Cancel-for-any-reason (CFAR) coverage.

### When to Skip
- **Cheap domestic trips**: Your credit card probably covers trip delay/cancellation to some degree.
- **Fully refundable bookings**: If everything is cancellable, insurance is redundant.

### Providers
- **Allianz, Travel Guard, World Nomads**: Reputable, competitive pricing.
- **Credit card coverage**: Chase Sapphire Reserve includes trip delay ($500/person), trip cancellation ($10K/person), lost baggage ($3K). Amex Platinum has comparable coverage. Check your card benefits BEFORE buying separate insurance.
- **CFAR (Cancel For Any Reason)**: Costs 40-60% more than standard but lets you cancel for literally any reason and get 75% refund. Worth it for expensive trips with uncertainty.

## GOLDEN RULES: TRAVEL EDITION

1. **Google Flights is your home base for flight search.** Always start there.
2. **Sign up for Scott's Cheap Flights / Going.** Error fares save hundreds to thousands.
3. **Book hotels direct** for best rate guarantee + loyalty points.
4. **Costco Travel for vacation packages.** Consistently best value.
5. **Credit card points are the #1 travel hack.** Earn on everyday spend, redeem for premium travel.
6. **Shoulder season is the ultimate travel hack** — lower prices, fewer crowds, similar weather.
7. **Annual passes pay for themselves in 2-3 visits.** Zoo, museum, theme park — do the math.
8. **Library museum passes are free.** Check before paying admission.
9. **Rental car insurance from your credit card.** Decline the rental counter CDW.
10. **For events, patience = savings.** Prices drop as showtime approaches (non-sellout events).
