# 02 - Feasibility & Due Diligence

> **Phase 2 of 10** | Predecessor: [[01 - Real Estate Pipeline & Site Selection]] | Successor: [[03 - Design — SD, DD, CD]]

---

## Purpose

Rigorous investigation of the site, market, financial viability, and regulatory environment to make an informed go/no-go decision before committing design capital.

---

## Key Activities

### Physical Due Diligence
- **Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA):** ASTM E1527 standard; identifies recognized environmental conditions (RECs). If RECs found → Phase II (sampling)
- **Phase II ESA (if triggered):** Soil/groundwater sampling to confirm or rule out contamination
- **Geotechnical Investigation:** Soil borings, bearing capacity, water table depth, foundation recommendations
- **ALTA/NSPS Land Survey:** Boundary, topographic, and improvement survey per ALTA standards. Shows easements, encroachments, flood zones, utilities
- **Existing Conditions Assessment:** For renovations — structural, MEP, hazmat (asbestos, lead paint), ADA compliance audit
- **Utility Capacity Confirmation:** Written confirmations from utility providers on available capacity (water pressure, electric service size, sewer capacity)

### Regulatory Due Diligence
- **Zoning analysis:** Permitted uses, setbacks, height limits, parking requirements, FAR (floor area ratio), lot coverage
- **Variance/conditional use requirements:** If the project doesn't conform by right, what's the approval path and timeline?
- **Historic preservation review:** Is the site in a historic district? National Register listing?
- **AHJ identification:** Which authorities have jurisdiction? (see [[AHJ Research Methodology]])
- **Impact fees and exactions:** What fees will the jurisdiction charge? (impact, tap, connection, park dedication)
- **Stormwater management:** Local requirements for detention/retention, water quality treatment

### Financial Due Diligence
- **Preliminary construction budget:** Based on historical $/SF data, comparable projects, and preliminary scope
- **Soft cost budget:** Architecture, engineering, permits, legal, financing, insurance, CM fees
- **Proforma development:** Revenue projections, operating expenses, NOI, cap rate, IRR, cash-on-cash return
- **Financing assessment:** Construction loan terms, permanent financing, equity requirements
- **TIA reconciliation (retail):** Is the TIA sufficient to cover projected construction costs? (see [[Tenant Improvement Allowances]])

### Legal Due Diligence
- **Title commitment review:** Identify all exceptions, easements, restrictions
- **Lease review (if tenant-driven):** Work letter terms, landlord approval requirements, rent commencement triggers
- **HOA/CC&R review:** Covenants, conditions, and restrictions that may limit construction
- **Access and utility easement review:** Confirm legal access and utility routing

## Go/No-Go Decision Framework

The agent should evaluate the following criteria:

| Category | Green | Yellow | Red |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Environmental | Clean Phase I | RECs identified, manageable | Known contamination requiring remediation |
| Geotech | Standard foundations | Special foundations needed (cost impact <10%) | Deep foundations, unstable soil (cost impact >15%) |
| Zoning | Permitted by right | Conditional use or minor variance needed | Rezoning required |
| Budget | Within program parameters | 5-10% over target (value-engineering possible) | >10% over target |
| Schedule | Meets program timeline | 1-2 month delay manageable | >3 month delay to critical dates |
| Title | Clean with standard exceptions | Easement requires accommodation | Title defect blocks development |

| DD budget authorization | Client/owner | Cannot engage consultants |
| Site access for testing | Seller/landlord | Cannot perform geotech/environmental work |
| Existing building plans (reno) | Landlord/municipality | Must field-verify all conditions |

---

## Common Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Phase I reveals RECs requiring Phase II | Budget for Phase II contingency; extend DD period in contract |
| Geotech reveals poor soil conditions | Include foundation contingency in preliminary budget |
| Zoning requires lengthy variance process | Pre-meeting with planning department before going hard on site |
| TIA insufficient for required scope | Negotiate TIA cap increase or reduce scope before lease execution |
| Construction costs exceed proforma | Engage cost estimator early with detailed scope, not just $/SF |

## Transition to Next Phase

**Gate criteria to advance to [[03 - Design — SD, DD, CD]]:**
1. Go decision confirmed by owner/client
2. All critical due diligence reports received and reviewed
3. No deal-breaker findings (or mitigation plans approved)
4. Design budget authorized
5. Architect/engineer selection initiated or complete
