# Sesame Cabbage Salad

**Japanese-inspired "addictive cabbage" salad** — sweet sesame dressing with loads of scallions and toasted almonds.

## 📋 Ingredients

### Salad
- 1/2 head green cabbage, shredded (280g)
- 1 bunch scallions, thinly sliced on a bias (35g)
- 2 tbsp finely sliced cilantro (chiffonade)
- 1/2 cup (60g) slivered almonds, toasted

### Dressing
- 2 tbsp (16g) sesame oil
- 2 tbsp (16g) soy sauce
- 2 tbsp (30g) honey *(or agave for vegan)*
- 2 tbsp (24g) lemon juice
- 1 clove garlic, grated
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 2 tbsp (12g) toasted sesame seeds / black sesame seeds

## 🔪 Instructions

1. **Prep the cabbage:** Quarter cabbage into wedges, remove core. Shred fairly thin using mandoline or knife. Add to large mixing bowl.

2. **Prep aromatics:** Thinly slice scallions on a bias, finely slice cilantro (chiffonade). Add to bowl with cabbage.

3. **Make dressing:** Whisk together sesame oil, soy sauce, honey, lemon juice, garlic, and white pepper in small bowl or jar.

4. **Toss salad:** Pour dressing over cabbage. Sprinkle slivered almonds and sesame seeds over top. Toss to combine.

5. **Adjust seasoning:** Taste and add salt if needed (depends on soy sauce saltiness).

6. **Serve immediately** with extra sesame seeds for garnish.

## 📝 Notes

- **Serve immediately:** Once dressed, cabbage releases water and loses crunch. Best eaten within an hour of dressing.
- **Flavor holds, texture doesn't:** If refrigerated for 12+ hours, flavor is still great but crunch is gone.
- **Nut swaps:** Can substitute toasted cashews or peanuts (roughly chopped) for almonds.
- **Cabbage varieties:** Green cabbage works best, but red cabbage and napa cabbage also work well.
- **Vegan:** Use agave instead of honey.
