# PT-Medical-Healthcare — Estimating Guide

> Covers: Medical Office Buildings (MOB), clinical suites, dental offices, imaging centers, ambulatory surgery centers (ASC), urgent care, and specialty clinics. Healthcare construction is among the most complex and expensive TI categories — driven by regulation, infection control, and MEP density.

---

## Why Healthcare Costs More

Healthcare construction routinely costs 1.5–3× equivalent office TI. The drivers:

1. **FGI Guidelines compliance** — Facility Guidelines Institute sets minimum room sizes, ventilation rates, surface requirements. Non-negotiable for licensed facilities.
2. **MEP density** — Medical gas, specialized HVAC (HEPA, negative pressure, ASHRAE 170), high electrical loads
3. **Infection control** — ICRA (Infection Control Risk Assessment) requirements in occupied facilities; adds 10–20% to cost
4. **Plumbing density** — Hand-washing sinks in every exam room (FGI requirement)
5. **Structural requirements** — Lead shielding for imaging; seismic bracing for equipment; floor flatness for surgery
6. **Specialized finishes** — Seamless flooring, antimicrobial surfaces, cleanable wall protection
7. **Regulatory approvals** — State health department, CMS (if Medicare), AAAHC or JCAHO accreditation

## Healthcare Facility Types & Benchmarks

### Medical Office Building (MOB) — General Practice / Specialty

Non-clinical or lightly clinical outpatient space:

| Scope | $/SF Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Admin-heavy MOB (low clinical) | $120–$180/SF | Standard office + some exam rooms |
| General practice (primary care) | $160–$240/SF | Exam rooms, nurse station, procedure room |
| Specialty clinic (ortho, cardio, etc.) | $180–$280/SF | Imaging rough-in, procedure rooms |
| Physical therapy | $120–$200/SF | Open gym, private rooms, hydro if included |

**Key differentiators from standard office:**
- Hand-washing sink at every exam room: $1,200–$2,500/sink + plumbing rough-in
- Medical gas (O2, vacuum at minimum): $4,000–$12,000/outlet pair installed
- FGI minimum room sizes (exam room: 120 SF minimum; toilet: 60 SF minimum)

### Dental Office

Dental is highly specialized — plumbing and casework dominate:

| Format | $/SF Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General dentistry (standard) | $180–$280/SF | 6–12 operatories typical |
| Specialty dental (oral surgery) | $250–$380/SF | More operatories, procedure rooms |
| Orthodontics | $150–$220/SF | Open bay, fewer plumbing-heavy stations |

**Dental-specific costs:**
- Dental operatory rough-in (plumbing, vacuum, electric, data): $8,000–$18,000/operatory
- Central vacuum/air system: $20,000–$50,000 for 8-10 chair office
- X-ray lead lining (per room): $3,000–$8,000
- Sterilization room (casework + plumbing): $15,000–$35,000
- Panoramic X-ray room (if included): $15,000–$30,000 construction + equipment separate
- Custom dental casework: $400–$1,000/LF

### Imaging Center (MRI, CT, X-Ray)

Imaging requires specialized structural and shielding work:

| Modality | Construction Cost Add | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Standard X-ray room | $25,000–$60,000 | Lead shielding, floor reinforcement |
| Fluoroscopy room | $40,000–$90,000 | Heavier shielding, floor load |
| CT scanner room | $80,000–$180,000 | Floor reinforcement, shielding, HVAC |
| MRI suite | $200,000–$500,000 | RF shielding (Faraday cage), magnet quench vent, structural |
| Nuclear medicine | $150,000–$400,000 | Radiation shielding, special waste systems |

**MRI-specific scope (construction only — equipment separate):**
- RF shielded room (Faraday cage): $150,000–$300,000
- Magnet quench pipe (helium vent to exterior): $15,000–$40,000
- Structural reinforcement for magnet weight (typically 10,000–25,000 lbs): $25,000–$75,000
- HVAC: dedicated precision cooling for magnet room
- Access control: MRI zone control (ferrous metal exclusion): $10,000–$25,000

### Ambulatory Surgery Center (ASC)

ASC is the most complex and expensive healthcare TI type:

| Size | $/SF Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Single OR + 4 PACU bays | $350–$500/SF | 3,000–5,000 SF |
| 2-OR + 8 PACU bays | $380–$550/SF | 6,000–10,000 SF |
| 3-OR + 12 PACU bays | $400–$650/SF | 10,000–15,000 SF |

**ASC-specific scope items:**
- OR HVAC (minimum 15 ACH, HEPA filtration, precise temp/humidity): $35,000–$80,000/OR
- Medical gas system (O2, N2O, vacuum, instrument air per OR): $20,000–$50,000/OR
- Nurse call system: $15,000–$40,000
- Scrub sinks (per OR): $3,000–$6,000 each
- Sterilization/CSSD: $30,000–$80,000
- Recovery (PACU) casework per bay: $5,000–$15,000
- Dictation/documentation stations: $2,000–$5,000/station
- Access control / patient tracking: $25,000–$60,000
- Emergency power (generator): $50,000–$150,000 (often required by code)

### Urgent Care / Retail Health

Hybrid between office and clinical — growing segment:

| Format | $/SF Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Urgent care (standard) | $150–$220/SF | 4–8 exam rooms, imaging rough-in |
| Urgent care with X-ray | $180–$260/SF | Add X-ray room shielding/structure |
| Retail health clinic (CVS/Walgreens tier) | $120–$180/SF | Small footprint, light clinical |

## Healthcare MEP — Critical Requirements

### HVAC (ASHRAE 170 — Healthcare Standard)

Healthcare HVAC is regulated by ASHRAE 170 (not 62.1 like commercial):

| Space Type | Min ACH (total) | Min ACH (outside air) | Pressure | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OR (Class B/C) | 20 | 4 | Positive | HEPA required |
| OR (Class A) | 15 | 3 | Positive | |
| PACU | 6 | 2 | Positive | |
| Exam room | 6 | 2 | Neutral | |
| Patient corridor | 6 | 2 | Positive | |
| Soiled utility | 10 | 10 | Negative | Negative pressure always |
| Toilet rooms | 10 | 10 | Negative | |
| Janitor's closet | 10 | 10 | Negative | |

**HVAC cost premium over standard office:** 40–80% higher $/SF due to ACH requirements, dedicated AHUs, and pressure relationships.

### Medical Gas (NFPA 99)

NFPA 99 governs medical gas systems. Healthcare construction requires licensed medical gas installer:

| Gas | Typical Use | Color Code |
|---|---|---|
| Oxygen (O2) | Patient care, OR | Green |
| Medical vacuum | Suction, waste anesthesia | White |
| Nitrous oxide (N2O) | Anesthesia, dental | Blue |
| Medical air | Respiratory, instruments | Yellow |
| Nitrogen (N2) | Instrument driving | Black |
| WAGD | Waste anesthesia gas | Magenta |

**Medical gas costs (installed, per outlet):**
- O2 zone valve assembly: $2,000–$4,000
- Per outlet (O2 or vacuum): $600–$1,200
- Medical gas manifold (central): $8,000–$25,000
- Full system for 10-room clinic: $30,000–$80,000
- Full system for 3-OR ASC: $80,000–$200,000

### Electrical — Healthcare Requirements

- Emergency power (Article 517, NEC): Life safety and critical branches required for licensed facilities
- Isolated power panels (OR): $8,000–$20,000/OR
- Line isolation monitors: $3,000–$6,000/OR
- Nurse call system: $15,000–$50,000 depending on complexity
- Code blue / emergency call: $5,000–$20,000
- Redundant utility feed (ASC, hospitals): significant cost

## Healthcare Regulatory & Compliance Costs

**State Health Department Review (Facility Licensure)**
- Required for: ASC, imaging, dialysis, most clinical facilities
- Timeline: 60–180 days review, often after permit
- Cost: $5,000–$30,000 in state fees; architectural coordination adds time
- Design must follow FGI Guidelines — cannot be waived

**CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services)**
- Required if facility accepts Medicare/Medicaid
- Triggers Life Safety Code compliance (NFPA 101)
- Adds cost for sprinkler, corridor widths, door hardware, egress

**ICRA (Infection Control Risk Assessment)**
- Required for any construction/renovation adjacent to occupied healthcare space
- Categories I–IV; Category III/IV requires physical barriers, negative pressure, HEPA filtration
- ICRA costs: $5,000–$30,000 for proper execution; much higher if ventilation systems needed

## Healthcare Finish Standards

### Surface Requirements (FGI / Infection Control)

| Location | Floor | Wall | Ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| OR / Procedure | Seamless sheet vinyl or epoxy | Impact-resistant, cleanable | Monolithic, cleanable |
| Exam room | Sheet vinyl or LVT | Scrub-resistant paint or FRP wainscot | ACT or GWB |
| PACU/patient care | Sheet vinyl or carpet | Scrub-resistant | GWB or ACT |
| Soiled utility | Sheet vinyl or epoxy | FRP or tile | GWB |
| Corridors | Sheet vinyl or LVT | Handrail + wall protection | GWB or ACT |

**Sheet vinyl (healthcare grade):** $8–$16/SF installed — significantly more than standard LVT
**Coved base (seamless, required in clinical):** $12–$20/LF vs. $3–$6/LF standard base

## Common Healthcare Estimate Errors

1. **FGI room sizes** — designing below minimums invalidates licensure; redesign costs money
2. **Missing medical gas** — routinely omitted in early estimates; add $40K–$200K
3. **Standard HVAC applied** — healthcare HVAC is 40–80% more expensive than office HVAC
4. **ICRA not budgeted** — occupied facility construction triggers significant protection costs
5. **X-ray shielding missed** — any imaging room needs physicist-specified lead shielding
6. **Emergency power** — licensed healthcare facilities almost always require backup generator
7. **Casework underestimated** — healthcare casework is higher spec than commercial; $400–$1,200/LF
8. **State fees and review time** — not in GC scope; 60–180 day review can delay project
9. **Acoustic requirements** — HIPAA-compliant sound isolation adds cost to partition systems
10. **ADA compliance in healthcare** — higher standards than commercial ADA; toilet rooms, reaches, etc.

---

## HIPAA / Acoustic Compliance

Speech privacy in healthcare is a regulatory requirement (HIPAA):
- Exam room STC rating: minimum STC 45 (vs. STC 35 for office)
- STC 45 partition: full-height to deck, insulation, seals around penetrations
- Sound masking system: $2–$5/SF (often recommended for open areas)
- Add $2–$5/SF to partition costs vs. standard office TI for HIPAA compliance
