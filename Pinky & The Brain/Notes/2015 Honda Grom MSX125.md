## Engine — 124.9cc SOHC 2-Valve

The Grom's engine is a Honda horizontal single derived from the legendary Super Cub / Wave family. Honda has manufactured billions of these engines worldwide since the 1960s — one of the most reliable engine designs ever produced.

### Design Highlights

- **Air-cooled:** No radiator, no water pump, no coolant, no thermostat
- **SOHC with screw-and-locknut valve adjustment** — simple, fast adjustments (no shims)
- **No oil filter cartridge** — only oil screen (strainer) in right crankcase cover + centrifugal filter on crankshaft
- Shares architecture with Honda Monkey 125, Super Cub C125, Trail 125

### ⚠️ Critical Oil Notes

- **JASO MA or MA2 rating is MANDATORY** — wet clutch shares oil with engine
- Do NOT use car oil with "Energy Conserving" or friction modifiers — causes clutch slippage
- Oil capacity is tiny (~0.7L) — measure carefully; over/underfilling is critical on this engine
- **No oil filter to replace** — clean oil screen and centrifugal filter every 8,000 miles

## FI Light — Self-Diagnostic System

The FI (Fuel Injection) warning light displays **blink codes** for engine management faults.

**Reading Codes:**
1. Turn ignition ON, engine OFF
2. FI light flashes: long blinks = tens, short blinks = ones (e.g., 1 long + 2 short = Code 12)
3. Codes repeat after pause

### Common FI Blink Codes

| Code | Description | Common Cause |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | MAP Sensor | Disconnected or failed |
| 7 | ECT (Engine Coolant Temp) | Sensor fault |
| 8 | TPS (Throttle Position) | Needs calibration or replacement |
| 9 | IAT (Intake Air Temp) | Sensor disconnected |
| 12 | Injector | Circuit open/short |
| 21 | O2 Sensor | Sensor failure |
| 54 | Bank Angle Sensor | Tip-over — cycle ignition to reset |

**Clearing Codes:** Disconnect battery 30 seconds, or use Honda-compatible scan tool.

## Recall

### NHTSA Recall 15V-064: Fuel Pump (CRITICAL)

- **Applies to:** 2014–2015 Honda Grom (MSX125)
- **Units Affected:** 17,643
- **Issue:** Resin bracket inside fuel pump swells and seizes impeller
- **Symptoms:** Engine stalls, cranks but won't start, loss of power
- **Risk:** Engine stall during riding — crash risk
- **Fix:** Replace fuel pump assembly
- **STATUS: VERIFY COMPLETED ON THIS VIN**

Check [NHTSA.gov](https://nhtsa.gov) with VIN MLHJC6110F5107144.

## Maintenance Schedule

### Every 600 Miles (First Service)
- Change engine oil
- Clean oil screen (strainer)
- Check/adjust valve clearances
- Inspect/adjust chain slack

### Every 2,500 Miles (or 6 Months)
- Change engine oil (0.7L, 10W-30 JASO MA/MA2)
- Check/adjust valve clearances
- Inspect/adjust clutch cable
- Check/adjust chain slack, lube chain
- Inspect brake pads
- Inspect tires

### Every 4,000 Miles (or Annually)
- Replace spark plug (NGK CPR6EA-9)
- Inspect air filter
- Inspect brake fluid

### Every 8,000 Miles (or 2 Years)
- Clean oil screen and centrifugal filter
- Replace air filter
- Replace brake fluid (DOT 4)
- Inspect steering head bearings
- Inspect wheel bearings

### Every 16,000 Miles
- Replace drive chain and sprockets
- Replace clutch cable (as needed)
- Fork oil change

## Valve Clearance Specifications

**Check every 2,500 miles** — critical on this engine.

| Valve | Clearance (Cold) |
|---|---|
| **Intake** | 0.10 mm ± 0.02 mm (0.004" ± 0.001") |
| **Exhaust** | 0.17 mm ± 0.02 mm (0.007" ± 0.001") |

### Adjustment Procedure (15–20 min)

1. Engine **COLD** (ambient temp)
2. Remove seat, valve adjustment caps (17mm), left crankcase inspection cap
3. Rotate to **TDC compression stroke**: "T" mark aligned, both valves have play
4. Check clearances with feeler gauges
5. Adjust: Loosen 9mm locknut, turn adjusting screw, retighten locknut, recheck

**Pro Tip:** Can do during oil change while draining — only 2 valves total, very simple.

## Key Procedures

### Oil Change (10 min)
1. Warm engine 2–3 min
2. Remove 12mm drain bolt, drain
3. Clean magnetic drain bolt, reinstall with crush washer — **24 Nm**
4. Add **0.7L** 10W-30 JASO MA/MA2
5. Check level with bike upright — dipstick rests on threads (don't thread in)

### Oil Screen Cleaning (30 min, every 8,000 mi)
1. Drain oil
2. Remove right crankcase cover (clutch cover) — 5-6 bolts (8mm)
3. Remove screen, clean with solvent, inspect
4. Clean centrifugal filter on crankshaft (remove sludge)
5. Reinstall with new gasket — refill **0.8L**

### Chain Adjustment (10 min, every 300–600 mi)
1. Check slack at midpoint: should be 20–30mm
2. If needed: Loosen 22mm rear axle nut
3. Turn chain adjusters **equally both sides**
4. Verify alignment marks match
5. Retighten axle nut — **59 Nm**
6. Recheck slack, lube chain

### Spark Plug (5 min, every 4,000 mi)
| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| **Plug** | NGK CPR6EA-9 or Denso U20EPR9 |
| **Gap** | 0.8–0.9 mm (0.031–0.035") |
| **Torque** | 16 Nm (12 ft-lb) |

## Performance Modifications (Quick Reference)

The Grom has the deepest aftermarket ecosystem of any modern motorcycle. Here's the progression:

### Entry Build (~$400–$600)
- Full exhaust system (Yoshimura RS-9, Akrapovic)
- ECU flash (DHM or CJR)
- Result: ~11–12 hp, 60–63 mph

### Street Build (~$1,200)
- Tier 1 + cam (DHM V2), clutch springs, suspension upgrades
- Result: ~13 hp, 65–70 mph

### Big Bore Kit (~$1,500–$3,500)
- DHM 186cc, Kitaco Light 181cc, or Takegawa 181cc
- Requires: ECU tune, oil cooler, clutch springs, high-flow oil pump
- Result: 14–22+ hp

### Full Race (~$6,000–$8,000+)
- Kitaco DOHC 181cc or Takegawa 4V Super Head
- Forged crank, 5-speed transmission, standalone ECU
- Result: 25–40+ hp (race build)

**Critical:** Fueling must match airflow. Oil cooler mandatory above 150cc. Upgrade suspension and brakes before making more power.
