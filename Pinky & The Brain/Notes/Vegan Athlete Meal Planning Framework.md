# 📅 Vegan Athlete Meal Planning Framework

> **Purpose:** Practical meal planning system for Nathan's lean bulk — integrating protein targets, caloric surplus, cuisine variety, workout schedule, and the vault's existing meal planning infrastructure.

---

## The Planning Hierarchy

```
Annual: Macrocycle goals (150 → 180 lbs)
Monthly: Mesocycle nutrition calibration (adjust calories if weight trends off)
Weekly: Meal plan + grocery list generation
Daily: Execution + tracking against targets
```

---

## Weekly Planning Process

### Step 1: Anchor the Week

Start every weekly plan by anchoring these fixed elements:

1. **Post-workout nutrition** — always the same structure (protein shake or whole food equivalent). This is non-negotiable.
2. **Breakfast** — consistent during the week for compliance. Alternate between 2-3 breakfast options.
3. **Protein sources** — decide this week's primary proteins (e.g., tempeh + seitan, or tofu + lentils).

### Step 2: Choose the Cuisine Theme

Rotate cuisine weekly for variety and sanity:

| Week | Primary Cuisine | Secondary Cuisine |
|------|----------------|------------------|
| 1 | Mexican | American |
| 2 | Asian (Japanese/Korean) | Mediterranean |
| 3 | Indian | Italian |
| 4 | Mediterranean | Mexican |
| 5 | Italian | Asian |
| 6 | Mix — "Fusion week" | Nathan's choice |
| Repeat | — | — |

### Step 3: Build Meals Around Protein Targets

Work backward from protein:
- Each of 4 daily meals must contribute 35–45g protein
- Post-workout shake = guaranteed 45–55g
- Remaining 3 meals each need 35–40g

### Step 4: Calculate Caloric Surplus

At 150 lbs: 2,600–2,800 cal target
- If weight is flat for 2+ weeks → increase by 200 cal
- If weight is gaining >1 lb/week for 3+ weeks → decrease by 200 cal

### Step 5: Build Grocery List (see [[Vegan Grocery Strategy & Pantry Stocking]])

## High-Protein Vegan Meal Templates

### Breakfast Templates (~35–45g protein)

**Template B1 — Power Overnight Oats:**
```
½ cup oats                     → 6g protein
1 cup soy milk                 → 7g protein
3 tbsp hemp seeds              → 10g protein
1 tbsp chia seeds              → 3g protein
2 tbsp peanut butter           → 8g protein
1 cup berries (topping)        → 1g protein
Total: ~35g protein | 550-600 cal
```

**Template B2 — Tofu Scramble Plate:**
```
200g firm tofu (scrambled)     → 16g protein
½ cup black beans              → 7g protein
2 slices whole grain toast     → 8g protein
½ avocado                      → 2g protein
1 tbsp nutritional yeast       → 4g protein
Total: ~37g protein | 650 cal
```

**Template B3 — Protein Smoothie Bowl:**

## Macro Check Calculator

Use this to verify any custom meal plan day:

**Minimum daily thresholds:**
- Protein: ≥150g (at 150 lbs) — no exceptions
- Carbs: ≥300g on training days; ≥250g on rest days
- Fat: ≥65g (minimum for hormonal health)
- Calories: 2,600–2,800 (at 150 lbs)

**Red flag thresholds for Sage to flag:**
- Protein <130g → **flag immediately**
- Calories <2,400 during bulk → flag
- Fat <50g → flag (hormonal risk)
- >3 consecutive days with same protein source → flag (variety needed)

## Nutrient Timing Around Training

### The Optimal Day Structure

**6:00 AM — Wake:**
Hydrate (16 oz water). Supplements (B12, D3, algae EPA/DHA, K2).

**6:30 AM — Breakfast (Pre-workout fuel):**
- 35–40g protein
- 60–80g carbs
- Moderate fat
- 16–20 oz water

**7:30 AM — Train:**
- Sip water during training (8–16 oz total)

**9:00 AM — Post-workout (critical window):**
- 45–60g protein (FIRST priority)
- 60–100g carbs
- Low fat (slows absorption here)
- Creatine (5g)
- 20 oz water

**12:30 PM — Lunch:**
- 40–50g protein
- Balanced carbs and fat
- Large vegetable component (micronutrients + fiber)

**4:00 PM — Snack (if needed):**
- 15–20g protein
- Carbs if pre-evening energy needed
- Hemp seeds, roasted chickpeas, edamame, pumpkin seeds

**7:00 PM — Dinner:**
- 40–50g protein
- Moderate carbs (lower than lunch — less physical demand ahead)
- Higher fat acceptable (slowed digestion = slow protein release overnight)

**9:00 PM — Pre-sleep (optional):**
- 15–20g slow-digesting protein
- Hemp + almond butter smoothie, edamame, soy protein

## Rest Day Meal Adjustment

### What Changes on Rest Days

| Macro | Training Day | Rest Day | Why |
|-------|-------------|---------|-----|
| Protein | 150–180g | 150–180g | Muscle protein turnover continues |
| Carbs | 300–380g | 250–300g | Less glycogen demand |
| Fat | 70–85g | 75–90g | Slightly higher acceptable |
| Calories | 2,600–2,800 | 2,300–2,500 | Slight reduction appropriate |

**Key principle:** Protein stays constant on rest days. Only carbs meaningfully decrease.

**Rest day meal shift:**
- Remove the high-carb post-workout component
- Shift toward fat + protein meals (slower, more satisfying)
- Legumes + seeds heavy
- Reduce fruit intake slightly (swap 1 fruit serving for additional vegetable)

## High-Calorie Days (Intentional Surplus Days)

When weight has been flat for 2+ weeks and a caloric nudge is needed, use these calorie-dense vegan additions:

| Addition | Calories | Protein | How |
|---------|----------|---------|-----|
| 2 tbsp almond butter | 200 | 7g | Add to oatmeal or smoothie |
| ½ avocado | 120 | 2g | Add to any bowl |
| 3 tbsp hemp seeds | 170 | 10g | Sprinkle on any meal |
| 1 oz dark chocolate (85%) | 170 | 2g | Snack |
| 4 medjool dates | 280 | 2g | Pre/post workout |
| 1 oz walnuts | 185 | 4g | Snack |
| Drizzle of EVOO (1 tbsp) | 120 | 0g | On any dish |
| 1 cup edamame | 188 | 18g | Snack or bowl addition |

Adding 3–4 of these per day = +500–700 cal without changing meal structure.

## Weekly Review Checklist (Nutrition)

**Every Sunday, Sage should review:**

- [ ] Did protein hit target ≥5/7 days?
- [ ] Did water hit target ≥5/7 days?
- [ ] Was weekly weigh-in consistent with target trend (0.3–0.7 lbs/week gain)?
- [ ] Was there enough cuisine variety (3+ different cuisines)?
- [ ] Did any single protein source dominate (>4/7 days = flag)?
- [ ] Were at least 2 servings of fermented foods per day on average?
- [ ] Was fiber target (~40g) approximately hit?
- [ ] Any micronutrient concerns (no greens all week, no vitamin C sources, etc.)?
- [ ] Supplements taken consistently?

**Adjustments based on review:**
- Protein consistently low → add protein shake to daily routine
- Weight gain too fast → remove 200 cal from carb sources
- Weight gain too slow → add 200 cal (see high-calorie additions above)
- Cuisine monotony → next week's plan must include 2 new cuisine days
