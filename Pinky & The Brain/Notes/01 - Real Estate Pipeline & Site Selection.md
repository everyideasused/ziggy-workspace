# 01 - Real Estate Pipeline & Site Selection

> **Phase 1 of 10** | Predecessor: Program/Client Engagement | Successor: [[02 - Feasibility & Due Diligence]]

---

## Purpose

This phase covers everything from initial market identification through securing site control. It is the entry point of the construction lifecycle and determines the universe of constraints for every subsequent phase.

---

## Key Activities

### Market Analysis & Site Identification
- **Demographic analysis:** Population density, traffic counts, income levels, growth projections
- **Competitive landscape:** Existing comparable developments, planned projects, market saturation
- **Location scoring:** Proximity to transportation, anchor tenants, complementary uses
- **Zoning pre-screening:** Is the intended use permitted by right, or will a variance/rezoning be needed?

### Site Evaluation (Preliminary)
- **Physical characteristics:** Lot size, topography, access points, visibility, existing structures
- **Utility availability:** Water, sewer, gas, electric, telecom — capacity and proximity to connections
- **Environmental red flags:** Brownfield indicators, flood zones (FEMA), wetlands, protected species
- **Title review (preliminary):** Easements, encumbrances, liens, deed restrictions

### Deal Structure & Letters of Intent
- **Purchase agreement** or **ground lease** negotiation
- **LOI (Letter of Intent):** Non-binding summary of key terms preceding formal contract
- **Earnest money / option payments:** Securing exclusivity during due diligence
- **Contingency periods:** Inspection, financing, entitlement contingencies with defined timelines
- **Assignment rights:** Critical for developers who may transfer to an entity or partner

### Retail-Specific Pipeline Considerations
- **Lease vs. purchase:** Most retail construction is tenant-driven on leased premises
- **Landlord approval process:** LOD (Letter of Direction) or landlord consent to improvements
- **TIA negotiation:** Tenant improvement allowance terms are set during lease negotiation (see [[Tenant Improvement Allowances]])
- **Site criteria packages:** National retailers provide detailed site requirements (pad size, parking ratios, drive-through geometry, visibility)
- **Real estate committee approvals:** Corporate approval gates before lease execution

### Residential-Specific Pipeline Considerations
- **Land entitlement value:** Raw land vs. entitled land pricing differential
- **Subdivision requirements:** Plat approval, infrastructure dedication
- **Homebuilder lot takedown schedules:** Phased lot purchases tied to absorption

### Civil/Infrastructure-Specific Pipeline Considerations
- **Public funding mechanisms:** Bond issues, grant programs, tax increment financing
- **Right-of-way acquisition:** Eminent domain processes, easement negotiations
- **Environmental review (NEPA/SEPA):** Federal and state environmental review requirements

---

## Key Stakeholders in This Phase

| Stakeholder | Role |
|------------|------|
| Owner/Developer | Decision maker, secures site control |
| Real Estate Broker | Identifies sites, negotiates terms |
| Real Estate Attorney | Reviews/drafts purchase agreements, leases |
| Market Analyst | Provides demographic and competitive data |
| Environmental Consultant | Preliminary Phase I assessment |
| Civil Engineer (preliminary) | Confirms buildability, utility availability |
| Landlord (if leased) | Negotiates lease terms including TIA |

---

## Key Deliverables / Outputs

- [ ] Site identified and scored against program criteria
- [ ] LOI or purchase agreement executed with appropriate contingencies
- [ ] Preliminary zoning confirmation obtained
- [ ] Preliminary budget estimate (order-of-magnitude, +/- 30%)
- [ ] Lease terms sheet or LOI with TIA framework (retail/commercial)
- [ ] Site control secured (option, contract, or lease execution)

---

## Dependencies & Predecessors

| Dependency | Source | Impact if Missing |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Program/client capital budget | Client/owner | Cannot determine site budget parameters |
| Site criteria or prototype requirements | Client/brand | Cannot evaluate site suitability |
| Market authorization | Client leadership | Cannot commit earnest money or LOI |

---

## Common Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Zoning incompatibility discovered late | Medium | High (kills deal) | Pre-screen zoning before LOI |
| Environmental contamination | Low-Medium | Very High | Phase I ESA before going hard |
| Title defects or encumbrances | Low | High | Preliminary title search early |
| Unrealistic TIA expectations | Medium | Medium | Market-rate TIA benchmarking during negotiation |
| Competition acquires target site | Medium | High | Move quickly from identification to LOI |

---

## Duration Benchmarks

| Project Type | Typical Duration |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Single retail location | 2–4 weeks (site selection from approved list) |
| Retail rollout program (50+ sites) | Ongoing pipeline (continuous) |
| Ground-up commercial development | 3–12 months |
| Residential subdivision | 6–18 months (includes land entitlement) |
| Civil/infrastructure | 6–24 months (includes funding authorization) |

---

## Transition to Next Phase

**Gate criteria to advance to [[02 - Feasibility & Due Diligence]]:**
1. Site control is secured (contract, option, or LOI with exclusivity)
2. Preliminary zoning is compatible or a variance path is identified
3. Preliminary budget is within program parameters
4. Ownership/client has authorized due diligence expenditures
