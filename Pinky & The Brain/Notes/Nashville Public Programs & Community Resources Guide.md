## How to Use This Guide

Nashville’s Metropolitan Government (Metro), the State of Tennessee, and a deep network of community organizations offer hundreds of programs for Davidson County residents. This guide is organized by life category so you can jump directly to what’s relevant. Many programs serve all income levels; where eligibility restrictions exist, they are noted.

**Universal Quick-Reference Numbers:**

- **Dial 2-1-1** — Tennessee’s 24/7 community services helpline. A live operator will connect you to local resources for housing, food, jobs, health, and more.
- **Metro Social Services Main Line:** 615-862-6432
- **Metro Action Commission:** 615-862-8860
- **Nashville.gov** — Official Metro Government portal for all departments

## 1. Education & Lifelong Learning

### 1.1 Free & Tuition-Free College Programs

**Tennessee Promise**
Tennessee was the first state in the nation to offer tuition-free community and technical college. Tennessee Promise covers tuition and mandatory fees (as a last-dollar scholarship after Pell/HOPE) for up to five semesters at a community college or eight trimesters at a TCAT. Requirements include graduating from a TN high school, completing FAFSA, maintaining a 2.0 GPA, and performing 8 hours of community service per enrolled term. Nashville-area institutions include Nashville State Community College and TCAT Nashville.

- **Apply:** collegefortn.org or via the TSAC Student Portal
- **Mentoring Partner:** tnAchieves (tnachieves.org)

**Tennessee Reconnect**
For adults who never earned an associate degree or higher, TN Reconnect provides tuition-free community or technical college for up to five years. You must be a Tennessee resident for at least one year, be an independent student on the FAFSA, and not already hold a degree. This is the single most powerful free education benefit available to working adults in Nashville.

- **Apply:** Via the TSAC Student Portal → “Tennessee Reconnect”
- **Nashville State Reconnect Advisor** available for one-on-one support

**Nashville GRAD (Getting Results by Advancing Degrees)**
A Metro-funded program at Nashville State modeled after CUNY’s ASAP initiative. Nashville GRAD provides wraparound support services — childcare assistance, nutrition support, textbooks, transportation, emergency financial assistance, and dedicated advising — to help Promise and Reconnect students actually complete their degrees. This addresses the reality that free tuition alone isn’t enough when students face systemic barriers.

- **Info:** nscc.edu → Foundation → Campaigns

### 1.2 Adult Education & GED/HiSET

Free in-person GED/HiSET preparation classes with free textbooks and bus passes. Opportunities for employment training and career certifications are also available.

- Vinyl record borrowing, artwork borrowing, and board game lending
- Free access to LinkedIn Learning, digital newspapers/magazines, and research databases
- “Be Well at NPL” — free wellness programming at branch locations (yoga, mindfulness, health workshops)

- “Jobs Lab” — one-on-one computer help, resume assistance, and extended computer time at multiple branches. Book an appointment with a librarian for personalized job search support.
- Free meeting room space for local nonprofit organizations and government agencies
- Curriculum Kits (100+ themed kits with up to 50 items each) available for checkout

**Digital Inclusion Programs:**

- **Cyber Seniors** — A five-week course for adults 50+ providing technology training, one-on-one mentoring, and a free tablet upon graduation. Supported by the West End Home Foundation and GFiber.
- **TechTies: Connected Services for the Community** — A $1.9M state-funded program providing technology training, free laptops, apprenticeship and job opportunities, and virtual health monitoring at a single Metro location (MAC facility at 1281 Murfreesboro Pike).

## 2. Housing & Property

### 2.1 Property Tax Relief Programs

Nashville has multiple layers of property tax assistance, which is especially important given the 2025 countywide reassessment.

**Metro Property Tax Relief Program**
Qualifying homeowners age 65+, disabled homeowners, and 100% disabled veterans (or surviving spouses) receive tax relief. Income limit for 2025: $37,530 for seniors/disabled. Disabled veterans have a higher property value cap ($175,000 market value basis). No repayment required.

- **Apply by:** April 5, 2026 (for Tax Year 2025)
- **Contact:** Office of the Metropolitan Trustee, 615-862-6330
- **Location:** Howard Office Building, 700 President Ronald Reagan Way, Suite 220

**Property Tax Freeze Program**
Locks in your property tax at the base-year amount permanently, regardless of future rate increases or reappraisals. Same eligibility categories as Tax Relief. Once approved, your taxes generally will not increase as long as you continue to qualify.

**Property Tax Deferral Program**
Allows homeowners to delay paying some or all property taxes until financial circumstances improve or the property is sold.

**Housing Resiliency Fund (The Housing Fund)**
Funded in part by Amazon’s Housing Equity initiative, this grant program helps pay for increases to homeowners’ property tax bills since the most recent reassessment. Operates as a grant — no repayment required. Available to Davidson County homeowners and small landlords providing affordable housing.

- **Info:** thehousingfund.org/property-tax-relief

### 2.2 Homebuyer Assistance

**Tennessee Housing Development Agency (THDA) Programs**
THDA oversees the state’s major first-time homebuyer programs including down payment assistance (forgivable or deferred second mortgages), low-interest mortgage loans, and Mortgage Credit Certificates (MCCs) for tax savings. A THDA-approved homebuyer education course is required.

- **Info:** thda.org

**MDHA HOME Investment Partnerships Program**
Funds affordable homeownership projects for households earning ≤80% of Area Median Income (AMI). Works through qualified developers to provide affordable homes for sale.

- **Contact:** MDHA Community Development Department

### 2.3 Rental & Housing Assistance

**MDHA Rental Assistance Voucher Program**
Provides resources for qualifying tenants and landlords through housing choice vouchers.

**Nesterly Homesharing Program**
Matches people with extra space in their homes with guests seeking long-term, affordable, healthy, and safe housing.

**MDHA Home Repair Programs**
For qualifying Davidson County homeowners — Accessibility, HVAC, Homeowner Rehabilitation, Roof, and Window programs for various home repairs.

**Affordable Housing Accelerator Program**
Connects emerging affordable housing developers and contractors with experts to increase capacity for affordable housing development.

**Faith-Based Development Initiative**
Equips faith-based institutions with tools, resources, and expertise to convert underutilized land into affordable housing.

- **Info:** nashville.gov/departments/mayor/how-nashville-helps

## 3. Health & Wellness

### 3.1 Metro Public Health Department Services

Metro Health operates three preventive health care centers serving Davidson County residents on a sliding fee scale.

**Locations:**

- Lentz Public Health Center (main campus)
- East Public Health Center
- Woodbine Public Health Center

**Services available (many at no cost or sliding scale):**

- Immunizations for children and adults (free federal vaccine for those who qualify)
- Family planning, pregnancy testing, and birth control counseling
- STD testing and treatment
- TB testing
- Breast and cervical cancer screening
- WIC clinic (nutrition assistance for women, infants, and children)
- Dental care for children (age 20 and below); limited adult emergency dental care
- Dental Sealant Program in selected Davidson County schools
- Pharmacy services (retail and redistribution center)
- **Info:** nashville.gov/departments/health | 615-340-5616

### 3.2 Behavioral Health & Mental Health

**Metro Health Department Behavioral Health Services**
Free behavioral health services including mental health screening, substance use/abuse screening, and referrals. Licensed mental health clinicians available by appointment at Lentz, East, and Woodbine centers. Walk-ins seen as time permits. No insurance required.

- Teen alcohol/drug and mental health screenings (ages 13-17) at no charge: 615-340-2172

**Metro Social Services Mental Health Support**
Dedicated team helping individuals facing mental health barriers or criminal background challenges access resources for independent living and well-being.

- **Contact:** 615-862-6432

**Mental Health Cooperative (MHC)**
Nashville’s largest community behavioral health provider. Whole-person support for mental health, substance use, primary care, and essential life services. Accepts TennCare and uninsured patients. Walk-in enrollment available.

- **New enrollment hours:** Monday 8AM-6PM; Tuesday-Friday 8AM-8PM
- **24-hour crisis walk-in center** available in Davidson County

**Key Helplines:**

- **Tennessee REDLINE:** 1-800-889-9789 — 24/7 alcohol, drug, gambling, and addiction information/referrals
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline:** Dial 988
- **Vets4Warriors:** 855-898-8255 (peer-to-peer counseling for all military members and veterans)

### 3.3 Healthcare for the Uninsured

**Project Access Nashville**
Links uninsured Davidson County residents to a network of approximately 35 primary health, dental, mental health, and substance abuse clinics that serve patients based on ability to pay. Includes pharmacy coverage and specialty care access.

- **Requirements:** Must be uninsured and live in Davidson County
- **Apply:** Pick up an application at Lentz Public Health Center or any Metro Health clinic
- **Info:** nashville.gov → Health → Project Access Nashville

**Health Assist Tennessee**
Provides under- and uninsured Tennesseans with information and access to low/no-cost community-based health care resources including medical, dental, vision, prescription, and mental health services.

**Community Health Centers (Sliding Scale)**
Nashville has 22+ free and income-based clinics including Neighborhood Health (multiple locations), Siloam Health, United Neighborhood Health Services, and Meharry Medical College clinics. Most offer sliding-scale fees based on household income.

## 5. Transportation

### 5.1 WeGo Public Transit

Nashville’s public transit system is undergoing its most significant expansion in decades through the voter-approved **Choose How You Move** program, funded by a dedicated ½-cent sales surcharge.

**Current Service Highlights (Winter/Summer 2026):**

- Increased weekday and weekend frequency on key routes (West End, Hillsboro, Nolensville Pike)
- Extended Sunday service hours on multiple routes
- New WeGo Link zones for first/last-mile connections
- Service running past 1 AM on Sundays on several routes
- 7.3% ridership increase year-over-year (Nov 2025)
- 12 new buses already in service; 25 more ordered for September 2026 delivery

**WeGo Access**
Door-to-door transportation for riders with disabilities, now with improved weekend availability seven days a week.

**Journey Pass (Fare-Free Program)**
Provides free transit to Davidson County residents living on limited income. Phase 1 (currently active) serves residents already receiving social services from Metro departments. Phase 2 expansion planned for 2026 with broader eligibility. Journey Passes are loaded onto WeGo QuickTicket cards.

- **Info:** WeGoTransit.com | journeypass@nashville.gov | 615-862-5150

### 5.2 Choose How You Move — Beyond Transit

The Choose How You Move program extends well beyond bus service:

- Approximately **86 miles of new sidewalks** across Nashville neighborhoods
- **~600 intersections** receiving new traffic signals tied to a new Transportation Management Center
- Safety improvements citywide
- Future bus rapid transit corridors (pending legislative approval for three corridors)
- **Info:** nashville.gov/transit

## 7. Community & Social Services

### 7.1 Metro Social Services

Metro Social Services is Davidson County’s direct-service agency for residents in need. Services are available to all Davidson County residents age 18+ regardless of income for some programs, with income-based eligibility for others.

**Family Support Services Program**
Case management for individuals and families at imminent risk of homelessness. Helps find housing, jobs, and navigate service delivery systems. No direct financial assistance, but provides critical connections and referrals.

- **Contact:** 615-862-6432

**Nutritional Supplements**
Available through Metro Social Services for qualifying residents.

### 7.2 Metro Action Commission (MAC)

MAC is Nashville’s Community Action Agency, using a Whole Family/Two-Generation approach to break cycles of poverty.

**Household Assistance Programs** (income eligibility required):

- **LIHEAP** (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program) — Helps with energy payments. Crisis criteria applicants served first; waitlist for others.
- **Loss of Income Assistance** — Mortgage, rent, water, prescriptions, special diet food, and delinquent property tax payments for those who lost income within 60 days
- **Senior Citizen Assistance** (60+) — Mortgage, rent, water, prescriptions, special diet food, and property tax help
- **Homeless Transition Assistance** — Deposits for rent, electric, and water for individuals who have secured housing after being homeless

**Head Start & Early Head Start**
Comprehensive early childhood education for income-eligible families.

- **Application:** nashville.gov/departments/metro-action → Application for Services
- **Hours:** In-person 8 AM - 2:45 PM, M-F; Call center open until 4:30 PM
- **Phone:** 615-862-8860

### 7.3 Dial 2-1-1

Tennessee’s 2-1-1 service connects you to a live, trained specialist who can sort out your needs and provide phone numbers and addresses for help centers near you. Covers housing, food, health, utility assistance, disaster recovery, and more.

### 7.4 Disaster Recovery

**Voluntary Organizations Active in Disaster (VOAD)**
Brings together critical support organizations after any impactful disaster in Nashville. Also a hub for volunteering after disasters.

**Community Foundation of Middle Tennessee (CFMT)**
Serves as a philanthropic leader, catalyst, and resource, often activating emergency funds after major events.

## 16. How Nashville Helps — Quick Situational Guide

**“I need a job.”**
→ Metro Social Services Workforce Readiness (615-862-6432) → Nashville Public Library Jobs Lab → American Job Center (TNWorkReady.com)

**“I want to go back to school but can’t afford it.”**
→ Tennessee Reconnect (tuition-free community/technical college for adults without a degree) → Nashville GRAD for wraparound support

**“I’m struggling to pay my property taxes.”**
→ Metro Trustee Tax Relief/Freeze (615-862-6330) → Housing Resiliency Fund (thehousingfund.org) → MAC Senior Assistance (if 60+)
