## HOME BUYING: COST REDUCTION

### Closing Costs (Negotiable Items)
Total closing costs: 2-5% of purchase price. On a $400K home = $8,000-20,000. Many line items are negotiable:
- **Origination fee**: Negotiate down or shop lenders. 0.5-1% of loan amount.
- **Title insurance**: Shop independently — don't just accept the lender's recommendation. Savings of $500-1,500.
- **Home inspection**: $300-500. Non-negotiable in importance — always get one. Negotiate repairs based on findings.
- **Appraisal**: $300-600. Required by lender.
- **Seller concessions**: Ask seller to cover 2-3% of closing costs. Common in buyer's markets.
- **Lender credits**: Accept a slightly higher interest rate in exchange for lender-paid closing costs. Worth it if you plan to refinance later.

### First-Time Buyer Programs
- **FHA loans**: 3.5% down payment, more flexible credit requirements. MIP (mortgage insurance) adds cost — refinance to conventional once at 20% equity.
- **VA loans**: 0% down, no PMI, competitive rates. For veterans, active duty, eligible spouses.
- **USDA loans**: 0% down for eligible rural/suburban areas. Income limits apply. Check eligibility map at usda.gov.
- **State/local programs**: Down payment assistance, first-time buyer grants. Check your state housing finance agency.
- **Employer assistance**: Some employers offer homebuying assistance (teachers, healthcare, first responders, corporate relocation programs).

### Mortgage Shopping
- **Get 3-5 quotes**: Rates vary 0.25-0.5% between lenders on the same day. Over 30 years on $300K, 0.25% = $15,000+ difference.
- **Credit unions**: Often 0.125-0.375% lower than banks.
- **Online lenders**: Better.com, LoanDepot, Rocket Mortgage — competitive, fast. Compare to local.
- **Mortgage brokers**: Shop multiple lenders on your behalf. Sometimes find rates unavailable direct.
- **Lock timing**: Lock your rate when satisfied. Rates change daily.

## MOVING

### Moving Cost Reduction
- **DIY (truck rental)**: U-Haul, Penske, Budget. $50-2,000+ depending on distance. Cheapest option.
- **Portable containers**: PODS, U-Pack, 1-800-PACK-RAT. $2,000-5,000 for long-distance. You pack, they move.
- **Full-service movers**: $1,000-5,000 local, $3,000-10,000+ long-distance. Get 3 quotes minimum.
- **Negotiation**: Moving companies negotiate. Get competing quotes, ask for price match.
- **Timing**: Avoid summer (June-August), end/beginning of month, and weekends — peak pricing. Mid-month weekday moves = cheapest.
- **Tax deduction**: Moving expenses are no longer deductible for most people (2018 tax reform). Exception: active-duty military.

### Moving Supplies
- **Free boxes**: Liquor stores, grocery stores (ask when they break down stock), Craigslist/Facebook "free moving boxes," Buy Nothing groups, Nextdoor.
- **Supplies**: Walmart and Home Depot have cheapest boxes/tape if buying new. Dollar Tree for packing tape.

## PROPERTY TAX APPEAL

### How to Appeal (Save Hundreds to Thousands Annually)
- **Check your assessment**: County assessor's website shows your home's assessed value.
- **Compare to recent sales**: If comparable homes sold for less than your assessed value, you have grounds to appeal.
- **File a protest**: Most counties have a formal appeal process. Deadlines are strict — check yours.
- **Evidence needed**: Comparable sales data, photos of property issues, independent appraisal if significant discrepancy.
- **Success rate**: 30-50% of appeals result in reduction. Average savings: $500-2,000/year.
- **Services**: If you don't want to DIY, property tax appeal companies (Ownwell, NTPTS) handle it for a percentage of savings (typically 25-40% of first-year savings).

## WEDDING COST REDUCTION

### The Biggest Levers
- **Day of week**: Saturday evening = most expensive. Friday evening, Sunday afternoon = 20-40% less. Weekday = 40-60% less.
- **Season**: June and October = peak. January-March = off-season = significant venue discounts.
- **Venue type**: Traditional wedding venues have "wedding markup." Restaurants, parks, community centers, Airbnb properties = dramatically less for equivalent experience.
- **Guest count**: The single biggest cost driver. Every additional guest = $75-250+ in food, drink, favors, etc.
- **Photography**: Biggest vendor expense after venue. Book emerging photographers (excellent work, building portfolio, $1,500-3,000 vs. $4,000-10,000 for established).

### Vendor Negotiation
- **Everything is negotiable**: Venues, photographers, florists, DJs, caterers — all have flexibility, especially off-season.
- **Package deals**: Venues that include catering, tables, chairs, linens = simpler negotiation and often better total value.
- **DIY elements**: Centerpieces, invitations (Canva + VistaPrint), wedding favors, signage, playlist instead of DJ.
- **Flowers**: Grocery store flowers (Trader Joe's, Costco, Whole Foods) for DIY arrangements at 70-80% below florist pricing. Dried/preserved flowers are trending and cheaper. Silk flowers have improved dramatically.
- **Costco party supplies**: Sheet cakes, appetizer platters, alcohol in bulk.

### Registry & Completion Discounts
- **Completion discounts**: After the wedding, most registries offer 10-20% completion discount on remaining items. Amazon (20% for Prime, 10% without), Target (15%), Crate & Barrel (10%), Bed Bath registry services (varies).
- **Cash registries**: Zola, Honeyfund — ask for cash/experiences instead of physical gifts if preferred.
- **Strategic registry timing**: Register early, add aspirational items. Use completion discount post-wedding to buy household items at 10-20% off.

## GIFTING INTELLIGENCE

### Gift Cards (When You Give Them)
- **Buy discounted**: Costco, Sam's Club, Raise.com — buy gift cards below face value to give as gifts. $100 restaurant card for $79.99.
- **Promotional periods**: Many retailers run gift card bonuses during holidays (buy $50, get $10 bonus card). Olive Garden, Chili's, Starbucks, and others do this November-December.

### Flowers
- **Direct from farms**: FarmGirl Flowers, The Bouqs, UrbanStems — direct farm-to-door, 30-50% below FTD/1-800-Flowers with fresher flowers.
- **Trader Joe's / Costco**: In-store bouquets for $5-15 that rival $40-60 delivered arrangements.
- **Avoid**: FTD, 1-800-Flowers, Teleflora for everyday sending. Use only if recipient is in a location where local delivery is needed and these services have reliable local florist partners.

## KIDS' CLOTHING

### The Fast-Growth Problem
Kids outgrow clothes every 3-6 months. Buying new at full retail is burning money.

### Best Sources for Kids' Clothing
- **ThredUp**: Online consignment. Kids' clothing at 50-90% off retail. Brand names available.
- **Once Upon A Child**: Physical resale stores. Buy and sell kids' clothing, toys, equipment.
- **Facebook Marketplace / Buy Nothing groups**: Free or near-free kids' clothing from families who've outgrown sizes.
- **Consignment sales**: Seasonal consignment events (Just Between Friends, Kids Closet Connection) — massive selection, 50-80% off retail.
- **Primary.com**: New basics without logos at reasonable prices. Good for mix-and-match.
- **Carter's/OshKosh**: Nearly always on sale. Never pay full price — 40-60% off sales run constantly. Stack with coupons.
- **Target Cat & Jack**: Excellent quality-to-price. Guaranteed 1-year quality (return if they wear out).

### Size-Up Strategy
For outerwear, shoes, and seasonal items: buy one size up at end-of-season clearance for next year. Kids' winter coats at 70% off in February = perfect for next winter.

## YOUTH SPORTS & ACTIVITIES

### Equipment Cost Management
- **Play It Again Sports**: Used sporting goods chain. Equipment at 40-70% off new.
- **Facebook Marketplace**: Parents constantly selling outgrown equipment.
- **Equipment swaps**: Many leagues and communities organize equipment swap events.
- **Don't buy top-tier for beginners**: Kids trying a sport don't need professional-grade equipment. Buy entry-level or used until commitment is established.

### League & Activity Cost
- **Parks & Recreation leagues**: $50-150/season. Coached, organized, recreational level.
- **Club/travel sports**: $500-5,000+/season. Only pursue if child is genuinely passionate and talented. Many families overspend on travel sports for children who don't continue past middle school.
- **Scholarship and financial aid**: Nearly every youth organization has need-based scholarships. Ask directly.

| Science center | $15-25/person | $100-200/family | 3-4 visits |

### Reciprocal Benefits
- **AZA (zoos)**: Membership at one zoo often grants free/discounted admission at 100+ zoos nationwide.
- **ASTC (science centers)**: Science museum membership grants admission at 300+ museums.
- **ACM (children's museums)**: Reciprocal admission at participating children's museums.
- **Check before traveling**: Look up reciprocal partner institutions at your travel destination.

### Library (The Ultimate Free Family Resource)
Your library card provides free access to:
- Books, audiobooks, ebooks (Libby/OverDrive)
- Museum/zoo/garden passes (many libraries lend these)
- Movie streaming (Kanopy, Hoopla)
- Music streaming (Hoopla, Freegal)
- Online learning (LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, Rosetta Stone)
- Kids' programs (story time, STEM clubs, coding, crafts)
- 3D printing, recording studios, maker spaces (at some libraries)
- Seed libraries, tool libraries, equipment lending
- **All free with a library card.** This may be the single highest-value free resource available to families.

## WHY SECONDHAND FIRST

### The Depreciation Gift
Most consumer goods lose 50-80% of their value the moment they leave the store. The secondary market lets you buy those same goods at depreciated prices while they still have 80-95% of their useful life remaining.

### Physical Secondhand Sources
- **Goodwill / Salvation Army / Thrift stores**: Furniture, housewares, clothing, books, electronics. Quality varies dramatically by location. Stores in affluent neighborhoods = better inventory.
- **Habitat for Humanity ReStore**: Building materials, fixtures, appliances, furniture donated from renovations and overstock. Dramatically below retail.
- **Estate sales**: EstateSales.net to find local sales. Arrive early for best selection. Last day = best prices (50-75% off listed prices typical).
- **Garage sales / yard sales**: Best on Saturday mornings. Multi-family sales = best selection. Negotiate everything.
- **Consignment shops**: Curated secondhand. Higher prices than thrift but better quality control.
- **Buy Nothing groups**: Facebook/app-based community gifting. Completely free items from neighbors.
- **Library of Things / Tool Libraries**: Some communities have lending libraries for tools, kitchen equipment, camping gear, and more. Free to borrow.
- **Auction houses**: Local auctions for furniture, equipment, tools. Estate auctions can yield extraordinary value.

### Secondhand Buying Best Practices
- **Test electronics before paying** (or buy from platforms with return policies)
- **Check serial numbers**: StolenPhone.org for phones, manufacturer databases for electronics
- **Meet in public**: Police station safe exchange zones for marketplace transactions
- **Cash = leverage**: "I have cash right now" moves prices down 10-20%
- **If listed >2 weeks**: Offer 25-40% below asking. Seller is motivated.
- **Inspect furniture carefully**: Check for bed bugs (seams, crevices), structural damage, odors
- **Avoid used**: Car seats (safety), mattresses (hygiene), helmets (integrity), cribs (safety standards)

## GOLDEN RULES: SECONDHAND

1. **Check secondhand BEFORE buying new.** Facebook Marketplace, eBay, Swappa for 5 minutes.
2. **Manufacturer refurbished = best value** in electronics, appliances, and tools.
3. **Estate sales for furniture, tools, and housewares.** Last day = deepest discounts.
4. **Buy Nothing groups** for free items from neighbors. Free is the best price.
5. **Thrift stores in affluent neighborhoods** have the best inventory.
6. **Always test electronics before paying** in person. Check serial numbers.
7. **Cash + immediacy = negotiating power** on marketplace platforms.
8. **Never buy used: car seats, mattresses, helmets, cribs.** Safety items only new.
9. **eBay "sold listings"** = the real market value of anything. Check before buying or selling.
10. **Habitat ReStore for building materials** before Home Depot/Lowe's.
