# Tenant Improvement Allowances

> Cross-cutting reference for [[Construction Program Management - Master Reference]]

---

## Definition

A **Tenant Improvement Allowance (TIA)** is a landlord-provided sum of money that reimburses the tenant for construction costs incurred in customizing a leased commercial space. Also called TI, TIA, TA, or Construction Allowance.

---

## TIA Structure

### How TIA Is Expressed
- **Per rentable square foot (RSF):** e.g., "$45/RSF on 3,200 RSF = $144,000"
- **Lump sum:** e.g., "$150,000 Construction Allowance"
- **Percentage of costs:** e.g., "Landlord covers 50% of eligible costs up to $X"

### Typical TIA Ranges (Market-Dependent)
| Space Type | TIA Range ($/RSF) | Notes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| Shell condition (first-gen) | $25-60+ | Complete build-out from bare shell |
| Second-generation (prior tenant) | $10-30 | Partial reuse of existing improvements |
| Refresh/remodel | $5-15 | Finishes and fixtures only |
| Restaurant | $40-80+ | Heavy MEP, kitchen, grease trap |
| Medical/dental | $50-100+ | Specialized MEP, radiology, infection control |

**Note:** These ranges vary enormously by market, building class, landlord motivation, and tenant creditworthiness.

---

## TIA-Eligible Expenses (Typical)

### Usually Covered
- Architectural and engineering design fees
- Permit fees
- Demolition of existing improvements
- Framing and drywall
- Flooring (tile, carpet, LVT)
- Ceiling systems
- Painting
- MEP modifications (HVAC, plumbing, electrical)
- Fire protection/sprinkler modifications
- Lighting and electrical devices
- Millwork and built-in casework
- ADA compliance upgrades
- Code-required improvements

### Usually NOT Covered
- Furniture, fixtures, and equipment (FF&E)
- Technology (computers, POS systems, AV equipment)
- Signage (sometimes covered, depends on lease)
- Moving costs
- Inventory and merchandise
- Soft costs like broker fees (sometimes covered)
- Security deposits on utilities

**Always verify eligible expenses in the lease Work Letter.**

## Build-Out Control Models

| Model | Who Controls | Pros | Cons |
|-------|-------------|------|------|
| **Turnkey** | Landlord manages everything | Hands-off for tenant; landlord guarantees cost | Limited customization; landlord controls budget |
| **Tenant-Controlled** | Tenant manages construction | Full control over scope, quality, contractor | Tenant assumes project management burden |
| **Landlord-Controlled with Tenant Input** | Landlord manages; tenant reviews | Balance of control | Potential for misalignment on priorities |

## TIA Disbursement Methods

### Reimbursement After Completion
- Tenant pays all costs, then submits documentation for reimbursement
- Landlord typically requires: invoices, lien waivers, CO, architect certification
- **Risk:** Cash flow burden on tenant; reimbursement processing delays

### Progress Payments (Draws)
- TIA disbursed in stages as construction progresses
- Tied to milestones (e.g., 50% complete, substantial completion, final completion)
- Each draw requires: conditional lien waivers, progress documentation
- **Better for tenants:** Reduces cash flow burden

### Lump Sum at Lease Signing
- Entire TIA paid upfront (less common)
- Landlord may require security deposit or strong credit

### Landlord Direct Payment to Contractors
- Landlord pays GC/subs directly up to TIA cap
- Tenant pays any amount above the TIA
- **Advantage:** May avoid TIA being treated as taxable income to tenant

## TIA Administration Workflow

```
Lease Executed (TIA terms set) →
Tenant Engages Architect → Design Approved by Landlord →
Tenant Engages GC → Landlord Reviews GC Contract →
Construction Begins →
[If Progress Payments]: Submit Draw Requests at Milestones →
Construction Complete → Final TIA Draw Submitted →
Landlord Reviews: CO + Lien Waivers + Invoices →
Final TIA Payment Released →
Reconcile: TIA Used vs. Allowance → Document Overage/Credit
```

### Required Documentation per Draw
- [ ] AIA G702/G703 pay application (or landlord's required form)
- [ ] Conditional lien waivers (GC + all subs)
- [ ] Unconditional lien waivers for previous draws
- [ ] Invoices and receipts for current work
- [ ] Progress photos
- [ ] Architect's certification of progress (if required)
- [ ] Updated budget summary showing TIA draw vs. total project costs

### Final TIA Draw Additional Requirements
- [ ] Certificate of Occupancy or TCO
- [ ] Unconditional final lien waivers from GC and ALL subs
- [ ] As-built drawings (sometimes required)
- [ ] Final reconciliation of all costs

## TIA Negotiation Strategies

1. **Know the market:** Research comparable TIA offers in the submarket
2. **Longer lease = higher TIA:** Landlords amortize TIA over the lease term
3. **Tenant credit matters:** Stronger credit = more generous TIA
4. **Negotiate unused TIA disposition:** Try to get unused TIA applied as rent credit
5. **Include soft costs:** Push for design and permit fees to be TIA-eligible
6. **Cap the management fee:** Landlords often charge 3-5% management fee against TIA
7. **Align TIA disbursement with cash flow:** Negotiate progress payments, not just reimbursement
8. **Specify rent commencement triggers clearly:** Tie rent start to CO or completion, not a fixed date (if possible)

---

## TIA Accounting Considerations

Under ASC 842 (current lease accounting standard):
- TIA is classified as a **lease incentive**
- If paid at or before lease commencement: reduces the Right-of-Use (ROU) asset
- If paid after commencement: reduces both the lease liability and ROU asset
- TIA is NOT recorded as revenue or income by the tenant
- Amortized over the lease term through reduced lease expense

## Common TIA Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|
| TIA insufficient for scope | Get contractor estimates BEFORE signing lease |
| Slow reimbursement from landlord | Negotiate progress payments and processing timelines in lease |
| Landlord disputes eligible expenses | Define eligible expenses explicitly in Work Letter |
| Missing lien waivers delay payment | Track lien waivers systematically from Day 1 |
| Change orders consume TIA buffer | Establish change management process; maintain contingency |
| Rent commencement before build-out complete | Negotiate completion-based (not date-based) rent start |
