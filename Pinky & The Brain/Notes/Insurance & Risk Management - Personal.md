# Insurance & Risk Management - Personal

> Cross-cutting reference for [[Personal Finance Knowledge Base]]
> **Priority:** 🟢 Ongoing — protects everything you've built. Review annually.

---

## The Purpose of Insurance

Insurance exists to transfer **catastrophic, low-probability risk** away from you and onto an insurance company in exchange for a predictable premium. You're not trying to make money on insurance — you're preventing financial ruin from events you can't absorb.

**The golden rule:** Insure against catastrophe, self-insure against inconvenience. A $500 deductible you can pay from your emergency fund is not worth insuring. A $500,000 medical bill or a lawsuit is.

---

## Essential Coverage Tiers

### Tier 1: Non-Negotiable (Have These Now)

**Health Insurance**
- The single most important insurance you carry. Medical debt is the #1 cause of bankruptcy in the US.
- Get the best plan your employer offers or shop the ACA marketplace.
- If choosing between plans: HDHPs (High-Deductible Health Plans) paired with an HSA can be excellent for healthy, young adults — lower premiums + triple tax-advantaged savings.
- Never go uninsured, even temporarily.

**Auto Insurance**
- Required by law in most states. Tennessee requires liability minimums of 25/50/15.
- Minimum liability limits are rarely enough. Recommended: 100/300/100 or higher.
- If your car is paid off and worth less than $5,000-$10,000, consider dropping collision/comprehensive and self-insuring.
- Uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage is critical — protects you from other drivers who are uninsured or underinsured.

**Renters or Homeowners Insurance**
- Renters insurance is cheap ($15-30/month) and covers personal property, liability, and additional living expenses if displaced.
- Homeowners insurance covers the structure, personal property, liability, and living expenses.
- Review coverage annually. Make sure dwelling coverage keeps pace with construction costs, not just home value.
- Document possessions with photos/video for claims. Store documentation off-site (cloud).

### Tier 2: Strongly Recommended

**Umbrella Insurance**
- Extra liability coverage above auto and homeowners limits. Typically $1-2 million.
- Costs $150-300/year for $1M coverage — extraordinarily cheap for the protection.
- Protects personal assets if you're sued for more than your auto/home policies cover.
- Increasingly important as net worth grows.
- Available from most auto/home insurers as a bundle.

**Disability Insurance**
- Protects your most valuable asset: your ability to earn income.
- Long-term disability insurance replaces 50-60% of income if you can't work due to illness or injury.
- Check if your employer provides group LTD — many do, but coverage may be insufficient.
- If self-employed or employer coverage is limited, consider individual LTD. It's expensive but critical.
- Short-term disability is less essential if your emergency fund can cover 3-6 months.

**Life Insurance (if you have dependents)**
- If anyone depends on your income (spouse, children, aging parents), you need life insurance.
- **Term life insurance** is the right choice for the vast majority of people: covers a specific period (20-30 years), cheap, simple.
- Rule of thumb: 10-15× annual income for coverage amount.
- **Do NOT buy whole life, universal life, or variable life insurance** unless a fee-only financial planner with a fiduciary duty specifically recommends it for estate planning purposes. These products are expensive, complex, and heavily commissioned. The investment component almost always underperforms buying term and investing the difference.
- If no one depends on your income, you likely don't need life insurance.

### Tier 3: Situational

**Identity Theft Protection**
- Free credit monitoring is available from most credit card issuers and Credit Karma.
- Freezing your credit with all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) is free and the most effective protection.
- Paid identity theft insurance may be included in your homeowners or renters policy.

**Pet Insurance**
- Relevant if you have pets. Emergency vet bills can easily reach $5,000-$15,000.
- Best purchased when pets are young and healthy.
- Compare premiums, deductibles, and coverage limits carefully.

**Flood / Earthquake Insurance**
- Standard homeowners policies exclude flood and earthquake.
- If in a flood zone or earthquake-prone area, separate policies are needed.
- Flood insurance is available through FEMA's National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or private insurers.

## Insurance Optimization Strategies

### Raise Deductibles
Higher deductibles = lower premiums. If your emergency fund can absorb $1,000-$2,500, raise your deductible to match. You're self-insuring the small stuff and letting the insurance handle the big stuff — which is the whole point.

### Bundle Policies
Auto + homeowners/renters with the same insurer typically saves 10-25%.

### Shop Annually
Insurance companies change their pricing algorithms regularly. What was the cheapest option last year may not be this year. Get quotes from 3-4 insurers annually.

### Review Coverage Limits
- After any major purchase (home, car) or life change (marriage, child, raise)
- Make sure liability limits keep pace with your net worth
- Increase umbrella coverage as wealth grows

### Don't Over-Insure
- Extended warranties on electronics: almost always a bad bet
- Credit card payment protection: waste of money
- Flight insurance: your life insurance already covers this
- Mortgage life insurance: regular term life is cheaper and more flexible

## The Annual Insurance Audit

During your annual financial review, check:

| Question | Action If No |
|----------|-------------|
| Is health insurance adequate for my current situation? | Review during open enrollment |
| Is auto liability at least 100/300/100? | Call agent and increase |
| Is renters/homeowners coverage current? | Update coverage amounts |
| Do I have umbrella coverage ($1M+)? | Get a quote — it's cheap |
| Is disability coverage at least 60% of income? | Check employer plan, consider individual |
| Is life insurance adequate for dependents? | Term life quote if needed |
| Are all deductibles aligned with my emergency fund? | Raise deductibles where comfortable |
| Have I shopped rates in the last 12 months? | Get competitive quotes |
