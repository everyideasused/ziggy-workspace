## Planck Units

Natural units where G = ℏ = c = k_B = 1. They define the scale where quantum gravity becomes important.

| Quantity | Expression | Value |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Planck length | √(ℏG/c³) | 1.616 × 10⁻³⁵ m |
| Planck time | √(ℏG/c⁵) | 5.391 × 10⁻⁴⁴ s |
| Planck mass | √(ℏc/G) | 2.176 × 10⁻⁸ kg |
| Planck energy | √(ℏc⁵/G) | 1.956 × 10⁹ J |
| Planck temperature | √(ℏc⁵/Gk_B²) | 1.417 × 10³² K |

Below the Planck length/time, our current physics breaks down. These are the frontiers where quantum gravity matters.

## The Hierarchy of Physics Theories

```
Quantum Gravity (?)
  ├── General Relativity (gravity, large scales)
  └── Quantum Field Theory
        ├── Standard Model
        │     ├── QED (electromagnetic force)
        │     ├── QCD (strong force)  
        │     └── Electroweak Theory (weak + EM)
        └── Condensed Matter QFT
              ├── BCS Superconductivity
              └── Quantum Hall Effect

Classical Limits:
  QM → Classical Mechanics (ℏ → 0)
  SR → Newtonian Mechanics (v << c)
  GR → Newtonian Gravity (weak field, low speed)
  Statistical Mechanics → Thermodynamics (N → large)
```

Each theory is an approximation valid in a specific regime. Physics is a nested hierarchy of increasingly accurate descriptions, with quantum gravity (still unknown) potentially at the top.
