# KNOWLEDGE BASE MODULE 05: VEHICLES — PURCHASE, LEASE & OWNERSHIP

## THE BIGGEST PURCHASE MOST PEOPLE MAKE (AFTER A HOUSE)
A $5,000 mistake on a car purchase is easy to make and hard to recover. This module prevents that.

---

## NEW CARS: THE BUYING SYSTEM

### When to Buy (Timing Is Everything)
- **End of month**: Salespeople have monthly quotas. Last 3-5 days = maximum motivation to deal.
- **End of quarter** (March, June, September, December): Dealership bonuses from manufacturers tied to quarterly volume. Extra leverage.
- **End of year** (December): Triple leverage — end of month + end of quarter + end of model year. December 26-31 is historically the best window to buy a new car.
- **Model year changeover** (August-October): When new model year arrives, outgoing models get clearance pricing. 10-20% below MSRP common.
- **Holiday weekends**: Memorial Day, Labor Day, 4th of July, Black Friday — dealers run promotional events with manufacturer incentives.
- **Monday-Thursday**: Less foot traffic = more attention from salespeople = more willingness to negotiate.

### Research Before Entering the Dealership
- **TrueCar**: Shows what others paid in your area. Provides a target price.
- **Edmunds**: Invoice pricing, true market value, incentive stacking calculator.
- **KBB (Kelley Blue Book)**: Fair purchase price range. Good for trade-in values.
- **CarGurus**: Listings with deal ratings (Great Deal, Good Deal, Fair, etc.).
- **Consumer Reports**: Reliability data and owner satisfaction scores. Subscription ($39/year) worth it for car-buying year.
- **NHTSA.gov**: Safety ratings and recall history. Check before purchasing any vehicle.

### Price Structure: What the Dealer Pays
- **Invoice price**: What the dealer pays the manufacturer. This is public information (Edmunds, TrueCar). Your target price is at or slightly above invoice.
- **Dealer holdback**: Manufacturers pay dealers 2-3% of MSRP as a hidden rebate. This means even at "invoice price," the dealer makes money.
- **Dealer incentives / bonuses**: Manufacturers offer volume bonuses to dealers. These are not public but create additional margin.
- **Destination charge**: Non-negotiable. Every car has it. Accept it.
- **Advertising fee**: Negotiable at some dealers. Push back.
- **Doc fee**: Varies by state (some states cap it). Ranges $0-800. Negotiate or find a lower-fee dealer.
- **Extended warranty / add-ons**: Maximum profit margin. Decline at the finance desk. If you want an extended warranty, buy third-party later (Endurance, CARCHEX) for 30-50% less.

### Negotiation Strategy
1. **Research invoice price** for your exact configuration (Edmunds)
2. **Get online quotes** from 3-5 dealers via email/website. Let them compete.
3. **Target price**: Invoice + $500-1,000 for common models. Invoice or below for slow sellers.
4. **Negotiate purchase price FIRST, then discuss trade-in and financing separately**. Dealers combine these to obscure the deal.
5. **Never negotiate monthly payment** — always negotiate total out-the-door (OTD) price.
6. **Walk away**: The single most powerful negotiating tool. If the deal isn't right, leave. They'll call.
7. **"What's your best out-the-door price?"** — This forces one number that includes all fees and taxes.

### Manufacturer Incentives (Stack These)
- **Cash rebates**: Manufacturer-to-consumer. Applied to price.
- **Low APR financing**: 0-2.9% from manufacturer. Often an either/or with cash rebate — do the math on which saves more.
- **Loyalty/conquest incentives**: Currently own a competing brand? Many manufacturers offer $500-1,500 conquest cash. Own same brand? Loyalty cash available.
- **Military, student, first responder**: $500-1,000 additional incentives from most manufacturers.
- **Check manufacturer website**: Current incentives are published monthly on manufacturer websites.

## USED CARS: WHERE THE REAL VALUE IS

### Depreciation Is Your Friend
- **Year 1**: A new car loses 20-30% of its value
- **Year 3**: Lost 40-50% of original value
- **Year 5**: Lost 55-65% of original value
- **Sweet spot**: 2-3 year old car with 20-40K miles. Modern car, most depreciation absorbed, warranty may still apply.

### Certified Pre-Owned (CPO) — The Best of Both Worlds
- CPO vehicles are used cars inspected and warrantied by the manufacturer.
- **Extended warranty**: Typically adds 1-2 years / 20-40K miles beyond original warranty.
- **Lower financing rates**: CPO often qualifies for new-car financing rates.
- **Worth the premium**: CPO premium over regular used is typically $1,000-2,000. The warranty and inspection often justify this.

### Where to Buy Used
- **CarMax**: No-haggle pricing. Large inventory. Quality inspection. 7-day return policy. Good option for people who hate negotiating.
- **Carvana / Vroom**: Online-only. Delivered to your door. 7-day return. Competitive pricing but inspect carefully upon delivery.
- **Dealership used lot**: Trade-ins and lease returns. Negotiate like a new car.
- **Private party**: Best prices, highest risk. No warranty. Must do your own inspection.
- **AutoTempest**: Aggregates listings from Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, Cars.com, AutoTrader, and more. Best search tool for used.
- **Facebook Marketplace**: Largest private-party used car marketplace. Negotiate freely.

### Used Car Inspection Checklist
Before buying any used car:
1. **Vehicle history report**: Carfax or AutoCheck. Check for accidents, title status, service history, odometer verification.
2. **Pre-purchase inspection (PPI)**: Pay a trusted mechanic $100-200 to inspect before purchasing. Non-negotiable for private party sales.
3. **Title status**: Clean title only. Avoid salvage, rebuilt, flood titles unless you're an expert and the price reflects it.
4. **Recall check**: NHTSA.gov — enter VIN to check for open recalls.

## LEASING: WHEN IT MAKES SENSE (AND WHEN IT DOESN'T)

### Leasing Makes Sense When:
- You want a new car every 2-3 years
- You drive under 12-15K miles/year
- You want lower monthly payments
- You don't want to deal with selling/trading
- Tax deduction for business use (consult accountant)

### Leasing Doesn't Make Sense When:
- You drive 15K+ miles/year (mileage overage fees are painful)
- You keep cars 5+ years (buying is dramatically cheaper long-term)
- You modify your vehicle
- You have poor credit (lease rates penalize low scores heavily)

### Lease Negotiation Secrets
- **Negotiate the capitalized cost (cap cost)**: This is the purchase price of the car. Negotiate it exactly like a new car purchase.
- **Money factor**: The interest rate on a lease, expressed as a decimal. Multiply by 2,400 to convert to APR equivalent. Negotiate this like an interest rate.
- **Residual value**: Set by the manufacturer, not negotiable. Higher residual = lower monthly payment.
- **MSD (Multiple Security Deposits)**: Some brands allow MSDs to lower your money factor. Refundable at lease end.
- **Lease transfer**: If you need to exit a lease early, use LeaseTrader or Swapalease to transfer the lease to someone else (avoids early termination fees).
- **Lease buyout at end**: If you like the car, compare buyout price to market value. Sometimes the buyout is below market = good deal to buy.

## FINANCING: DON'T LEAVE MONEY AT THE DEALERSHIP

### Get Pre-Approved Before Visiting
- **Credit union**: Almost always the best rates. Check your local CU, PenFed, Navy Federal (if eligible), DCU, Alliant.
- **Online lenders**: Capital One Auto, LightStream, Bank of America — competitive rates.
- **Walk in with pre-approval**: This forces the dealer to beat your rate. Often they can (manufacturers subsidize dealer financing), but now you have a floor.

### Rate Comparison Matters
On a $30,000 car financed over 60 months:
- 3.0% APR: Total interest = $2,323
- 5.0% APR: Total interest = $3,968
- 7.0% APR: Total interest = $5,664
- **2% difference = $1,600-3,300 over the loan**. Worth 30 minutes of rate shopping.

### Loan Term Strategy
- **36-48 months**: Best total cost. Higher monthly payment.
- **60 months**: Most common. Reasonable balance of payment and total cost.
- **72-84 months**: Low monthly payment BUT high total interest and risk of being underwater (owing more than car is worth). Avoid if possible.
- **Rule**: If you can't afford the 60-month payment, you're buying too much car.

## TRADE-IN MAXIMIZATION

### Know Your Value First
- **KBB Trade-In Value**: Most widely used reference. Get the range.
- **Edmunds Appraisal**: Second opinion on value.
- **CarMax offer**: Free, no-obligation appraisal. Offers are valid 7 days. Use this as your floor.
- **Carvana instant offer**: Online offer based on condition. Often competitive.

### Trade-In vs. Private Sale
- **Trade-in**: Convenient. Tax advantage in most states (trade-in value reduces taxable price of new car). Lower price than private sale.
- **Private sale**: 10-20% more than trade-in but more effort (listing, showing, test drives, payment handling).
- **Math**: If trade-in = $15,000 and private sale = $18,000, the $3,000 difference minus tax savings of $1,000-1,500 = $1,500-2,000 actual gain for significant effort.

## TOTAL COST OF OWNERSHIP (BEYOND PURCHASE PRICE)

### The Real Cost of a Car
Purchase price is only part of the equation:
- **Insurance**: Varies dramatically by vehicle. Get quotes BEFORE buying. Sports cars, luxury vehicles, high-theft vehicles = expensive to insure.
- **Fuel**: Calculate annual fuel cost. MPG matters — a 25 MPG car vs. 35 MPG car at 15K miles/year and $3.50/gallon = $600/year difference.
- **Maintenance**: Luxury brands (BMW, Mercedes, Audi) cost 2-3x more to maintain than Japanese brands (Toyota, Honda). Check RepairPal for estimated annual maintenance costs by model.
- **Depreciation**: The biggest cost. Toyota and Honda depreciate least. Luxury brands depreciate most. A $50K BMW loses $25K in 3 years. A $35K Toyota loses $10K.
- **Tires**: Performance/staggered tire setups cost 2-3x more than standard. Factor in.
- **Registration/taxes**: Some states charge annual property tax on vehicles. Factor in.

### Most Reliable Brands (Long-Term Cost Savings)
Based on Consumer Reports, JD Power, and repair frequency data:
1. **Toyota / Lexus**: Benchmark reliability. Lowest long-term ownership cost.
2. **Honda / Acura**: Close second. Slightly more engaging to drive.
3. **Mazda**: Underrated reliability. Excellent driving dynamics.
4. **Subaru**: Strong AWD, good reliability (some model-specific issues).
5. **Hyundai / Kia**: Dramatically improved quality. 10-year/100K powertrain warranty is industry-leading.

## ELECTRIC VEHICLES (EVs)

### EV Buying Intelligence
- **Federal tax credit**: Up to $7,500 for qualifying EVs (new) and $4,000 for qualifying used EVs. Income limits and vehicle price caps apply. Check fueleconomy.gov/feg/tax2024.shtml for current eligible vehicles.
- **State incentives**: Many states offer additional rebates ($500-5,000). Check dsireusa.org for your state.
- **Utility incentives**: Some utilities offer EV charging rebates or time-of-use rates (cheap overnight charging).
- **Total cost of ownership**: EVs have lower fuel costs (electricity vs. gas) and lower maintenance (no oil changes, less brake wear). Often cheaper to own over 5+ years despite higher purchase price.
- **Charging infrastructure**: Check PlugShare app for charger availability on your routes and near your home. Home charging (Level 2, 240V) is ideal.

### EV Timing
- **New EV models are flooding the market (2024-2026)**: Competition is driving prices down. Inventory is increasing.
- **Year-end deals**: Same dynamics as ICE vehicles but amplified — dealers want to move EV inventory.
- **Used EV market**: Depreciation on early EVs was steep. Used Chevy Bolts, Nissan Leafs, and even Teslas offer extreme value.

## CAR INSURANCE OPTIMIZATION

### How to Save
- **Shop annually**: Insurance rates change. Get 3-5 quotes every year at renewal.
- **Comparison tools**: The Zebra, Policygenius, Gabi — compare multiple carriers in minutes.
- **Bundle**: Home + auto bundle saves 5-25% at most insurers.
- **Increase deductible**: Raising deductible from $500 to $1,000 can save 15-30% on premium. Make sure you have the deductible in savings.
- **Discounts to ask about**: Multi-car, good driver, low mileage, defensive driving course, good student, military, professional organization, paperless billing, autopay.
- **Usage-based programs**: State Farm Drive Safe, Progressive Snapshot, Allstate Drivewise. If you're a safe, low-mileage driver, these can save 10-30%.
- **Drop unnecessary coverage**: If your car is worth less than $5,000, comprehensive and collision coverage may not be worth the premium.

## MAINTENANCE COST REDUCTION

### DIY Savings
Simple maintenance anyone can do (YouTube tutorials for your specific vehicle):
- **Oil change**: Shop charges $50-100. DIY cost: $25-35 (oil + filter). Saves $75-200/year.
- **Air filter**: Shop charges $30-50. DIY cost: $10-15. 2-minute job.
- **Cabin air filter**: Shop charges $50-80. DIY cost: $10-20. 5-minute job.
- **Wiper blades**: Shop charges $30-50 installed. DIY cost: $15-25. 2-minute job.
- **Battery replacement**: Shop charges $200-300. DIY cost: $100-150 (Interstate from Costco). 10-minute job.
- **Brake pads**: Shop charges $200-400/axle. DIY cost: $30-60/axle. Requires basic tools and YouTube education.

### Where to Get Service
- **Independent mechanic**: 30-50% cheaper than dealership for out-of-warranty work. Find a trusted one via recommendations, RepairPal, or Mechanic Advisor.
- **Dealership**: Required for warranty work. Optional for everything else. Service coupons frequently available on dealer websites.
- **Costco Tire Center**: Best tire value (covered in main KB).
- **AutoZone / O'Reilly free services**: Free battery test, alternator test, OBD code reading, loaner tools.

## GOLDEN RULES: VEHICLES

1. **Never negotiate monthly payment. Always negotiate total out-the-door price.**
2. **Get pre-approved financing BEFORE visiting the dealer.**
3. **Buy end of month, end of quarter, or end of year** for maximum leverage.
4. **2-3 year old CPO is the sweet spot** for value vs. reliability.
5. **Get 3-5 insurance quotes annually.** Loyalty is expensive.
6. **Total cost of ownership matters more than purchase price.** Factor maintenance, fuel, insurance, depreciation.
7. **Toyota/Honda for long-term reliability.** The data is clear and consistent.
8. **Trade-in to CarMax/Carvana first for a floor price** before accepting dealer trade-in offer.
9. **Every used car needs a pre-purchase inspection.** $100-200 can save thousands.
10. **Check EV tax credits.** $7,500 federal + state incentives can make EVs cost-competitive or cheaper than gas vehicles.
