## The Two Paths

### Path 1 — EET Degree (Nashville State Community College)

**What it teaches:** How electricity works — from electrons in a wire to programmable logic controllers in factories.

**Delivery:** Night classes + online courses (designed for working adults)

**Accreditation:** ATMAE (Association of Technology, Management, and Applied Engineering)

**Total Credits:** ~60

**Duration:** 3–4 years part-time (6–9 credits per semester)

**Total Tuition (full price):** ~$10,400  
**Your Cost:** $0 (TN Reconnect Grant covers all tuition)

---

### Path 2 — ASE Certification (Self-Study)

**What it is:** Industry-standard credential for automotive technicians. 9 core areas (A1–A9); pass all 9 = **ASE Master Technician**.

**Study method:** Self-study using books, online practice tests, YouTube (ScannerDanner, South Main Auto, ChrisFix), and hands-on work on own vehicles.

**Testing:** Computer-based exams at Prometric test centers (Nashville location available).

**Cost per test:** ~$50–$60  
**Total cost for all 9 A-series:** ~$565

**Experience requirement:** 
- 2 years hands-on work, OR
- 1 year hands-on + 2-year degree (EET qualifies)

**Key insight:** Working on your own vehicles (Wrangler, Grand Cherokee, Grom) counts toward experience. Keep a repair log.

## Curriculum — EET Degree Topics

| Subject Area | What You Learn | Real-World Application |
|---|---|---|
| **DC Circuit Analysis** | Ohm's law, Kirchhoff's laws, series/parallel circuits, Thevenin's theorem | The grammar of electricity |
| **AC Circuit Analysis** | Alternating current, impedance, reactance, power factor, transformers | How house wiring, shops, power grid work |
| **Electronic Devices** | Diodes, transistors, op-amps, power supplies | Building blocks of every circuit board |
| **Digital Electronics & Ladder Logic** | Binary, logic gates, computer-controlled systems | Foundation for automation |
| **Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs)** | Programming industrial computers | $70k+/year skill; automates manufacturing |
| **AC & DC Motors and Generators** | Motor theory, wiring, troubleshooting | Directly applicable to EV work |
| **Power Electronics & Distribution** | How power moves from plant to panel to outlet | Electrical infrastructure |
| **Electrical Codes** | NEC compliance, safety, installation standards | Legal and safe installations |
| **Computer Aided Drafting (CAD)** | Reading and creating electrical drawings | Technical documentation |
| **General Education** | English, math (algebra/trig), social science, humanities | Associate degree requirements |

## How Night School Works at Nashville State

### Delivery Formats

**Online courses** — Gen ed courses fully online, asynchronous. Do them on your schedule (10 PM Tuesday, 6 AM Saturday, whenever).

**Evening sections** — EET technical courses offered evenings at White Bridge campus (120 White Bridge Pike — 15 min from downtown Nashville).

**Accelerated 7-week terms** — Semesters split into two 7-week blocks. Take one intense course at a time instead of juggling multiple.

**Weekend College** — Select courses available on weekends.

**Part-time pace options:**
- 6 credits/semester (2 classes) = 3.5–4 years to completion
- 9 credits/semester (3 classes) = 2.5–3 years to completion

### Credit for Previous Experience (PLA)

Nashville State offers **Prior Learning Assessment** credit for knowledge from work or self-learning. 

**Potential credit sources:**
- Construction PM experience
- Self-hosted tech infrastructure
- Electrical knowledge from managing commercial construction projects

**Action:** Ask about PLA during advising appointment.
