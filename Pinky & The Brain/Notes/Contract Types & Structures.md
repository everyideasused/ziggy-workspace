# Contract Types & Structures

> Cross-cutting reference for [[Construction PM Knowledge Base]]

---

## Payment Structure Types

### Stipulated Sum (Lump Sum / Fixed Price)
- Contractor agrees to complete all work for a single fixed price
- **Risk:** Contractor bears cost risk; owner gets price certainty
- **Best for:** Well-defined scope, complete CDs, minimal expected changes
- **AIA Form:** A101
- **Key consideration:** Contractor's profit is the gap between bid price and actual costs — incentivizes efficiency but also incentivizes cutting corners or aggressive change order claims

### Cost Plus Fee
- Owner reimburses contractor for all documented costs, plus a fee (fixed or percentage)
- **Risk:** Owner bears cost risk; no incentive for contractor to control costs
- **Best for:** Undefined or evolving scope, emergency work, trusted relationships
- **AIA Form:** A103 (Cost Plus Fee, no GMP)
- **Key consideration:** Requires robust cost tracking and audit rights

### Cost Plus with Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP)
- Like cost plus, but with a contractual ceiling. Contractor absorbs overruns above GMP.
- **Risk:** Shared — contractor bears risk above GMP; savings may be shared or returned to owner
- **Best for:** Projects where scope is mostly defined but some unknowns remain; CMAR delivery
- **AIA Form:** A102 (Cost Plus with GMP)
- **Key provisions:** Contingency allocation, savings-sharing clause, open-book accounting, audit rights

### Time & Materials (T&M)
- Owner pays for actual labor hours at agreed rates, plus materials at cost (plus markup)
- **Risk:** Owner bears cost risk; usually capped with a Not-to-Exceed (NTE) clause
- **Best for:** Small projects, repairs, investigation work where scope is truly unknown
- **Key consideration:** Always include NTE clause to prevent runaway costs

### Unit Price
- Payment based on actual quantities of work at pre-agreed unit rates
- **Risk:** Owner bears quantity risk; contractor bears efficiency risk per unit
- **Best for:** Civil/infrastructure (earthwork, paving, pipe installation), maintenance/repair contracts
- **Key consideration:** Commonly used for subcontract trades even within other contract structures

## Standard Contract Form Families

### AIA (American Institute of Architects)
- Most widely used in commercial construction in the US
- **Key documents:**
  - A101: Owner-Contractor (Stipulated Sum)
  - A102: Owner-Contractor (Cost Plus with GMP)
  - A103: Owner-Contractor (Cost Plus Fee, no GMP)
  - A133: Owner-CM as Constructor (GMP)
  - A141: Owner-Design-Builder
  - A201: General Conditions of the Contract for Construction (incorporated by reference into A-series)
  - B101: Owner-Architect
  - C191: Multi-Party Agreement (IPD)
  - G702/G703: Application and Certificate for Payment / Continuation Sheet (SOV)
  - G704: Certificate of Substantial Completion

### ConsensusDocs
- Collaboratively drafted by owner, contractor, and design associations
- Considered more balanced than AIA (less architect-centric)
- Growing adoption, especially with contractor organizations

### EJCDC (Engineers Joint Contract Documents Committee)
- Common for infrastructure, utility, and civil engineering projects
- Engineer (not architect) is the design professional
- **Key document:** C-700 (Standard General Conditions for Construction)

### DBIA (Design-Build Institute of America)
- Specifically for design-build delivery
- Alternative to AIA A141

## Critical Contract Provisions

### Change Order Management
- Clear process for identifying, pricing, and approving changes
- PCO → COR → Change Order approval chain
- Time and cost impact must both be addressed
- CCD (Construction Change Directive) for owner-directed changes before pricing is agreed

### Retainage
- Typically 5-10% of each progress payment withheld until project completion
- State laws vary on retainage limits and release requirements
- Reduction of retainage at substantial completion is common (e.g., 10% → 5%)
- Retainage release typically requires: final lien waivers, CO, punch list completion

### Liquidated Damages
- Pre-agreed daily amount the contractor pays for late completion
- Must be a reasonable estimate of actual damages (not a penalty)
- Often paired with a bonus for early completion

### Insurance Requirements
- Builder's Risk (property damage during construction)
- Commercial General Liability (CGL)
- Workers' Compensation
- Professional Liability (E&O — for design professionals)
- Umbrella/Excess Liability
- Owner as Additional Insured on contractor's policies

### Bonding
- **Payment Bond:** Guarantees subcontractors and suppliers will be paid
- **Performance Bond:** Guarantees contractor will complete the work
- Typically required on public projects and large private projects
- Bond amount = 100% of contract value (each)

### Dispute Resolution
- **Negotiation** → **Mediation** → **Arbitration** or **Litigation**
- AIA contracts default to mediation, then binding arbitration
- Some owners modify to require litigation instead of arbitration
- Standing neutral / dispute review board for large projects

### Warranty
- Standard 1-year workmanship warranty from substantial completion
- Equipment manufacturer warranties (vary by product)
- Extended warranties available for specific systems (roofing, waterproofing)

## Contract Selection Guide for the Agent

| Project Characteristic | Recommended Contract Type |
|-----------------------|--------------------------|
| Well-defined scope, competitive market | Stipulated Sum (A101) |
| Scope mostly defined, some unknowns | GMP (A102/A133) |
| Scope undefined, emergency, investigation | T&M with NTE |
| Infrastructure with variable quantities | Unit Price (EJCDC C-700) |
| Fast-track, single accountability | Design-Build (A141) |
| Complex, collaborative, innovative | IPD (C191) |
