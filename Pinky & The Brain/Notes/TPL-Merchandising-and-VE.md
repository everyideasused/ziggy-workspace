# WF-Value-Engineering — VE Methodology & Swap Library

> VE is not scope reduction. It is equivalent value at lower cost. The best VE happens at Design Development — not after GMP. Every item needs: description, savings, scope impact, and decision deadline.

---

## VE Timing and Impact

| Phase | VE Potential | Effort | Recommended? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-design / program | Very high | Low | ✅ Best time |
| Schematic design | High | Low-Medium | ✅ Excellent |
| Design development | High | Medium | ✅ Good |
| 50% CDs | Moderate | Medium-High | ⚠️ Some redesign |
| 100% CDs | Low | High | ⚠️ Change order risk |
| During construction | Very low | Very high | ❌ Change orders |

## VE Swap Library — By Division

### Division 03 — Concrete
| Original | VE Alternative | Typical Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ceramic/stone tile floor (kitchen) | Epoxy coating | $6–$12/SF | Equivalent durability; different look |
| Custom polished concrete w/ scoring | Standard polished concrete | $3–$6/SF | Less decorative detail |
| New slab (if existing can be used) | Patch and prep existing | $5–$10/SF | Requires survey to confirm |

### Division 06 — Millwork / Casework
| Original | VE Alternative | Typical Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Painted MDF casework | Plastic laminate casework | $80–$200/LF | Same function; different aesthetic |
| Custom millwork (built) | Semi-custom modular | $100–$300/LF | Lead time may also improve |
| Solid surface countertops | Plastic laminate countertop | $30–$80/LF | Durability trade-off |
| Complex profile details | Simplified profiles | $15–$40/LF | Aesthetic impact only |

### Division 07 — Roofing / Insulation
| Original | VE Alternative | Typical Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80-mil TPO | 60-mil TPO | $1.50–$3/SF | Reduced life expectancy |
| Built-up roof (BUR) | TPO | $2–$5/SF | TPO standard; performance equivalent |
| High R-value rigid insulation | Code-minimum insulation | $1–$3/SF | Evaluate vs. energy cost savings |

### Division 08 — Openings
| Original | VE Alternative | Typical Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thermally broken storefront | Standard aluminum storefront | $15–$30/SF | Energy performance trade-off |
| Wood interior doors | Hollow metal doors | $300–$700/door | Different aesthetic; same function |
| Custom hardware sets | Standard commercial hardware | $150–$400/door | Aesthetic only |
| Motorized overhead door | Manual with hardware | $1,500–$3,000/door | Convenience only |

### Division 09 — Finishes (Largest VE opportunity)
| Original | VE Alternative | Typical Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Large-format porcelain tile (24"×24") | Standard 12"×12" ceramic | $10–$20/SF | Aesthetic impact |
| Luxury vinyl tile (premium) | Standard LVT | $3–$6/SF | Durability may vary |
| Sheet vinyl (seamless) | LVT with heat-welded seams | $2–$5/SF | Comparable infection control |
| Carpet (broadloom) | Carpet tile | $5–$12/SY | Easier replacement; different look |
| Custom open-to-structure ceiling | Standard ACT | $3–$8/SF | Significant aesthetic change |
| GWB ceiling (painted) | ACT | $2–$5/SF | Acoustic improvement actually |
| Custom ACT tiles | Standard 2×4 white ACT | $4–$12/SF | Aesthetic impact |
| Decorative wall panels | Paint with feature wall | $10–$30/SF of wall | Aesthetic impact |

### Division 23 — HVAC
| Original | VE Alternative | Typical Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Custom AHU units | Packaged RTUs | $8–$18/SF | Function equivalent; less flexibility |
| Full BAS/DDC controls (small project) | Basic thermostatic controls | $3–$6/SF | Reduced monitoring capability |
| High-efficiency RTUs (above code) | Code-minimum RTU efficiency | $1,000–$3,000/unit | Evaluate vs. energy savings payback |
| Ductwork (sheet metal, all new) | Reuse existing ductwork where feasible | Site-specific | Requires survey; risk of hidden conditions |

### Division 26 — Electrical
| Original | VE Alternative | Typical Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Designer LED fixtures | Standard commercial LED | $5–$15/SF | Aesthetic impact; same lumens |
| Full lighting controls (beyond code) | Code-minimum controls | $2–$5/SF | Energy/comfort trade-off |
| Decorative pendants | Standard troffers | $8–$18/SF | Aesthetic only |
| Underfloor conduit routing | Overhead conduit routing | Site-specific | May affect access; aesthetic |

### Structural / Systems
| Original | VE Alternative | Typical Savings | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wide flange steel beams | Open web steel joists | $4–$10/SF | Span and load dependent |
| Concrete slab over deck | Metal deck (no fill where code allows) | $3–$6/SF | Load and fire rating dependent |
| Tilt-up concrete walls | Metal panel or EIFS | $5–$12/SF of wall | Aesthetic; durability trade-off |

## VE Log Template

Maintain a running VE log throughout design:

| # | Division | Description | Gross Savings | Net Savings* | Scope Impact | Decision By | Decision | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| VE-001 | 09 | LVT → standard VCT (dining) | $18,000 | $18,000 | Aesthetic change | [date] | Approved | Complete |
| VE-002 | 23 | Eliminate BAS; basic controls | $12,000 | $12,000 | No monitoring | [date] | Rejected | Closed |
| VE-003 | 06 | Laminate vs. painted casework | $24,000 | $24,000 | Minor aesthetic | [date] | Open | |

*Net savings = gross savings minus any coordination or redesign cost

**VE decision deadline rule:** Every VE item must have a decision date tied to the design/procurement schedule. An "open" VE item past its deadline becomes a change order.
