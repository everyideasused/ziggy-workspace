## **New Orleans Jazz Fest Trip | April 22-27, 2026**

### **Key Info**
- **Jazz Fest Schedule:** April 23-26 (Weekend 1) + April 30-May 3 (Weekend 2) at the Fair Grounds
- **Your Window:** April 22-27 catches the first full weekend, plus 3 days between
- **Lineup Highlights:** Eagles, Stevie Nicks, Rod Stewart, Kings of Leon, The Black Keys, Jon Batiste, Nas, St. Vincent, Tyler Childers, Lainey Wilson
- **Tickets:** 4-day weekend pass ~$319 + fees (Louisiana residents get discount); single-day tickets sell in March

---

## **Day-by-Day Itinerary**

### **Wednesday, April 22 — Arrival Day**
- **Afternoon:** Fly into NOLA (book flight)
- **Evening:** Check into hotel (book lodging — recommend French Quarter or Marigny/Bywater for walkability)
- **Dinner:** **Meals from the Heart Cafe** (vegan paradise — their beignets + gumbo are legendary)
- **Nightlife:** Walk Frenchmen Street — live jazz every night, zero cover charge at most clubs (Snug Harbor, The Spotted Cat, Frenchmen Street bars)

### **Thursday, April 23 — Jazz Fest Day 1**
- **Morning:** Fair Grounds opens ~10am. Grab a map + mobile app
- **Strategy:** Mix iconic stages (main stage headliners) with smaller stages (authentic NOLA jazz, funk, brass bands)
- **Pro tip:** The "Jazz Tent" has traditional New Orleans jazz all day
- **Lunch:** Fair Grounds has food vendors — pre-scout vegan options (they usually have veggie plates)
- **Late afternoon:** Rest at hotel or explore nearby neighborhood
- **Dinner:** **Seed** (tofu po'boy + vegan gumbo — seriously good)
- **Evening:** Bourbon Street OR Frenchmen Street jazz (your call: touristy vs. local)

### **Friday, April 24 — Jazz Fest Day 2**
- **Morning:** Fair Grounds again
- **Different strategy:** Hit the stages you missed yesterday; catch artist interviews/panel discussions (they happen throughout the day)
- **Lunch:** Try **G's Tasty Pizza** (vegan pizza + their rotating vegan raw cakes)
- **Afternoon date activity:** **New Orleans Botanical Garden** (City Park) — romantic, beautiful, great for couples photos
- **Dinner:** **Sneaky Pickle** (Bywater neighborhood, locally-sourced, excellent vegan options + cocktails)
- **Evening:** Live music crawl — hit 2-3 different jazz clubs

### **Saturday, April 25 — Jazz Fest Day 3 + City Exploration**
- **Morning:** Fair Grounds (final day of Weekend 1)
- **Afternoon:** Leave early, explore neighborhoods:
  - **French Quarter:** Historic, touristy but iconic — walk Royal Street, see antique shops, Cathedral Square
  - **Marigny/Bywater:** Artsy, colorful, local vibe — art galleries, vintage shops, street art
  - **Garden District:** Gorgeous mansions, tree-lined streets, romantic walk
- **Dinner:** **Parkway Poor Boys** (they have vegan Beyond Burger option — local classic)
- **Evening:** Rooftop bar or stay in Bourbon Street area

### **Sunday, April 26 — Jazz Fest Final + Relaxation**
- **Morning:** Optional: Sleep in, or catch early Jazz Fest sessions
- **Early afternoon:** Leave Fair Grounds by 2pm
- **Activity:** **Mississippi Riverfront walk** or **Audubon Park** (sunset picnic with wine)
- **Dinner:** Date night — **Bouligny Tavern + Lilette** (cocktails then dinner, romantic classic)

### **Monday, April 27 — Off-Fest Day (Between Weekends)**
- **All day:** Explore the city without Jazz Fest pressure
  - **Frenchmen Street jazz crawl** (no festival crowds)
  - **Museum option:** Presbytere Museum (Mardi Gras history) or New Orleans Jazz Museum
  - **Food tour:** Hit multiple vegan spots you haven't tried yet
- **Evening:** Dinner at a spot you've been wanting to try; live music somewhere low-key

## **Pro Tips**

1. **Jazz Fest pacing:** Don't try to see everything. Grab a schedule, pick 5-6 must-see artists, build around those
2. **Wear comfy shoes:** Fair Grounds is huge — you'll walk miles
3. **Weather:** April in NOLA = warm + humid (80s–90s). Bring sunscreen, hydrate constantly
4. **Crawfish season:** It's peak crawfish in April — try it at least once
5. **Vegan dining:** Most NOLA food is seafood-heavy, but the vegan spots are genuinely good (not afterthought options)
6. **Music discovery:** Between Jazz Fest stages, catch unknown artists — you'll find gems
7. **Between weekends:** Don't force yourself back to Fair Grounds April 27 if you're tired — explore the city instead
